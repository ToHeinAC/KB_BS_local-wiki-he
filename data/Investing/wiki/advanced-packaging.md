# Advanced Packaging

Advanced packaging refers to the sophisticated methods used to assemble and interconnect multiple semiconductor dies to enhance system-level performance, particularly as front-end transistor scaling reaches diminishing returns [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Importance and Drivers
Advanced packaging is considered a prime lever for system performance, moving beyond the traditional role of merely protecting finished dies. The increasing complexity of assembly, coupled with the need for higher bandwidth and improved electrical and thermal reliability under harsh conditions, drives innovation in this area [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Key Methodologies
The industry is adopting several advanced techniques:

*   **Chiplet Architecture:** This approach involves assembling heterogeneous dies, which improves scalability, cost efficiency, and yield by breaking down large chips into smaller, manageable components [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **3D Packaging:** This involves stacking dies vertically for higher integration. The structure typically includes TSV-Connected Die, Logic, and Memory layers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **2.5D Packaging:** This method places multiple dies horizontally on an interposer, which can integrate Memory, Logic, and a Substrate [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Flip-chip Packaging:** This technique inverts the die for direct substrate connection [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Industry Leadership
Foundries and IDMs are expected to control about two-thirds of advanced packaging investments, driving major breakthroughs. OSATs are expanding into fan-out and 2.5D packaging to handle overflow volume [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Market Growth
The global advanced packaging market is projected to grow, with the 2.5D/3D segment showing a CAGR of +10.6% through 2030F, reaching $76 Billion [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Strategic Importance
Advanced packaging is becoming a critical component of the semiconductor supply chain, alongside front-end scaling, especially in regions like Taiwan, Korea, and the US, which are investing heavily in 2.5D/3D capacity [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
