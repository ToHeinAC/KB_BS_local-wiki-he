# Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) refers to a hypothetical intelligence capable of understanding, learning, and applying its intelligence to solve any problem that a human being can, across a broad range of tasks [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Development Timeline and Milestones
*   **Partial AGI:** Partial AGI is anticipated within 2 to 5 years [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Full AGI:** Complete AGI is expected in over 10 years [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Semiconductor Requirements for AGI
The progress toward AGI is critically dependent on semiconductor advancements, which must provide the necessary computational muscle to process vast, high-quality datasets [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

*   **Processing Power:** Ever-larger AI models demand faster, more efficient logic dies built on smaller nodes and linked with high-density 2.5D or 3D packaging [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Memory:** High-bandwidth, low-latency memory is essential for storing and moving the massive datasets used in training [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Novel Architectures:** Research is shifting toward neuromorphic and in-memory computing (PIM) approaches. PIM prototypes place compute elements near or inside DRAM arrays, which helps reduce data-movement energy and delay [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Hardware Backbone:** These semiconductor advances form the backbone for moving from specialized AI today to more general, capable intelligence tomorrow [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Applications and Integration
The integration of AI into physical systems, such as robotics, requires specialized hardware:
*   **Edge Computing:** NPU-based edge computing allows robots to process data autonomously even without a stable network connection [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Sensors:** CMOS image sensors, Time-of-Flight (ToF), and LiDAR technologies are crucial for enabling robots to "see" and map their surroundings [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
### Infrastructure Demand
The rapid development and deployment of AI systems are fundamentally linked to power consumption and data processing capacity.
