# Application-Specific Integrated Circuits (ASICs)

Application-Specific Integrated Circuits (ASICs) are highly customized semiconductor chips designed to meet the specific requirements of a single product or a very limited set of customers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Characteristics and Advantages
ASICs are purpose-built, offering superior performance, power efficiency, and reliability when compared to more flexible general-purpose counterparts [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Customization:** They are tailored to perform specific tasks, which allows for optimization that general-purpose chips cannot match [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Market Driver:** The high costs and overspecification concerns associated with Application-Specific System-level Products (ASSPs) have driven increased demand for ASICs [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Challenges
The primary challenge associated with ASICs is cost competitiveness. Due to their highly customized nature, securing sufficient production volume is necessary to offset high development costs and maintain price competitiveness [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Comparison to General-Purpose Chips
ASICs represent a trade-off: they sacrifice the versatility of general-purpose chips for specialized efficiency. While general-purpose chips support a broad range of workloads and software updates, this versatility often results in lower specialized efficiency [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
