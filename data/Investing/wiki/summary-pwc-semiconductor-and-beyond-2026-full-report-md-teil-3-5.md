# PwC Semiconductor and Beyond 2026 Full Report (Part 3/5)

This document summarizes key findings from the PwC Semiconductor and Beyond 2026 Full Report, detailing market trends, technological shifts, and regional strategies across the semiconductor ecosystem through the end of 2025.

## Supply Chain Overview
The semiconductor supply chain is characterized by escalating complexity and volatility, driven by geopolitical factors, localization efforts, and increased customer expectations [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md]. The supply chain is broken down into several critical categories:
*   **Equipment:** Machines used in semiconductor manufacturing, such as EUV lithography [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Material:** Essential substances for chip fabrication, including wafers, chemicals, and gases [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Design:** Defining chip function and circuit design [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Packaging and Testing:** Interconnecting and packaging chips into a usable form and testing [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Fabrication:** Includes IP and EDA [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].

## Design, IP, and EDA Trends
The global semiconductor design market is experiencing intense competition due to increasing complexity at advanced process nodes. The costs associated with IP licensing and EDA tool usage have risen sharply, making cost reduction a critical competitive factor [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].

*   **Design Shift:** There is a notable shift from general-purpose chips to application-specific semiconductors, with a growing emphasis on low-power consumption and thermal management [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Regional Focus:** The United States concentrates on AI and HPC, China aims for broad in-house self-sufficiency, Europe focuses on wide-bandgap power chips, Japan targets automotive semiconductors and image sensors, and Korea combines memory/foundry strengths with design expertise for mobile, AI, and automotive applications [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Workforce Gap:** By 2030, over 300,000 semiconductor design engineers may be required, compared to the current figure of around 200,000 [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].

### Logic Chip Architectures
Logic semiconductors are categorized as General-purpose (flexible, but less specialized efficiency) and Application-Specific (purpose-built for superior performance and power efficiency) [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **ASPs vs. ASICs:** The application-specific category includes ASSPs (tailored for a broader domain) and ASICs (highly customized for a specific product/limited customer set). Demand for ASICs may increase as high costs and overspecification concerns in ASSPs persist [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **P-P-A Triangle:** Engineers balance performance, power efficiency, and area. The industry is moving toward improving performance per watt, utilizing techniques like dynamic voltage-frequency scaling, chiplet partitioning, and AI-assisted thermal management [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].

### Intellectual Property (IP)
Semiconductor IP refers to pre-designed blocks. Interface IP (for data exchange/connectivity) and Processor IP (CPUs, MCUs, cores) are the most prominent segments [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **IP Market Share (2030F):** Processor IP is projected to hold 47.5% of the market, followed by Interface IP at 28.3% [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Cost Containment:** IP license & royalty can account for 25–35% of a leading project’s budget. Cost-saving measures include building reusable in-house IP, using chiplets, adopting open-source like RISC-V, and strengthening cloud collaboration [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].

### Electronic Design Automation (EDA)
EDA tools allow engineers to model and verify designs before physical mask writing. The market is dominated by established vendors (Synopsys, Cadence, Siemens) who benefit from proven reliability and high technology barriers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md]. The depth of proprietary datasets used to train AI-based EDA tools is becoming a key competitive edge [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].

## Fabrication and Capacity Shifts
Global fab spending from 2024 to 2030 is projected to exceed $1.5 trillion [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Regional Spending:** The US and China are leading spenders. China focuses on self-sufficiency using mature nodes, while the US invests heavily in advanced facilities [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Wafer Size Trends:** While 300 mm remains the workhorse for advanced node logic and DRAM/3D NAND ramps, 200 mm and 150 mm fabs remain active for specialty areas like SiC, GaN, analog, and imaging sensors [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **DAO Growth:** Discrete, Analog, and Optoelectronic (DAO) semiconductors show steady growth, with SiC and GaN leading the charge, particularly in EVs and power conversion [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].

## Memory and Advanced Components
*   **HBM:** The surge in generative AI has made High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM) critical. HBM addresses data bottlenecks by stacking multiple DRAM dies on a base-logic die using Through-Silicon Vias (TSVs), delivering terabytes-per-second bandwidth [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md]. The HBM market is projected to grow at a CAGR of +27.8% from 2024 to 2030 [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Memory Cycles:** The memory market is prone to boom-and-bust cycles. Future cycles may be moderated by tighter supply chain analytics or fueled by explosive AI workloads and autonomous vehicle data capture [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].

## Transistor Technology Evolution
The industry is moving through transistor generations: Planar $\rightarrow$ FinFET (at 28 nm) $\rightarrow$ Gate-All-Around (GAA) nanosheet devices (at 16 nm) $\rightarrow$ Complementary FETs (CFETs) or Forksheet (for sub-5 nm) [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 3/5].md].
