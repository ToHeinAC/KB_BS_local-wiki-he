# Autonomous Vehicles

Autonomous Vehicles (AVs) refer to vehicles capable of operating without continuous human intervention, ranging from driver assistance systems to fully autonomous operation [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Levels of Automation
The industry standardizes automation levels from Level 0 (No automation) to Level 5 (Full automation), where the vehicle can operate anywhere without a human fallback [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Current Status:** Most production cars are at Level 2 (requiring human supervision for steering and braking in defined scenarios). Level 3 (hands-free driving on specific highways) is offered by a handful of pilot models [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Future Outlook:** Limited Level 4 services (e.g., robo-taxis or hub-to-hub freight) are expected to scale in select cities around 2030. Level 5 is projected to arrive much later, potentially in the 2040s or beyond [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Technological Requirements
Achieving high levels of autonomy requires convergence across multiple domains:
*   **On-board Systems:** High-performance compute SoCs, multi-modal sensor suites (camera, radar, LiDAR), robust software stacks, and precise GNSS/HD maps are necessary [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Infrastructure:** This includes low-latency 5G/6G networks, edge/cloud data hubs, and Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) units [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Regulation:** Safety certification, cybersecurity rules, and clear liability frameworks are crucial for mass adoption [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Impact on Semiconductors
The shift to autonomy significantly increases the semiconductor requirement per vehicle.
*   **Market Size:** Conventional vehicles typically contain 200 to 300 semiconductors, whereas autonomous vehicles at Level 3 and above could require more than 1,000 [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Chip Requirements:** Demand is growing for high-performance computing chips (like Domain Control Units or DCUs), sensors, and communication chips for V2X [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Design Shift:** For higher levels of autonomy, specialized chips tailored to each company are likely, potentially leading to more OEMs designing and producing their own chips, moving away from standard industry-standard components [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 4/5].md].
