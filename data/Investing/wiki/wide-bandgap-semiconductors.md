# Wide-Bandgap Semiconductors (WBG)

Wide-Bandgap (WBG) semiconductor materials, specifically Silicon Carbide (SiC) and Gallium Nitride (GaN), are gaining significant traction in power electronics due to their superior operational characteristics compared to traditional Silicon (Si) [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Advantages Over Silicon
WBG materials are favored because they can operate at:
*   Higher temperatures [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   Higher voltages [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   Faster switching speeds [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Material Specific Applications
*   **Silicon Carbide (SiC):** SiC is highly effective for high-voltage and high-current applications, making it ideal for electric vehicle (EV) traction inverters, renewable energy converters, and heavy industrial drives [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md]. Manufacturers are transitioning SiC wafers from 150-mm to 200-mm to improve cost and quality [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Gallium Nitride (GaN):** GaN excels in applications requiring rapid switching and compact size, such as USB-C fast chargers, laptop adapters, 5G base station power supplies, and high-frequency data center converters [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md]. GaN's structure also facilitates easy integration with standard CMOS control chips [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Supply Constraints
Despite the growing demand, supply limitations persist due to the costly and time-intensive nature of processes like crystal boule growth, wafer polishing, and advanced epitaxy [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md]. Short-term shortages are possible, particularly for 200-mm SiC substrates and high-voltage GaN wafers. Continued investment in larger wafers and improved yields is necessary to narrow the cost gap with silicon [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
