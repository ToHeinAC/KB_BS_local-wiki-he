# Hybrid Quantum Computing

Hybrid quantum computing describes the current computational landscape that combines quantum computers with classical supercomputers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Necessity and Function
The integration of classical and quantum resources is necessary because:
*   Supercomputers are typically employed in the processes required for quantum computation [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   Fault-tolerant quantum systems still rely on fast classical processors for tasks such as error correction, scheduling, and data pre/post-processing [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Semiconductor Requirements
To facilitate this hybrid model, semiconductor development must focus on interconnectivity and control:
*   **Interconnects:** There is a growing demand for semiconductors capable of enhancing data communication speed between quantum computers and supercomputers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Hardware Links:** Chip makers should supply high-bandwidth links that tie quantum modules to classical HPC clusters [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Control Logic:** Low-latency control ASICs are required for managing the classical side of the computation [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
