The source document provides an analysis of semiconductor demand across several key sectors, including smart home appliances, wearables, and general computing trends, based on PwC's "Semiconductor and beyond 2026" analysis [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Smart Home and Connected Appliances
The integration of AI features is driving demand for AI processors in the home-appliances market, even if shipments of traditional appliances do not grow exponentially [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md]. Key drivers include:
*   **AI Features:** AI is being integrated into TVs, robot vacuums, and refrigerators [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Power Efficiency:** Growing focus on efficiency, including standby mode regulations, is expected to increase demand for application processors designed for AI workloads, PMICs, and compact battery management ICs [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Hyperconnectivity:** Home appliances are becoming increasingly connected, creating a seamless smart-home experience. The Matter smart-home standard, introduced in 2022, enables communication between devices from different manufacturers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Connectivity Protocols:** Devices utilize multiple channels: Wi-fi for large, high-speed data transfers (e.g., smart TVs, smart refrigerators with displays); Bluetooth for short-range communication; and Thread, which is energy-efficient and suitable for battery-powered devices or smart speakers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].

**Market Projections (Home Appliances):**
*   **SoCs in AI home appliance (Unit: $ Billion):** Projected to grow from 6.4 in '24 to 12.6 in '25F, with a CAGR of +12.0% [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Connectivity ICs in home appliance (Unit: $ Billion):** Projected to grow from 4.2 in '24 to 8.1 in '25F, with a CAGR of +11.6% for the overall category [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Wearable Devices and Sensors
Wearables (including earphones, smartwatches, fitness bands, and AR/VR devices) are integral to daily life, driving rapid demand for sensors [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Sensor Types:** Key sensors include Inertial sensors (tracking movement speed), Magnetic field sensors (sensing body motion), and emerging non-invasive blood glucose sensors using saliva or photoacoustic methods [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Data Challenges:** Data collected from these sensors can be noisy and irregular due to biological fluctuations or external electromagnetic interference [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Industry Focus:** The semiconductor industry is focusing on advanced processors and wearable-specific SoCs to enable efficient sensor data processing [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].

**Market Projections (Wearables):**
*   **Sensor ICs in wearable devices (Unit: $ Billion):** Projected to grow with a CAGR of +10.7% from '24 to '30F [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Computing and AI Trends
The shift toward "edge AI" is a major growth driver, moving complex AI processing from the cloud directly onto personal devices [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **AI Processors:** The adoption of Neural Processing Units (NPUs)—dedicated AI processing cores integrated within an AP/SoC—is expected to accelerate, complementing GPUs, CPUs, and ISPs [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Memory:** LPDDR (low-power double data rate DRAM) is vital for balancing high-speed processing with power efficiency in mobile and PC devices. LPDDR 6 (expected in 2026) may achieve roughly 50% lower power consumption compared to LPDDR5 [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Smartphones/PCs:** AI-enabled smartphones and PCs are expected to continue driving the chip market forward, with AI-capable chip demand projected to grow significantly through 2030 [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Industrial and Energy Applications
Semiconductors are crucial across industrial sectors:
*   **Renewable Energy:** The transition requires semiconductors capable of surviving high-voltage environments, making Silicon Carbide (SiC) critical. Smart grids and Energy Storage Systems (ESS) rely on inverters, converters, and communication ICs [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Smart Manufacturing:** Automation advances from Level 0-1 to Level 5, increasing demand for connectivity ICs, sensors, and AI processors in smart factories [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Medical:** Semiconductors enable advanced diagnostics (CT, MRI, 3D ultrasound) and remote monitoring through biosensors, AI analytics, and robotics, requiring MEMS, GPUs, and communication ICs [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 2/5].md].
