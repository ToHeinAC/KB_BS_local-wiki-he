# PwC Semiconductor and Beyond 2026 Full Report (Part 5/5)

This document provides insights into the future trajectory of the semiconductor industry, focusing on emerging technologies such as quantum computing and Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI), and outlining the necessary semiconductor advancements to support these fields [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Quantum Computing Landscape

Quantum computers represent a novel technological frontier, though in the short term, they may rely significantly on conventional semiconductor chips [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

### Challenges and Requirements
*   **Decoherence:** Qubits can quickly decohere even with low noise, stemming from the unique superposition states inherent to quantum systems [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Error Correction:** To effectively utilize quantum computers, developing technologies for stabilizing qubits and implementing Quantum Error Correction (QEC) to rectify decohered qubits is critical [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Hybrid Computing:** Supercomputers are typically employed in these processes, giving rise to the current landscape of "hybrid quantum computing," which combines quantum computers and supercomputers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Semiconductor Demand:** There is a growing demand for semiconductors capable of executing complex QEC algorithms using the parallel computing capabilities of GPUs, as well as those that enhance data communication speed between quantum computers and supercomputers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

### Market Outlook
The report suggests that when quantum computers reach commercialization (generally considered to be when they incorporate more than one million qubits), it may not lead to the decline of the existing silicon semiconductor market. Instead, silicon semiconductors may serve as integral components within quantum computers, fostering a mutually beneficial relationship that expands the market for both technologies [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

### Path Forward for Quantum
*   **Balancing Resources:** Fault-tolerant quantum systems can still rely on fast classical processors for error correction, scheduling, and data pre/post-processing. Chip makers should align their roadmaps to supply low-latency control ASICs, cryo-compatible interfaces, and high-bandwidth links that tie quantum modules to classical HPC clusters [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Quantum Paths:** Two paths influence semiconductor strategies: Superconducting qubits, which can leverage existing semiconductor processes offering high scalability, or trapped ions, which have relatively lower error rates and can operate at room temperature [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) Technology

BCI technology aims to translate thoughts confined within the mind into external outputs and, conversely, allow external signals to influence the brain [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

### Functionality and History
*   **Mechanism:** The concept relies on electrodes to detect brain signals and electronic circuits for communication between the brain and external devices [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Applications:** BCI holds great potential for individuals with paralysis, sensory impairments, or neurological disorders [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Development:** BCI research began in the 1970s, and invasive technology like deep brain stimulation (DBS) is already used to treat epilepsy [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Types:**
    *   **Invasive BCI:** Successful trials have documented restoration of movement and communication in paralyzed patients. Implantation methods such as the vascular method are emerging to reduce surgical risks [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
    *   **Non-invasive BCI:** Though less precise, it is more accessible. EEG (Electroencephalogram)-based technology enables users to control robotic prosthetics, track stress levels, and interact with games with brainwaves [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

### Semiconductor Requirements for BCI
Processing the massive and complicated data input from the brain requires advanced, ultra-low-power components [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Processing:** Advanced, ultra-low-power AI accelerators, analog-to-digital converters, and amplifiers are required [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Implantable Devices:** For long-term implanted devices, reducing size, heat, and power consumption is crucial, increasing the demand for ASICs tailored for BCI [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Data Interpretation:** Low-latency processing requires SoCs including AI accelerators and Digital Signal Processors (DSPs) to interpret data into intent and actions [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Communication:** Low-power, short-distance communication chips, such as RFIC or low-power Bluetooth-low-energy (BLE), are needed for transmitting signals to external receivers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Market Expansion:** The market may increase demand for network chips, GPUs, AI accelerators, and SoCs capable of handling both graphical computations and signal processing [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].

### Strategic Considerations
*   **Security:** Since BCIs handle highly sensitive neural data, security technology is a competitive edge. Chip design must integrate data protection mechanisms at the early stage, defining key protection areas and incorporating encryption capabilities within SoC to prevent unauthorized access [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Regulation:** Invasive BCIs require rigorous safety validation from the government, necessitating chip design compliance with regulations such as those from the FDA [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Integration:** Seamless integration between hardware and software is mandatory, requiring companies to work closely with ecosystem partners to enhance interoperability from chip design to system implementation [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md [Teil 5/5].md].
