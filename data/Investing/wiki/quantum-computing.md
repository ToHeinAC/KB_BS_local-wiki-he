# Quantum Computing

Quantum computing represents a novel technological frontier that utilizes quantum mechanics principles to perform computations [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Core Concepts
*   **Qubits:** Unlike classical bits, quantum bits (qubits) utilize unique superposition states, which can lead to rapid decoherence even in the presence of low noise [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Quantum Error Correction (QEC):** To effectively utilize quantum computers, it is critical to develop technologies for stabilizing qubits and implementing QEC to rectify decohered qubits [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Computational Architectures
The field is characterized by the development of hybrid systems:
*   **Hybrid Quantum Computing:** This landscape combines quantum computers with supercomputers, as supercomputers are typically employed in the processes required for quantum computation [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Semiconductor Role:** The existing silicon semiconductor market is projected to remain integral to quantum computers, fostering a mutually beneficial relationship even when quantum computers reach commercialization (estimated at incorporating more than one million qubits) [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Technological Paths and Semiconductor Alignment
The development path is influenced by different qubit technologies:
1.  **Superconducting Qubits:** These can leverage existing semiconductor processes, offering high scalability [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
2.  **Trapped Ions:** These offer relatively lower error rates and can operate at room temperature [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

Chip makers are advised to align roadmaps to supply:
*   Low-latency control ASICs [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   Cryo-compatible interfaces [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   High-bandwidth links connecting quantum modules to classical HPC clusters [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Applications
Quantum technologies are expected to be applied in solving complex problems in logistics and supply chain management, accelerating drug development through molecular simulations, enhancing cybersecurity through advanced encryption methods, and improving artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
# Hybrid Quantum Computing
Hybrid quantum computing describes the current computational landscape that combines quantum computers with classical supercomputers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
## Necessity and Function
The integration of classical and quantum resources is necessary because:
*   Supercomputers are typically employed in the processes required for quantum computation [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   Fault-tolerant quantum systems still rely on fast classical processors for tasks such as error correction, scheduling, and data pre/post-processing [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
## Semiconductor Requirements
To facilitate this hybrid model, semiconductor development must focus on interconnectivity and control:
*   **Interconnects:** There is a growing demand for semiconductors capable of enhancing data communication speed between quantum computers and supercomputers [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Hardware Links:** Chip makers should supply high-bandwidth links that tie quantum modules to classical HPC clusters [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Control Logic:** Low-latency control ASICs are required for managing the classical side of the computation [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
