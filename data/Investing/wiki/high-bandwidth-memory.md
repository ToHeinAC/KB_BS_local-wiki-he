# High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM)

High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM) is a critical component for modern data center servers, driven by the surge in generative AI training and inference workloads [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Function and Necessity
HBM is designed to solve the data bandwidth bottleneck that occurs when compute performance (from CPUs, GPUs, and accelerators) outpaces the memory's ability to supply data fast enough [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Architecture:** It achieves high bandwidth by stacking multiple DRAM dies on a tiny base-logic die. These dies are connected using Through-Silicon Vias (TSVs) and mounted on a silicon interposer placed immediately next to the compute chip [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Performance:** This 3D integration delivers terabytes-per-second bandwidth within a very small physical footprint, making it essential for power-hungry AI and HPC systems [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].

## Market Outlook and Supply Risks
The HBM market is projected for substantial growth, with a CAGR of +27.8% from 2024 to 2030 [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
*   **Supply Constraints:** Despite high demand, supply shortages can persist due to several factors:
    1.  Demand potentially outrunning optimistic forecasts.
    2.  The long lead time (18-to-24 months) for new TSV lines, limiting quick fixes [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
    3.  Supply chain bottlenecks in supporting infrastructure, such as silicon interposers, advanced bumping capacity, or the specialized base-logic die [pwc-semiconductor-and-beyond-2026-full-report.md].
