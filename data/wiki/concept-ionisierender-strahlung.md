# Ionisierende Strahlung

Ionisierende radiation refers to radiation capable of causing ionization in matter [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md]. The use, establishment, and operation of facilities generating this radiation are heavily regulated under the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG).

## Legal Requirements for Facilities
The establishment of facilities generating ionizing radiation is subject to strict permitting procedures:
*   **Permit Required Facilities (§ 10):** A permit is required for establishing facilities, including:
    1. Accelerator or plasma facilities capable of generating more than $10^{12}$ neutrons per second [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    2. Electron accelerators with an electron end energy of more than 10 Megaelectronvolts, if the average beam power can exceed 1 kilowatt [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    3. Electron accelerators with an electron end energy of more than 150 Megaelectronvolts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    4. Ion accelerators with an ion end energy of more than 10 Megaelectronvolts per nucleon, if the average beam power can exceed 50 watts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    5. Ion accelerators with an ion end energy of more than 150 Megaelectronvolts per nucleon [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **Permit Conditions (§ 11):** A permit requires guaranteeing, among other things:
    *   No doubts regarding the reliability of the applicant or the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    *   That the exposure of persons due to operation does not exceed the limits permitted for individuals in generally accessible areas outside the operational site [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    *   Compliance with environmental protection and disturbance measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].

## Operational Activities
*   **Permit Required Operations (§ 12):** A permit is required for operating facilities generating ionizing radiation (with exceptions) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **X-ray Facilities (§ 19):** Operating an X-ray facility requires notification or a permit depending on the specific use (e.g., technical radiography, treatment, teleradiology) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].

## Specific Applications
*   **Medical Use (§ 14):** Permits for activities involving the application of ionizing radiation to humans require the applicant or radiation protection officer to be approved as a doctor or dentist, among other requirements [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **Teleradiology:** Specific requirements govern the operation of X-ray facilities for teleradiology, including the availability of the teleradiologist and the technical execution by qualified personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
