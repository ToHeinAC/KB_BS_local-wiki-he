Under the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG), **Radioactive Substances** are defined as all substances containing one or more radionuclides whose activity or specific activity cannot be disregarded according to the law or a related ordinance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Composition and Types
*   **Core Fuels:** Specific radioactive substances include plutonium 239 and plutonium 241, uranium enriched with isotopes 235 or 233, and any substance containing these materials [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Disregardable Activity:** The activity or specific activity of a substance can be disregarded if it falls below set release limits, or if it is a naturally occurring substance not subject to monitoring under the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Scope Limitations
The definition of radioactive substances does not apply to substances encountered in the context of existing or emergency exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
