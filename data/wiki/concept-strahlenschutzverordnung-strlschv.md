# Strahlenschutzverordnung - StrlSchV

The *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) is a detailed implementing regulation that specifies the operational requirements derived from the overarching *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG). It governs the practical application of radiation protection measures in various professional and technical contexts.

## Core Regulatory Areas
The StrlSchV addresses several critical areas of radiation safety management:

### 1. Monitoring and Dose Determination
The regulation details the mandatory procedures for assessing radiation exposure:
*   **Body Dose Measurement:** Procedures for determining the body dose are outlined, specifying the use of dosimeters worn at representative body sites (e.g., front of the torso) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
*   **Measurement Methods:** The body dose can be determined using dosimeters, or by measuring body activity or the activity of excretions, depending on the exposure conditions [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
*   **Professional Monitoring:** Professional monitoring requires assigning individuals to specific risk categories (Category A or B) based on potential dose accumulation [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
*   **Special Monitoring:** Specific rules apply to flight personnel, requiring specialized calculation programs or measuring devices for body dose determination [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

### 2. Source and Material Control
The StrlSchV imposes strict administrative requirements on the handling of radioactive materials:
*   **Inventory Control:** The responsible radiation protection officer must ensure that the competent authority is informed within one month of any acquisition, generation, purchase, release, or other disposition of radioactive substances, detailing the type and activity [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
*   **Record Keeping:** A detailed record must be kept of all such dispositions, and an inventory of substances with half-lives over 100 days must be reported annually [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

### 3. Personnel Protection
The regulation provides specific guidelines for protecting vulnerable groups and managing occupational risk:
*   **Pregnant/Nursing Persons:** Specific measures must be implemented to exclude internal professional exposure when pregnancy or nursing is known [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
*   **Schools:** X-ray equipment use in general education schools is restricted to school X-ray facilities, and student/trainee participation must be supervised by qualified teaching staff [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
*   **Special Permits:** Under exceptional circumstances, the StrlSchV allows for temporary deviations from standard dose limits, setting higher, defined thresholds for specific procedures [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
