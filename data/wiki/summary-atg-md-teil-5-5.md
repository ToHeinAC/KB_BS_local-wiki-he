# Gesetz über die friedliche Verwendung der Kernenergie und den Schutz gegen ihre Gefahren (Atomgesetz)

This document provides excerpts from the German Atomic Energy Act (*Atomgesetz* - AtG), detailing provisions concerning liability, statutes of limitations, and safety requirements related to nuclear incidents.

## Liability and Compensation (Paris Convention Context)
The Act addresses liability for damage resulting from nuclear events:
*   **Damage Limitation:** For damage to property under the Paris Convention in connection with $\S 25$ Abs. 1, 2 und 4, the liable party is only responsible up to the common value of the damaged property, plus costs for securing against the radiation hazard emanating from it [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Nuclear Damage to Transport:** If liability arises under the Paris Convention in connection with $\S 25$ Abs. 1, 2 und 4, compensation for nuclear damage to the means of transport carrying the nuclear materials at the time of the nuclear event is only required if the sum is not reduced below 80 million Euros, which would otherwise be available for satisfying other claims for damages [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Statutes of Limitations
*   **Damage Claims ($\S 32$):** Claims for damages arising from a nuclear event expire three years from the time the claimant became or should have become aware of the damage and the identity of the liable party, or thirty years from the damaging event [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Paris Convention Claims ($\S 32$):** Claims based on the Paris Convention that are brought within ten years of the nuclear event against the owner of the nuclear facility for nuclear damage, but which do not involve the death or injury of a person, take precedence over claims filed after this period [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **General Law:** Otherwise, the provisions of the Civil Code (Bürgerliches Gesetzbuch) regarding statutes of limitations apply [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Multiple Polluters and State Guarantees
*   **Joint Liability ($\S 33$):** If multiple parties are legally obliged to compensate a third party for damage caused by a nuclear event or by the effect of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation, they are jointly and severally liable to the third party, unless Article 5 Abs. d of the Paris Convention stipulates otherwise [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Indemnification ($\S 34$):** If the owner of a nuclear facility or the owner of radioactive substances incurs legal compensation obligations under the Paris Convention, the Federal Republic of Germany must indemnify them to the extent that these obligations are not covered by the provision for coverage or cannot be met by it. The maximum amount of the indemnification obligation is 2.5 billion Euros [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Federal Compensation ($\S 38$):** If a person damaged by a nuclear event suffers damage domestically and no claim for compensation can be made under the applicable law of another state or international treaty, the Federal Republic of Germany shall provide compensation up to the maximum amount of the state indemnification obligation [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Accident Safety and Protection Concepts
The Act details comprehensive safety requirements for nuclear facilities:
*   **Integrated Safety and Protection Concept ($\S 41$):** This concept comprises safety measures from the license holder (protection against disruptions or other third-party influences) and protection measures from the state. These measures must be coordinated [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Protection Goals ($\S 42$):** The goals of the measures under $\S 41$ for the protection of nuclear facilities and activities are:
    1. Preventing the release and misuse of ionizing radiation from nuclear fuels or their by-products in significant quantities on site [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
    2. Preventing the simple or repeated removal of nuclear fuels or their by-products in significant quantities with the aim of releasing or misusing ionizing radiation at any location [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
    3. Preventing the simple or repeated removal of nuclear fuels in quantities sufficient in total to create a critical assembly [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Scope of Protection ($\S 43$):** The license holder must ensure protection against disruptions or other third-party influences according to specific sections of the Act, including technical, personnel, and organizational measures [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Functional Reserve ($\S 44$):** The required disruptions or other third-party influences are determined by the responsible authorities based on the current state of knowledge (Lastannahmen). For the determination of requirements and measures, an effective dose of 100 millisieverts up to the age of 70 years, calculated as the sum of inhalation and seven days of external irradiation, is to be used as a guideline [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **IT Security ($\S 44b$):** License holders must immediately report impairments of their IT systems, components, or processes that could lead to or have led to a danger or disruption of the nuclear safety of the affected facility or activity to the Federal Office for Information Security (BSI) [AtG.md [Teil 5/5].md].
