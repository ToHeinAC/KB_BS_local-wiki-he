The source document, titled "Strahlenschutzgesetz (StrlSchG) - Teil 2," provides detailed excerpts from the German Federal Law for the Protection against the Harmful Effects of Ionizing Radiation (StrlSchG). This section focuses on the principles governing radiation protection in planned exposure situations.

## Justification of Activities (§ 6)
The law mandates that any new activity potentially involving human or environmental exposure to ionizing radiation must be justified. This justification requires a careful balancing of the activity's economic, societal, or other benefits against the potential health risks. The assessment must consider professional, population, and medical exposures [StrlSchG_02.md]. Furthermore, existing activities can be reviewed if significant new findings regarding their benefits or impacts become available [StrlSchG_02.md].

## Review Procedures (§ 7)
If a competent authority has grounds to doubt the justification of an activity, it must submit the relevant documentation to the Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety (or the Federal Radiation Protection Agency, BfS) [StrlSchG_02.md]. The BfS is responsible for conducting scientific reports on the justification of activities, which must be completed within twelve months of receiving the documentation [StrlSchG_02.md].

## Exposure Minimization and Dose Limitation (§ 8 & § 9)
Individuals planning or conducting activities are legally obligated to:
1.  Avoid any unnecessary exposure or contamination of humans and the environment [StrlSchG_02.md].
2.  Keep any exposure or contamination as low as possible, even if below established limits. This requires considering the state of science and technology, depending on the specific activity [StrlSchG_02.md].
3.  Ensure that established dose limits set by the law and its associated regulations are not exceeded [StrlSchG_02.md].
