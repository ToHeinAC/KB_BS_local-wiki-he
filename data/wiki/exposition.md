In the context of radiation protection, **Exposition** refers to the incidence of ionizing radiation on the human body [summary-strlschg-01-md.md]. This concept is fundamental to the Strahlenschutzgesetz (StrlSchG).

## Types of Exposure
Exposure can manifest in two primary ways:
1. **External Exposure:** The impact of ionizing radiation from sources located outside the body [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
2. **Internal Exposure:** The impact of ionizing radiation from sources located within the body [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].

## Types of Exposure Situations
The law differentiates between three types of exposure situations, which dictate the regulatory approach:
*   **Planned Exposure Situation:** An exposure situation arising from activities that are planned or can be caused [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   **Emergency Exposure Situation:** An exposure situation resulting from an emergency event [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   **Existing Exposure Situation:** An exposure situation that already exists and requires a decision regarding its control [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].

## Categories of Exposure
The law further categorizes exposure based on the affected group:
*   **Population Exposure:** Exposure of individuals who are neither professionally nor medically exposed [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   **Professional Exposure:** Exposure sustained by persons engaged in professional activities regulated by the law [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   **Medical Exposure:** Exposure occurring when radioactive substances or ionizing radiation are applied to a patient or person for medical or dental purposes [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
