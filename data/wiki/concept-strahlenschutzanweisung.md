# Strahlenschutzanweisung (Radiation Protection Directive)

A *Strahlenschutzanweisung* is a mandatory, comprehensive written directive detailing the specific rules and procedures for handling radioactive substances within a radionuclide laboratory. It serves as the foundational document for radiation safety management, ensuring compliance with the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) and the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].

## Purpose and Scope
The directive provides general guidelines and suggestions for establishing safety protocols, applicable whether the handling occurs in small labs or large facilities with high activity inventories [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md]. It forms the basis for regular instruction of non-specialized personnel [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].

## Required Components
According to DIN 25425-4, Annex D, the *Strahlenschutzanweisung* must contain detailed information across several domains [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md]:

### Administrative and Legal Framework
*   **Legal Basis:** Must cite the legal basis and the specific approval notice granted under the StrlSchV [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Organization:** Must describe the radiation protection organization, identifying the Radiation Protection Responsible Person, Authorized Person, and Officer [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Area Definition:** Must list all rooms where radioactive substances can be handled according to the approval notice, including any activity restrictions [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Personnel Roles:** Must name the Radiation Protection Officers, including their department and scope of responsibility [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].

### Operational Safety Rules
*   **General Conduct:** Must establish rules prohibiting eating, drinking, or makeup within the controlled area, and specifically forbidding mouth pipetting of solutions [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Equipment Use:** Must specify working procedures for fume hoods, glove boxes, and hot cells [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Monitoring:** Must detail requirements for physical radiation protection control, including the implementation of RiPhyko guidelines and the mandatory wearing of dosimeters [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Personnel Safety:** Must outline rules for personnel classification (e.g., Category A) and medical monitoring [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].

### Emergency and Waste Management
*   **Contamination:** Must detail procedures for personnel contamination, following SSK recommendations [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Waste:** Must provide regulations for waste disposal, mandating separate collection and labeling [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Emergency Response:** Must include sections for emergency behavior, fire procedures, and designated reporting routes [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].

## Documentation
The directive requires maintaining an operational or laboratory book (*Betriebs- bzw. Laborbuch*) and must be signed by the Radiation Protection Responsible Person or Radiation Protection Officer [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
