# Permit/Authorization (Radiation Protection)

A permit (*Genehmigung*) is a formal authorization required by the law for certain activities involving ionizing radiation or radioactive substances, establishing specific conditions under which the activity may proceed [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md]. The required scope of the permit depends heavily on the nature of the activity.

## Types of Permits and Scope
1. **General Activities (§ 12):** Permits are required for certain activities, such as the handling of radioactive substances or the operation of facilities generating ionizing radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
2. **X-ray/Interference Devices (§ 20, § 22):** Specific permits are required for the operation, testing, maintenance, or repair of X-ray devices and interference sources, demanding detailed documentation regarding technical standards and personnel competence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
3. **Transport (§ 27):** A permit is required for transporting certain radioactive substances, although exemptions exist for specific types of transport [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
4. **Medical Research (§ 31):** A full permit is mandatory for medical research applications if they are not covered by notification rules, requiring rigorous justification of medical necessity, risk minimization, and ethical approval [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
5. **Consumer Goods (§ 40, § 42):** Permits are required for the addition of radioactive substances to consumer goods or for the cross-border transfer of such goods [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
6. **Facility Operation (§ 208):** Building type approvals (*Bauartzulassungen*) for devices and installations containing radioactive substances or generating ionizing radiation are regulated, with provisions detailing the continuation of approvals granted before December 31, 2018 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Key Procedural Elements and Continuity
* **Documentation:** Applications must include extensive documentation, such as expert certificates, proof of technical compliance (e.g., EU 2017/745 compliance), and proof of trained personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Duration:** Permits can be granted for defined periods, such as a maximum of five years for certain employment scenarios (§ 25) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Review:** The granting of a permit is contingent upon the authority confirming that all statutory requirements, including safety measures and personnel qualifications, are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Transitional Rules:** Many permits issued before December 31, 2018, remain valid under the new law, provided certain conditions (e.g., proving expertise by December 31, 2021, for transport permits) are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
