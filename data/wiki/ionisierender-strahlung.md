Ionizing radiation refers to radiation that has enough energy to remove electrons from atoms or molecules, thereby ionizing them [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (1)].

### Types and Effects
*   **Exposure:** The law defines "Exposure" as the incidence of ionizing radiation on the human body, which can occur either externally (outside the body) or internally (inside the body), or it can refer to the measure of this incidence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (1)].
*   **Sources:** Sources of ionizing radiation include radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (1)].
*   **Protection:** The *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) establishes comprehensive rules for protecting humans and the environment from the harmful effects of ionizing radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 1].

### Legal Context
The management of ionizing radiation is governed by strict legal procedures, including:
*   **Planned Activities:** Requiring assessment of planned exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (2)].
*   **Emergency Response:** Establishing protocols for emergency exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (3)].
*   **Dose Limitation:** Setting specific dose limits for professional and population exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 77, § 80].
