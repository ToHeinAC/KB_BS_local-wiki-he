The concept of "Justification of Activities" (*Rechtfertigung von Tätigkeitsarten*) is a cornerstone principle within the German Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) [StrlSchG_02.md].

## Principle of Balancing Benefits and Risks
Under this principle, any new activity that may lead to exposure of humans or the environment to ionizing radiation must undergo a rigorous assessment. This assessment requires balancing the potential benefits of the activity—which may be economic, societal, or otherwise—against the potential health detriment it might cause [StrlSchG_02.md].

## Scope of Consideration
When justifying an activity, the following types of exposure must be taken into account:
*   Professional exposure (occupational) [StrlSchG_02.md].
*   Population exposure (public) [StrlSchG_02.md].
*   Medical exposure [StrlSchG_02.md].

## Review Process
The justification status of existing activities is not permanent. It can be re-evaluated if significant new scientific knowledge regarding the activity's benefits or impacts becomes available [StrlSchG_02.md]. If doubts arise, the responsible authority must initiate a review process involving federal agencies [StrlSchG_02.md].
