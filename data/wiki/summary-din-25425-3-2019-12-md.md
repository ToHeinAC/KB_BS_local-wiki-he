# DIN 25425-3:2019-12 Radionuklidlaboratorien – Teil 3: Regeln für den vorbeugenden Brandschutz

This document, DIN 25425-3:2019-12, provides technical rules for the design and construction of radionuclide laboratories, specifically focusing on preventive fire protection (*vorbeugender Brandschutz*) [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md]. It is an update to DIN 25425-3:2012-02, incorporating adjustments to the radiation protection legislation (*Strahlenschutzgesetzgebung*) [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].

## Scope and Applicability
The standard applies to the design of radionuclide laboratories where open radioactive substances (as defined in DIN 25425-1) or additionally enclosed radioactive substances are handled [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md]. The primary purpose is to establish requirements for preventive fire protection during the planning and construction of such laboratories [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].

**Note on Scope:**
*   For requirements concerning storage rooms and storage facilities for radioactive substances, DIN 25422 applies [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
*   The document only mentions facilities within radionuclide laboratories insofar as they must be considered for preventive fire protection [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
*   Additional protection measures for handling fissionable materials (see DIN 25403-1) and hot cells (see DIN 25460) must be observed [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].

## Key Concepts and Principles

### Room Group (Raumgruppe)
A room group is defined as a group of connected rooms where no fire protection technical requirements for the building components between these rooms exceed conventional requirements [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
*   **Note 1:** Regarding the activity inventory, the rooms of a room group are considered like a single room [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
*   **Note 2:** Grouping rooms into a room group allows for the division of the radionuclide laboratory based on functional aspects (see DIN 25425-1) and optimizing the fire protection design [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].

### Fire Hazard Levels (Gefährdungsstufen - GS)
The required fire resistance of building components is determined by the Hazard Level (GS) of each room or room group, which measures the necessary protection against radiation risk in case of fire (external irradiation, contamination, incorporation) [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md]. Higher hazard levels demand higher requirements for the fire resistance of building components [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].

The Hazard Level is determined according to Table 1, and potentially considering whether the radioactive substance is stored in a fireproof enclosure [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].

**Table 1 — Hazard Levels:**
| Hazard Level | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| GS 1 | $1 < X \leq 10^4$ |
| GS 2 | $10^4 < X \leq 10^7$ |
| GS 3 | $10^7 < X \leq 10^{10}$ |
| GS 4 | $X > 10^{10}$ |

The calculation for the classification uses the formula:
$$
\sum (A_{o,i} + A_{u,i}/1000)
$$
$$
F_i = X \quad (1)
$$
Where:
*   $A_{o,i}$ is the activity of the individual radionuclide $i$, open and not fireproofly enclosed [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
*   $A_{u,i}$ is the activity of the individual radionuclide $i$, fireproofly enclosed [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
*   $F_i$ is the release limit of the radionuclide $i$ according to Annex 4, Table 1, Column 2 StrlSchV [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
*   $X$ is a multiple of the release limit for classification according to Table 1 [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].

### Fire Protection Measures
To prevent the occurrence and spread of fire, and to prevent the spread of contaminated smoke gases into adjacent rooms and the environment, several measures are mandated [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md]:

1.  **Construction Materials:** Non-combustible building materials should be used [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
2.  **Compartmentalization:** Fire-resistant building components (walls, ceilings, doors) must be selected according to Table 2 [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
3.  **Utilities:** Necessary passages for cables, pipelines, residual material shafts, or ventilation ducts must be executed in a fire-resistant manner according to Table 2 [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
4.  **Ventilation:** Ventilation and smoke extraction systems must be designed to prevent the crossing of smoke into other areas by creating defined pressure conditions [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].

### Fire Resistance Requirements (Table 2 Summary)
The required fire resistance of building components depends on the Hazard Level (GS) [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md]. For instance:
*   For GS 2, non-load-bearing/other components require *feuerbeständig* (fire-resistant) [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
*   For GS 3, non-load-bearing/other components require *feuerbeständig* (fire-resistant) [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].

### Residual Material Management
Radioactive residual materials (*Reststoffe*) with an activity per container exceeding 10⁴ times the release limit according to Annex 4, Table 1, Column 2 StrlSchV, must be stored in non-combustible, tightly sealed storage facilities during interim storage until handover to the regional collection point or third parties [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md]. The required fire resistance for the residual material room must be determined according to Table 2 and referencing DIN 25422 [DIN 25425-3_2019-12.md].
