# Cloud Applications

Cloud applications refer to software solutions delivered and operated over the internet, allowing users to access functionality and data without needing to host or maintain the underlying infrastructure [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].

## Characteristics and Trends
The adoption of cloud applications is characterized by subscription-based models, scalability, and rapid integration of advanced technologies like Artificial Intelligence (AI) [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].

### Modernization Cycle
A significant trend observed in the enterprise software market is the modernization cycle, where large organizations migrate from older, legacy on-premise systems (such as SAP's ECC) to modern, cloud-native platforms (such as S/4HANA Cloud) [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md]. This transition is often driven by end-of-support deadlines, such as the 2027 deadline mentioned in the context of SAP [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].

### AI Integration
Modern cloud applications are increasingly incorporating generative AI capabilities. For example, the integration of AI Copilots, such as Joule, is becoming a standard feature, enhancing user productivity across modules like Finance, Supply Chain, and HR [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].

## Market Dynamics
The market for cloud applications is highly competitive, with major players including SAP SE, Oracle, and Microsoft [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md]. The growth in cloud revenue streams, such as the Cloud ERP Suite, demonstrates the industry's shift toward subscription-based, scalable models [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].
