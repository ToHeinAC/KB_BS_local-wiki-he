The document provides a comprehensive overview of the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG), or the "Law for the Protection against the Harmful Effect of Ionizing Radiation."

## General Provisions and Scope

The law regulates the protection of human beings and, concerning the long-term protection of human health, the environment, particularly in cases of:
1. Planned exposure situations.
2. Emergency exposure situations.
3. Existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

The law does not regulate:
*   Exposure of individual persons or workers due to cosmic radiation, except for flight and space personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   Above-ground exposure to radionuclides naturally present in the undisturbed Earth's crust [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   Exposure to radionuclides naturally present in the human body, and cosmic radiation near the ground [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Definitions and Categories

The StrlSchG defines several key terms:

*   **Exposure (Exposition):** This is defined as the incidence of ionizing radiation on the human body, whether external (outside the body) or internal (inside the body), or the extent of this incidence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Planned Exposure Situation (Geplante Expositionssituation):** This is an exposure situation that arises from activities and in which an exposure is caused or can be caused [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Emergency Exposure Situation (Notfallexpositionssituation):** This is an exposure situation that arises from an emergency, provided the situation does not fall under Paragraph 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Existing Exposure Situation (Bestehende Expositionssituation):** This is an exposure situation that already exists when a decision regarding its control must be made [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Exposition Categories:** Three categories are distinguished: 1) Population exposure, 2) Occupational exposure, and 3) Medical exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
    *   **Occupational Exposure:** This applies to a person in an employment relationship performing an activity governed by the law, or performing such an activity themselves. It also covers flight and space personnel, and persons performing tasks under the Atomic Energy Act (§ 19 or § 20) or under § 172 or § 178 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
    *   **Medical Exposure:** This covers exposure to a patient or asymptomatic person during medical or dental examination/treatment, or exposure for the purpose of medical research, or voluntary exposure by a person supporting or caring for another person [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Radioactive Substances and Activities

*   **Radioactive Substances:** These are all substances containing one or more radionuclides whose activity or specific activity cannot be disregarded according to the law or a related ordinance. Core fuels include specific types of plutonium and uranium [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Activities (Tätigkeiten):** Activities include handling, acquiring, or transferring radioactive substances; establishing, operating, or decommissioning facilities; and the use of ionizing radiation or radioactive substances in various contexts, such as medical research or consumer goods [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Operational Requirements

The law details requirements for various operational aspects, including:
*   **Facility Operation:** Requirements for facilities generating ionizing radiation (e.g., § 10, § 12) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Transport:** Regulations for the transport of radioactive substances, including cross-border transport [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Occupational Safety:** Provisions for the operational organization of radiation protection (Betriebliche Organisation des Strahlenschutzes) and setting dose limits for workers and the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
