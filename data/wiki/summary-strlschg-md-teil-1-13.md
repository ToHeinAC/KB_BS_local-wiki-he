This document provides the first part of the *Gesetz zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), effective as of June 27, 2017. The law regulates the protection of humans and the environment from the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, particularly concerning planned, emergency, and existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Scope and Applicability (§ 1)
The law applies to the protection of the human body and, regarding long-term human health, the environment from the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, specifically in three contexts:
1. Planned exposure situations.
2. Emergency exposure situations.
3. Existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

The law explicitly excludes regulations for:
*   Exposure of individuals from cosmic radiation, except for flight and space personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   Above-ground exposure from radionuclides naturally present in the undisturbed Earth's crust [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   Exposure from radionuclides naturally present in the human body, and from cosmic radiation near the ground [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Key Definitions and Concepts (§ 2, § 3, § 4, § 5)

### Exposure and Situations (§ 2)
The law defines several key terms related to radiation exposure:
*   **Exposition:** The impact of ionizing radiation on the human body, which can be external (from sources outside the body) or internal (within the body), or the extent of this impact [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Planned Exposure Situation:** An exposure situation arising from activities where an exposure is caused or can be caused [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Emergency Exposure Situation:** An exposure situation resulting from an emergency, provided the situation does not fall under Paragraph 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Existing Exposure Situation:** An exposure situation that already exists when a decision regarding its control must be made [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Exposition Categories:** Three categories are distinguished: population exposure, occupational exposure, and medical exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
    *   **Occupational Exposure:** Applies to individuals employed in a capacity under this law, or who perform such activities, including flight and space personnel, and personnel fulfilling tasks under the Atomic Energy Act (§ 19 or § 20) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
    *   **Medical Exposure:** Applies to patients or asymptomatic persons undergoing examination or treatment, or persons voluntarily exposing themselves for research purposes [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

### Radioactive Substances (§ 3)
Radioactive substances (including nuclear fuels and other radioactive substances) are defined as all substances containing one or more radionuclides whose activity or specific activity cannot be disregarded according to the law or a subsequent regulation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Nuclear Fuels:** Specific fissile materials include Plutonium 239 and Plutonium 241, uranium enriched with isotopes 235 or 233, and any substance containing these materials [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Exclusion:** Activity or specific activity can be disregarded if the substance falls below established release limits or if it is a substance of natural origin not subject to monitoring under the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Procedural Requirements (Incorporating Part 2 Details)
The law establishes strict procedural requirements for activities involving ionizing radiation:

### Justification and Dose Control
*   **Justification:** New activities must be justified by balancing benefits against potential health impairments, considering occupational, population, and medical exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **Dose Reduction:** Activities must be planned to keep exposure as low as possible, considering the state of science and technology [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **Dose Limitation:** It is mandatory to ensure that established dose limits are not exceeded [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].

### Facility Operation
*   **Permit-Required Facilities:** Permits are required for establishing facilities generating ionizing radiation, such as accelerators exceeding specific energy or neutron emission levels [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **Notification-Required Operations:** Certain operations, such as operating specific types of X-ray facilities or plasma facilities, require prior notification to the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
