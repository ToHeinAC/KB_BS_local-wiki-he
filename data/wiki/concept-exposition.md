# Exposition

Exposition, in the context of radiation protection, refers to the impact of ionizing radiation on the human body [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Definition
Legally, exposure is defined as:
* The impact of ionizing radiation on the human body through external sources (outside the body) and internal sources (inside the body), or the measure of this impact [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Types of Exposure Situations
The Act categorizes exposure situations based on their origin:
1. **Planned Exposure Situation (Geplante Expositionssituation):** An exposure situation resulting from activities where an exposure is caused or can be caused [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
2. **Emergency Exposure Situation (Notfall-Expositionssituation):** An exposure situation arising from an emergency, provided it does not fall under the provisions of Paragraph 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
3. **Existing Exposure Situation (Bestehende Expositionssituation):** An exposure situation that already exists and requires a decision regarding its control [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Exposure Categories
The Act distinguishes three main categories of exposure:
1. **Population Exposure:** Exposure of persons who are neither professionally nor medically exposed [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
2. **Occupational Exposure:** Exposure of persons who are employed in a capacity regulated by the Act, or who perform such activities, including flight and space personnel, or personnel fulfilling duties under the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
3. **Medical Exposure:** Exposure to patients or asymptomatic persons during medical or dental examinations/treatments, or exposure for medical research purposes, or voluntary exposure by support persons [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Professional Exposure Limits
For professional exposure, specific dose limits are defined:
* The effective dose must not exceed 1 millisievert in a calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
* The organ equivalent dose for the lens of the eye must not exceed 15 millisievert in a calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
* The organ equivalent dose for the skin, averaged over any 1 square centimeter, must not exceed 50 millisievert in a calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
