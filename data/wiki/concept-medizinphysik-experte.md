# Medizinphysik-Experte

A Medizinphysik-Experte is defined as a person with a Master's degree in medical physics. Within the context of radiation protection, the role of the expert is highly regulated by the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) and detailed in the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV).

## Qualifications and Appointment
The appointment of experts is governed by strict criteria:
*   **General Requirements:** An individual expert must possess a university or university of applied sciences degree in a natural science or technical field, possess the required specialized knowledge in radiation protection, and have undergone training by an experienced professional (§ 181 StrlSchV) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
*   **Expert Organizations:** Expert organizations must also meet criteria regarding the reliability and independence of their representatives and the technical equipment available [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
*   **Appointment Duration:** The appointment as an expert is limited to five years and must be renewed [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## Duties and Responsibilities
The duties of the expert are comprehensive and include:
*   **Examination:** Experts are responsible for conducting examinations and assessments related to professional exposure [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
*   **Reporting:** State-appointed experts must report on the subject and scope of their expert activity, summarizing records annually [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
*   **Compliance:** Experts must ensure that the measuring devices and testing equipment used are properly maintained and suitable for the respective measurement task [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## Relationship to Authorized Physicians
The StrlSchV also details the role of the *ermächtigte Ärzte* (authorized physicians) (§ 175 StrlSchV), who are specifically authorized to perform medical monitoring and are responsible for maintaining the health record (§ 175 StrlSchV). While distinct roles, both groups are central to the medical oversight of professional radiation exposure.
