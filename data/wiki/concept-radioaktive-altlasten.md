# Radioaktive Altlasten (Radioactive Contaminated Sites)

Radioactive contaminated sites (*radioaktive Altlasten*) are defined under the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) and require specialized management protocols detailed in the StrlSchV.

## Definition and Scope
These sites are areas contaminated by radioactive substances, often resulting from historical industrial or nuclear activities [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/184].md].

## Management Principles (StrlSchV)
The StrlSchV establishes comprehensive rules for dealing with these sites:
*   **Exposure Assessment:** Protocols exist for determining the expected exposure from radioactive contaminated sites (§ 160) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/184].md].
*   **Test Values:** The regulation specifies test values for radioactive contaminated sites during the decommissioning and remediation of uranium ore mining facilities and operational sites (§ 161) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/184].md].
*   **Remediation:** Principles for optimizing remediation measures are set forth, including the requirement for a comprehensive remediation plan (*Sanierungsplan*) (§ 163, § 164) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/184].md].
*   **Monitoring:** Emissions and immissions monitoring are required during the decommissioning and remediation process (§ 162) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/184].md].

## Disposal and Waste Management (AtG)
The AtG provides detailed requirements for the disposal of radioactive waste generated from nuclear facilities:
*   **Waste Disposal:** Operators must ensure that radioactive residues and dismantled components are safely recycled or disposed of as radioactive waste (direct final disposal) according to the specified purposes [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Intermediate Storage:** Operators must provide proof of adequate interim storage capacity for irradiated nuclear fuel and waste requiring retrieval from processing facilities until delivery to a final disposal facility [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **National Infrastructure:** The states are responsible for establishing state collection points for radioactive waste generated in their territory, while the federal government must establish facilities for the securing and final disposal of radioactive waste [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Disposal Context (AtEV)
The AtEV reinforces the structured management of waste derived from such sites by detailing the mandatory procedures for the disposal of radioactive waste generated during or related to these activities, requiring specific reporting and delivery protocols to federal facilities [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Technical Standards and Guidelines
The management of radioactive waste and contaminated sites is supported by detailed technical standards, including:
*   **Waste Storage:** Guidelines for the interim storage of radioactive waste with negligible heat development are provided [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 3/3].md].
*   **Shielding:** Specific standards dictate the construction of shielding walls against ionizing radiation, such as DIN 25407-1, DIN 25407-2, and DIN 25407-3 [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 3/3].md].
