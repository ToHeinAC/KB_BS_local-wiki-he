# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 13/13)

This section of the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) details provisions concerning emergency planning, public information, and specific technical annexes related to radiation safety.

## Emergency Planning and Competencies (Anlage 6)
The law provides a depiction of the legal bases, tasks, and competencies applicable within the scope of the special emergency plan. This includes:
*   The responsibilities and competencies of the Federal and State governments, their competent authorities for emergency response measures, and the private and public law organizations and persons participating in the emergency response [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   A depiction of the procedures and precautions for information exchange, cooperation, assistance, and coordination at the federal level, between the Federation and the States, with EU organs, bodies, and member states, with third countries, and with international organizations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Specification and explanation of interfaces with other procedures and precautions for information exchange, cooperation, assistance, and coordination listed in the general emergency plans of the Federation and the States [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   For the concretization, supplementation, and application of optimized protection strategies set out in the general emergency plan, including a depiction of priority and other measures for the protection of the population and emergency personnel, as well as precautions and criteria for area-specific concretization, application, and adaptation of trigger criteria and limit values, considering the legal provisions applicable to the respective protective measure and the actual characteristics of the emergency, insofar as such a depiction is possible in advance during emergency planning [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

## Public Information and Behavior Recommendations (Anlage 7)
In the event of an emergency, the affected population receives the following information and recommendations, depending on the applicable emergency plans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md]:

**1. Information in an Emergency:**
*   Information about the emergency and, if possible, its characteristics such as origin, spread, and expected development [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Behavior recommendations, which may include:
    *   Restrictions on consuming certain possibly contaminated foodstuffs and possibly contaminated water, simple hygiene and decontamination rules, recommendations for staying indoors, collection and use of iodine tablets or other protective agents, and precautions in case of evacuation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
    *   Specific warning notices for certain population groups [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Announcements recommending compliance with the instructions and calls of the competent authorities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

**2. Information in the Pre-Warning Phase:**
If the emergency is preceded by a pre-warning phase, the potentially affected population receives relevant information and recommendations, such as:
*   A call to switch on relevant communication channels [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Preparatory recommendations for facilities with public tasks [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Recommendations for particularly affected professions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

**3. Basic Concepts:**
If time permits, the potentially affected population is informed again about the basic concepts of radioactivity and its effects on humans and the environment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

## Radon and Mineral Sources (Anlage 8 & 9)
*   **Elevated Radon Exposure Work Areas (Anlage 8):** Workplaces in underground mines, shafts, and caves, including visitor mines; workplaces in radon spas and radon adits; and workplaces in facilities for water extraction, treatment, and distribution [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   **Radiologically Relevant Mineral Primary Raw Materials (Anlage 9):**
    *   Acidic magmatic rocks as well as resulting metamorphic and sedimentary rocks [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
    *   Sedimentary rock with high organic content such as oil, copper, and alunite shale [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
    *   Travertine [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
