This summary details the final sections of the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG), covering specific annexes related to emergency planning, public information, and material classification.

## Anlage 6 (zu § 98): Besonderer Notfallplan
This annex details the legal frameworks, tasks, and responsibilities applicable within the scope of the special emergency plan. It outlines:
1.  A depiction of the legal bases, tasks, and responsibilities of the federal and state governments, their responsible authorities for emergency response measures, as well as the private and public law organizations and persons involved in the emergency response [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
2.  A depiction of the procedures and precautions for information exchange, cooperation, assistance, and coordination during the emergency response at the federal level, between the Federation and the states, with bodies, offices, facilities, and other member states of the European Union, with third states, and with international organizations, and the bodies and facilities responsible for this information exchange, cooperation, assistance, and coordination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
3.  Specification and explanation of interfaces with other procedures and precautions for information exchange, cooperation, assistance, and coordination listed in the further emergency plans of the Federation and the states, and other bodies and facilities responsible for this information exchange, cooperation, assistance, and coordination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
4.  For the concretization, supplementation, and application of the optimized protection strategies established in the general federal emergency plan, including:
    a) A depiction of the priority and other measures for the protection of the population and emergency personnel considered within the scope of the special emergency plan, and
    b) The precautions and criteria for a sector-specific concretization, application, and adaptation of the trigger criteria and limit values specified in the general federal emergency plan, taking into account the legal provisions applicable to the respective protective measure and the actual characteristics of the emergency, insofar as such a depiction is possible in advance within the scope of emergency planning [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

## Anlage 7 (zu § 112): Information der Bevölkerung und Empfehlungen für das Verhalten bei Notfällen
This annex provides guidelines for information and behavioral recommendations for the affected population during emergencies:

### 1. Information to be provided in an emergency
In the event of an emergency, the affected population receives the following quickly and repeatedly, according to the emergency plans applicable to the respective emergency:
*   Information about the emergency that has occurred and, if possible, about its characteristics such as origin, spread, and expected development;
*   Behavioral recommendations, which, depending on the circumstances of the respective emergency, may include:
    *   aa) specifically the following points: restriction of consumption of certain possibly contaminated foodstuffs and of possibly contaminated water, simple hygiene and decontamination rules, recommendations for staying indoors, for the collection and use of iodine tablets or other protective agents, precautions in case of evacuation;
    *   bb) which can be connected with special warning notices for certain population groups;
*   Announcements recommending compliance with the instructions and calls of the responsible authorities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

### 2. Information and recommendations in the pre-warning phase
If the emergency is preceded by a pre-warning phase, the potentially affected population receives relevant information and recommendations already in this phase, such as:
*   A call to switch on relevant communication channels;
*   Preparatory recommendations for facilities that have public tasks;
*   Recommendations for particularly affected professions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

### 3. Supplementary information on basic concepts of radioactivity and its effects on humans and the environment
If time permits, the potentially affected population is informed again about the basic concepts of radioactivity and its effects on humans and the environment. Attention may also be drawn to the information published pursuant to § 105 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

## Anlage 8 (zu § 127 Absatz 1 Nummer 2): Arbeitsfelder mit erhöhter Exposition durch Radon
This annex lists workplaces with increased exposure due to Radon:
1.  Workplaces in underground mines, shafts, and caves, including visitor mines,
2.  Workplaces in radon spas and radon tunnels,
3.  Workplaces in facilities for water extraction, treatment, and distribution [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

## Anlage 9 (zu § 134 Absatz 1): Radiologisch relevante mineralische Primärrohstoffe
This annex lists radiologically relevant mineral primary raw materials for the manufacture of buildings with living spaces:
1.  Acidic magmatic rocks as well as metamorphic and sedimentary rocks derived therefrom,
2.  Sedimentary rock with a high organic content such as oil, copper, and alunite shale,
3.  Travertine [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
