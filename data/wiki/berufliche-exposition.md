Professional exposure refers to the radiation dose received by an individual due to their employment or professional activities, regulated by detailed provisions within the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## Dose Determination and Calculation
*   **Adding Doses (§ 166):** Body doses must be added if they are determined from multiple sources, including activities as a professionally exposed person, connection with Radon at the workplace, remediation measures for radioactive contaminated sites, and reportable existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
*   **Dose Measurement (§ 175):** For the determination of the organ equivalent dose, both external and internal exposure must be considered. For internal exposure, the dose from radionuclides ingested outside the reference period must also be accounted for [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## Documentation and Measurement
*   **Record Keeping (§ 167):** The responsible parties are obligated to record detailed information, including personal data, the results of measurements/estimates, and employment characteristics. These records must be retained until the person reaches age 75 or for at least 30 years after employment ends [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
*   **Measurement Stations (§ 169):** The competent authority can designate specialized measurement stations to assess exposure from various sources, including external, internal, emergency, Radon, remediation, and existing situations. These stations must meet strict requirements regarding personnel, equipment, and quality management [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
*   **Data Submission (§ 168):** Responsible parties must provide data to the measurement stations or the competent authority for dose determination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## Central Registry
All data regarding professional exposures are collected and stored in the Radiation Protection Register (Strahlenschutzregister) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
