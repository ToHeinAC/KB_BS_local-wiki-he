This document provides excerpts from the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV), which details the administrative and procedural implementation of the German Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG). It covers specific requirements for dose measurement, expert qualifications, documentation, and administrative offenses.

## § 169 Messstellen und Messgeräte
The facility designated as the measurement station (§ 169 Abs. 1 StrlSchG) must provide specific dosimeters upon request:
1. To the Radiation Protection Officer: Personal dosimeters required for determining body dose according to § 65 Abs. 1 Satz 1 and § 66 Abs. 1 Nummer 1 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
2. To the party obligated according to § 145 Abs. 2 StrlSchG: Personal dosimeters required for determining body dose according to § 165 Abs. 1 Nummer 1 in conjunction with § 65 Abs. 1 Satz 1 and § 66 Abs. 1 Nummer 1 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
3. To the responsible party according to § 153 Abs. 1 StrlSchG: Personal dosimeters required for determining body dose according to § 166 Abs. 1 Nummer 1 in conjunction with § 65 Abs. 1 Satz 1 and § 66 Abs. 1 Nummer 1 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
4. To the party obligated according to § 131 Abs. 1 StrlSchG: Measuring devices required for determining exposure according to § 157 Abs. 2 Nummer 1 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

The measurement station (§ 169 Abs. 1 StrlSchG) can utilize an accredited body (§ 155 Abs. 3) for the evaluation of measuring devices (§ 157 Abs. 2 Nummer 1), provided the measurement station continues to meet the requirements of § 169 Abs. 2 StrlSchG [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md]. Furthermore, designated measurement stations participate in quality assurance measures conducted by the Federal Institute for Physics and Technology (BPT) for body dose determination (§ 66 Abs. 1 und 2 Satz 3) and by the Federal Office for Radiation Protection (BfS) for body dose determination (§ 65 Abs. 4 und § 157 Abs. 3) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 173 Strahlenschutzregister
The Federal Office for Radiation Protection (BfS) determines the technical procedure for generating and the structure of the personal identification number according to § 170 Abs. 3 StrlSchG [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md]. The BfS can accept an identification number issued by a competent authority outside the scope of the StrlSchG if it meets three criteria:
1. It can be clearly assigned to the monitored person [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
2. It remains unchanged throughout the monitored person's lifetime [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
3. It is available at the monitored person or the employing company [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 174 Strahlenpass (Radiation Pass)
The party responsible for ensuring that specified persons are only occupied with radiation sources (§ 68 Abs. 1, § 158 Abs. 1 Satz 1, § 165 Abs. 2 Nummer 2 oder § 166 Abs. 2 Nummer 2) is responsible for maintaining the Radiation Pass. This party must ensure that the pass is registered with the authority responsible according to § 174 Abs. 2 StrlSchG [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

The competent authority registers a Radiation Pass for a period of six years if:
1. The data required by Abs. 3 Nummer 1 Buchstabe a to c and Nummer 4 are entered [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
2. The pass is manually signed by the pass holder and the responsible party for maintaining the pass [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
3. Sufficient space is provided in the pass for further entries required by Abs. 3 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

The authority can extend the validity of a registered Radiation Pass for up to five years if the requirements of Abs. 2 Satz 1 are met [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

The pass must contain, at minimum:
1. From the responsible party: The personal data of the pass holder and personal identification number according to § 170 Abs. 3 StrlSchG; data about the responsible party, including operating number and contact details; the balance sheet of official dose values from professional exposure for every calendar year and every month of the calendar year; and any exceeding of dose limits [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
2. From the responsible party for the external facility: Data on the exposure in the external facility, setup, or operating site [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
3. From the responsible party or authorized physician: Data on the medical monitoring performed, especially the content of the certificate according to § 79 Abs. 1 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
4. From the authority: Data on the issuance of the pass and the authority itself [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

The pass is the property of the pass holder and is non-transferable. Upon termination of employment, the responsible party must ensure the pass is returned to the pass holder. If it cannot be returned, it must be handed over to the registering authority, which can destroy it after ten years following the expiration of its validity [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 175 Ermächtigte Ärzte (Authorized Physicians)
The competent authority authorizes physicians to perform medical monitoring according to §§ 77, 78, 79, and 81, including in conjunction with §§ 151, 158 Abs. 3, §§ 165, or 166 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md]. The authorization can only be granted to a physician who proves the specialized knowledge required for medical monitoring in radiation protection for professional exposure. The authorization is limited to five years [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

The authorized physician is responsible for conducting initial examinations, repeat examinations, and assessments according to §§ 77 and 78, as well as special medical monitoring according to § 81. They must propose measures necessary for the prevention and defense against health damage in case of increased exposure. Furthermore, they must maintain a health record (§ 79 Abs. 2 StrlSchG) for every person subject to medical monitoring [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 176 Duldungspflichten (Duty to Tolerate)
Persons subject to medical monitoring (§§ 77 und 78, etc.) must tolerate the required medical examinations [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md]. Similarly, persons for whom body dose determination (§ 64 Abs. 1 Satz 1 oder Absatz 2, etc.) or contamination determination (§ 57 Abs. 1 oder § 58 Abs. 1) is required must tolerate the necessary measurements and determinations [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 177 Bestimmung von Sachverständigen (Appointment of Experts)
The competent authority appoints individual experts (§ 172 Abs. 1 StrlSchG) if:
1. There are no facts suggesting doubts regarding the reliability or independence of the applicant [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
2. The applicant meets the requirements for training, professional knowledge, and skills required by § 181 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
3. The technical and organizational equipment required for the proper execution of the examination task is available [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

The authority appoints expert organizations (§ 172 Abs. 1 StrlSchG) if:
1. There are no facts suggesting doubts regarding the independence of the expert organization [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
2. There are no facts suggesting doubts regarding the reliability or independence of the authorized representatives [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
3. There are no facts suggesting doubts regarding the reliability of the examining person [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
4. The examining person meets the requirements for training, professional knowledge, and skills required by § 181 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
5. The technical and organizational equipment required for the proper execution of the examination task is available [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

The appointment as an expert is limited to five years [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 180 Unabhängigkeit (Independence)
Independence is given if the individual expert or the expert organization is not subject to any economic, financial, or other influence that could affect their judgment or call into question trust in the impartial performance of their duties [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md]. Independence is generally not given if the expert or the organization is involved in the development, manufacture, distribution, or maintenance of devices or facilities that are to be examined [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 181 Fachliche Qualifikation (Professional Qualification)
An individual expert or examining person must:
1. Possess a university or university of applied sciences degree in a natural science or technical field [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
2. Possess the required specialized knowledge in radiation protection [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
3. Have been trained in the expert activity by a person who has been active as an individual expert or examining person for at least three years [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
4. Have completed examinations according to Annex 19 during the training period [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 182 Prüfmaßstab (Examination Criteria)
The expert must assess the extent to which the technical design, function, and safety of the examined device, facility, or contained radioactive substance, as well as the structural conditions, guarantee the protection of personnel, the population, and the examined or treated persons [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 183 Pflichten des behördlich bestimmten Sachverständigen (Duties of the State-Appointed Expert)
The state-appointed individual expert is obliged to:
1. Immediately notify the competent authority of any changes affecting the prerequisites for the appointment [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
2. Ensure that the measuring devices and testing equipment used are properly maintained, suitable for the respective measurement task, and available in sufficient quantity [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
3. Regularly check and maintain the measuring equipment regarding its proper condition and functionality [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
4. Participate in the measures of exchange of opinions and experience specified in the appointment decree [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
5. Regularly carry out and document the quality assurance measures specified in the appointment decree [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
6. Submit a copy of the examination report to the authority responsible for the party obligated to the expert examination within four weeks after an examination [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
7. Report to the authority in whose jurisdiction they operate on the subject and scope of their expert activity, either regularly or on special occasion; specifically, records made during every examination must be summarized and presented to the authority upon request annually [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
8. Inform the authority responsible for the party obligated to the expert examination, as well as the Radiation Protection Officer or Radiation Protection Officer, immediately if they determine or suspect that life or health of persons or the environment is endangered by the examined device, facility, contained radioactive substance, or the examined workplace with exposure to naturally occurring radioactivity [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].

## § 184 Ordnungswidrigkeiten (Administrative Offenses)
The source lists numerous specific administrative offenses (Ordnungswidrigkeiten) related to non-compliance with the StrlSchG, covering areas such as failure to perform quality control, failure to provide documentation, and improper handling of radioactive substances [StrlSchV.md [Teil 9/15].md].
