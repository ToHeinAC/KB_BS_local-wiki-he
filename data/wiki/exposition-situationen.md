Expositionssituationen (Exposure Situations) are defined within the context of the Strahlenschutzgesetz (StrlSchG) and are crucial for determining the scope of radiation protection measures. The law distinguishes between three primary categories of exposure situations [summary-strlschg-01-md]:

## Categories of Exposure Situations
1. **Planned Exposure Situations (Geplante Expositionssituation):** These are exposure situations that arise from activities and in which an exposure is caused or can be caused [summary-strlschg-01-md].
2. **Emergency Exposure Situations (Notfallexpositionssituation):** These situations arise from an emergency, provided the situation does not fall under the provisions of a specific paragraph (Paragraph 4, § 2) [summary-strlschg-01-md].
3. **Existing Exposure Situations (Bestehende Expositionssituation):** These are exposure situations that already exist when a decision regarding their control must be made [summary-strlschg-01-md].

## Contextual Relevance and Principles
The classification of an exposure situation dictates which set of protective regulations applies. The concept is intrinsically linked to the definition of "Exposure," which is the incidence of ionizing radiation on the human body, whether external or internal, or the measure of this incidence [summary-strlschg-01-md].

The determination of these situations is critical when assessing the necessity of implementing protective measures, especially in the context of managing radioactive materials and industrial operations [summary-strlschg-01-md]. Furthermore, the law requires that any activity causing or related to an exposure situation must undergo a process of **Justification**, ensuring the benefits outweigh the risks [summary-strlschg-02-md].
