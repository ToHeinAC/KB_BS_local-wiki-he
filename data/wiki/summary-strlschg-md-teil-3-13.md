This summary details sections of the German Radiation Protection Act ($\text{StrlSchG}$), covering requirements for reporting, inspection, and operation of X-ray equipment, and procedures for research involving ionizing radiation.

### Reporting Requirements for X-ray Equipment ($\S 19$)

The submission of documentation for reporting an X-ray facility depends on the specific reporting category:

*   **Reporting under $\S 19$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1:** Required documents include:
    1.  A copy of the certificate from an officially designated expert ($\text{Sachverständiger}$) according to $\S 172$, including the test report, which must describe the X-ray equipment and the intended operation. This report must confirm:
        a) The X-ray source is type-approved ($\text{bauartzugelassen}$) or the X-ray equipment is marked as a medical device according to the Medical Device Act (in the version valid up to May 25, 2021) or according to Regulation (EU) 2017/745.
        b) That the necessary equipment is available and measures have been taken to ensure compliance with protection regulations according to the state of the art for the intended operation.
        c) If the X-ray equipment is used on humans, that the prerequisites of $\S 14$ Paragraph 1 Number 5 Letter a are met and the required acceptance test ($\text{Abnahmeprüfung}$) has been conducted.
        d) If the X-ray equipment is used for examination where operation outside an X-ray room is mandatorily required in individual cases (according to Paragraph 2 Number 5), that special precautions for the protection of third parties from X-rays have been taken.
    2.  If the X-ray equipment falls under Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1 Letter a, a copy of the approval certificate ($\text{Zulassungsschein}$) according to $\S 47$ for the type of the X-ray source.
    3.  Proof that the necessary number of radiation protection officers ($\text{Strahlenschutzbeauftragten}$) has been appointed and granted the necessary authority to fulfill their duties.
    4.  Proof that every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise, or, if no officer is necessary, that the person obliged to report, their legal representative, or, for legal persons or other associations of persons, the authorized representative for representation or management possesses the required expertise in radiation protection.
    5.  Proof that other persons working with the X-ray equipment possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding potential radiation hazards and applicable protective measures.
    6.  If the X-ray equipment is used on humans, proof that the prerequisites mentioned in $\S 14$ Paragraph 1 Number 1, 2 Letter b or c, Number 3 Letter b, and Number 4 are met.
    7.  If the X-ray equipment is used on animals in veterinary medicine, proof that the prerequisites mentioned in $\S 15$ are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    *   If the expert refuses to issue the certificate according to Sentence 1 Number 1, the competent authority decides on the application of the requirements of Sentence 1 Number 1 upon request. It may impose conditions for the operation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Reporting under $\S 19$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 2:** Required documents include:
    1.  A copy of the approval certificate ($\text{Zulassungsschein}$) according to $\S 47$ for the type of the X-ray equipment.
    2.  Proof of quality control conducted according to a legal ordinance under $\S 49$ Number 4, showing that the X-ray equipment corresponds to the essential characteristics of the type approval for radiation protection.
    3.  If it is a basic or high-protection device or a school X-ray equipment, the proofs according to Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 Numbers 4 to 6 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Changes in Operation:**
    *   For a substantial change in the operation of equipment reported under Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1, Paragraphs 1 to 3 apply accordingly.
    *   For a substantial change in the operation of equipment reported under Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 2, Paragraphs 1 and 4 apply accordingly.

### Inspection of Reported Operations ($\S 20$)

*   **Inspection Timeline:** The competent authority must review the documents within two weeks of receiving the report [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Operation After Notification:** If the authority notifies the reporter in writing before the deadline that all proofs according to $\S 19$ Paragraph 3 or 4 have been provided, the reporter may operate the X-ray equipment upon receipt of the notification [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Suspension of Procedure:** If the authority initiates a procedure to examine the justification according to $\S 7$ for a report under $\S 19$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1, it suspends the inspection procedure for the duration of the justification examination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Prohibition of Operation/Change:** The authority may prohibit the operation of an X-ray equipment under $\S 19$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1 or the change of operation under $\S 19$ Paragraph 5 if:
    1.  A requirement to be proven under $\S 19$ Paragraph 3 is not or no longer met (this only applies after the deadline if remedial action is not taken in a reasonable time) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    2.  Facts exist raising concerns about the reliability of the person obliged to report, their legal representative, or the authorized representative for legal persons or other associations of persons, or the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    3.  Facts exist raising concerns about whether the necessary personnel for the safe execution of the activity is available [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    4.  It concerns a non-justified activity type according to a legal ordinance under $\S 6$ Paragraph 3, or if there are significant doubts about the justification of the activity type considering a report published under $\S 7$ Paragraph 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    5.  There is a significant or repeated violation of the regulations of this law or the legal ordinances issued based on this law, or against the orders and decisions based thereon of the supervisory authorities, and remedial action is not taken in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    6.  This is necessary due to a significant danger to employees, third parties, or the general public [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    7.  Other public law regulations contradict the intended activity [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Prohibition for Basic/High-Protection Devices:** The authority may prohibit the operation of a basic or high-protection device or a school X-ray equipment under $\S 19$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 2 or the change of operation under $\S 19$ Paragraph 5 if a requirement to be proven under $\S 19$ Paragraph 4 is not or no longer met. This also applies to $\S 3$ Numbers 2, 4, and 7 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Prohibition for Full-Protection Devices:** The authority may prohibit the operation of a full-protection device under $\S 19$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 2 if:
    1.  Facts exist raising concerns about the reliability of the radiation protection responsible person [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    2.  The report does not include the documents required by $\S 19$ Paragraph 4 Numbers 1 and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

### Termination of Operation ($\S 21$)

Anyone who terminates the approved or reported operation of a facility for generating ionizing radiation, an X-ray equipment, or a stray radiation source, or the approved handling of radioactive materials, must notify the competent authority immediately [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

### Reporting for Testing, Commissioning, Maintenance, and Repair ($\S 22$)

*   **Reporting Obligation:** Anyone who commercially tests, commissions, maintains, or repairs X-ray equipment or stray radiation sources, or tests or commissions X-ray equipment or stray radiation sources in connection with their manufacture, must report this to the competent authority in writing before commencing the activity [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Required Documents:** The report must include proof that:
    1.  Every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise, or, if none is necessary, that the person obliged to report, their legal representative, or the authorized representative for legal persons or other associations of persons possesses the required expertise in radiation protection.
    2.  Other persons working during the testing, maintenance, commissioning, or repair possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding potential radiation hazards and applicable protective measures.
    3.  The necessary equipment is available and measures have been taken to ensure compliance with protection regulations according to the state of the art [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    4.  The necessary number of radiation protection officers has been appointed and granted the necessary authority for fulfilling their duties [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Prohibition of Activity:** The authority can prohibit activities under Paragraph 1 if:
    1.  Facts exist raising concerns about the reliability of the person obliged to report, their legal representative, or the authorized representative for legal persons or other associations of persons, or the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    2.  A requirement to be proven under Paragraph 2 is not or no longer met, or facts exist raising concerns about the availability of necessary personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

### Relationship to Regulation (EU) 2017/745 ($\S 23$)

Requirements for the nature of radiation devices, radioactive materials, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, and X-ray equipment that are medical devices or accessories under Regulation (EU) 2017/745 are governed by the applicable requirements of that regulation. Requirements of Regulation (EU) 2017/745 concerning the nature of devices and facilities for recording, storing, evaluating, reproducing, and transmitting X-ray images and digital examination and treatment data remain unaffected [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

### Regulatory Powers ($\S 24$)

The Federal Government is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to determine:
1.  Exceptions to the requirement of approval or reporting for activities, insofar as no damage due to the effect of ionizing radiation can be expected due to the quantity or nature of the radioactive materials, or due to specific protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
2.  Under what conditions the necessary precautions for fulfilling statutory compensation obligations for the approval according to $\S 12$ Paragraph 1 Number 3 are not required [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
3.  Under what conditions the manufacturer or importer may transfer a stray radiation source to another party [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
4.  Which X-ray equipment may be operated in schools, which radioactive materials may be handled in schools, which type-approved devices containing radioactive materials may be used in schools, and which special requirements apply to activities in schools [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
5.  The extent and manner in which the owner of a nuclear facility, a facility according to $\S 9a$ Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 second part of the Atomic Energy Act, or a facility for generating ionizing radiation, in which radioactive materials are handled or are to be handled, must inform the supervisory authority about any deviations from the data in the application for approval, including the attached documents, or from the approval [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
6.  That in cases where the handling of radioactive materials or the operation of a facility for generating ionizing radiation, an X-ray equipment, or a stray radiation source falls under the responsibility of several radiation protection responsible persons, this must be reported to the competent authorities, specifying who must report it and what documents must be submitted [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
7.  That radioactive materials:
    a) may not be used or placed on the market in a specific manner or for specific purposes, or
    b) may not be transported across borders,
    if this is necessary for the protection of life and health of the population from the dangers of radioactive materials or for the enforcement of decisions of international organizations of which the Federal Republic of Germany is a member [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
8.  How the protection of radioactive materials, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, X-ray equipment, and stray radiation sources against interference measures and other influences of third parties must be ensured [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
9.  Under what conditions an approval according to $\S 12$ Paragraph 1 Number 3 can be granted:
    a) for interim storage of radioactive waste that deviates from the obligation of delivery of radioactive waste to the state collection points and to the federal facilities according to $\S 9a$ Paragraph 3 of the Atomic Energy Act, regarding the extent of the associated danger, or
    b) for the granting of other exceptions to the obligation of delivery [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
10. Which activity and specific activity values of radioactive materials are considered release limits [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
11. From what activity a contained radioactive material is considered a high-radioactive radiation source [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

The ordinance can also stipulate the provisions of the ordinance for which the radiation protection responsible person must ensure compliance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

### Employment in Third-Party Facilities ($\S 25$ and $\S 26$)

*   **Employment in Third-Party Facilities ($\S 25$):** An approval is required if a person is employed in, or performs tasks in, third-party nuclear facilities, facilities according to $\S 9a$ Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 second part of the Atomic Energy Act, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, or facilities under the supervision of the state, and this can lead to an effective dose of more than 1 millisievert in a calendar year for the employed person or for themselves. An approval under Sentence 1 is waived in connection with third-party facilities where X-ray equipment or stray radiation sources are operated if a report according to $\S 26$ Paragraph 1 is submitted [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Employment in Connection with Third-Party Facilities ($\S 26$):** A report must be submitted to the competent authority before commencing the activity if a person is employed in connection with the operation of a third-party X-ray equipment or stray radiation source, or performs tasks themselves, and this can lead to an effective dose of more than 1 millisievert in a calendar year. Holders of an approval according to $\S 25$ are exempt from the reporting obligation under Sentence 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

### Transport of Radioactive Materials ($\S 27$ to $\S 30$)

*   **Approval for Transport ($\S 27$):** An approval is required for transporting other radioactive materials. The approval can be issued to the sender or carrier, or to the party arranging the shipment or transport. It is issued for the individual transport operation but can be generally granted for up to three years for a variety of transports. The approval also extends to sections of a transport operation that do not take place on public or publicly accessible transport routes, provided no approval for the handling of radioactive materials exists for these sections [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Exemptions from Approval ($\S 28$):** No approval is required for transporting:
    1.  Materials for which handling is exempt according to a legal ordinance under $\S 24$ Sentence 1 Number 1.
    2.  Materials exempted from the provisions for the transport of dangerous goods for radioactive materials.
    3.  Other radioactive materials:
        a) under the conditions for exempt shipments according to dangerous goods transport regulations,
        b) according to the regulations of the Sea Dangerous Goods Ordinance, or
        c) with aircraft and the required permit according to $\S 27$ of the Air Navigation Act.
    *   *Note:* Sentence 1 does not apply to the transport of large sources according to $\S 186$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 2. Sentence 1 Number 3 Letter a does not apply to the transport of high-radioactive sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Conditions for Approval ($\S 29$):** The competent authority must grant the approval if:
    1.  No facts exist raising concerns about the reliability of the sender, shipper, carrier, and persons arranging the shipment and transport, and, if no radiation protection officer is necessary, one of the mentioned natural persons possesses the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    2.  No facts exist raising concerns about the reliability of the radiation protection officers, and they possess the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    3.  The necessary number of radiation protection officers has been appointed and granted the necessary authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    4.  It is guaranteed that the transport is carried out by persons who possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding potential radiation hazards and applicable protective measures for the intended type of transport [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    5.  It is guaranteed that the other radioactive materials are transported while observing the applicable regulations for the transport of dangerous goods for the respective carrier, or, if such regulations are missing, that necessary precautions against damage from the transport of the other radioactive materials have been taken in other ways [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    6.  The necessary precautions for fulfilling statutory compensation obligations have been taken when transporting:
        a) other radioactive materials according to $\S 3$ Paragraph 1, whose activity per shipment exceeds 109 times the release limits set in a legal ordinance under $\S 24$ Sentence 1 Number 10, or 1015 Becquerel; or
        b) spent nuclear fuel according to $\S 3$ Paragraph 3, whose activity per shipment exceeds 105 times the release limits set in a legal ordinance under $\S 24$ Sentence 1 Number 10, or 1015 Becquerel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    7.  It is guaranteed that the necessary protection against interference measures or other influences of third parties is guaranteed [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    8.  It is guaranteed that when transporting other radioactive materials with an activity greater than 10¹⁰ times the release limits set in a legal ordinance under $\S 24$ Sentence 1 Number 10, the necessary personnel and necessary equipment are available to contain and eliminate dangers that may arise in connection with the transport due to accidents or emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    9.  It is guaranteed that the choice of the type, time, and route of transport does not contradict the protection of the population from the harmful effect of ionizing radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

### Medical Research ($\S 31$ to $\S 36$)

*   **Approval for Medical Research ($\S 31$):** An approval is required if radioactive materials or ionizing radiation are applied to humans for medical research purposes, unless the application is reportable under $\S 32$ Paragraph 1. An approval is also required if there is a substantial deviation from an application approved under this provision.
*   **Reportable Application ($\S 32$):** A report must be submitted if it is planned to apply radioactive materials or ionizing radiation to humans for medical research purposes, provided that:
    1.  The research project involves the testing of safety or efficacy of a procedure for adult, sick people, and
    2.  The application of radioactive materials or ionizing radiation itself is not the subject of the research project.
*   **Procedure for Reporting ($\S 33$):**
    *   **Completeness Check:** If the report under $\S 32$ is complete, the authority confirms this within 14 calendar days. If incomplete, the authority requests the deficiencies within 14 calendar days, and the applicant must remedy them within 10 calendar days of receipt. The authority completes the completeness check within 12 calendar days of receiving supplementary information.
    *   **Substantive Review:** The authority completes the substantive review within 28 calendar days after confirmation under Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, or within 21 calendar days after the submission of amended or supplementary application documents under Paragraph 1 Sentence 3.
    *   **Commencement of Application:** The application may begin when:
        1.  The period for substantive review under Paragraph 2 has expired or the competent authority has notified the applicant that it waives this period [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
        2.  The competent authority confirms receipt of a favorable statement from an ethics committee ($\text{Ethikkommission}$) according to $\S 36$ Paragraph 1 to the research project [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
        3.  The application has not been prohibited according to $\S 34$ Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Prohibition of Application ($\S 34$):** The authority can prohibit the reported application if:
    1.  A requirement mentioned in $\S 32$ Paragraph 2 to 4 is not met during the substantive review period [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    2.  After the substantive review period expires, a requirement mentioned in $\S 32$ Paragraph 2 to 4 is not or no longer met and remedial action is not taken in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
    3.  Against the provisions of this law or the legal ordinances issued based on this law, or against the orders and decisions based thereon of the supervisory authorities, there is a significant or repeated violation and remedial action is not taken in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Compensation Security ($\S 35$):** During the reporting procedure, proof of the required compensation security must be provided by submitting a confirmation of existing insurance that grants benefits in the event that a person is killed or injured or impaired in health due to the application of radioactive materials or ionizing radiation for medical research purposes, even if no other party is liable. The insurer must be licensed in an EU member state or other EEA contracting state. The scope must be proportionate to the risks, ensuring at least 500,000 Euros for the case of death or permanent disability of every person involved [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
*   **Ethics Committee ($\S 36$):** An ethics committee operating within the scope of this law must be independent, interdisciplinary, established according to state law, and registered with the competent authority. It must consist of medical experts and non-medical members who possess the respective required expertise. Registration requires listing the members, the procedure, and the address in a published procedural ordinance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
