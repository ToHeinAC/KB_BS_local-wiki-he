This document summarizes excerpts from the *Gesetz zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), detailing requirements for the operation, testing, and use of X-ray equipment and radioactive substances, particularly in the context of medical research and transport.

### § 19 Anzeige nach Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 1
For the notification according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1, the following documents must be attached:
1. A copy of the certificate from an officially designated expert according to § 172, including the expert report, in which:
    a) the X-ray equipment and the intended operation are described,
    b) it is established that the X-ray source is type-approved or the X-ray equipment is marked as a medical device according to the Medical Device Act in the version applicable up to and including May 25, 2021, or according to the provisions of Regulation (EU) 2017/745,
    c) it is established that the equipment is available for the intended operation and that the measures required according to the state of the art have been taken to ensure compliance with protection regulations,
    d) in the case of an X-ray equipment for the application of X-rays to humans, it is established that the prerequisites according to § 14 Paragraph 1 Number 5 Letter a are met and the acceptance test required by a legal ordinance according to § 86 Sentence 2 Number 13 has been carried out,
    e) in the case of an X-ray equipment for an examination whose operation according to Paragraph 2 Number 5 is mandatorily required in an individual case outside an X-ray room, it is established that special precautions have been taken to protect third parties from X-rays;
2. A copy of the approval certificate according to § 47 for the type of the X-ray source, in the case of an X-ray equipment according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1 Letter a,
3. Proof that the necessary number of radiation protection officers has been appointed for the safe operation of the X-ray equipment and that they have been granted the powers required for the fulfillment of their tasks,
4. Proof that every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise or, if a radiation protection officer is not necessary, the person obliged to notify, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal persons or other associations of persons, the authorized person for representation or management according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation possesses the required expertise in radiation protection,
5. Proof that the persons otherwise working with the X-ray equipment possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding the possible radiation hazard and the applicable protective measures,
6. In the case of an X-ray equipment for application to humans, proof that the prerequisites mentioned in § 14 Paragraph 1 Number 1, 2 Letter b or c, Number 3 Letter b, and Number 4 are met, and
7. In the case of an X-ray equipment for application to animals in veterinary medicine, proof that the prerequisites mentioned in § 15 are met.

If the expert refuses to issue the certificate according to Sentence 1 Number 1, the competent authority decides upon application whether the requirements to be proven according to Sentence 1 Number 1 are met. In this case, it may stipulate conditions for the operation. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 19 Anzeige nach Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 2
For the notification according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 2, the following documents must be attached:
1. A copy of the approval certificate according to § 47 for the type of the X-ray equipment,
2. Proof of quality control carried out according to a legal ordinance according to § 49 Number 4, with the result that the X-ray equipment corresponds to the essential features of the type approval for radiation protection, and
3. In the case of a basic or high-protection device or a school X-ray equipment, the proof according to Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 Numbers 4 to 6. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 20 Prüfung des angezeigten Betriebs einer Röntgeneinrichtung
(1) The competent authority examines the documents within two weeks of receiving the notification. If the authority informs the notifier in writing before the expiry of the period that all proofs according to § 19 Paragraph 3 or 4 have been provided, the notifier may operate the X-ray equipment immediately upon receipt of the notice. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

(3) The competent authority may prohibit the operation of an X-ray equipment according to § 19 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1 or the change of operation according to § 19 Paragraph 5, if:
1. one of the requirements to be proven according to § 19 Paragraph 3 is not or no longer met; this applies after the expiry of the period according to Paragraph 1 only if remedial action is not taken in a reasonable time,
2. facts exist indicating concerns regarding the reliability of the person obliged to notify, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal persons or other associations of persons, the authorized person for representation or management according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation, or the radiation protection officer,
3. facts exist indicating concerns about whether the necessary personnel for the safe execution of the activity is available,
4. it is an unjustified type of activity according to a legal ordinance according to § 6 Paragraph 3 or if there are significant doubts about the justification of the type of activity considering a report published according to § 7 Paragraph 2,
5. there is a significant or repeated violation of the provisions of this law or the legal ordinances issued on the basis of this law, or against the based orders and decisions of the supervisory authorities, and remedial action is not taken in a reasonable time,
6. this is necessary due to a significant danger to the employees, third parties, or the general public, or
7. other public law regulations of the intended activity are contrary to. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 22 Anzeigebedürftige Prüfung, Erprobung, Wartung und Instandsetzung von Röntgeneinrichtungen oder Störstrahlern
(1) Anyone who:
1. professionally tests, trials, maintains, or repairs X-ray equipment or stray sources, or
2. tests or trials X-ray equipment or stray sources in connection with their manufacture,
must notify the competent authority in writing before commencing the activity. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

(2) The following documents must be attached to the notification:
1. Proof that every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise in radiation protection or, if a radiation protection officer is not necessary, that the person obliged to notify, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal persons or other associations of persons, the authorized person for representation or management according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation possesses the required expertise in radiation protection,
2. Proof that the persons otherwise working during the testing, maintenance, trial, or repair of the X-ray equipment possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding the possible radiation hazard and the applicable protective measures,
3. Proof that the equipment is available and the measures have been taken during the testing, maintenance, trial, or repair of the X-ray equipment that are required according to the state of the art to ensure compliance with protection regulations, and
4. Proof that the necessary number of radiation protection officers has been appointed for the safe testing, trial, maintenance, or repair and that they have been granted the powers required for the fulfillment of their tasks. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 25 Genehmigungsbedürftige Beschäftigung in fremden Anlagen oder Einrichtungen
(1) Anyone who employs persons in foreign nuclear facilities, facilities according to § 9a Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 second part of the Atomic Energy Act, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, or facilities who perform tasks under their supervision, or performs tasks themselves, requires a permit if this can lead to an effective dose of more than 1 millisievert in the calendar year for the employed persons or for themselves. In connection with foreign facilities where X-ray equipment or stray sources are operated, a permit according to Sentence 1 is unnecessary if a notification according to § 26 Paragraph 1 is submitted. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 26 Anzeigebedürftige Beschäftigung im Zusammenhang mit dem Betrieb fremder Röntgeneinrichtungen oder Störstrahlers
(1) Anyone who employs persons under their supervision or performs tasks themselves in connection with the operation of a foreign X-ray equipment or a foreign stray source, must notify the competent authority in writing before commencing the activity if this can lead to an effective dose of more than 1 millisievert in the calendar year for the employed persons or for themselves. Owners of a permit according to § 25 for the activity according to Sentence 1 are exempt from the notification requirement. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 27 Genehmigungsbedürftige Beförderung sonstiger radioaktiver Stoffe
(1) Anyone transporting other radioactive substances requires a permit. The permit can be issued to the sender or carrier within the meaning of the regulations on the transport of dangerous goods, the consignor, or the person who arranges the shipment or transport. It must be issued for the individual transport operation; however, it can be generally issued to an applicant for a maximum of three years for a variety of transports. The permit also extends to sections of a transport operation that do not take place on public or publicly accessible transport routes, insofar as no permit for the handling of radioactive substances is available for these sections. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 29 Voraussetzungen für die Erteilung der Genehmigung
(1) The competent authority must issue the permit according to § 27 Paragraph 1 if:
1. there are no facts indicating concerns regarding the reliability of the consignor, the sender, the carrier, and the persons arranging the shipment and transport, their legal representatives, or, in the case of legal persons or other associations of persons, the authorized persons for representation or management according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation, and, if a radiation protection officer is not necessary, one of the mentioned natural persons possesses the required expertise in radiation protection,
2. there are no facts indicating concerns regarding the reliability of the radiation protection officers and if they possess the required expertise in radiation protection,
3. the necessary number of radiation protection officers has been appointed for the safe execution of the transport and they have been granted the powers required for the fulfillment of their tasks,
4. it is guaranteed that the transport is carried out by persons who possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding the possible radiation hazard and the applicable protective measures for the intended type of transport,
5. it is guaranteed that the other radioactive substances are transported while observing the legal regulations on the transport of dangerous goods applicable to the respective carrier, or, where such regulations are missing, that the necessary precautions against damage from the transport of the other radioactive substances are taken in other ways according to the state of science and technology,
6. the necessary precautions for the fulfillment of legal liability obligations have been taken when transporting:
    a) other radioactive substances according to § 3 Paragraph 1, whose activity per shipment exceeds 109 times the limit values set in a legal ordinance according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10 or 1015 Becquerel, or
    b) spent nuclear fuel according to § 3 Paragraph 3, whose activity per shipment exceeds 105 times the limit values set in a legal ordinance according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10 or 1015 Becquerel,
7. it is guaranteed that the required protection against interference or other influences of third parties is guaranteed,
8. it is guaranteed that when transporting other radioactive substances with an activity greater than 1010 times the limit values set in a legal ordinance according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10, the necessary personnel and necessary means of assistance are available to contain and eliminate dangers that may arise in connection with the transport due to accidents or emergencies,
9. it is guaranteed that the choice of the type, time, and route of the transport does not contradict the protection of the population against the harmful effect of ionizing radiation. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 31 Genehmigungsbedürftige Anwendung radioaktiver Stoffe oder ionisierender Strahlung am Menschen zum Zweck der medizinischen Forschung
(1) Anyone who applies radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for the purpose of medical research requires a permit, unless the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for the purpose of medical research is subject to notification according to § 32 Paragraph 1. A permit is also required for anyone who deviates significantly from an application permitted under this provision. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

(4) The authority may only issue the permit if:
1. the radiation-related risks associated with the application to the person included in the research project are medically justified, measured against the expected importance of the results for the further development of medical examination methods or treatment procedures or medical science, potentially taking into account the medical benefit for the person,
2. the radioactive substances or types of ionizing radiation intended for medical research correspond to the purpose of the research project and cannot be replaced by other examination and treatment methods that lead to no or less exposure for the person,
3. the exposure and the activity of the radioactive substances occurring during the application cannot be further reduced according to the state of science and technology without jeopardizing the fulfillment of the purpose of the research project,
4. the number of persons included in the research project is limited to the necessary measure for the fulfillment of the purpose of the research project,
5. a favorable opinion from an ethics committee according to § 36 on the research project is available,
6. the applications are conducted by a physician who possesses the required expertise in radiation protection and at least two years of experience in the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans,
7. the necessary precautions for the fulfillment of legal liability obligations have been taken, and
8. a permit according to § 12 Paragraph 1 Number 1 to 4 for application to humans is available or the operation of an X-ray equipment notified according to § 19 Paragraph 1 for application to humans is permissible. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 32 Anzeigebedürftige Anwendung radioaktiver Stoffe oder ionisierender Strahlung am Menschen zum Zweck der medizinischen Forschung
(1) Anyone intending to apply radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for the purpose of medical research must notify the competent authority in writing or electronically beforehand, if:
1. the research project has the examination of safety or efficacy of a procedure for treating adult, sick people as its subject, and
2. the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation itself is not the subject of the research project. Notification is also required for anyone intending to deviate significantly from an application notified according to this provision. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

(3) With the notification, proof must be attached that the necessary precautions for the fulfillment of legal liability obligations according to § 35 have been taken. Federal and state institutions are not obliged to provide this proof insofar as the principle of self-insurance of the respective body applies. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 33 Prüfung der Anzeige durch die zuständige Behörde
(1) If the notification according to § 32 is complete, the competent authority confirms this to the notifier within 14 calendar days of receipt of the notification and informs them of the date of receipt of the notification. If the notification is incomplete, the competent authority requests the notifier once within 14 calendar days of receipt of the notification to remedy the deficiencies named by it within a period of ten calendar days after receipt of the request. Within twelve calendar days after receipt of the supplementary information or documents, the competent authority concludes the completeness check in the case of Sentence 2 and informs the notifier of the result of the completeness check and the date of receipt of the supplementary information or documents. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

(2) The competent authority concludes the substantive examination of the notification within 28 calendar days after the confirmation according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 or the notice according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 3. If the competent authority has objections to the notified application, it transmits its objections with reasons to the notifier once within the period mentioned in Sentence 1 and requests them to amend their notification accordingly within 21 calendar days of receipt of the request. In the case of Sentence 2, the competent authority concludes the substantive examination of the notification within 21 calendar days after receipt of the amended or supplementary notification documents. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 34 Untersagung der angezeigten Anwendung radioaktiver Stoffe oder ionisierender Strahlung am Menschen zum Zweck der medizinischen Forschung
(1) Within the period of the substantive examination of the notification according to § 33 Paragraph 2 Sentence 1, including in connection with the Sentences 2 and 3, the competent authority may prohibit the notified application if one of the requirements mentioned in § 32 Paragraph 2 to 4 is not met. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

(2) After the expiry of the period of the substantive examination, the competent authority may prohibit the notified application if:
1. one of the requirements mentioned in § 32 Paragraph 2 to 4 is not or no longer met and remedial action is not taken in a reasonable time,
2. a favorable opinion from an ethics committee according to § 36 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 on the research project is not available to the competent authority after a reasonable period communicated to the notifier, or
3. there is a significant or repeated violation of the provisions of this law or the legal ordinances issued on the basis of this law, or against the based orders and decisions of the supervisory authorities, and remedial action is not taken in a reasonable time. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 35 Deckungsvorsorge bei der anzeigebedürftigen Anwendung radioaktiver Stoffe oder ionisierender Strahlung am Menschen zum Zweck der medizinischen Forschung
(1) In the notification procedure, proof of the necessary liability insurance must be provided by submitting a confirmation of existing insurance, which guarantees benefits in the event that a person is killed or the body or health of a person is injured or impaired due to the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for the purpose of medical research, even if no other party is liable for the damage. The insurance must be taken out with an insurer authorized for operation in a member state of the European Union or another contracting state of the Agreement on the European Economic Area for the benefit of the persons to whom the radioactive substances or ionizing radiation are applied. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

(2) The scope of the insurance must be in an appropriate ratio to the risks associated with the applications. It must be set based on the risk assessment so that at least 500,000 Euros are available in the event of death or permanent disability of every person to whom the radioactive substances or ionizing radiation are applied. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]

### § 36 Ethikkommission
(1) An ethics committee operating in the scope of this law must be independent, interdisciplinary, established according to state law, and registered with the competent authority. The ethics committee must consist of medical experts and non-medical members who possess the respective required expertise. Registration only occurs if the members, the procedure, and the address of the ethics committee are listed in a published procedural ordinance. Changes to the composition of the committee, the procedure, or the remaining specifications of the procedural ordinance must be reported immediately to the authority responsible for registration. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]
