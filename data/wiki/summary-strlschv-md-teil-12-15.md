# Strahlenschutzverordnung - StrlSchV (Teil 12/15)

This document provides detailed excerpts and appendices from the German Radiation Protection Ordinance (*Strahlenschutzverordnung - StrlSchV*), which supplements the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG). It covers complex technical aspects of radiation protection, including radioactive decay chains, criteria for the clearance of contaminated materials, and principles for calculating public exposure.

## Radioactive Decay Chains (Anlage 4)
The source includes extensive tables detailing the decay chains of various radionuclides, showing parent nuclides and their daughter products (e.g., Pb-210 $\rightarrow$ Bi-210 $\rightarrow$ Po-210) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].

**Key Examples of Decay Chains:**
*   **U-238 Series:** The decay chain involving U-238+ leads through several isotopes, including Pb-210+ $\rightarrow$ Bi-210 $\rightarrow$ Po-210 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
*   **Actinium Series:** The decay from Ra-228+ proceeds through Ac-228, Ac-224+, and subsequent daughters, including Th-228+ [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
*   **Radon Series:** The decay chain starting from Rn-222+ is detailed, showing multiple subsequent products like Tl-210, Pb-209, and Po-214 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].

## Monitoring and Clearance Criteria (Anlage 5)
Appendix 5 sets the monitoring limits and disposal pathways for residues.

1.  **General Monitoring Limit:** For the evaluation of residues, the sum of the maximum specific activities of U-238 and Th-232 must satisfy the formula: $C_{U238max} + C_{Th232max} \leq C$ [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
    *   The monitoring limit $C$ is set to $1 \text{ Bq/g}$ for dry mass.
    *   If dry mass cannot be determined (e.g., oils), a suitable procedure must be used [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
2.  **Alternative Limits:** Lower limits apply if:
    *   More than 5,000 Megagramm of residues are deposited in a usable groundwater catchment area in a calendar year, or if more than 50 percent of residues are added to construction materials in sports/play areas [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
    *   The residues are disposed of underground, the limit is $C_{U238max} + C_{Th232max} \leq 5 \text{ Bq/g}$ [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
3.  **Factor R Adjustment:** If the largest specific activity of Pb-210 and its relevant daughter products exceeds the largest specific activity of other U-238 series radionuclides by a factor $A > 5$, the limit is adjusted by a factor $R$ (e.g., $R=0.5$ for surface disposal) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].

## Exposure Assessment Principles (Anlage 6 & 7)
Appendix 6 outlines the general principles for determining exposure when residues are handled.

*   **Exposure Pathways:** When assessing exposure for individuals or professionals, realistic exposure pathways and assumptions must be used [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
*   **Comprehensive Inclusion:** For residues, all potential exposures arising from the planned disposal or recycling route must be included [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
*   **Groundwater Deposition (Anlage 7):** For common disposal, the assumption is that exposures will not exceed an effective dose rate of $1 \text{ mSv}$ per calendar year, provided the sum of specific activities meets certain criteria based on the disposal area size (e.g., $C_M = 0.05 \text{ Bq/g}$ for areas $> 15 \text{ Hektar}$) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].

## Site-Specific Clearance (Anlage 8)
Appendix 8 details specific clearance values for various materials:

*   **Building/Room Clearance:** Surface contamination values must not exceed the values specified in Anlage 4 Tabelle 1 Spalte 12 if future reuse is not excluded, or Spalte 13 if the structure is to be demolished [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
*   **Soil Clearance:** For surface contamination, the averaging area can be up to $100 \text{ m}^2$. Alternatively, for mass-based clearance, the averaging mass can be up to 1 Megagramm [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
