# Data Center Infrastructure

Data Center Infrastructure refers to the physical, technological, and energy systems required to operate modern computing centers, which are increasingly central to the global economy due to the proliferation of AI and cloud computing.

## Growth Drivers and Trends

The infrastructure sector is experiencing massive growth driven by:

*   **AI Compute Demand**: The computational requirements of Artificial Intelligence models necessitate exponentially more processing power, leading to a boom in data center construction and upgrades [^8][^11].
*   **Cloud Migration**: The continued shift of enterprise workloads to the cloud requires robust, scalable physical hosting solutions.
*   **Energy Management**: Managing the massive power draw and ensuring energy efficiency (cooling, power distribution) is a primary technical challenge and focus area [^13].

## Technological Focus Areas

Key technological components include:

*   **Power and Cooling**: Advanced solutions for power distribution and thermal management are critical for operational uptime.
*   **Automation**: The infrastructure itself benefits from industrial automation principles, requiring automated monitoring and management systems.
*   **Investment**: Major investments are being directed into U.S. data center manufacturing capacity [^11].
