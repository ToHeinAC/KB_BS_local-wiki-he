Data Center Infrastructure refers to the physical hardware, cooling systems, power management, and networking required to support modern computing and data storage operations [Siemens AG — Investment Research Overview].

## Growth Drivers
The sector is experiencing massive growth driven primarily by:
*   **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**: AI workloads require exponentially more computational power, leading to increased demand for high-density computing racks [Siemens AG — Investment Research Overview].
*   **Cloud Computing**: The continuous expansion of cloud services necessitates larger and more resilient data center footprints [Siemens AG — Investment Research Overview].

## Technological Requirements
Modern data centers require advanced infrastructure solutions, including:
*   **Advanced Cooling**: Efficient cooling systems are critical to manage the heat load generated by high-performance computing components [Siemens AG — Investment Research Overview].
*   **Power Management**: Reliable and scalable power solutions are paramount, often requiring integration with smart grid technologies [Siemens AG — Investment Research Overview].
*   **Automation**: Implementing industrial automation principles to manage the physical buildout and operational maintenance of the facility itself [Siemens AG — Investment Research Overview].
