# Public Exposure

Public exposure refers to the incidental or planned exposure of individuals in the general population to ionizing radiation or radioactive materials, outside of professional or medical contexts.

## Dose Limits
The StrlSchG sets strict limits for the general population:
*   **Annual Effective Dose:** The limit for the sum of effective doses from various sources (e.g., licensed activities, state storage) is 1 millisievert (mSv) per calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Organ Limits:** The sum of organ-equivalent doses is limited to 15 mSv/year for the eye lens and 50 mSv/year for the local skin dose [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Control and Planning
Control measures for the public include:
*   **Environmental Monitoring:** The law requires determining the expected exposure of the general population from planned activities, including setting dose limits for derived emissions in air and water [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Risk Assessment:** The competent authority must ensure that cumulative dose limits are respected when multiple activities are considered [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Exemptions:** Certain non-medical applications are regulated, and the law allows for exceptions to the requirement of a justifiable indication in specific screening programs [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
