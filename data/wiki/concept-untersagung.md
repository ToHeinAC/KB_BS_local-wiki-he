# Prohibition/Suspension (Radiation Protection)

Prohibition (Untersagung) refers to the action taken by the competent authority to halt an activity involving radiation, either during the review process or after the initial review period [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

## Grounds for Prohibition
Prohibition can be enforced if:
1. **Non-Compliance:** One of the required criteria is not met or is no longer met, and remedial action is not taken in a timely manner [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
2. **Reliability Concerns:** Concerns arise regarding the reliability of the responsible person, their legal representative, or the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
3. **Personnel Shortages:** Concerns exist regarding the availability of necessary personnel for the safe execution of the activity [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
4. **Legal/Technical Issues:** The activity involves a non-justified activity type, or there are significant doubts regarding the justification of the activity type [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
5. **Breach of Law:** There is a significant or repeated breach of the law or associated ordinances, and no timely remedy is implemented [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
6. **Danger:** Prohibition is necessary due to a significant danger to employees, third parties, or the general public [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

## Scope
Prohibitions can apply to:
* The operation of X-ray devices or interference sources (§ 20, § 22) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* The application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation in medical research (§ 34) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
