A *Veränderungssperre* (Change Restriction) is a legal mechanism established under the *Atomgesetz* (AtG) to protect sites designated for the disposal or investigation of radioactive waste.

## Purpose and Scope
The primary purpose of the restriction is to secure planning areas for projects related to radioactive waste disposal facilities, preventing changes that would significantly enhance the value of the land or substantially complicate the project [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].

## Implementation Details
*   **Establishment:** The restriction can be established by legal ordinance for a maximum duration of ten years. It is permissible to extend this restriction twice, with each extension limited to another ten years, provided the initial conditions remain valid [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Scope of Prohibition:** During the period of the restriction, significant value-enhancing or project-hindering changes are prohibited on the affected areas and in the subsurface [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Exemptions:** Maintenance work and the continuation of previously lawful use are not affected by the restriction [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **Duration and Compensation:** If the change restriction lasts longer than five years, the owner or other rights holders have the right to claim appropriate compensation for the resulting financial disadvantages from the project developer [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
