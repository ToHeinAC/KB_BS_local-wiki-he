# E-Mobility

In the context of industrial and automotive technology, **E-Mobility** refers to the segment of the market focused on electric vehicle (EV) and electrified powertrain components.

## Scope and Importance
E-Mobility is a critical growth area for manufacturers like Schaeffler AG, representing a substantial portion of the automotive sector's revenue [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md]. The division focuses on developing and supplying:
*   Electric drives and controls.
*   Mechatronics solutions.
*   E-axles and related power electronics [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].

## Market Dynamics and Challenges
The transition to electric vehicles is fundamentally reshaping the automotive supply chain. While this presents massive growth opportunities, it also introduces specific financial and technological challenges:
*   **Profitability**: The E-Mobility segment is noted to be loss-making, with an expected margin of **-14% to -17%** in 2025 [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].
*   **Competition**: The sector is highly competitive, with rivals like Bosch and ZF actively developing e-drives and power electronics [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].
*   **Technological Focus**: Key technological advancements include 8-in-1 inverter platforms and compact e-axle systems [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].

## Strategic Significance
For companies like Schaeffler AG, success in E-Mobility is crucial for future revenue stability. The ability to integrate mechanical expertise (bearings) with advanced electronics (power controls) is central to the company's long-term investment thesis [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].
