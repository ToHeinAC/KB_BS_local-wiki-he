E-Mobility, in the context of Schaeffler AG, refers to the segment of the automotive and industrial technology market dedicated to electric propulsion systems and associated control technologies. This area represents a critical growth vector for the company, marking its transition from a traditional mechanical supplier to an electrification powerhouse [^7][^8].

## Core Components
The E-Mobility offering encompasses several high-tech components:
*   **Electric Drives and E-Axles**: These are integrated units combining the motor, gearbox, and power electronics necessary for electric vehicle propulsion [^7].
*   **Controls and Mechatronics**: The development of sophisticated control systems that manage power flow and optimize vehicle performance [^7].
*   **Bearings**: Schaeffler leverages its core expertise in bearings, particularly in developing specialized, high-efficiency bearings designed for the unique operational demands of electric motors and e-axles, focusing on noise reduction and efficiency [^15].

## Market Dynamics and Risks
The E-Mobility sector is characterized by rapid technological change and intense competition.
*   **Market Driver**: The global shift away from Internal Combustion Engine (ICE) vehicles mandates continuous investment in electric drive solutions [^7].
*   **Financial Risk**: Despite its strategic importance, the segment carries significant financial risk, with reports indicating potential losses in the near term (estimated -14% to -17% margin in 2025) [^17].
*   **Competitive Pressure**: The segment is highly competitive, requiring continuous, substantial R&D investment to keep pace with rivals like Bosch and ZF [^13].
