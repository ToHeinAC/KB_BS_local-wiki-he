The *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) is the ordinance detailing the implementation of the German Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG). This summary provides excerpts covering cross-border transport, technical requirements for equipment type approval, and detailed procedures for the management and release of radioactive residues.

## Cross-Border Transport of Radioactive Materials
The ordinance specifies conditions under which radioactive materials can be transported across borders, particularly concerning non-EU member states.

### Requirements for Non-EU Transport
A permit is required for transporting radioactive substances that are not merely temporarily used within the scope of an approved handling process, if the destination state is not an EU member state [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md]. Specific criteria include:
*   **High-Level Sources:** Sources whose activity equals or exceeds ten times the value for high-level radioactive sources in Annex 4 Table 1 Column 4, or those lacking required markings or documentation [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
*   **Other Substances/Fuel:** Other radioactive substances (§ 3 Abs. 1 StrlSchG) or nuclear fuels (§ 3 Abs. 3 StrlSchG) whose activity per shipment equals or exceeds 108 times the release limits in Annex 4 Table 1 Column 2 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].

### Notification Requirements
For transporting other radioactive substances or nuclear fuels between non-EU states, or from the German jurisdiction to a non-EU state, if no permit is required, the transport must be electronically notified to the competent authority (§ 13 Abs. 1) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].

## Technical Requirements for Type Approval (Bauartzulassung)
The StrlSchV sets detailed technical standards for the approval of equipment generating or containing radioactive substances.

### Equipment Containing Other Radioactive Substances (§ 16)
A device containing other radioactive substances (§ 3 Abs. 1 StrlSchG) may only be approved if:
1.  It contains substances that are enclosed and protected from contact [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
2.  The ambient dose rate at a distance of 0.1 meter from the touchable surface does not exceed 1 microsievert per hour under normal operating conditions [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
3.  The design ensures safe containment, and no further leak tests are required beyond manufacturer quality control and potential leak testing (§ 25 Abs. 4) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
4.  The activity does not exceed ten times the release limits in Annex 4 Table 1 Column 2 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].

### Specific Equipment Types
The ordinance details requirements for various devices:
*   **Scatter Sources (§ 17):** Must not exceed 1 microsievert per hour at 0.1 meter from the touchable surface, and operation must rely on safety devices [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
*   **X-ray Sources (§ 18):** Requirements vary based on application (human/animal) and voltage, specifying dose rates at 1 meter and 0.1 meter [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
*   **Basic Protection Devices (§ 19):** Must enclose the object and maintain a dose rate of 10 microsievert per hour at 0.1 meter from the touchable surface [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
*   **High Protection Devices (§ 20):** Must fully enclose the object and maintain a dose rate of 10 microsievert per hour at 0.1 meter from the touchable surface (excluding internal areas) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
*   **Full Protection Devices (§ 21):** Must fully enclose the object and maintain a dose rate of 3 microsievert per hour at 0.1 meter from the touchable surface [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
*   **Waste Generation Devices (§ 23):** Must ensure that radioactive material generation via activation is excluded, and the dose rate must not exceed 3 microsievert per hour at 0.1 meter [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].

## Management of Radioactive Residues (Rückstände)
The StrlSchV establishes comprehensive rules for the handling, release, and disposal of residues.

### Release Criteria (Freigabe)
*   **General Release Criterion:** For materials and objects to be used, recycled, or disposed of as non-radioactive, the effective dose for individuals must not exceed 10 microsievert per calendar year [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
*   **Specific Releases:** Criteria are set for different waste types, including building rubble (Bauschutt) and soil, referencing specific Annex 4 Table 1 columns (e.g., 1000 Megagramm limit for rubble) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].

### Disposal and Recycling
*   **Waste Disposal:** Requires an explanation of the future fate of the waste and an acceptance declaration from the disposal facility operator [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
*   **Recycling:** Specific criteria apply for metal scrap recycling, including adherence to Annex 8 Part A Number 1 and Part G [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].

## Owner Obligations
The owner of a type approval must maintain a quality assurance system, conduct quality control before delivery, and provide specific documentation to the buyer, including the operating manual [StrlSchV.md [Teil 2/15].md].
