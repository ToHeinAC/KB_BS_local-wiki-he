This document summarizes excerpts from the German Radiation Protection Act (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), specifically detailing regulations concerning occupational exposure, contaminated sites, and building materials.

### Reporting Obligations and Exposure Assessment
The Act details mandatory reporting procedures for workplaces and third parties.
*   **Workplace Reporting ($\S 128$):** The responsible party must immediately report a workplace to the competent authority if measurements ($\S 128$ Abs. 2 Satz 1) do not show a reduction below the reference value ($\S 126$). The report must include information on the workplace type and number of affected workers, results of measurements ($\S 127$ Abs. 1), measures taken to reduce Radon-222 activity, and planned further measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md]. If no measures are taken based on $\S 128$ Abs. 4, the workplace must be reported immediately upon becoming aware of the special reasons. The competent authority may order reduction measures if the stated reasons do not justify foregoing measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Third-Party Activity ($\S 129$):** A third party operating or having operate activities in foreign premises must immediately report this activity if it occurs at multiple workplaces requiring reporting under $\S 129$ Abs. 3 Satz 1. The responsible parties must provide the necessary information to the third party [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Assessment ($\S 130$):** The obligated party must conduct an assessment of Radon-222 exposure, potential alpha energy exposure, or body dose from Radon exposure within six months of registration. If the assessment shows the effective dose cannot exceed 6 millisieverts in a calendar year, regular checks are required, and exposure must be kept as low as possible using suitable radiation protection measures. If the effective dose *can* exceed 6 millisieverts, the requirements of professional radiation protection ($\S 131$) must be met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

### Professional Radiation Protection and Site Changes
*   **Professional Radiation Protection ($\S 131$):** If the assessment requires professional radiation protection measures, the obligated party must take suitable measures to keep exposure as low as possible, determine the exposure via measurement, ensure dose limits are not exceeded, and comply with requirements set out in the relevant ordinance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Change of Workplace ($\S 131a$):** The responsible party must immediately notify the competent authority of: 1) the abandonment of the workplace; 2) changes proving the Radon-222 activity concentration no longer exceeds the reference value ($\S 126$); or 3) changes proving the effective dose assessment no longer exceeds 6 millisieverts in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

### Building Materials and Construction
*   **Reference Value ($\S 133$):** The reference value for the effective dose from external exposure in residential rooms due to gamma radiation from building products is 1 millisievert in the calendar year, in addition to the dose from outdoor exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Specific Activity ($\S 134$):** Producers or importers of building products containing specified mineral primary raw materials or residues must determine the specific activity of Radium-226, Thorium-232, or its decay product Radium-228, and Potassium-40 before placing the products on the market. These results must be recorded and kept for five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Measures ($\S 135$):** Products can only be marketed if the expected exposure does not exceed the reference value, which is determined by an activity index. If the expected effective dose exceeds the reference value, the producer must immediately inform the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

### Radioactive Contaminated Sites (Altlasten)
*   **Definition ($\S 136$):** Radioactive contaminated sites are properties, parts of properties, buildings, or waters contaminated by completed human activity, where the reference value for the effective dose of 1 millisievert in the calendar year is exceeded or can be exceeded for the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Responsibility ($\S 137$):** Responsibility lies with those who caused the contamination, those who succeed to them, the owner, those who exercise actual control, or those who abandon the property [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Procedure ($\S 138$):** If grounds for suspicion exist, the person must notify the competent authority immediately. The authority can compel persons named in $\S 137$ to conduct necessary investigations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Authority Powers ($\S 139$):** The competent authority can compel the responsible party to conduct investigations, ensure remediation measures, monitor population exposure, and monitor emissions/immissions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Planning ($\S 143$):** The authority can compel the responsible party to submit a remediation plan, which must include investigation results, current/future use, and planned measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Public Information ($\S 142$):** The competent authority must inform the affected public about the contaminated site, the exposure, and the measures taken [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

### Emergency and Other Situations
*   **Emergency Situations ($\S 151, \S 152$):** Specific rules apply to contaminated areas resulting from emergencies, using reference values set out in $\S 93$ or $\S 118$ respectively [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Other Situations ($\S 153$):** Responsibility for other existing exposure situations lies with the manufacturer, supplier, consignor, or owner of the radiation source, or those with actual control over it [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
