# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 9/13)

This section of the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) details comprehensive regulations concerning contaminated sites, remediation, and specific exposure scenarios beyond the initial parts covered in earlier sections.

## Chapter 3: Protection against Radioactivity in Building Products (§ 133 - § 135)
This chapter addresses the control of radiation from construction materials.

*   **Reference Value (§ 133):** The reference value for the effective dose from external exposure of individuals in occupied rooms due to gamma radiation from building products is set at 1 millisievert in the calendar year, in addition to the effective dose from external exposure outdoors [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Specific Activity Determination (§ 134):** Producers or importers of building products containing mineral primary raw materials or residues listed in Annex 9 must determine the specific activity of Radium-226, Thorium-232, or its decay product Radium-228, and Potassium-40 before placing the products on the market. These results must be recorded and kept for five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Measures (§ 135):** A producer must only market building products containing materials listed in Annex 9 if they can prove that the expected exposure from the radiation emitted by the product does not exceed the reference value. This is considered met if the Activity Index calculated according to the ordinance does not exceed the specified values. If the expected effective dose exceeds the reference value, the producer must immediately inform the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md]. The authority can then order measures or prohibit the use of the product for buildings with occupied rooms if the reference value cannot be maintained [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Chapter 4: Radioactively Contaminated Sites (§ 136 - § 148)
This chapter governs contaminated sites resulting from past human activities.

*   **Definition of Contaminated Sites (§ 136):** Radioactive contaminated sites are grounds, parts of grounds, buildings, or bodies of water contaminated by completed human activities, where an exposure exceeding the reference value of 1 millisievert in the calendar year for individuals can be caused or is caused [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Responsibility (§ 137):** Responsibility for a contaminated site lies with:
    1.  Whoever caused the contamination;
    2.  A person who follows in the overall legal succession of the person listed in number 1;
    3.  The owner of the contaminated site;
    4.  Whoever exercises actual control over the contaminated site; or
    5.  Whoever relinquishes ownership of the contaminated site [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Suspicion and Authority Powers (§ 138, § 139):** If there are grounds for suspicion, the person must report this immediately to the competent authority (§ 138). The authority can compel the responsible parties to conduct necessary investigations to determine the type, extent, and associated exposure, and to implement remediation measures to ensure the reference value is undercut [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Remediation Planning and Execution (§ 143, § 144):** The authority can require a remediation plan, which must detail investigation results, current/future land use, and planned measures. The authority can also create or supplement this plan if the responsible party fails to do so or if the situation is too complex [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Worker Protection (§ 145):** For remediation measures, an estimation of the body dose for workers must be conducted before commencement. If the estimation suggests exceeding professional dose limits, the measures must be reported to the authority, detailing planned precautions to keep the professional exposure as low as possible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Financial Aspects (§ 146, § 147):** Costs for ordered measures are borne by the responsible parties. Furthermore, the law addresses the right to compensation and value equalization, where the responsible party may have to compensate the public cost carrier for the increase in property value resulting from the remediation measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Other Residues (§ 148):** The provisions apply to mine workings and other residues from completed mining and industrial activities, provided the contamination is attributable to completed human activities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Chapter 5: Other Existing Exposure Situations (§ 153 - § 155)
This chapter addresses situations not covered by contaminated sites.

*   **Responsibility (§ 153):** Responsibility lies with the manufacturer, supplier, transporter, or owner of the radiation source causing the situation, or the holder of actual control over that source [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Assessment (§ 154):** The competent authority must take necessary measures to determine the cause, scope, and extent of the situation, and to assess associated occupational and population exposures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Emergency and Existing Contamination (§ 151, § 152)
The law adapts the rules for contaminated sites to specific emergency and existing exposure situations:

*   **Emergency Situation (§ 151):** For sites contaminated due to an emergency, the provisions regarding contaminated sites apply, using reference values set out in § 93 Abs. 1 or § 93 Abs. 2 or 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   **Existing Situation (§ 152):** For sites contaminated due to an existing situation, the provisions apply, using reference values set out in § 118 Abs. 4 or 6 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
