This summary details sections of the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) concerning specific regulatory procedures for exposure assessment, contaminated sites, and emergency situations, covering §§ 130 through § 155.

## Radon-222 Exposition und Abschätzung (§ 130 - § 132)

### § 130 Abschätzung der Exposition
The responsible party must perform an assessment of Radon-222 exposure, potential alpha energy exposure, or body dose due to Radon within six months of registration.
*   **Assessment Scope:** If registered by a third party (§ 129 Abs. 3 Satz 1), the assessment must cover the entire activity.
*   **Reassessment:** The assessment must be repeated immediately if the workplace changes in a way that could lead to higher exposure.
*   **Record Keeping:** Results must be recorded and submitted to the competent authority immediately. Results must be kept for five years.
*   **Dose Thresholds:**
    *   If the assessment shows the effective dose cannot exceed 6 millisievert (mSv) in a calendar year, the responsible party must regularly check the exposure and keep it as low as possible using suitable radiation protection measures based on general occupational safety regulations and considering all individual circumstances. The competent authority can demand corresponding proof.
    *   If the assessment shows the effective dose *can* exceed 6 millisievert (mSv) in a calendar year, the requirements of professional radiation protection according to § 131 and the ordinance according to § 132 Satz 2 Nummer 6 must be met.

### § 131 Beruflicher Strahlenschutz
If the assessment under § 130 Absatz 3 requires compliance with professional radiation protection requirements, the responsible party must take suitable measures to keep Radon exposure as low as possible, considering all individual circumstances.
*   **Measurement:** The Radon-222 exposure, potential alpha energy exposure, or body dose of employees at registered workplaces must be determined in a suitable manner by measurement.
*   **Dose Limits:** The responsible party must ensure that dose limits are not exceeded and that body doses are determined according to § 166. The regulations and limits of §§ 77 and 78 Absatz 1 und 3 Satz 1 und 3 apply accordingly.
*   **Legal Entity Status:** If the responsible party is a legal entity or a legally capable partnership, § 69 Absatz 2 applies accordingly.

### § 131a Aufgabe oder Änderung des angemeldeten Arbeitsplatzes
The responsible party must immediately notify the competent authority of:
1. The abandonment of the workplace.
2. Changes that demonstrably mean the Radon-222 activity concentration in the air at the registered workplace no longer exceeds the reference value according to § 126. Proof must be provided by measurement according to § 127 Absatz 1.
3. Changes that demonstrably mean an assessment of the exposure for the registered workplace no longer exceeds 6 millisievert in the calendar year according to § 130 Absatz 1.

### § 132 Verordnungsermächtigung
The Federal Government is authorized to establish by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council:
1. How multiple workplaces are to be considered a workplace in the sense of this section.
2. How the Radon-222 activity concentration at workplaces is to be averaged over the calendar year.
3. How the measurement of the Radon-222 activity concentration in the air at workplaces according to §§ 127 and 128 must take place, requiring it to be carried out by a recognized body, and specifying requirements for the measurement and the body performing the measurement, as well as the recognition procedure.
4. Which information the responsible party must provide to the recognized body regarding measurements according to §§ 127 and 128, and how the recognized body transmits this information, including measurement results, to the Federal Office for Radiation Protection for the fulfillment of its official duties, and how the Federal Office for Radiation Protection processes this information.
5. How the workplace-related assessment of Radon-222 exposure, potential alpha energy exposure, or body dose due to Radon according to § 130 Absatz 1 must be conducted, and what requirements apply to the assessment procedure and the person conducting the assessment.
6. That the measures and requirements of professional radiation protection listed in § 76 Absatz 1 and § 79 are to be applied even in the case of § 130 Absatz 3.
7. How the Radon-222 exposure, potential alpha energy exposure, or body dose in the case of § 131 Absatz 1 Nummer 2 must be determined, and what requirements apply to the determination procedure.
8. That the determination according to § 131 Absatz 1 Nummer 2 must be carried out by a measuring facility designated by the authority according to § 169, and what information must be provided to the measuring facility for the determination.
9. Which recording, retention, notification, and submission obligations exist in connection with the duties under § 131 and numbers 1 to 8.

## Kapitel 3: Schutz vor Radioaktivität in Bauprodukten (§ 133 - § 135)

### § 133 Referenzwert
The reference value for the effective dose from external exposure to individuals in residential rooms due to gamma radiation from building products is 1 millisievert in the calendar year, in addition to the effective dose from external exposure outdoors.

### § 134 Bestimmung der spezifischen Aktivität
Any party manufacturing or placing building products containing the mineral primary raw materials or residues listed in Annex 9 into circulation must determine the specific activity of the radionuclides Radium-226, Thorium-232, or its decay product Radium-228, and Potassium-40, before placing the building products on the market.
*   **Record Keeping:** The results of the determination of the specific activities determined under Paragraph 1 must be recorded and kept for five years.
*   **Authority Request:** The competent authority can require the party obligated to determine the specific activity to inform it about the results of the determination and the activity index determined according to the ordinance according to § 135 Absatz 1 Satz 3, as well as other quantities used for calculating the activity index specified in the ordinance.

### § 135 Maßnahmen; Verordnungsermächtigung
1. **Market Restriction:** The party obligated to determine the specific activity may only place building products containing the mineral primary raw materials or residues listed in Annex 9 into circulation without restriction if it proves that the expected exposure from the radiation emitted by the building product does not exceed the reference value. The reference value is considered met if the activity index determined according to the ordinance according to Satz 3 does not exceed the values set there. The Federal Government is authorized to establish by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council how the activity index is to be calculated and which values the activity index must not exceed.
2. **Notification of Exceedance:** If the expected effective dose from a building product containing the mineral primary raw materials or residues listed in Annex 9 exceeds the reference value, the party manufacturing or placing the building product into circulation must immediately inform the competent authority.
3. **Authority Action:** The competent authority can, within one month of receiving the information:
    1. Order measures necessary to maintain the reference value when using the building product for the construction of buildings with residential rooms, or
    2. Prohibit the use of the building product for the construction of buildings with residential rooms, if the reference value cannot be maintained.
    The building product may only be placed on the market after the expiration of the one-month period or according to the official decision.
4. **Information Duty:** The party must inform the building owner, the design engineer, and the contractor, within the meaning of the respective state building codes, regarding the restrictions imposed. If these persons are unknown, the building product must be provided with accompanying papers indicating the use restrictions.

## Kapitel 4: Radioaktiv kontaminierte Gebiete (§ 136 - § 148)

### § 136 Begriff der radioaktiven Altlast; Verordnungsermächtigung
1. **Definition:** Radioactive contaminated sites are properties, parts of properties, buildings, or bodies of water contaminated by completed human activities, where an exposure is caused or can be caused, which exceeds the reference value of the effective dose of 1 millisievert in the calendar year for individuals in the population.
2. **Authority Regulation:** The Federal Government is authorized to establish by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council the requirements for determining the exposure and test values below which a radioactive contaminated site does not exist.
3. **Planning Consideration:** When determining the exposure to determine a radioactive contaminated site, the legally permissible use of the properties and their surroundings and the resulting need for protection must be observed. If no planning regulations exist, the characterization of the area must be based on the foreseeable development. If there are uses on partial areas that deviate from the use to be based on the provisions of paragraphs 1 or 2, which can lead to higher exposures, these must be considered.
4. **Groundwater:** If there is concern that a radioactive contaminated site influences a groundwater aquifer, the use of groundwater must generally be assumed, deviating from paragraph 3.

### § 137 Verantwortlichkeit für radioaktive Altlasten
1. **Responsible Parties:** Responsible for a radioactive contaminated site is any person who:
    1. Caused the contamination,
    2. Succeeds to a person according to number 1 in total legal succession,
    3. Is the owner of the radioactive contaminated site,
    4. Exercises actual control over the radioactive contaminated site, or
    5. Disposes of the property of the radioactive contaminated site.
2. **Corporate Responsibility:** Also responsible is any person who is liable for a legal entity based on commercial or corporate law, who owns a radioactive contaminated site.
3. **Former Owner Liability:** Also responsible is the former owner of a radioactive contaminated site if they knew or should have known about the contamination and if the property was transferred after December 31, 2018. This does not apply to the person who acquired the property relying on the assumption that no contamination existed, if this trust is protectable considering the circumstances of the individual case.

### § 138 Verdacht auf radioaktive Altlasten
1. **Reporting Suspicion:** If any person listed in § 137 has grounds for suspecting the existence of a radioactive contaminated site, they must report this immediately to the competent authority.
2. **Authority Action:** If the competent authority has grounds for suspecting a radioactive contaminated site, it shall take appropriate measures to determine the facts.
3. **Compelling Investigation:** If there is sufficient suspicion of a radioactive contaminated site, the competent authority can require the persons listed in § 137 to carry out the necessary investigations, especially regarding the type, extent, and scope of the contamination and the exposure.
*   **Sufficient Suspicion:** Sufficient suspicion generally exists if investigations show an exceedance of the test values set in the ordinance according to § 136 Absatz 2 or suggest an exceedance, or if it is predominantly likely based on other findings that a radioactive contaminated site exists.

### § 139 Behördliche Anordnungsbefugnisse für Maßnahmen; Verordnungsermächtigung
1. **Authority Orders:** If a radioactive contaminated site exists, the competent authority can require one of the responsible parties to:
    1. Conduct investigations into the type and extent of the radioactive contaminated site, as well as the exposure and possible remediation and other measures to prevent or reduce the exposure,
    2. Report the results of these investigations to the competent authority,
    3. Take specific remediation measures, other measures to prevent or reduce the exposure, or follow-up measures to ensure that the reference value according to § 136 Absatz 1 is undercut,
    4. Monitor the exposure of the population resulting from the remediation work,
    5. Carry out further measures even after implementing measures according to number 3, insofar as this is necessary to secure the goal of remediation or other measures to prevent or reduce the exposure, or
    6. Monitor the emissions and immissions of radionuclides emanating from the radioactive contaminated site, including direct radiation.
*   **Legal Basis:** §§ 13 Bundes-Bodenschutzgesetzes and § 18 Satz 1 of the Federal Soil Protection Act apply accordingly.
2. **Basis for Measures:** Measures carried out according to Paragraph 1 Satz 1 Nummer 3 and 5 must be based on scientifically justified, technically and economically feasible procedures that are proven or proven in practice, or appear to be practically suitable. The type, scope, and duration of the measures must be optimized.
3. **Temporary Increase:** If the exposure is temporarily increased during remediation measures, this should not exceed a guideline value of 6 millisievert in the calendar year for individuals in the population. The guideline value for the effective dose of 1 millisievert in the calendar year for individuals in the population must not be exceeded due to discharges into above-ground waters.
4. **Ordinance Power:** The Federal Government is authorized to establish by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council:
    1. Specifications for emission and immission monitoring according to Paragraph 1 Satz 1 Nummer 6, and
    2. Requirements for the optimization of the measures according to Paragraph 2 Satz 2.

### § 140 Weitere Pflichten im Zusammenhang mit der Durchführung von Maßnahmen
1. **Reporting:** The responsible party must immediately notify the competent authority of the commencement and completion of the measures and submit appropriate proof of the effectiveness of the implemented measures.
2. **Changes:** Anyone intending to make changes to the affected property after implementing measures according to § 139 Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 3 and 5, especially changes in use or the deposition or removal of materials, must notify the competent authority four weeks before the intended start and prove that the exposure will not increase due to the change.

### § 141 Anwendung der Vorschriften für Tätigkeiten mit Rückständen
The provisions of Part 2 Chapter 2 Section 8 Subsection 2 of this law apply, deviating from §§ 138 to 140, if residues or other materials from the contaminated property are removed, even for the purpose of remediating the property, unless the residues or materials are used in the remediation of other radioactive contaminated sites.

### § 142 Information der Öffentlichkeit; Erfassung
1. **Public Information:** The competent authority must inform the affected public about the radioactive contaminated site and the resulting exposure, as well as the remediation measures, other measures to prevent or reduce the exposure, and follow-up measures.
2. **Registration:** The competent authorities record the identified radioactive contaminated sites and contaminated areas.

### § 143 Sanierungsplanung; Verordnungsermächtigung
1. **Remediation Plan:** For radioactive contaminated sites where a coordinated approach is necessary due to the variety of required measures, or from which risks to the individual or the public arise due to the type or extent of the contamination, the competent authority can require a responsible party to submit a remediation plan. The remediation plan must specifically include:
    1. A presentation of the results of the investigations into the type and extent of the radioactive contaminated site and a summary of the exposure assessment,
    2. Information on the previous and future use of the properties to be remediated, and
    3. A presentation of the planned remediation measures, other measures to prevent or reduce the exposure, and follow-up measures.
*   **Ordinance Power:** The Federal Government is authorized to issue regulations on the content of remediation plans.
2. **Binding Nature:** §§ 136 Absatz 3 and 4 and § 139 Absatz 2 of this law, as well as §§ 13 Absatz 2 and 4 and § 18 Satz 1 of the Federal Soil Protection Act apply accordingly. The competent authority can declare the remediation plan, even with amendments or side provisions, to be binding.

### § 144 Behördliche Sanierungsplanung
1. **Authority Plan:** The competent authority can create or supplement the remediation plan according to § 143 Absatz 1 itself, or have it created or supplemented by an expert, if:
    1. The plan has not been, or not within the period set by the authority, or is technically insufficient,
    2. A responsible party for the radioactive contaminated site cannot or does not can be involved, or
    3. Due to the complexity of the contaminated site situation, especially due to the large-scale extent of the contamination or the number of affected responsible parties, a coordinated approach is necessary.
*   **Binding Nature:** The competent authority can declare the remediation plan, even with amendments or side provisions, to be binding.
*   **Contract:** With the remediation plan, a draft of a remediation contract regarding the execution of the plan can be submitted. The remediation contract can provide for the inclusion of third parties.

### § 145 Schutz von Arbeitskräften; Verordnungsermächtigung
1. **Worker Assessment:** For remediation and other measures to prevent and reduce exposure at radioactive contaminated sites, the person who carries out the measures professionally or has them carried out by employees under their supervision must perform a body dose assessment for the workers before the start of the measures. The assessment must be repeated immediately if the work situation changes in a way that could lead to higher exposure. The results must be recorded, kept for five years, and submitted to the competent authority upon request. For other activities related to radioactive contaminated sites, the competent authority can require the person who carries out the activities professionally or has them carried out by employees under their supervision to perform a body dose assessment for the workers.
2. **Notification of Exceedance:** If the assessment shows that the body dose can exceed one of the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, the responsible party must notify the competent authority before the start of the measures. The notification must include:
    1. Information about the measures to be carried out,
    2. The body dose assessment,
    3. The number of affected workers, and
    4. Information about the precautions and measures planned for the reduction of professional exposure during the implementation of the measures.
3. **Worker Protection Measures:** The responsible party must:
    1. Take suitable measures to keep the professional exposure as low as possible, considering all individual circumstances,
    2. Ensure that the dose limits are not exceeded and that the body doses are determined according to § 166 for workers whose assessment showed that the body dose can exceed one of the values for classification as a professionally exposed person; the regulations and limits of §§ 77 and 78 apply accordingly,
    3. Ensure that the requirements of professional radiation protection according to the ordinance enacted according to Paragraph 5 are met.
*   **Legal Status:** For the responsible party, the duty of operational cooperation according to § 71 Absatz 3 applies accordingly. If the responsible party is a legal entity or a legally capable partnership, § 69 Absatz 2 applies accordingly.
5. **Ordinance Power:** The Federal Government is authorized to establish by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council:
    1. That the measures and requirements of professional radiation protection listed in §§ 73, 76 Absatz 1, §§ 79 and 89 for the protection of workers according to Paragraph 1 must be applied, and
    2. That the responsible party must consult with persons with the required expertise or the required knowledge in radiation protection when carrying out the measures.

## Kapitel 5: Sonstige bestehende Expositionssituationen (§ 153 - § 155)

### § 153 Verantwortlichkeit für sonstige bestehende Expositionssituationen
1. **Responsible Parties:** Responsible for an other existing exposure situation is any person who is the manufacturer, supplier, or consignor of the radiation source causing the other existing exposure situation, or who has actual control over this radiation source.
2. **Exclusions:** Not responsible for an other existing exposure situation is:
    1. A person who has transferred the actual control over the radiation source to a third party as manufacturer, supplier, or consignor according to the provisions of this law or the ordinance enacted based on this law, if this third party had knowledge of its property as a radiation source when acquiring actual control,
    2. The owner of consumer goods or other goods originating from the economic cycle that contain a radiation source causing the other existing exposure situation, or who has actual control over such consumer goods or other goods,
    3. A tenant or lessee who possesses actual control over a radiation source causing the other existing exposure situation, or
    4. A person who found a radiation source causing the other existing exposure situation, or who acquired actual control over it without their will, or who acquired actual control over it without knowing that it was a radiation source.

### § 154 Ermittlung und Bewertung einer sonstigen bestehenden Expositionssituation
1. **Authority Action:** Upon grounds for an other existing exposure situation or for a proven other existing exposure situation that cannot be disregarded from a radiation protection point of view, the competent authority shall take the necessary measures to:
    1. Determine the cause, further circumstances, and extent of the other existing exposure situation,
    2. Determine the associated professional exposures and exposures of the population, and
    3. Evaluate the collected findings overall.
*   **Atomic Law:** § 53 of the Atomic Law remains unaffected.
2. **Contaminated Goods:** If the other existing exposure situation concerns contaminated consumer goods or other goods in the economic cycle, the exposure situation cannot be disregarded if these goods or other goods:
    1. Contain artificially produced radionuclides whose activity and specific activity exceed the release limits set in an ordinance according to § 24 Satz 1 Nummer 10, or
    2. Contain naturally occurring radionuclides that can cause an effective dose for an individual in the population of more than 1 millisievert in the calendar year.
3. **Compelling Investigation:** The competent authority can require one or more responsible parties for the other existing exposure situation to carry out the measures according to Paragraph 1 and report the results.
