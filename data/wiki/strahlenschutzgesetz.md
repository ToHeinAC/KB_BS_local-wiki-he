The Strahlenschutzgesetz (StrlSchG) is the German federal law governing the protection of human health and the environment against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation [summary-strlschg-01-md.md]. It serves as the foundational legal framework for radiation safety in Germany.

## Scope of Application
The law's regulations are designed to cover three primary types of exposure scenarios:
1. **Planned Exposure Situations:** Exposures arising from activities that are planned or can be caused [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
2. **Emergency Exposure Situations:** Exposures resulting from an emergency event [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
3. **Existing Exposure Situations:** Exposures that already exist and require a decision regarding their control [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].

## Regulated Activities
The StrlSchG details numerous activities that are subject to regulation, including:
*   The handling, acquisition, storage, processing, or disposal of radioactive substances [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   The operation of facilities designed to generate ionizing radiation, such as accelerators or X-ray equipment [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   The use of radioactive substances in consumer goods, pharmaceuticals, or fertilizers [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].

## Exclusions and Limitations
The law explicitly excludes certain types of exposure from its direct regulation, such as:
*   Cosmic radiation exposure for individuals (except for flight/space personnel) [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   Exposure to naturally occurring radionuclides in the undisturbed Earth's crust [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
