The Strahlenschutzgesetz (StrlSchG) is the German federal law governing the protection against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md]. It establishes comprehensive rules for the handling, use, and disposal of radioactive materials and ionizing radiation sources.

## Scope and Key Regulations
The law regulates various aspects of radiation safety, including:
*   **Permitting and Announcements:** It defines requirements for permits (Genehmigungen) and announcements (Anzeigen) for activities involving radiation, such as employment in foreign facilities (§ 202) or cross-border transport (§ 207) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Medical Research:** Specific provisions govern the use of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation on humans for medical research (§ 205) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Facility Approval:** The law addresses the type approval (Bauartzulassung) of equipment and facilities generating ionizing radiation (§ 208) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Workplace Safety:** It sets rules for workplace monitoring and the designation of radiation protection officers (§ 211) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Transitional Rules
The source text contains numerous transitional provisions, ensuring continuity for activities and permits established under previous legal versions. For instance, permits issued before December 31, 2018, often remain valid until specified future dates (e.g., § 202, § 204) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Emergency Preparedness
The Act incorporates detailed planning requirements, including:
*   **Emergency Plans:** Annexes 5 and 6 detail the essential elements of the general and special federal emergency plans, covering risk assessment, reference scenarios, and optimized protection strategies for population protection in case of radiological incidents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Information:** Annex 4 compiles historical recommendations and guidelines from the Radiation Protection Commission regarding emergency response and public information [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
