Environmental radioactivity refers to the presence and measurement of radioactive substances in the natural environment, governed by specific federal and state monitoring mandates under the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## Monitoring Scope
The monitoring of environmental radioactivity is divided between federal and state responsibilities:

### Federal Monitoring (§ 161)
The federal government is responsible for large-scale determination of radioactivity in several matrices:
*   Air, precipitation, federal waterways, and the North and Baltic Seas outside federal waterways and in marine organisms [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
*   The ground surface, and gamma site dose rate [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
The federal government must also develop procedures for measuring and analyzing this radioactivity, compile data from various sources, and create dispersion forecasts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

### State Monitoring (§ 162)
The states are responsible for determining radioactivity in matrices that serve as indicators for environmental radioactivity, including:
*   Food, feed, and consumer goods [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
*   Medicines and their active ingredients [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
*   Drinking water, groundwater, and surface waters outside federal waterways [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
*   Sewage, sludge, and waste [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
*   Soil and plants [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## Data Management and Assessment
*   **Central System (§ 163):** The Federal Radiation Protection Agency operates an integrated measurement and information system to compile data from both federal and state monitoring efforts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
*   **Evaluation (§ 164):** The Federal Ministry for Environment evaluates the data on environmental radioactivity and is required to submit an annual report on the development of radioactivity in the environment to the German Bundestag and the Bundesrat [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
