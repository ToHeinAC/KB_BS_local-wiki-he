The source document provides excerpts and detailed appendices from the *Verordnung zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (Strahlenschutzverordnung - StrlSchV), detailing technical aspects of radiation protection, dose calculation methodologies, and specific regulations for radioactive materials.

## Radioactive Scrap Metal Recycling
The document outlines specific conditions for the recycling of radioactive scrap metal:
*   A release of scrap metal for recycling requires that the scrap metal, for which a determination was made according to § 42 Absatz 1, must be smelted [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
*   The values listed in Anlage 4 Tabelle 1 Spalte 14 do not apply to composites made of metallic and non-metallic components [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
*   Only smelting plants are suitable that can guarantee a mixing ratio of 1:10 of released scrap metal to other metals or that have a throughput of at least 40,000 Megagramm in a calendar year [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
*   For a release of scrap metal for recycling contaminated with a single radionuclide from the list (Be-7, C-14, Mn-53, Mn-54, Co-57, Ni-59, Ni-63, Nb-93m, Mo-93, Tc-97, Tc-99, Ru-103, Ag-105, Ag-108m, Cd-109, Sb-125, Te-132, I-129, Eu-155, Ti-204, Pa-231, Es-254 or Fm-255), the mass is limited to 10 Megagramm in a calendar year. A contamination with a single radionuclide exists if all other radionuclides together do not exceed an activity fraction of one thousandth [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].

## Dose Calculation Parameters (Anlage 11)
The document details the assumptions for calculating exposure (Expositionspfade) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md]:

**Part A: Exposure Pathways**
1.  **With Air:**
    *   1.1 Exposure through beta radiation within the exhaust plume (Betasubmersion) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   1.2 Exposure through gamma radiation from the exhaust plume (Gammasubmersion) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   1.3 Exposure through gamma radiation of radioactive substances deposited on the ground (Gammabodenstrahlung) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   1.4 Exposure through inhalation of radioactive substances (Inhalation) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   1.5 Exposure through ingestion of radioactive substances (Ingestion) via food [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   1.5.1 Air – Plant (Luft – Pflanze) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   1.5.2 Air – Feed Plant – Cow – Milk (Luft – Futterpflanze – Kuh – Milch) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   1.5.3 Air – Feed Plant – Animal – Meat (Luft – Futterpflanze – Tier – Fleisch) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   1.5.4 Air – Breast Milk (Luft – Muttermilch) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   1.5.5 Air – Food – Breast Milk (Luft – Nahrung – Muttermilch) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
2.  **With Water:**
    *   2.1 Exposure through staying on sediment (Gammabodenstrahlung) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   2.2 Exposure through ingestion of radioactive substances (Ingestion) via food [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   2.2.1 Drinking Water (Trinkwasser) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   2.2.2 Water – Fish (Wasser – Fisch) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   2.2.3 Livestock Water – Cow – Milk (Viehtränke – Kuh – Milch) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   2.2.4 Livestock Water – Animal – Meat (Viehtränke – Tier – Fleisch) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   2.2.5 Rainfall – Feed Plant – Cow – Milk (Beregnung – Futterpflanze – Kuh – Milch) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   2.2.6 Rainfall – Feed Plant – Animal – Meat (Beregnung – Futterpflanze – Tier – Fleisch) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   2.2.7 Rainfall – Plant (Beregnung – Pflanze) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
        *   2.2.8 Breast Milk due to ingestion of radioactive substances by the mother via the above mentioned ingestion pathways (Muttermilch infolge der Aufnahme radioaktiver Stoffe durch die Mutter über die oben genannten Ingestionspfade) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
3.  **From Nuclear Facilities/Radiation Sources:**
    *   3.1 Gamma radiation (Gammastrahlung) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   3.2 X-ray radiation (Röntgenstrahlung) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   3.3 Neutron radiation (Neutronenstrahlung) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
4.  **From Contaminated Ground:**
    *   4.1 Exposure through staying on contaminated grounds or next to contaminated deposits (Gammabodenstrahlung) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   4.2 Exposure through inhalation of resuspended soil or material from deposits (Staubinhalation) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   4.3 Exposure through ingestion of contaminated soil or material from deposits via the mouth (Bodeningestion) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
    *   4.4 Exposure through ingestion of food produced in private gardens next to contaminated deposits (dust path) or on contaminated grounds (Staubpfad) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].

*Note: Exposure pathways remain disregarded or additional exposure pathways (e.g., the seepage water path in contaminated deposits) must be considered if this is due to local site specifics or the type of nuclear facility/radiation source [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].*

**Part B: Consumption Rates (Verzehrsraten)**
The document provides tables for average consumption rates of representative persons in kg/a across various food groups and age groups (e.g., Drinking Water: 551 kg/a for $\le 1$ year; Milk, Milk Products: 160 kg/a for $> 1 – \le 2$ years) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].

**Part C: Breathing Rates (Atemraten)**
Breathing rates are provided in $\text{m}^3/\text{Year}$ for different age groups (e.g., $\le 1$ year: 1 $\text{m}^3/\text{Year}$; $> 17$ years: 8,100 $\text{m}^3/\text{Year}$) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].

**Part D: Exposure Calculation Factors**
*   **Table 3 (Aufenthaltsdauern, Aufenthaltsorte und Reduktionsfaktoren):** Provides exposure durations and locations (e.g., Beta radiation within the exhaust plume: 1,760 hours/year outdoors – 7,000 hours/year indoors) and reduction factors (e.g., Gamma radiation from the exhaust plume: 0.3) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
*   **Table 4 (Generische radionuklidspezifische Faktoren):** Provides generic radionuclide-specific factors (e.g., Gammabodenstrahlung: 2 for I-125, I-131) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
*   **Table 5 (Expositionspfadspezifische Faktoren):** Provides pathway-specific factors (e.g., Ingestion of food: 3) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].

## Radionuclide Activity Concentrations ($C_i$)
The document contains extensive tables detailing maximal permissible activity concentrations ($C_i$) for various radionuclides in air and water, categorized by binding state (A = adsorbed to particulate matter, E = elemental, O = organic) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].

*   **Table 6:** Lists $C_i$ for radionuclides like H-3, Be-7, C-14, etc., in air and water [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
*   **Subsequent Tables:** Provide comprehensive data for dozens of radionuclides (e.g., Cs-137, I-131, Sr-90, etc.) detailing concentrations in $\text{Bq}/\text{m}^3$ for both air and water [StrlSchV.md [Teil 13/15].md].
