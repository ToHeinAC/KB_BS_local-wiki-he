Radioactive residues are materials defined under the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) that result from various industrial processes involving radioactive substances. The law provides a detailed classification of what constitutes such residues and governs their handling, storage, and disposal.

## Definition and Composition
According to the law, residues include, but are not limited to:
*   Sludges and deposits from the extraction, processing, and refining of crude oil and natural gas, and deep geothermal energy [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   Gravel, sands, resins, and granular activated carbon generated during groundwater treatment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   Unprocessed phosphate gypsum, sludges from its processing, and dusts and slags from the processing of raw phosphate (phosphorite) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   Byproducts from the extraction and processing of various ores, including Bauxite, Columbite, Pyrochlore, Microlith, Euxenite, Copper Pyrite, Tin, Rare Earth, and Uranium ores [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   Dusts and sludges from flue gas cleaning during primary smelting in the iron and non-ferrous metallurgy [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Management and Disposal
The law specifies that residues can also include:
*   Materials derived from the ores listed above if the accumulation of these materials is purposefully induced [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   Molded items made from the materials listed in the first sentence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   Excavated or stripped soil and building rubble from demolition, provided this soil and rubble contain residues listed in the first sentence and are removed according to $\S 64$ or $\S 141$ [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Exclusions
Materials are explicitly excluded from the definition of residues if:
*   Their specific activity for every radionuclide of the U-238 decay series and the Th-232 decay series is below 0.2 Becquerel per gram (Bq/g), and they are not intended for use as building products, or
*   They are introduced into the technological processes mentioned above as raw materials [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
