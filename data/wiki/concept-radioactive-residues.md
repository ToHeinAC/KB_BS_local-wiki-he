Radioactive residues are materials defined under the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) that result from various industries [StrlSchG.md].

## Recycling and Disposal Regulations
The management and recycling of radioactive residues are strictly regulated, with specific rules detailed in the StrlSchV:
*   **Scrap Metal Recycling:** A release of scrap metal for recycling requires that the scrap metal, for which a determination was made according to § 42 Absatz 1, must be smelted [summary-strlschv-md-teil-13-15.md].
*   **Facility Requirements:** Only smelting plants are suitable that can guarantee a mixing ratio of 1:10 of released [summary-strlschv-md-teil-13-15.md].

## Detailed Characterization of Spent Fuel and Reprocessing Waste (AtEV)
The AtEV annexes provide highly detailed technical requirements for characterizing residues originating from reactor operations and reprocessing:

### Spent Fuel Elements and Canisters
The characterization of spent fuel elements and associated materials requires documenting:
*   **Spent Fuel Elements:** Standzeit24 X, Entladedatum X, Mittlerer Entladeabbrand X, Anzahl der unterzogenen Reaktorzyklen X, Abbrandzuwachs je Zyklus X, Mittlere Brennelementleistung X, Peaking-Faktor X, Oxidschichtdicke am Brennstab, Auslegungswert, axial und umfangs-gemittelt25 X, and Isotopenzusammensetzung des U und Pu zum Zeitpunkt der Entladung aus dem Reaktor X [AtEV.md [Teil 2/2].md].
*   **Pellet Fuel Elements:** Data must be recorded for the number of fuel element cans per container, identification number of the fuel element can, empty mass of the fuel element can, position of the fuel element can in the container, number of pellets per fuel element can, material of the fuel element can, total mass of the fuel element can, average burnup of the pellets in a fuel element can, highest burnup of a pellet in a can, burnup distribution of the pellets in a fuel element can, number of pellets by type (HEU/LEU/ Moderator/Absorber) X, and maximal core fuel inventory (U-233, U-235, Pu-239, Pu-241, Th-232) X [AtEV.md [Teil 2/2].md].

### Vitrified and Compacted Waste
*   **Vitrified Waste:** Characterization requires detailing the quality of the fixing agent (fixation in glass with optimized chemical composition), the glass matrix composition, the mixing ratio (Waste : Fritte : Zuschlagstoffe) X, the proportion of waste oxides (actual loading) X, the chemical composition of the glass frit, mixing, whether the waste is vitrified/incorporated (yes/no), whether the activity distribution is homogeneous (yes/no), the volume of the ingot X, the mass of the glass body X, and the glass product state (Density [g/cm3], Transformation Temperature [°C], storage below transformation temperature yes/no) [AtEV.md [Teil 2/2].md].
*   **Compacted Waste:** Details include the position of the ingot in the container X, ingot material X, specified pressing pressure X, weight of the pressling X, diameter of the pressling X, height of the pressling X, whether casings and structural parts were compacted together with technology waste (yes/no) [AtEV.md [Teil 2/2].md].
