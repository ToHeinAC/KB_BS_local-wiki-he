Radioactive residues are materials defined under the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) that result from various industries [StrlSchG.md].

## Recycling and Disposal Regulations
The management and recycling of radioactive residues are strictly regulated, with specific rules detailed in the StrlSchV:
*   **Scrap Metal Recycling:** A release of scrap metal for recycling requires that the scrap metal, for which a determination was made according to § 42 Absatz 1, must be smelted [summary-strlschv-md-teil-13-15.md].
*   **Facility Requirements:** Only smelting plants are suitable that can guarantee a mixing ratio of 1:10 of released scrap metal to other metals or that have a throughput of at least 40,000 Megagramm in a calendar year [summary-strlschv-md-teil-13-15.md].
*   **Contamination Limits:** For scrap metal contaminated with a single radionuclide from a specified list, the mass is limited to 10 Megagramm in a calendar year, provided all other radionuclides together do not exceed an activity fraction of one thousandth [summary-strlschv-md-teil-13-15.md].
