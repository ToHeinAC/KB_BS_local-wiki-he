# DIN 25422 Aufbewahrung und Lagerung sonstiger radioaktiver Stoffe

This document outlines the technical and organizational requirements for the storage and warehousing of other radioactive substances (*sonstige radioaktive Stoffe*). The guidelines detail security measures, barrier requirements, and specific conditions for storage rooms and hot cells, referencing multiple German regulations.

## Storage Room Requirements
### General Requirements (Section 6.4.3)
*   Storage rooms for other radioactive substances should not contain easily flammable furniture, although mandatory operational furniture made of easily flammable materials is permitted in exceptional cases [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].
*   If the storage rooms are intended to store other radioactive substances prone to spontaneous ignition or exothermic reactions, special protective facilities against these dangers must be provided [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].

### Hot Cell Storage (Section 6.4.4)
*   When hot cells are used for storage, DIN 25460 must be observed [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].
*   If fire protection is not fully ensured across the installation area, the fire protection enclosure of the hot cells must comply with the requirements of Table 3 [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].

## Theft Protection and Security Levels (Section 7)
The required security measures for other radioactive substances are determined by their activity in relation to the radionuclide-specific HRQ values and limits according to the StrlSchV [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].

### Applicable Guidelines
*   If no HRQ value is set for the radionuclide in the Radiation Protection Ordinance, a substitute HRQ value according to 7.2.3 e) must be used [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].
*   The required theft protection measures are detailed in the SEWD guidelines:
    *   **SisoraSt:** For handling other radioactive substances within the scope of a permit according to § 12 StrlSchG [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].
    *   **SisoraK:** For handling other radioactive substances within the scope of a permit according to the Atomic Energy Act (AtG), extending to handling other radioactive substances according to § 10a AtG [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].

### Security Levels (7.2.1)
The determination of the security level is based on the Security Value Z and the multiple of the limit X, using formulas (2) through (6) [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].

| Security Level | Activity Range / Security Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| F | Limit $\le$ Activity $<$ 100-fold Limit |
| E | 100-fold Limit $\le$ Activity $<$ 0.01 HRQ-Value |
| D | 0.01 HRQ-Value $\le$ Activity $<$ HRQ-Value |
| C | HRQ-Value $\le$ Activity |

### General Security Concept (7.3)
The required protection is ensured by suitable and coordinated structural-technical, administrative-organizational, and purely personnel measures. Generally, structural-technical measures should precede administrative measures, which in turn should precede personnel measures [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].

## Barrier Technical Requirements (Section 7.4)
The required structural-technical protection measures are defined by specific Theft Protection Classes (Diebstahlschutzklassen):

*   **Table 7 (Room Barriers):** Specifies minimum requirements for openings (doors, windows, etc.) based on the class (DR1 to DR5) and the height of the opening. For example, for DR2, openings up to 3 m require anti-burglary doors, gates, windows, etc., according to DIN EN 1627, resistance class RC 3 [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].
*   **Table 9 (Container Barriers):** Specifies minimum requirements for container-like storage facilities (DB1 and DB2). For instance, DB1 requires a lockable steel container or steel cabinet secured against unauthorized opening (e.g., in massive construction or multiple locking) [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].

## Additional Requirements (Section 8)
*   **Storage Rooms (8.1.1):** Storage areas must be sufficiently separated or shielded (e.g., by a basin for liquids) if an incorporation risk is determined during hazard assessment, and DIN 25425-1 requirements must be considered [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].
*   **Hot Cells (8.3):** Storage facilities in hot cells must have shielding if temporary entry is permitted, and suitable structural and technical facilities for easy decontamination must be provided [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 2/3].md].
