This document, DIN 25422:2026-04, provides technical safety specifications for the storage and keeping of other radioactive materials, mandated under the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) and the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md]. It details requirements concerning radiation protection, fire safety, and theft protection for storage facilities and their installation rooms.

### Scope and Applicability
The document applies to the storage and keeping of other radioactive materials according to § 3 Abs. 1 StrlSchG, and to nuclear fuels considered other radioactive materials under § 3 Abs. 3 StrlSchG [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Exclusions:** It is *not* applicable to the final disposal of radioactive waste. For interim storage of radioactive waste with negligible heat generation in approved facilities, or for the decay storage of radioactive materials exceeding five years, reference is made to ESK guidelines [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Legal Basis:** The requirements are based on the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) of June 27, 2017, and the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) of November 29, 2018, as amended by the Act of October 23, 2024 [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].

### Key Definitions
The document defines several key terms for the purpose of these regulations [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md]:
*   **Lagerung (Storage):** Safe housing of other radioactive materials that are not being processed, used, or otherwise utilized, and which are not on a transport route, in an operational position, or under constant supervision [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Aufbewahrung (Keeping):** Storage (3.1) for the purpose of imminent use. This typically lasts no longer than 4 weeks [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Aufbewahrungseinrichtung (Storage Facility):** A container- or room-like facility used for keeping (3.2) or storing (3.1) other radioactive materials. Examples include (cooled) cabinets, radionuclide cabinets, transport containers, working containers, protection containers, irradiation facilities, measuring/testing/calibration devices, glove boxes, or hot cells [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Aufstellungsraum (Installation Room):** A walkable room where storage facilities (3.3) can be installed or erected, and which can serve other purposes [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Lagerraum (Storage Room):** A walkable room primarily used for storing (3.1) other radioactive materials [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Raumgruppe (Room Group):** A group of interconnected rooms that are assessed together regarding fire or theft protection requirements. For activity inventory purposes, the rooms are treated as a single unit, summing the activities of all rooms in the group [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].

### Technical Requirements
The requirements are structured around three main protection aspects:

#### 1. Fire Protection (Brandschutz)
Fire protection classes are determined by the activity class and the type of area:
*   **Activity Classes (AK):**
    *   AK1: Above the release limit up to 10⁴ times the release limit [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
    *   AK2: Above 10⁴ times the release limit up to 10⁷ times the release limit [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
    *   AK3: Above 10⁷ times the release limit up to 10¹⁰ times the release limit [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
    *   AK4: Above 10¹⁰ times the release limit [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Room/Room Group Classes (BR):** Requirements range from BR0 (no special requirements) to BR3 (120 min fire resistance) [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Container Classes (BB):** Requirements are detailed in Table 2, specifying that the container must enclose its radioactive content after 30 minutes of heating at 800 °C ambient temperature, tested according to IAEA Safety Standards Series No. SSR-6 [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **General Fire Measures:** Measures must be planned for the preparation of fire fighting with competent authorities (§ 54 StrlSchV). The accessibility for emergency personnel (*Einsatzkräfte*) must be ensured [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].

#### 2. Theft Protection (Diebstahlschutz)
Theft protection levels (DR1 to DR5) are assigned based on requirements, with the first measure ideally being effective at a distance from other radioactive materials [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].

#### 3. Radiation Protection (Strahlenschutz)
*   **Shielding:** Shielding against ionizing radiation and dense enclosures to prevent the spread of contaminated materials should generally enclose the radioactive materials at a short distance (see Section 8) [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
*   **Combined Measures:** Protection measures can be combined, provided they do not impair the protection level required by radiation and fire protection laws [DIN 25422_2026-04.PDF [Teil 1/3].md].
