This document provides excerpts from the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV), which details the operational and procedural requirements for radiation protection in Germany. It covers duties of responsible persons, the establishment and management of controlled areas, and required personnel qualifications.

## Operational Duties and Responsibilities

### Duties of the Responsible Person (Inhaber einer Freigabe)
*   **§ 42 Pflichten des Inhabers einer Freigabe:** The Radiation Protection Responsible Person (Strahlenschutzverantwortliche) must confirm the conformity of any material used, processed, stored, or transferred as non-radioactive material with the content of the release notice.
*   **§ 42 (2):** Measurements required to confirm conformity (Freimessungen) and their results must be documented by the responsible person.
*   **§ 42 (3):** The responsible person must immediately inform the competent authority if any requirement upon which the release depends is no longer met.

### Radiation Protection Officer and Instruction
*   **§ 43 Pflichten des Strahlenschutzbeauftragten:** The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure compliance with the duties assigned to the responsible person by this ordinance, provided the corresponding tasks and powers have been transferred according to § 70 Absatz 2 des Strahlenschutzgesetzes.
*   **§ 45 Strahlenschutzanweisung:** The responsible person must ensure that a Radiation Protection Instruction (Strahlenschutzanweisung) is issued. This instruction can be part of other required operating instructions (e.g., occupational safety, emissions protection). It must specify:
    *   Protective measures required in the operation.
    *   A plan for the organization of radiation protection, including designating responsible officers.
    *   Rules for determining dose limits for employees and other persons.
    *   The maintenance of an operating logbook.
    *   Rules for avoiding, investigating, and reporting incidents.
    *   Regular functional testing and maintenance of equipment.
    *   Rules for protection against interference or loss of radioactive materials.
    *   A plan for regular alarm drills and emergency response.
*   **§ 45 (3):** The Radiation Protection Instruction must be updated immediately upon substantial changes.

## Radiation Protection Areas (Strahlenschutzbereiche)
The ordinance details the establishment, demarcation, and monitoring of restricted areas:

*   **§ 52 Einrichten von Strahlenschutzbereichen:** Radiation protection areas must be established for activities where the exposure of persons can exceed the limits for individuals according to § 80 Absatz 1 and 2 of the Radiation Protection Act.
    *   **Überwachungsbereich (Monitoring Area):** Required if persons can receive an effective dose of more than 1 mSv or an organ-equivalent dose of more than 50 mSv for hands, forearms, feet, or ankles, or a local skin dose of more than 50 mSv over a calendar year.
    *   **Kontrollbereich (Control Area):** Required if persons can receive an effective dose of more than 6 mSv or an organ-equivalent dose of more than 15 mSv for the eye lens or 150 mSv for hands, or a local skin dose of more than 150 mSv over a calendar year.
    *   **Sperrbereich (Exclusion Area):** Required if the site dose rate can be higher than 3 mSv per hour.
*   **§ 53 Abgrenzung, Kennzeichnung und Sicherung:** Control areas must be clearly marked with "Kontrollbereich." Exclusion areas must be marked with "Sperrbereich – Kein Zutritt" and secured so that people cannot enter uncontrolled.
*   **§ 55 Zutritt zu Strahlenschutzbereichen:** Access is restricted. For example, entry to a Control Area requires the person to be performing operations or requiring the application of radiation to themselves or a care/accompanying person, and requires consent from a qualified medical professional.
*   **§ 56 Messtechnische Überwachung:** The responsible person must ensure that the site dose or dose rate, the concentration of radioactive substances in the air, or the contamination of the workplace is measured. Records must be kept for at least five years.
*   **§ 57 Kontamination und Dekontamination:** Measures must be taken if contamination is found, especially if the non-adherent surface contamination exceeds specific thresholds (e.g., 100 times the values of Anlage 4 Tabelle 1 Spalte 5 in Control Areas).

## Personnel and Qualifications
*   **§ 47 Erforderliche Fachkunde:** The required expertise must be proven by a certificate issued by the competent authority. Proof requires:
    1.  Evidence of suitable training for the respective application area.
    2.  Proof of practical experience.
    3.  Proof of successful participation in recognized courses.
*   **§ 48 Aktualisierung der Fachkunde:** The required expertise must be updated at least every five years through recognized courses or other suitable continuing education measures.
*   **§ 63 Unterweisung:** The responsible person must ensure that certain persons receive instruction (Unterweisung) covering working methods, possible dangers, safety measures, and data processing related to dose limits. This instruction must be repeated at least once a year.
