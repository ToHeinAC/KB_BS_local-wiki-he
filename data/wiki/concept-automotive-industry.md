# Automotive Industry

The Automotive Industry encompasses the design, manufacturing, and servicing of motor vehicles. It is a sector undergoing a profound structural transformation driven by technological advancements and regulatory changes.

## Structural Transformation
The industry is transitioning from reliance on Internal Combustion Engines (ICE) to Electric Vehicles (EVs) [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md]. This shift impacts every component supplier, requiring a pivot from mechanical expertise to expertise in power electronics, software, and battery management systems [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].

## Key Business Dynamics
*   **Cyclicality**: The sector remains highly cyclical, meaning revenue is sensitive to global economic downturns and production cycles [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].
*   **Supply Chain**: The structure is characterized by complex, multi-tiered relationships, with Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) acting as primary clients for Tier-1 suppliers like Schaeffler AG [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].
*   **Competition**: The market is consolidating, with intense competition from established players (Bosch, ZF) and emerging, technologically advanced competitors, including Chinese suppliers [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].

## Supplier Role
Suppliers are increasingly required to offer integrated solutions rather than discrete parts. The ability to merge mechanical strength (bearings, powertrains) with electrical expertise (e-drives, inverters) is becoming a key determinant of market leadership [Schaeffler AG – Investment Overview.md].
