# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 5/13)

This document provides a detailed summary of sections 67 through 76 of the German Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG), focusing on operational organization, dose monitoring, waste management, and site contamination control [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## General Provisions (§ 67)
*   **Exemption from Permit/Notification:** Individuals employed or supervised in activities requiring a permit or notification under the Act are exempt from needing either a permit or submitting a notification [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Waste Management (Rückstände) (§ 60 - § 63)

### Waste Generation and Concept (§ 60)
*   **Reporting Requirement:** Entities conducting industrial or mining processes generating over 2,000 tonnes of residues annually must report this to the competent authority and the authority under the Circular Economy Act (§ 47 Abs. 1 Satz 1 KrWG) at the beginning of each calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Residue Concept (Rückstandskonzept):** The responsible party must create and submit a concept detailing the type, mass, specific activity, and whereabouts of the residues, including estimates for the next five years, and outlining planned disposal or utilization measures for the next five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. This concept must be updated every five years or upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Residue Balance (Rückstandsbilanz):** An annual balance sheet detailing the type, mass, specific activity, and whereabouts of utilized and disposed residues must be created, kept for five years, and submitted upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### Controlled Residues (§ 61)
*   **Controlled Status:** Residues are considered controlled (*überwachungsbedürftig*) if it cannot be ensured that established monitoring limits and utilization/disposal paths are maintained upon disposal or utilization [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Protection Measures:** If residues are expected to exceed the reference value of 1 millisievert (mSv) for the effective dose of individuals in a calendar year, protective measures must be taken, and consultation with a qualified person is required [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Storage:** Controlled residues must be secured against loss and unauthorized access. Cross-border transport of residues into Germany for disposal is prohibited [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### Release from Control (§ 62)
*   **Release Criteria:** Controlled residues can be released from monitoring if:
    1.  Protection of the population from exposure is ensured by the measures taken for utilization or disposal.
    2.  The body dose of professionally active persons cannot exceed the values for classification as professionally exposed persons during disposal or utilization.
    3.  There are no concerns regarding the legal admissibility of the planned utilization or disposal path and its compliance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Dose Limit:** The reference value for the effective dose of individuals in the population, resulting from disposal or utilization, must not exceed 1 mSv in a calendar year, even without further measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### Residues Remaining Under Control (§ 63)
*   If release from monitoring (§ 62) is not possible, the responsible party must report the type, mass, and specific activity of the residues remaining under monitoring, along with a planned disposal or utilization, within the deadline set in § 61 Abs. 1 Satz 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Site Contamination (§ 64)
*   **Remediation:** When industrial or mining processes involving controlled residues cease, contamination must be removed before the property is used by third parties, no later than five years after the end of use. The standard for unrestricted use is that the exposure of individuals from the remaining residues does not exceed the reference value of 1 mSv in a calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Other Materials and Organization (§ 65 - § 72)
*   **Other Materials (§ 65):** The competent authority can issue orders regarding materials that are not residues but whose handling significantly increases the exposure of the population, mandating protective measures or specifying handling/storage locations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Personnel Responsibilities (§ 69 - § 72):**
    *   **Radiation Protection Officer (Strahlenschutzverantwortlicher):** Must be appointed for activities requiring permits or notifications. They are responsible for ensuring protective measures are implemented, including preventing unnecessary exposure and keeping exposures as low as possible, and ensuring compliance with various regulations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    *   **Radiation Protection Officer (Strahlenschutzbeauftragter):** Must be appointed by the Radiation Protection Officer for supervising activities. They must be qualified and report deficiencies to the Radiation Protection Officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    *   **Collaboration:** The Radiation Protection Officer and the Radiation Protection Officer must collaborate with the Works Council/Personnel Council and safety experts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Dose Monitoring (§ 76):** The Federal Government is empowered to regulate requirements for physical radiation protection control and set guidelines for monitoring areas, control areas, and exclusion zones.
