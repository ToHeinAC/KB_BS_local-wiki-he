This document provides excerpts from the *Gesetz zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), specifically covering sections § 57 through § 75, detailing operational requirements, waste management, and organizational duties in radiation protection.

### § 57 Prüfung der angezeigten Tätigkeit
The responsible authority (§ 57) is responsible for checking the reported activity.
*   **Review Period:** The authority must review the report within four weeks of receiving the documents. If the authority informs the applicant that all necessary evidence has been provided before this deadline, the applicant may commence the activity immediately upon receipt of the notice [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Suspension:** If the authority initiates a procedure to check the justification (§ 7), it suspends the review of the report for the duration of the justification procedure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Prohibition:** The authority may prohibit the activity if:
    1.  An requirement specified in § 56 Abs. 2 Satz 1 is not met or no longer met; this applies after the deadline in Abs. 1 only if remedy is not provided in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  Facts exist that raise concerns regarding the reliability of the person obliged to report, their legal representative, or the authorized person for representation or management of legal entities or other associations of persons, or the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    3.  Facts exist that raise concerns about whether the necessary personnel for the safe execution of the activity are available [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    4.  It concerns an activity type that is not justified according to a legal ordinance under § 6 Abs. 3, or if there are significant doubts about the justification of the activity type considering a report published under § 7 Abs. 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    5.  There is a significant or repeated violation of the provisions of this law or the legal ordinances issued based on this law, or against the resulting orders and decisions of the supervisory authorities, and no remedy is provided in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    6.  This is necessary due to a significant danger to employees, third parties, or the general public [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Non-compliance:** If the person obliged to estimate according to § 55 Abs. 2 fails to comply with the enforceable administrative order, the authority may prohibit the activity, wholly or partially, until the order is fulfilled [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 58 Beendigung der angezeigten Tätigkeit
Anyone who terminates or changes an activity reported according to § 56 Abs. 1 Satz 1, such that an estimation according to § 55 Abs. 1 Satz 2 shows that the body dose can no longer exceed the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, must notify the responsible authority immediately [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 59 Externe Tätigkeit
*   **Scope:** The obligation to estimate the body dose according to § 55 Abs. 1 applies correspondingly to those who carry out the mentioned activities in a foreign operating site in their own responsibility or have persons under their supervision do so [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Documentation:** If an estimation for the foreign operating site exists, the owner of the operating site must immediately transmit a copy of the records on the estimation to the person obliged according to Satz 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Reporting:** If the estimation according to Abs. 1 Satz 1 or 3 shows that the body dose can exceed one of the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, the person obliged according to Abs. 1 Satz 1 or 3 must report the activity to the responsible authority according to § 56 Abs. 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Required Documents:** The report according to Abs. 2 must include:
    1.  Proof that every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise in radiation protection, or, if a radiation protection officer is not necessary, that the applicant, their legal representative, or the authorized person for representation or management of legal entities or other associations of persons possesses the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  Proof that other persons engaged in the activity possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding possible radiation hazards and the applicable protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    3.  Proof that the employed persons are obliged to comply with the orders of the radiation protection officers and radiation protection officers in the operating sites where an activity reported according to § 56 Abs. 1 is carried out, which these individuals issue due to their duties under this law and the legal ordinances issued based on this law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    4.  Proof that the equipment is available and the measures have been taken for the employment in the operating sites for which a report according to § 56 Abs. 1 is not permitted, to ensure that the protection regulations are met according to the state of the art [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Scope Extension:** § 56 Abs. 3 and §§ 57 and 58 apply correspondingly to the activity reported according to Abs. 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 60 Anfall, Verwertung oder Beseitigung von Rückständen
*   **Reporting Obligation:** Any entity conducting or having industrial and mining processes in its operating site that will generate, recycle, or dispose of more than a total of 2,000 tons of waste annually must report this to the responsible authority and the authority responsible according to § 47 Abs. 1 Satz 1 of the Circular Economy Act at the beginning of each calendar year. This obligation also applies to those who recycle or accept waste that has occurred abroad and has been brought into the country [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Waste Concept (Rückstandskonzept):** The obliged entity must create a concept on the recycling and disposal of waste and submit it to the responsible authority upon request. This concept must include:
    1.  Data on the type, mass, specific activity, and location of the waste, including estimates of the waste generated in the next five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  A presentation of the measures taken and planned for disposal or recycling for the next five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Updates:** The waste concept must be updated every five years or at an earlier time upon request from the responsible authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Waste Balance (Rückstandsbilanz):** The obliged entity must annually create a balance sheet for the preceding year on the type, mass, specific activity, and location of the recycled and disposed waste, keep it for five years, and submit it to the responsible authority upon request. The authority may also require corresponding proof according to § 21 of the Circular Economy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 61 Anfall und Lagerung überwachungsbedürftiger Rückstände
*   **Protection Measures:** Anyone conducting or having industrial and mining processes in their own responsibility that generate controlled waste, where the storage, recycling, or disposal could exceed the reference value of an effective dose of 1 millisievert for individual members of the population in a calendar year, must take measures to protect the population to ensure that the reference value is not exceeded, and must consult with a person with the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Definition of Controlled Waste:** Waste is controlled if it cannot be ensured that the monitoring limits and recycling/disposal paths specified by legal ordinance according to Satz 2 are maintained during its disposal or recycling. The Federal Government is authorized by legal ordinance to establish the monitoring limits and the applicable recycling and disposal paths for waste [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Handling:** Generated waste must not be mixed or diluted before the intended disposal or recycling to comply with the monitoring limits according to Abs. 2. Satz 1 also applies to waste generated abroad and brought into the country for recycling [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Storage:** If controlled waste is stored on the operating site of the person obliged according to Abs. 1, this must be reported to the responsible authority. The termination of storage must be reported immediately to the responsible authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Cross-border Transport:** The cross-border transport of waste into the country for disposal is prohibited [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 62 Entlassung von Rückständen aus der Überwachung
*   **Reporting:** The person obliged according to § 61 Abs. 1 Satz 1 must immediately report the intended recycling or disposal of the waste to the responsible authority, stating the type, mass, and specific activity, as soon as they have determined its controlled status according to § 61 Abs. 2. This report is unnecessary if a report according to § 63 Abs. 1 is filed due to the type and specific activity of the controlled waste [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Release from Control:** The responsible authority releases controlled waste from monitoring upon application if:
    1.  The required protection of the population from exposures is ensured by the measures taken for the recycling or disposal [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  The body dose of the professionally employed persons during the disposal or recycling cannot exceed the values for classification as a professionally exposed person [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    3.  There are no concerns regarding the legal admissibility of the intended recycling or disposal path and its compliance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Standard:** The standard for population protection is that the effective dose for individual members of the population, caused by the disposal or recycling, does not exceed a reference value of 1 millisievert in the calendar year, even without further measures after the completion of the recycling or disposal [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 63 In der Überwachung verbleibende Rückstände
*   **Reporting:** If a release from monitoring according to § 62 Abs. 2 is not possible, the person obliged according to § 61 Abs. 1 Satz 1 must report the type, mass, and specific activity of the waste remaining under monitoring, as well as a planned disposal or recycling of this waste or its handover for this purpose, within the period specified in Satz 2. The report must be made within one month after the rejection of an application according to § 62 Abs. 2, or immediately after the obliged person has determined the controlled status according to § 61 Abs. 2, if otherwise applicable [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 64 Entfernung von Kontaminationen von Grundstücken
*   **Removal Obligation:** Anyone who terminates industrial or mining processes that generated controlled waste must remove contaminations by controlled waste before the use of the property by third parties, no later than five years after the termination of use, such that the waste does not restrict the use. The standard for property use without restrictions is that the exposure of individual members of the population to the non-removed waste does not exceed the reference value of an effective dose of 1 millisievert in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Notification:** The person obliged according to Abs. 1 Satz 1 must notify the responsible authority of the completion of the contamination removal, along with suitable proof, within three months of the date specified in Satz 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Exemption:** The responsible authority may exempt, in individual cases, from the obligation under Abs. 1 if the intended use of the property or protective measures prevent an exposure of more than 1 millisievert effective dose in the calendar year for individual members of the population even without the removal of the contamination. It may also postpone the performance of the obligation under Abs. 1 if industrial or mining processes are still to be carried out on the property according to § 61 Abs. 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 65 Überwachung sonstiger Materialien
*   **Authority to Order:** If activities according to § 4 Abs. 1 Satz 1 Nummer 10 involve materials originating in the country or abroad that are not waste, or if the execution of industrial or mining processes generating such materials significantly increases the exposure of individual members of the population such that radiation protection measures are necessary, the responsible authority may issue orders. These orders can specifically mandate:
    1.  What and which protective measures must be taken [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  What and how the materials must be further treated or stored at a place designated by the authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    3.  That the person who accepted materials originating abroad and brought them into the country must return them to the original owner in the country of origin [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 66 Mitteilungspflichten zur Betriebsorganisation
If the authorized body of legal persons consists of multiple members or if several authorized persons are present in other associations of persons, it must be reported to the responsible authority who among them performs the obligations under this subsection. The overall responsibility of all body members or authorized members of the association of persons remains unaffected [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 67 Ausnahme von dem Erfordernis der Genehmigung und der Anzeige
Employees or those supervised in the scope of an activity requiring a permit or report under this law are neither required to obtain a permit nor to file a report [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 68 Verordnungsermächtigung; Verwendungs- und Verwertungsverbot
*   **Release Authorization:** The Federal Government is authorized by legal ordinance to determine the conditions, ancillary provisions, and procedure for the release of radioactive materials for the purpose of release from monitoring under this law or a legal ordinance issued based on this law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Restriction:** If the release of radioactive materials according to a legal ordinance issued based on this law requires disposal according to the Circular Economy Act or the legal ordinances issued based on it, these materials may not be reused or recycled according to the mentioned regulations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 69 Strahlenschutzverantwortlicher
*   **Definition:** A Radiation Protection Officer (Strahlenschutzverantwortlicher) is defined as:
    1.  Someone requiring a permit according to § 10, § 12 Abs. 1, § 25 or § 27, a permit according to §§ 4, 6, 7 or 9 of the Atomic Energy Act, the planning approval according to § 9b of the Atomic Energy Act, or the permit according to § 9b Abs. 1a of the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  Someone carrying out an activity according to § 5 of the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    3.  Someone who has filed a report according to §§ 17, 19, 22, 26, 50, 52, 56 or 59 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    4.  Someone who does not require a permit according to § 12 Abs. 4 of § 12 Abs. 1 Number 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Corporate Structure:** If the Radiation Protection Officer is a legal person or a legally capable partnership, the tasks are performed by the person authorized for representation according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation. If the authorized body consists of multiple members or if several authorized persons are present in other associations of persons, the responsible authority must be informed which of these persons performs the tasks. The overall responsibility of all body members or members of the association of persons remains unaffected [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 70 Strahlenschutzbeauftragter
*   **Appointment:** The Radiation Protection Officer must immediately appoint the required number of radiation protection officers for the management or supervision of an activity, if necessary for ensuring radiation protection during the activity. The Radiation Protection Officer remains responsible for compliance with the duties imposed by this law and the legal ordinances issued based on it, even in the event of such an appointment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Duties:** The Radiation Protection Officer must define the duties, the internal operational scope, and the powers required for the performance of the duties of the radiation protection officer in writing. The radiation protection officer is only obliged to perform the duties imposed by this law and the legal ordinances issued based on it within the scope of their powers [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Qualifications:** Only persons may be appointed as radiation protection officers who have no facts suggesting concerns about their reliability and who possess the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Notification:** The Radiation Protection Officer must immediately notify the responsible authority in writing of the appointment of a radiation protection officer, stating the defined duties and powers. The certificate of required expertise in radiation protection must be attached to the notification. A copy of the notification must be forwarded to the radiation protection officer and the works council or personnel council. Paragraphs 1 and 3 apply correspondingly in case of changes in the duties or powers of a radiation protection officer, and in case of the radiation protection officer leaving their function. Paragraph 2 applies in case of a change if there is an expansion of the duties or powers of a radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Authority Review:** The responsible authority can determine that a person cannot be considered a radiation protection officer if the person can only perform their duties as a radiation protection officer insufficiently due to insufficient powers, insufficient expertise in radiation protection, lack of reliability, or for other reasons [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Protection:** The radiation protection officer must not be hindered or disadvantaged in performing their duties. If the radiation protection officer is in an employment relationship with the Radiation Protection Officer obliged to appoint them, termination of the employment relationship is inadmissible, unless facts exist that entitle the Radiation Protection Officer to terminate for good cause without notice. After revocation as radiation protection officer, termination is inadmissible within one year after the end of the appointment, unless the Radiation Protection Officer is entitled to terminate for good cause without notice [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 71 Betriebliche Zusammenarbeit im Strahlenschutz
*   **Information Flow:** The Radiation Protection Officer must immediately inform the radiation protection officer about all administrative acts and measures concerning the duties or powers of the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Deficiency Reporting:** The radiation protection officer must immediately inform the Radiation Protection Officer of all deficiencies that impair radiation protection. If the radiation protection officer and the radiation protection officer cannot agree on a measure proposed by the radiation protection officer to remedy the deficiencies, the Radiation Protection Officer must inform the radiation protection officer of the rejection of the proposal in writing and provide a reason; the Radiation Protection Officer must forward a copy of the notification, including the reason, to the works council or personnel council and the responsible authority. If the notification or forwarding to the responsible authority fails, the radiation protection officer can contact the responsible authority directly [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Collaboration:** The Radiation Protection Officer and the radiation protection officer must cooperate with the works council or personnel council, the occupational safety experts, and the authorized physician according to § 79 Abs. 1 Satz 2 Nummer 9 Buchstabe a when performing their duties and inform them about important matters of radiation protection. The radiation protection officer must advise the works council or personnel council on the request of the works council or personnel council regarding matters of radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 72 Weitere Pflichten des Strahlenschutzverantwortlichen und des Strahlenschutzbeauftragten
*   **Radiation Protection Officer Duties:** The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that, for activities according to § 4 Abs. 1 Satz 1 Number 1 to 7 and 9, while observing the state of science and technology, and for activities according to § 4 Abs. 1 Satz 1 Number 8, 10, and 11, while observing the state of art, suitable protective measures are taken to protect humans and the environment from the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, particularly by providing suitable rooms, equipment, and devices, by suitable regulation of the operating process, and by providing sufficient and suitable personnel, ensuring that:
    1.  In the sense of § 8 Abs. 1, every unnecessary exposure or contamination of human and environment is avoided, and in the sense of § 8 Abs. 2, every exposure or contamination of human and environment is kept as low as possible, even below the limit values, considering all circumstances of the individual case [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  The following regulations are observed:
        a) § 27 Abs. 3, § 77 Satz 1, § 78 Abs. 1 to 4, § 80 Abs. 1 and 2, § 83 Abs. 1, 3 Satz 1 and 4 and Abs. 5, and § 166, as well as according to § 115 Abs. 1 Number 1 and Abs. 2 Number 1 the regulations of §§ 113, 114, and 116;
        b) § 76 Abs. 2, § 85 Abs. 1 to 3, § 90 Abs. 2, the §§ 167 and 168 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    3.  The regulations and protective regulations of a legal ordinance issued based on §§ 24, 37 Abs. 1, of § 68 Abs. 1, §§ 73, 76 Abs. 1, of § 79 Abs. 1, §§ 81, 82, 85 Abs. 4, §§ 86, 87, 89, 90 Abs. 1, of § 170 Abs. 9, § 171, to the extent that the legal ordinance stipulates this, and
    4.  The necessary measures against an unintentional critical state of nuclear fuel are taken [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Radiation Protection Officer Duties:** The radiation protection officer must ensure that:
    1.  Within the tasks and powers assigned to them according to § 70 Abs. 2, the regulations mentioned in Abs. 1 Satz 1 Number 1 and 2 are observed, and the regulations and protective measures mentioned in Abs. 1 Satz 1 Number 3 are observed, unless the Radiation Protection Officer is solely responsible for compliance according to the legal ordinance according to Satz 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  The provisions of the decision on the permit, release, or construction approval and the orders and requirements issued by the responsible authority are observed, to the extent that the execution and fulfillment have been assigned to them according to § 70 Abs. 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 73 Verordnungsermächtigung für den Erlass einer Strahlenschutzanweisung
The Federal Government is authorized by legal ordinance to determine that the Radiation Protection Officer has issued a radiation protection instruction and what content the radiation protection instruction must have [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 74 Erforderliche Fachkunde und Kenntnisse im Strahlenschutz
*   **Acquisition:** The required expertise in radiation protection is generally acquired through training suitable for the respective field of application, practical experience, and successful participation in courses recognized by the responsible authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. Required knowledge is generally acquired through training suitable for the respective field of application and practical experience [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Regulation:** The Federal Government is authorized by legal ordinance to specify in detail the required expertise and knowledge in radiation protection depending on the field of application and the tasks of the person who must possess the required expertise or knowledge [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Scope:** The Federal Government is also authorized by legal ordinance to determine:
    1.  What proof of required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection must be provided [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  How and on what basis the existence of the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection is checked and certified [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    3.  What requirements are to be placed on the recognition of courses for acquiring the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection, on the recognition of vocational training that includes the acquisition of the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection, and on courses for their updating [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    4.  What contents must be taught in courses for acquiring the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection or for their updating [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    5.  Which persons must acquire the required knowledge in radiation protection according to § 74 Abs. 2 Satz 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    6.  At what intervals and in what manner persons must update the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    7.  Under what conditions comparable expertise or knowledge in radiation protection acquired outside the scope of this law, or participation in a course that took place outside the scope of this law, can be recognized [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    8.  Under what conditions the responsible authority can revoke a certificate of required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection, can equip the continued validity of the certificate with conditions, or can order a review of the expertise or knowledge [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    9.  What duties apply to course providers regarding cooperation with the responsible authorities and authorities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 75 Überprüfung der Zuverlässigkeit
For the verification of the reliability of persons against unauthorized actions that could lead to the misappropriation or release of other radioactive materials, § 12b of the Atomic Energy Act and the Atomic Law Reliability Review Ordinance must be applied [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
