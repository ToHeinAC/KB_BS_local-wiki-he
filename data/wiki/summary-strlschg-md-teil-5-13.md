This document details sections § 57 through § 76 of the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG), covering operational requirements, residue management, and professional roles within radiation protection.

## Operational Procedures and Notifications (§ 57 - § 59)

### § 57 Prüfung der angezeigten Tätigkeit
The competent authority reviews the announced activity within four weeks of receiving the documents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   If the authority informs the applicant that all required evidence has been provided before the expiration of this period, the applicant may commence the activity upon receipt of the notice [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   If the authority initiates a procedure for the review of justification (§ 7), it suspends the review procedure for the announcement for the duration of the justification review procedure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The authority may prohibit the activity if:
    1.  An requirement specified in § 56 Paragraph 2 Sentence 1 is not met or no longer met; this applies after the deadline in Paragraph 1 only if remedy is not provided in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  Facts exist suggesting doubts regarding the reliability of the person obligated to announce, their legal representative, or, for legal entities or other associations of persons, the person authorized to represent or manage according to law, statute, or articles of association, or the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    3.  Facts exist suggesting doubts about the availability of necessary personnel for the safe execution of the activity [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    4.  It concerns a non-justified type of activity according to a statutory ordinance under § 6 Paragraph 3, or if there are significant doubts regarding the justification of the activity type considering a report published under § 7 Paragraph 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    5.  There is a significant or repeated violation of the provisions of this law or the statutory ordinances issued based on this law, or against the based orders and decisions of the supervisory authorities, and remedy is not provided in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    6.  This is necessary due to a significant danger to employees, third parties, or the general public [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   If the person obligated to estimate according to § 55 Paragraph 2 fails to comply with the enforceable administrative order, the competent authority may prohibit the activity wholly or partially until the order is fulfilled [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 58 Beendigung der angezeigten Tätigkeit
Anyone who terminates or changes an activity announced according to § 56 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, such that an estimation according to § 55 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 shows that the body dose can no longer exceed the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, must notify the competent authority immediately [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 59 Externe Tätigkeit
*   The obligation to estimate the body dose according to § 55 Paragraph 1 applies analogously to those who carry out the mentioned activities in a foreign facility in their own responsibility or have persons under their supervision carry them out [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   If an estimation for the foreign facility is available, the owner of the facility must immediately transmit a copy of the records on the estimation to the person obligated according to Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   If the estimation according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 or 3 shows that the body dose can exceed one of the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, the person obligated according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 or 3 must announce the activity to the competent authority according to § 56 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The announcement according to Paragraph 2 must include:
    1.  Proof that every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise in radiation protection, or, if a radiation protection officer is not necessary, that the applicant, their legal representative, or, for legal entities or other associations of persons, the person authorized to represent or manage according to law, statute, or articles of association possesses the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    2.  Proof that other persons working during the activity possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding potential radiation hazards and the applicable protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    3.  Proof that the employees are obliged to comply with the orders of the radiation protection officers and radiation protection officers in the facilities where an activity announced according to § 56 Paragraph 1 is carried out, which they issue due to their obligations under this law and the statutory ordinances issued based on this law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    4.  Proof that for the employment in facilities for which an announcement according to § 56 Paragraph 1 is not permitted, the equipment and measures required to comply with the protection regulations are available [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   § 56 Paragraph 3 and §§ 57 and 58 apply analogously to the activity announced according to Paragraph 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Residue Management (§ 60 - § 66)

### § 60 Anfall, Verwertung oder Beseitigung von Rückständen
*   Anyone who carries out or has industrial and mining processes carried out in their facility that will generate more than a total of 2,000 tons of residues annually, which are to be recovered or disposed of, must announce this to the competent authority and the authority competent according to § 47 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 of the Circular Economy Act at the beginning of each calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. This announcement obligation applies analogously to those who recover or accept residues that originated abroad and were brought into the country [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The person obligated to announce according to Paragraph 1 must create a concept on the recovery and disposal of residues (Residue Concept) and submit it to the competent authority upon request. The Residue Concept must contain:
    1.  Information on the type, mass, specific activity, and location of the residues, including estimates of residues generated in the next five years, and
    2.  A presentation of the measures taken and planned for the next five years for disposal or recovery [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The Residue Concept must be updated every five years or at an earlier time upon request of the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The person obligated to announce according to Paragraph 1 must create an annual balance sheet for the preceding year on the type, mass, specific activity, and location of the recovered and disposed residues (Residue Balance Sheet), keep it for five years, and submit it to the competent authority upon request. Additionally, the competent authority may require the submission of corresponding evidence according to § 21 of the Circular Economy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The competent authority may require that the form and content of the Residue Concept and the Residue Balance Sheet meet specific requirements, and check the factual correctness [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 61 Anfall und Lagerung überwachungsbedürftiger Rückstände
*   Anyone who carries out or has industrial and mining processes carried out in their own responsibility, where monitored residues are generated, and whose storage, recovery, or disposal could exceed the reference value of the effective dose of 1 millisievert for individual persons in a calendar year, must take measures to protect the population to ensure that the reference value is not exceeded, and must consult with a person with the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. Paragraph 1 applies analogously to those who recover or accept monitored residues that originated abroad and were brought into the country [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   Residues are monitored if it is not ensured that the monitoring limits and recovery and disposal routes specified by statutory ordinance according to Paragraph 2 are maintained during their disposal or recovery [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. The Federal Government is authorized to specify the monitoring limits and the applicable recovery and disposal routes for residues by statutory ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   Residues may not be mixed or diluted before the intended disposal or recovery to comply with the monitoring limits according to Paragraph 2. Paragraph 1 also applies to residues originating abroad and brought into the country for recovery [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   If monitored residues are stored on the premises of the person obligated according to Paragraph 1, this must be announced to the competent authority. The termination of storage must be notified immediately to the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The competent authority may require that for residues that are not monitored, compliance with the monitoring limits and recovery and disposal routes specified by statutory ordinance according to Paragraph 2 is proven. It may establish technical procedures, suitable measurement methods, and other requirements, especially those for determining representative measurement values of the specific activity [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The person obligated according to Paragraph 1 must secure the residues against loss and unauthorized access before their disposal or recovery. They may only be handed over to other persons for the purpose of disposal or recovery [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   Cross-border transport of residues into the country for disposal is prohibited [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 62 Entlassung von Rückständen aus der Überwachung
*   The person obligated according to § 61 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 must immediately announce the intended recovery or disposal of the residues, stating the type, mass, and specific activity, to the competent authority as soon as they have determined the monitored nature of the residues according to § 61 Paragraph 2. An announcement according to Sentence 1 is unnecessary if an announcement according to § 63 Paragraph 1 is made due to the type and specific activity of the monitored residues [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The competent authority releases monitored residues from monitoring upon application of the person obligated according to § 61 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, if:
    1.  The required protection of the population from exposures is ensured by the measures taken for the recovery or disposal,
    2.  the body dose of the professionally active persons during the disposal or recovery can no exceed the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, and
    3.  there are no concerns regarding the legal admissibility of the intended recovery or disposal route and its compliance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The release from monitoring is done by a written decision [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The benchmark for population protection is that the effective dose of 1 millisievert for individual persons in the calendar year shall not be exceeded, even without further measures, by the exposure caused by the disposal or recovery. If the monitored residues are to be recovered as construction products, the benchmark for the protection of the population is that the requirements of §§ 133 to 135 are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The exposure from residues is determined using the principles of the statutory ordinance according to Paragraph 6 Number 1. A legal disposal or recovery of monitored residues without release from monitoring is not permissible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   Paragraphs 1 to 4 apply analogously to the transport of monitored residues that originated abroad. Anyone intending to transport residues originating abroad into the country for recovery must previously prove to the competent authority that:
    1.  the monitoring limits and recovery routes specified by statutory ordinance according to § 61 Paragraph 2 Sentence 2 are maintained, or
    2.  the prerequisites for release from monitoring for the purpose of specific recovery are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 63 In der Überwachung verbleibende Rückstände
*   If a release from monitoring according to § 62 Paragraph 2 is not possible, the person obligated according to § 61 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 must announce the type, mass, and specific activity of the residues remaining under monitoring, as well as a planned disposal or recovery of these residues or the disposal for this purpose, to the competent authority within the deadline specified in Paragraph 2. The announcement must be made within one month after the rejection of an application according to § 62 Paragraph 2, otherwise immediately after the person obligated has determined the monitored nature according to § 61 Paragraph 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The competent authority may order what protective measures are to be taken and how the residues remaining under monitoring are to be further treated or stored at a location designated by it [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 64 Entfernung von Kontaminationen von Grundstücken
*   Anyone who terminates industrial or mining processes where monitored residues have been generated must remove contamination by monitored residues before the use of the property by third parties, no later than five years after the termination of use, so that the residues do not restrict the use. The benchmark for property use without restrictions is that the exposure of individual persons of the population to the non-removed residues does not exceed the reference value of the effective dose of 1 millisievert in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The person obligated according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 must notify the competent authority of the completion of the contamination removal, along with suitable evidence according to Sentence 2, within three months [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The evidence according to Sentence 1 must be provided using the principles set out in a statutory ordinance according to § 62 Paragraph 6 Number 1. The authority may require proof of the remaining contamination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The competent authority may, in individual cases, exempt from the obligation of Paragraph 1 if the intended use of the property or protective measures prevent an exposure of more than 1 millisievert effective dose in the calendar year for individual persons of the population even without removing the contamination. It may also postpone the performance of the obligation according to Paragraph 1 if industrial or mining processes are to be carried out on the property according to § 61 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 65 Überwachung sonstiger Materialien
*   If materials that originated in the country or abroad and are not residues, or from which residues are not generated by industrial or mining processes, increase the exposure of individual persons of the population so significantly that radiation protection measures are necessary, the competent authority may issue orders. In particular, it may order:
    1.  What protective measures are to be taken,
    2.  What and how the materials are to be further treated or stored at a location designated by it, or
    3.  That the person who received the materials that originated abroad and were brought into the country returns them to the original owner in the country of dispatch [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The Federal Government is authorized to specify by statutory ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council how materials are to be disposed of [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 66 Mitteilungspflichten zur Betriebsorganisation
If the authorized body of legal persons consists of several members or if there are several authorized persons in other associations of persons, it must be reported to the competent authority who among them performs the obligations under this subsection. The overall responsibility of all members of the body or authorized members of the association of persons remains unaffected [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Exemptions and General Provisions (§ 67 - § 76)

### § 67 Ausnahme von dem Erfordernis der Genehmigung und der Anzeige
Anyone employed as an employee or employee or otherwise supervised in the scope of an activity requiring a permit or notification according to this law is neither required to obtain a permit nor to submit a notification [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 68 Verordnungsermächtigung; Verwendungs- und Verwertungsverbot
*   The Federal Government is authorized to specify by statutory ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council:
    1.  Under what conditions and with what ancillary provisions, and in what procedure a release of radioactive substances for the purpose of release from monitoring according to this law or a statutory ordinance issued based on this law takes place,
    2.  who can apply for the release, and
    3.  which obligations must be observed in connection with the release, in particular that a register must be kept and that a notification must be made to the competent authority on what and how these substances are disposed of [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The statutory ordinance may also regulate the procedures and notification obligations for cases where the prerequisites for the release no longer exist [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   If a release of radioactive substances according to a statutory ordinance issued based on this law requires disposal according to the provisions of the Circular Economy Act or the statutory ordinances issued based on it or the Circular Economy and Waste Act applicable until June 1, 2012, these substances may not be reused or recovered according to the mentioned provisions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 69 Strahlenschutzverantwortlicher
*   The Radiation Protection Officer (Strahlenschutzverantwortlicher) is:
    1.  Who requires a permit according to § 10, § 12 Paragraph 1, § 25 or § 27, a permit according to §§ 4, 6, 7 or 9 of the Atomic Energy Act, the planning approval according to § 9b of the Atomic Energy Act, or the permit according to § 9b Paragraph 1a of the Atomic Energy Act,
    2.  Who carries out an activity according to § 5 of the Atomic Energy Act,
    3.  Who has submitted a notification according to §§ 17, 19, 22, 26, 50, 52, 56 or 59, or
    4.  Who does not require a permit according to § 12 Paragraph 4 of § 12 Paragraph 1 Number 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   If the Radiation Protection Officer is a legal entity or a legally capable partnership, the tasks of the Radiation Protection Officer are performed by the person authorized to represent according to law, statute, or articles of association. If the authorized body consists of several members or if there are several authorized persons in other associations of persons, it must be reported to the competent authority which of these persons performs the tasks of the Radiation Protection Officer. The overall responsibility of all members of the body or members of the association of persons remains unaffected [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 70 Strahlenschutzbeauftragter
*   The Radiation Protection Officer (Strahlenschutzverantwortlicher) must immediately appoint the required number of Radiation Protection Officers (Strahlenschutzbeauftragter) in writing for the management or supervision of an activity, if this is necessary for ensuring radiation protection during the activity. The Radiation Protection Officer remains responsible for compliance with the duties imposed on them by this law and the statutory ordinances issued based on it, even in the event of such an appointment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The Radiation Protection Officer must define in writing the duties, the internal decision-making area, and the powers required for the performance of the duties of the Radiation Protection Officer. The Radiation Protection Officer is only obliged to perform the duties imposed on them by this law and the statutory ordinances issued based on it within the scope of their powers [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   Only persons may be appointed as Radiation Protection Officers who have no facts suggesting doubts regarding their reliability and who possess the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The Radiation Protection Officer must immediately notify the competent authority in writing of the appointment of a Radiation Protection Officer, stating the defined duties and powers. The certificate of required expertise in radiation protection must be attached to the notification. A copy of the notification must be forwarded to the Radiation Protection Officer and the works council or personnel council. Paragraphs 1 and 3 apply analogously in the event of a change in the duties or powers of a Radiation Protection Officer, and in the event of the Radiation Protection Officer leaving their function. Paragraph 2 applies in the event of a change analogously, if there is an expansion of the duties or powers of a Radiation Protection Officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 71 Betriebliche Zusammenarbeit im Strahlenschutz
*   The Radiation Protection Officer must immediately inform the Radiation Protection Officer of all administrative acts and measures concerning the duties or powers of the Radiation Protection Officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The Radiation Protection Officer must immediately inform the Radiation Protection Officer of all deficiencies that impair radiation protection. If the Radiation Protection Officer cannot agree with the Radiation Protection Officer on a measure proposed to remedy the deficiencies, the Radiation Protection Officer must communicate the rejection of the proposal in writing and provide a reason; the Radiation Protection Officer must forward a copy of the communication, including the reason, to the works council or personnel council and the competent authority. If the notification or the forwarding to the competent authority fails, the Radiation Protection Officer can address the competent authority directly [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The Radiation Protection Officer and the Radiation Protection Officer must cooperate with the works council or personnel council, the occupational safety experts, and the authorized physician according to § 79 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 Number 9 Letter a, and inform them about important matters of radiation protection. The Radiation Protection Officer must advise the works council or personnel council on the request of the works council or personnel council regarding matters of radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 72 Weitere Pflichten des Strahlenschutzverantwortlichen und des Strahlenschutzbeauftragten
*   **Radiation Protection Officer (RPO):** The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that for activities according to § 4 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1 to 7 and 9, while observing the state of science and technology, and for activities according to § 4 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 8, 10 and 11, while observing the state of technology, for the protection of man and the environment against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation through suitable protective measures, in particular by providing suitable rooms, equipment, and devices, by suitable regulation of the operational process, and by providing sufficient and suitable personnel, that:
    1.  In the sense of § 8 Paragraph 1, every unnecessary exposure or contamination of man and environment is avoided, and in the sense of § 8 Paragraph 2, every exposure or contamination of man and environment is kept as low as possible, even below the limit values, considering all circumstances of the individual case;
    2.  the following provisions are observed:
        a) § 27 Paragraph 3, § 77 Sentence 1, § 78 Paragraph 1 to 4, § 80 Paragraph 1 and 2, § 83 Paragraph 1, 3 Sentence 1 and 4 and Paragraph 5 and § 166, as well as according to § 115 Paragraph 1 Number 1 and Paragraph 2 Number 1 the provisions of §§ 113, 114 and 116;
        b) § 76 Paragraph 2, § 85 Paragraph 1 to 3, § 90 Paragraph 2, the §§ 167 and 168;
    3.  The provisions and protection regulations of the statutory ordinance issued based on §§ 24, 37 Paragraph 1, of § 68 Paragraph 1, §§ 73, 76 Paragraph 1, of § 79 Paragraph 1, §§ 81, 82, 85 Paragraph 4, §§ 86, 87, 89, 90 Paragraph 1, of § 170 Paragraph 9, § 171, insofar as the statutory ordinance determines, and
    4.  The necessary measures against an unintentional criticality of nuclear fuel must be taken.
    For activities according to § 4 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, Sentence 1 applies analogously [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Radiation Protection Officer (RPO):** The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that:
    1.  The provisions mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1 and 2 are observed within the scope of the duties and powers transferred to them according to § 70 Paragraph 2,
    2.  The provisions of the decision on the permit, release, or construction approval and the orders and requirements issued by the competent authority are observed, insofar as their execution and fulfillment have been transferred to them according to § 70 Paragraph 2.
*   The Federal Government is authorized to specify by statutory ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council that the Radiation Protection Officer alone is responsible for ensuring compliance with certain provisions and protection regulations mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 3. The Federal Government is authorized to specify by statutory ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council how the powers of the Radiation Protection Officer required according to § 29 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 3 are to be structured [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   The Radiation Protection Officer and the Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that suitable measures are taken immediately to avert danger to man and environment in case of danger [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 73 Verordnungsermächtigung für den Erlass einer Strahlenschutzanweisung
The Federal Government is authorized to specify by statutory ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council that the Radiation Protection Officer has issued a radiation protection instruction and what content the radiation protection instruction must have [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 74 Erforderliche Fachkunde und Kenntnisse im Strahlenschutz
*   (1) The required expertise in radiation protection is generally acquired through training suitable for the respective field of application, practical experience, and successful participation in courses recognized by the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   (2) The required knowledge in radiation protection is generally acquired through training suitable for the respective field of application and practical experience. Persons specified in a statutory ordinance according to Paragraph 4 Number 5 generally acquire the required knowledge in radiation protection through suitable training, practical experience, and successful participation in courses recognized by the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   (3) The Federal Government is authorized to specify by statutory ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council the details regarding the required expertise and the required knowledge in radiation protection depending on the field of application and the tasks of the person who must possess the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   (4) The Federal Government is also authorized to specify by statutory ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council:
    1.  What evidence of the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection must be provided,
    2.  How and on what basis the existence of the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection is to be checked and certified,
    3.  What requirements are to be set for the recognition of courses for acquiring the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection, for the recognition of vocational training that includes the acquisition of the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection, as well as for courses for their updating,
    4.  What contents must be taught in the courses for acquiring the required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection or for their updating,
    5.  Which persons must acquire the required knowledge in radiation protection according to Paragraph 2 Sentence 2,
    6.  At what intervals and in what manner persons must update their required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection,
    7.  Under what conditions comparable expertise or knowledge in radiation protection acquired outside the scope of this law, or participation in a course that took place outside the scope of this law, can be recognized,
    8.  Under what conditions the competent authority can revoke a certificate of required expertise or knowledge in radiation protection, attach conditions to the continued validity of the certificate, or order a review of the expertise or knowledge, and
    9.  What obligations apply to course providers regarding cooperation with the competent authorities and authorities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

### § 75 Überprüfung der Zuverlässigkeit
For the verification of the reliability of persons against unauthorized actions that could lead to the loss or release of other radioactive substances, § 12b of the Atomic Energy Act and the Atomic Law Reliability Review Ordinance are to be applied [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
