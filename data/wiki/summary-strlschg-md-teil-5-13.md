The source document provides detailed excerpts from the German Radiation Protection Act (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), covering various operational requirements, waste management protocols, and personnel responsibilities related to ionizing radiation protection.

## § 57 Prüfung der angezeigten Tätigkeit (Review of the Announced Activity)
The responsible authority (zuständige Behörde) must review the announced activity within four weeks of receiving the documents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. If the authority informs the applicant, in the case of an estimation according to § 55 Absatz 1 Satz 1, in writing before the expiration of this period that all required evidence has been provided, the applicant may commence the activity immediately upon receipt of the notification [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

If the authority initiates a procedure to examine the justification according to § 7 within the deadline set in Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, it suspends the review procedure for the announcement for the duration of the justification review procedure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The responsible authority may prohibit the activity if:
1. An requirement that must be proven according to § 56 Absatz 2 Satz 1 is not or no longer met; this applies after the deadline in Paragraph 1 only if remedy is not created in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
2. Facts exist that raise concerns regarding the reliability of the person obliged to announce, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal persons or other associations of persons, the person authorized to represent or manage according to law, statute, or articles of association, or the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
3. Facts exist that raise concerns about whether the personnel necessary for the safe execution of the activity is available [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
4. It is an unjustified type of activity according to a legal ordinance according to § 6 Absatz 3, or if there are significant doubts about the justification of the activity type considering a report published according to § 7 Absatz 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
5. There is a significant or repeated violation of the provisions of this Act or the legal ordinances issued based on this Act, or against the based orders and decisions of the supervisory authorities, and no remedy is created in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
6. This is necessary due to a significant danger to employees, third parties, or the general public [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

If the person obliged to estimate according to § 55 Absatz 2 fails to comply with the enforceable administrative order, the responsible authority may prohibit the activity entirely or partially until the order is fulfilled [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 58 Beendigung der angezeigten Tätigkeit (Cessation of the Announced Activity)
Anyone who ceases an activity announced according to § 56 Absatz 1 Satz 1, or changes it in such a way that an estimation according to § 55 Absatz 1 Satz 2 shows that the body dose can no longer exceed the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, must notify the responsible authority immediately [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 59 Externe Tätigkeit (External Activity)
The obligation to estimate the body dose according to § 55 Absatz 1 applies correspondingly to the person who carries out the mentioned activities in a foreign operating site in their own responsibility or has persons under their supervision carry them out [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. If an estimation related to the workplace is available for the foreign operating site, the owner of the operating site must immediately transmit a copy of the records on the estimation to the person obliged according to Sentence 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. § 55 Absatz 2 and 3 apply correspondingly [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

If the estimation according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 or 3 shows that the body dose can exceed one of the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, the person obliged according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 or 3 must announce the activity to the responsible authority according to § 56 Absatz 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The announcement according to Paragraph 2 must include:
1. Proof that every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise in radiation protection, or, if a radiation protection officer is not necessary, that the applicant, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal persons or other associations of persons, the person authorized to represent or manage according to law, statute, or articles of association possesses the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
2. Proof that other persons working during the activity possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding the possible radiation hazard and the applicable protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
3. Proof that the employees are obliged to comply with the instructions of the radiation protection officers and radiation protection advisors in the operating sites where an activity announced according to § 56 Absatz 1 is carried out, which these persons issue due to their duties under this Act and the legal ordinances issued based on this Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
4. Proof that the equipment is available and the measures have been taken for the employment in those operating sites for which an announcement according to § 56 Absatz 1 is not permitted, to ensure that the protection regulations are met according to the state of the art [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

§ 56 Absatz 3 and §§ 57 and 58 apply correspondingly to the activity announced according to Paragraph 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 60 Anfall, Verwertung oder Beseitigung von Rückständen (Occurrence, Recovery, or Disposal of Waste)
Anyone who carries out or has carried out industrial and mining processes in their operating site where more than a total of 2,000 tons of waste are expected to occur annually and are to be recovered or disposed of, must register this with the responsible authority and the authority competent according to § 47 Absatz 1 Satz 1 of the Circular Economy Act at the beginning of each calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. The registration obligation applies correspondingly to those who recover or accept waste that occurred abroad and was brought into the country [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The person obliged to register according to Paragraph 1 must create a concept on the recovery and disposal of the waste (Rückstandskonzept) and submit it to the responsible authority upon request. The waste concept must contain:
1. Information on the type, mass, specific activity, and location of the waste, including estimates of the waste expected in the next five years, and
2. A presentation of the measures taken and planned for disposal or recovery for the next five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The waste concept must be updated every five years or at an earlier time upon request from the responsible authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The person obliged to register according to Paragraph 1 must annually create a balance sheet on the type, mass, specific activity, and location of the recovered and disposed waste for the preceding year (Rückstandsbilanz), keep it for five years, and submit it to the responsible authority upon request. Additionally, the responsible authority may require the submission of corresponding evidence according to § 21 of the Circular Economy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The responsible authority may require that the form and content of the waste concept and the waste balance sheet meet specific requirements, and check the factual correctness [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 61 Anfall und Lagerung überwachungsbedürftiger Rückstände; Verordnungsermächtigung (Occurrence and Storage of Controlled Waste)
Anyone who carries out or has carried out industrial and mining processes in their own responsibility where controlled waste occurs, where the storage, recovery, or disposal thereof could exceed the reference value of the effective dose of 1 millisievert for individual members of the population in the calendar year, must take measures to protect the population to ensure that the reference value is not exceeded, and must consult a person with the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. Sentence 1 applies correspondingly to those who recover or accept controlled waste that occurred abroad and was brought into the country [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

Waste is controlled if it is not ensured that the monitoring limits and recovery and disposal routes specified by legal ordinance according to Sentence 2 are maintained during its disposal or recovery. The Federal Government is authorized to specify the monitoring limits and the applicable recovery and disposal routes for waste by legal ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

Incoming waste must not be mixed or diluted before the intended disposal or recovery in order to comply with the monitoring limits according to Paragraph 2. Sentence 1 also applies to waste that occurred abroad and was brought into the country for recovery [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

If controlled waste is stored on the operating site of the person obliged according to Paragraph 1, this person must register the storage with the responsible authority. The termination of storage must be notified immediately to the responsible authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The responsible authority may require that for waste that is not controlled, compliance with the monitoring limits and recovery and disposal routes specified by legal ordinance according to Paragraph 2 Sentence 2 is proven. It can establish technical procedures, suitable measurement methods, and other requirements, especially those for determining representative measurement values of the specific activity [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The person obliged according to Paragraph 1 must secure the waste against loss and against access by unauthorized persons before its disposal or recovery. It may only be handed over to other persons for the purpose of disposal or recovery [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The cross-border transport of waste into the country for disposal is prohibited [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 62 Entlassung von Rückständen aus der Überwachung; Verordnungsermächtigung (Release of Waste from Monitoring)
The person obliged according to § 61 Absatz 1 Satz 1 must immediately notify the responsible authority of the intended recovery or disposal of the waste, stating the type, mass, and specific activity, as soon as they have determined its controlled status according to § 61 Absatz 2. A notification according to Sentence 1 is unnecessary if an announcement according to § 63 Absatz 1 is made due to the type and specific activity of the controlled waste [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The responsible authority releases controlled waste from monitoring at the request of the person obliged according to § 61 Absatz 1 Satz 1, if:
1. The required protection of the population from exposures is ensured by the measures taken for the recovery or disposal [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
2. The body dose of the professionally active persons during the disposal or recovery cannot exceed the values for classification as a professionally exposed person [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
3. There are no concerns regarding the legal admissibility of the planned recovery or disposal route and its compliance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
The release from monitoring is done by written notice [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The benchmark for the protection of the population is that the effective dose of 1 millisievert for individual members of the population, which is due to the disposal or recovery, does not exceed this value in the calendar year even without further measures after the completion of the recovery or disposal. If the controlled waste is to be recovered as a building product, the benchmark for the protection of the population is that the requirements of §§ 133 to 135 are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

Exposure from waste is determined using the principles of the legal ordinance according to Paragraph 6 Number 1. A legal recovery or disposal of controlled waste without release from monitoring is not permissible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

Paragraphs 1 to 4 apply correspondingly to the transport of controlled waste that occurred abroad. Anyone intending to bring waste that occurred abroad into the country for recovery must previously prove to the responsible authority that:
1. The monitoring limits and recovery routes specified by legal ordinance according to § 61 Absatz 2 Satz 2 are maintained, or
2. The prerequisites for release from monitoring for the purpose of specific recovery are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 63 In der Überwachung verbleibende Rückstände; Verordnungsermächtigung (Waste Remaining Under Monitoring)
If a release from monitoring according to § 62 Absatz 2 is not possible, the person obliged according to § 61 Absatz 1 Satz 1 must notify the responsible authority of the type, mass, and specific activity of the waste remaining under monitoring, as well as a planned disposal or recovery of this waste or its handover for this purpose within the deadline set in Sentence 2. The notification must occur within one month after the rejection of an application according to § 62 Absatz 2, otherwise immediately after the person obliged has determined the controlled status according to § 61 Absatz 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The responsible authority can order what and which protective measures must be taken and how the waste remaining under monitoring must be further treated or stored at a place designated by it [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 64 Entfernung von Kontaminationen von Grundstücken (Removal of Contamination from Sites)
Anyone who ceases industrial or mining processes where controlled waste has occurred must remove contamination by controlled waste before the use of the property by third parties, no later than five years after the cessation of use, such that the waste does not restrict the use. The benchmark for property use without restrictions is that the exposure of individual members of the population to the non-removed waste does not exceed the reference value of an effective dose of 1 millisievert in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The person obliged according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 must notify the responsible authority of the completion of the contamination removal, along with suitable evidence, within three months according to Sentence 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The responsible authority may exempt entirely or partially from the obligation of Paragraph 1 if the intended use of the property or protective measures prevent an exposure of more than 1 millisievert effective dose in the calendar year for individual members of the population even without the removal of the contamination. It may also allow the performance of the obligation of Paragraph 1 at a later date if industrial or mining processes according to § 61 Absatz 1 are still to be carried out on the property [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 65 Überwachung sonstiger Materialien; Verordnungsermächtigung (Monitoring of Other Materials)
If materials that occurred in the country or abroad and are not waste, or from the exercise of industrial or mining processes where such materials occur, can increase the exposure of individual members of the population so significantly that radiation protection measures are necessary, the responsible authority can issue orders [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. Specifically, it can order:
1. What and which protective measures must be taken,
2. What and how the materials are to be further treated or stored at a place designated by it, or
3. That the person who accepted the materials that occurred abroad and were brought into the country returns them to the original owner in the country of origin [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 66 Mitteilungspflichten zur Betriebsorganisation (Notification Obligations for Operational Organization)
If the authorized body of legal persons consists of several members or if there are several authorized persons in other associations of persons, it must be notified to the responsible authority who among them performs the obligations according to this subsection. The overall responsibility of all members of the body or authorized members of the association of persons remains unaffected [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 67 Ausnahme von dem Erfordernis der Genehmigung und der Anzeige (Exemption from the Requirement of Permit and Notification)
A person employed as an employee or employee or otherwise supervised in the scope of an activity requiring a permit or notification according to this Act is neither required to obtain a permit nor to make a notification [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## § 72 Weitere Pflichten des Strahlenschutzverantwortlichen und des Strahlenschutzbeauftragten (Further Duties of the Radiation Protection Officer and the Radiation Protection Advisor)
The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that for activities according to § 4 Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 1 to 7 and 9, while observing the state of science and technology, and for activities according to § 4 Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 8, 10, and 11, while observing the state of art, for the protection of man and the environment from the harmful effects of ionizing radiation through suitable protective measures, in particular by providing suitable rooms, equipment, and devices, by suitable regulation of the operational process, and by providing sufficient and suitable personnel, that:
1. In the sense of § 8 Absatz 1, every unnecessary exposure or contamination of man and environment is avoided, and in the sense of § 8 Absatz 2, every exposure or contamination of man and environment is kept as low as possible, even below the limit values, considering all circumstances of the individual case [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
2. The following regulations are observed:
    a) § 27 Absatz 3, § 77 Satz 1, § 78 Absatz 1 to 4, § 80 Absatz 1 and 2, § 83 Absatz 1, 3 Satz 1 and 4 and Paragraph 5 and § 166, as well as according to § 115 Absatz 1 Nummer 1 and Paragraph 2 Nummer 1 the regulations of §§ 113, 114, and 116, and
    b) § 76 Absatz 2, § 85 Absatz 1 to 3, § 90 Absatz 2, the §§ 167 and 168 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

For activities according to § 4 Absatz 1 Satz 2, Sentence 1 applies correspondingly [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

The Radiation Protection Advisor must ensure that:
1. Within the tasks and powers assigned to them according to § 70 Absatz 2,
    a) the regulations mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1 and 2 are observed,
    b) the regulations and protective measures mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 3 are observed, insofar as the Radiation Protection Officer is solely responsible for compliance according to the legal ordinance according to Sentence 2, and
2. The provisions of the decision on the permit, release, or construction approval and the orders and requirements issued by the responsible authority are observed, insofar as the execution and fulfillment of these have been assigned to them according to § 70 Absatz 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
