# Strahlenschutzverordnung - StrlSchV (Teil 4/15)

This document provides excerpts from the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV), detailing specific regulations concerning occupational exposure monitoring, dose measurement procedures, and the classification of professionally exposed persons.

## § 64 Pflicht zur Ermittlung der Körperdosis; zu überwachende Personen
The responsible radiation protection officer (*Strahlenschutzverantwortliche*) must ensure that the body dose is determined for persons staying in a radiation protection area, according to § 65 Abs. 1 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

*   **Exemption from Body Dose Measurement:** Body dose determination can be waived for persons in a monitoring area if it is not expected that the effective dose in a calendar year exceeds 1 millisievert (mSv), the organ-equivalent dose for the eye lens exceeds 15 mSv, or the local skin dose exceeds 50 mSv. This exemption does not apply if the competent authority requires it. For stays in a control area, the exemption applies if the competent authority consents [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
*   **Non-Area Activities:** Paragraph 1 applies analogously to persons performing activities not connected to a radiation protection area if they can receive an effective dose exceeding 1 mSv, an organ-equivalent dose for the eye lens exceeding 15 mSv, or a local skin dose exceeding 50 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
*   **Flight Personnel:** For flight personnel, Paragraph 1 applies analogously if the effective dose received during the flight from cosmic radiation can exceed 1 mSv in the calendar year. The flight time also includes the positioning time according to § 13 Satz 1 of the Second Implementing Ordinance to the Airworthiness Code [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
*   **Documentation:** The responsible radiation protection officer must ensure that the professional exposure received by every person under their supervision is provided in writing upon request, unless a radiation pass is maintained. For aircraft operations requiring notification, the professional exposure must also be provided in writing once per calendar year and after the last deployment for flight personnel [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 65 Vorgehen bei der Ermittlung der Körperdosis
The responsible radiation protection officer must ensure that the body dose is determined using the person dose according to § 66 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md]. The competent authority may determine that, in addition to or instead of Paragraph 1, the following are measured:
1.  The site dose, the site dose rate, the concentration of radioactive substances in the air, or the contamination of the workplace [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
2.  The body activity or the activity of excretions [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
3.  Other properties of the radiation field or the source of ionizing radiation [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

If a measurement is omitted or faulty, the competent authority must be informed, and the dose must be estimated. The competent authority sets a substitute dose, which must be transmitted to the Radiation Protection Register according to § 170 of the Radiation Protection Act [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 66 Messung der Personendosis
The person dose measurement must be performed using:
1.  A dosimeter available from a site specified according to § 169 of the Radiation Protection Act, or
2.  A dosimeter evaluated under the responsibility of the radiation protection officer, and whose use has been permitted by the competent authority following the consent of a site specified according to § 169 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

The dosimeter must be worn at a site on the body surface considered representative for the exposure, usually on the front of the torso. The dosimeter reading is to be interpreted as a measure of the effective dose if the body dose for individual body parts, organs, or tissues has not been determined more precisely. If it is foreseeable that the organ-equivalent dose for the hands, forearms, feet, or ankles, or the local skin dose, may exceed 150 mSv in a calendar year, or the organ-equivalent dose of the eye lens may exceed 15 mSv, the responsible radiation protection officer must ensure that the person dose is determined by additional dosimeters on individual body parts. The competent authority may order that the person dose be measured by another suitable or by two independent procedures based on the exposure conditions [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 67 Ermittlung der Körperdosis des fliegenden Personals
Differently from § 65, the responsible radiation protection officer must ensure that for the body dose determination of flight personnel during the operation of an aircraft requiring notification, a calculation program or a suitable measuring device recognized by the competent authority is used [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 68 Beschäftigung mit Strahlenpass
A person who is the radiation protection officer based on a permit according to § 25 Abs. 1, or based on a notification according to § 26 Abs. 1 or § 59 Abs. 2 of the Radiation Protection Act, must ensure that persons under their supervision are only employed in foreign radiation protection areas if every single professionally exposed person possesses a completely maintained and registered radiation pass with the competent authority. This does not apply to radiation protection areas where body dose determination can be waived [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 71 Kategorien beruflich exponierter Personen
The responsible radiation protection officer must ensure that professionally exposed persons are assigned to one of the following categories before commencing their activity for control and medical monitoring:
1.  **Category A:** Persons exposed to professional exposure from activities that can lead to an effective dose exceeding 6 mSv, an organ-equivalent dose for the eye lens exceeding 15 mSv, or 150 mSv for the hands, forearms, feet, or ankles, or a local skin dose exceeding 150 mSv in a calendar year [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
2.  **Category B:** Persons not classified in Category A and exposed to professional exposure from activities that can lead to an effective dose exceeding 1 mSv, an organ-equivalent dose for the hands, forearms, feet, or ankles exceeding 50 mSv, or a local skin dose exceeding 50 mSv in a calendar year [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 72 Dosisrichtwerte bei Tätigkeiten
The responsible radiation protection officer must check within six months of commencing an activity whether the determination of dose reference values for professionally exposed persons is a suitable instrument for optimizing radiation protection. If dose reference values are set, they must be set for the effective dose or for an organ-equivalent dose for individual persons and related to a period [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 73 Dosisbegrenzung bei Überschreitung von Grenzwerten
If a limit was exceeded due to a violation of § 78 of the Radiation Protection Act, continued employment as a professionally exposed person is only permissible if the responsible radiation protection officer ensures that the exposures in the following four calendar years are limited, taking into account the exceeded limit, such that the sum of the doses does not exceed five times the respective limit [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 74 Besonders zugelassene Expositionen
Under exceptional, case-by-case assessable circumstances, the competent authority may permit professional exposures deviating from § 78 Abs. 1, 2, and 4 Satz 1 of the Radiation Protection Act. For such particularly permitted exposure, the limits are:
1.  Effective dose limit: 100 mSv [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
2.  Organ-equivalent dose limit for the eye lens: 100 mSv [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
3.  Organ-equivalent dose limit for the hands, forearms, feet, and ankles: 1 Sv each [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
4.  Local skin dose limit: 1 Sv [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 75 Sonstige Schutzvorkehrungen
The responsible radiation protection officer must ensure that the protection of professionally exposed persons against external and internal exposure is primarily ensured by structural and technical facilities or by suitable working procedures [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 77 Ärztliche Überwachung beruflich exponierter Personen
For Category A persons, the responsible radiation protection officer must ensure that tasks are only performed if the person has been examined by a physician authorized according to § 175 Abs. 1 Satz 1 within one year before the initial performance of the tasks, and a certificate is available confirming no health concerns [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].

## § 85 Buchführung und Mitteilung
The responsible radiation protection officer must ensure that:
1.  The competent authority is informed within one month of the acquisition, generation, purchase, release, and other disposition of radioactive substances, stating the type and activity of the substances [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
2.  A record is kept of the acquisition, generation, purchase, release, and other disposition of radioactive substances, stating the type and activity of the substances [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
3.  The inventory of radioactive substances with half-lives greater than 100 days must be reported to the competent authority by January 31 of the following year at the end of a calendar year [StrlSchV.md [Teil 4/15].md].
