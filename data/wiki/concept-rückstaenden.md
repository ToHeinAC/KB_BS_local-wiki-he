# Residues (Rückstände)

In the context of radiation protection, residues (*Rückstände*) refer to materials generated during industrial or mining processes that contain radioactive substances and require specific management protocols regarding their disposal and utilization [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Management Requirements
*   **Reporting:** Entities generating over 2,000 tonnes of residues annually must report this to the competent authority and the authority under the Circular Economy Act (§ 47 Abs. 1 Satz 1 KrWG) at the start of each calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Residue Concept:** A comprehensive concept (*Rückstandskonzept*) must be created and submitted, detailing the type, mass, specific activity, and whereabouts of the residues, along with planned disposal or utilization measures for the next five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md]. This concept must be updated every five years or upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Residue Balance:** An annual balance sheet (*Rückstandsbilanz*) detailing the utilized and disposed residues must be kept for five years and submitted upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Controlled Residues (Überwachungsbedürftige Rückstände)
*   **Definition:** Residues are considered controlled if it cannot be ensured that established monitoring limits and utilization/disposal paths are maintained upon disposal or utilization [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Dose Limit:** If residues are expected to exceed the reference value of 1 millisievert (mSv) for the effective dose of individuals in a calendar year, protective measures must be taken, and consultation with a qualified person is required [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Disposal Prohibition:** Cross-border transport of controlled residues into Germany for disposal is prohibited [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Release and Disposal
*   **Release from Monitoring:** Residues can be released from monitoring if:
    1.  Protection of the population from exposure is ensured by the measures taken for utilization or disposal.
    2.  The body dose of professionally active persons cannot exceed the values for classification as professionally exposed persons during disposal or utilization.
    3.  There are no concerns regarding the legal admissibility of the planned utilization or disposal path and its compliance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
*   **Site Remediation:** When processes cease, contamination must be removed before the property is used by third parties, no later than five years after the end of use. The standard for unrestricted use is that the exposure of individuals from the remaining residues does not exceed the reference value of 1 mSv in a calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
