# Radioaktive kontaminierte Gebiete

This concept refers to areas contaminated by radioactive substances, encompassing both historical contamination (Altlasten) and contamination arising from emergencies or existing sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Types of Contamination
The law distinguishes between several types of contaminated areas:
1. **Radioactive Contaminated Sites (Altlasten):** Sites contaminated by completed human activities, where the effective dose exceeds 1 millisievert in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
2. **Emergency Situations:** Sites contaminated due to an emergency. In this case, the provisions for contaminated sites apply, using reference values set out in § 93 Abs. 1 or § 93 Abs. 2 or 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
3. **Existing Situations:** Sites contaminated due to an existing situation. The provisions apply, using reference values set out in § 118 Abs. 4 or 6 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Management Principles
Management requires establishing responsibility, creating remediation plans, and ensuring worker protection during cleanup efforts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
