This summary details sections of the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) concerning dose limits, application requirements, record-keeping, and emergency preparedness, covering §§ 77 through § 88.

## Dose Limits and Exposure Categories

### Occupational Exposure Limits (§ 78)
For professionally exposed persons, the law sets specific dose limits:
*   **Effective Dose:** The limit is 20 millisievert (mSv) in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Exceptional Limits:** The competent authority may permit an effective dose of 50 mSv in a single year, provided that a total of 100 mSv is not exceeded over five consecutive years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Organ-Equivalent Dose:**
    *   Eye Lens: 20 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    *   Skin (local dose, averaged over any 1 cm²): 500 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    *   Hands, Forearms, Feet, and Ankles: Each has a limit of 500 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Minors:** For professionally exposed persons under 18 years, the effective dose limit is 1 mSv in the calendar year. The eye lens limit is 15 mSv, and the local skin dose limit is 50 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

### Population Exposure Limits (§ 80)
For individual persons of the general population, the limits are:
*   **Effective Dose:** The limit for the sum of effective doses is 1 millisievert (mSv) in the calendar year from activities listed in § 80 (1) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Organ-Equivalent Dose:**
    *   Eye Lens: 15 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    *   Local Skin Dose: 50 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

### Environmental and Planning Limits (§ 81)
The law permits the federal government to regulate planning requirements for protecting the population and environment, including setting dose limits for derivations with air or water [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Application of Radiation to Humans (§ 83)
Ionisierende Strahlung and radioaktive Stoffe may only be applied to humans:
1.  In the context of a medical exposure, or
2.  In the context of population exposure for examination in cases provided by law, general occupational safety, or immigration regulations of other states (non-medical application) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

The application must achieve a sufficient benefit. The assessment requires balancing the overall potential benefit (diagnostic or therapeutic) against the potential damage caused by the exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md]. The procedure requires a *rechtfertigende Indikation* (justifying indication) established by a physician or dentist with the required expertise [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Record Keeping and Data Management (§ 85)
The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that records are kept immediately following the application of ionizing radiation or radioactive substances to humans. These records must include:
*   Details of the justifying indication and the date of indication [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   The date and type of application [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   Details of the exposure of the person or the method of determining this exposure, and of accompanying persons if their body dose must be determined [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   The findings of the examination, the radiation plan, and the treatment protocol [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

Retention periods for records and images are:
*   Treatments: 30 years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   Examinations (Adult): 10 years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   Examinations (Minor): Until the completion of the 28th year of life [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Emergency and Incident Management (§ 82)
The federal government is authorized to regulate the duties of the Radiation Protection Officer regarding preparation for accidents and emergencies. These duties include:
*   Ensuring necessary personnel and equipment are available to contain and eliminate dangers arising from accidents or emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   Informing the population about protective measures and recommended behavior during possible emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   Taking immediate measures to avert or limit dangers to humans and the environment during emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## High-Radioactive Source Register (§ 88)
A register is maintained by the Federal Office for Radiation Protection for data on high-radioactive sources. This register records details such as the license holder, identification number, properties, control, and location of handling or storage [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md]. Data stored in the register must be kept for 30 years after the last update [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
