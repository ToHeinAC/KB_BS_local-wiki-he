# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 6/13)

This section provides detailed provisions regarding dose limits, record-keeping, and specific requirements for applying ionizing radiation or radioactive substances to humans and the environment.

## Dose Limits and Professional Exposure (§ 77, § 78, § 80)

* **Professional Lifetime Dose Limit (§ 77):** The limit for the sum of effective doses in all calendar years for professionally exposed persons is 400 millisieverts (mSv). The competent authority may permit additional professional exposure if it does not exceed 10 mSv of effective dose in a calendar year and the professionally exposed person consents in writing [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
* **Annual Professional Dose Limits (§ 78):**
    * The limit for the effective dose for professionally exposed persons is 20 mSv in a calendar year. The competent authority may permit an effective dose of 50 mSv in an individual case for a single year, provided that 100 mSv is not exceeded in five consecutive years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    * Organ-equivalent dose limits for professionally exposed persons include:
        * Lens of the eye: 20 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
        * Skin (averaged over any 1 cm² area): 500 mSv in the calendar year (local skin dose) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
        * Hands, forearms, feet, and ankles: 500 mSv in the calendar year each [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    * For professionally exposed persons under 18 years:
        * Effective dose limit: 1 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
        * Organ-equivalent dose limits: Lens of the eye (15 mSv), local skin dose (50 mSv), hands/forearms/feet/ankles (50 mSv each) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    * Exceptions for trainees and students aged 16 to 18 years allow for an effective dose limit of 6 mSv in the calendar year and an organ-equivalent dose limit of 150 mSv in the calendar year for the skin, hands, forearms, feet, and ankles, if necessary for achieving the educational goal [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    * For capable women, the organ-equivalent dose limit for the uterus is 2 mSv per month. For an unborn child exposed due to the mother's employment, the effective dose limit is 1 mSv from the date of notification of pregnancy until the end [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
* **Population Dose Limits (§ 80):**
    * For individuals in the population, the limit for the sum of effective doses is 1 mSv in the calendar year from exposures resulting from:
        1. Permitting or notification-required activities under this Act or the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
        2. State storage of nuclear fuel according to § 5 Abs. 3 Sentence 1 of the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
        3. Plan-approval-required establishment, operation, or decommissioning of facilities mentioned in § 9a Abs. 3 of the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
        4. Searching, mining, or processing of radioactive mineral deposits subject to the operational planning obligation under § 51 of the Federal Mining Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    * The limit for the sum of organ-equivalent doses for individuals in the population is:
        * Lens of the eye: 15 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
        * Local skin dose: 50 mSv in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Medical Application and Record Keeping (§ 83, § 85, § 86)

* **Application to Humans (§ 83):** Ionizing radiation and radioactive substances may only be applied to humans:
    1. In the context of a medical exposure, or
    2. In the context of population exposure for examination in cases provided for by law or approved cases, or according to general occupational safety or immigration regulations of other states (non-medical application) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
* **Justification:** The application must provide sufficient benefit. When assessing whether the application provides sufficient benefit, the total potential for diagnostic or therapeutic benefit, including immediate health benefit for the individual and benefit for society, must be weighed against the potential damage caused by the exposure to the individual [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
* **Record Keeping (§ 85):** The Radiation Protection Officer (*Strahlenschutzverantwortliche*) must ensure that records are immediately created regarding the application of ionizing radiation or radioactive substances to humans. These records must contain:
    1. Details of the justifying indication and the date of indication [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    2. The date and type of the application [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    3. Details of the exposure of the examined or treated person, or the determination of this exposure, and of accompanying persons, if their body dose must be determined according to § 86 Sentence 2 Number 3 of the Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    4. The findings of an examination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    5. The irradiation plan and the treatment protocol [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
* **Retention Periods (§ 85):** Records and images must be kept:
    * For treatments: 30 years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    * For examinations:
        * Adult person: ten years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
        * Minor person: until the completion of their 28th year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Radiation Protection Officer Duties (§ 82, § 86)

* **Emergency Response (§ 82):** The government is authorized to regulate the duties of the Radiation Protection Officer (*Strahlenschutzverantwortliche*) regarding preparation for accidents, potential emergencies, and emergencies. This includes ensuring that necessary personnel and equipment are available to contain and eliminate dangers arising from accidents or emergencies related to the officer's activities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
* **General Protection Measures (§ 86):** The government is authorized to regulate measures for protecting persons exposed to ionizing radiation or radioactive substances, including:
    * How every single exposure must be justified [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    * How medical and non-medical exposures must be limited [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    * The determination and assessment of medical and non-medical exposures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
    * Measures to be taken before, during, and after the application to ensure the quality required for radiation protection, considering medical science requirements [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## High-Activity Sources Register (§ 88)

* **Register Purpose:** Data on high-activity radiation sources are collected in a register maintained by the Federal Office for Radiation Protection for the purpose of security and control of radiation sources to protect life and health [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
* **Data Recorded:** The register includes information such as the owner of the permit, issuance date and duration of the permit, identification number of the high-activity radiation source, properties, controls, and use of the source, location of handling or storage, acquisition or relinquishment of ownership, and loss, theft, or finding of the source [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
* **Retention:** Data stored in the register must be kept for 30 years after the last update of the information about the high-activity radiation source [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
