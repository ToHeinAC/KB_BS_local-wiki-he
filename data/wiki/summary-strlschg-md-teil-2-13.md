This summary details definitions and regulatory requirements extracted from Part 2 of the Radiation Protection Act (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), effective as of 27.06.2017.

## Definitions (Sections 13-40)

**Key Definitions:**
*   **(13) Einsatzkraft:** A person who performs a designated task during an emergency or other dangerous situation and who may be exposed during their deployment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(14) Einzelperson der Bevölkerung:** A person who is not exposed to professional or medical exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(15) Freigrenzen:** Values of activity and specific activity of radioactive substances, stipulated in a legal ordinance according to § 24 Satz 1 Nummer 10, which serve as a standard for monitoring necessity for activities related to these radioactive substances under this law and its derived legal ordinances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(16) Früherkennung:** The application of X-rays or radioactive substances within the scope of a medical exposure for the examination of persons who show no symptoms of disease and have no concrete suspicion of illness (asymptomatic persons), in order to determine a specific disease [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(17) Innenräume:** Enclosed, fixed rooms within and outside buildings where people can stay, including caves and mines [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(18) Kerntechnische Anlage:** A nuclear technical facility according to § 2 Absatz 3a Nummer 1 of the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(19) Körperdosis:** A general term for effective dose and organ-equivalent dose [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(20) Konsumgüter:** Goods intended for the end consumer in the sense of the Food and Feed Code, as well as goods and items of daily use for use in the household and professional area. Construction products and construction-approved devices are not consumer goods if they contain other radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(21) Kontamination:** Contamination with substances containing one or more radionuclides [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(22) Materialien:** Substances containing naturally occurring radionuclides or contaminated with such substances. Materials are not: 1. substances containing natural and artificial radionuclides that are the subject of activities according to § 4 Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 1 bis 9 and 11, or were; 2. substances containing natural and artificial radionuclides originating from emergencies; or 3. substances present in the environment and contaminated due to nuclear weapons tests [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(23) Medizinische Forschung:** Further development of medical examination methods, treatment procedures, or medical science. Medical research does not exist if the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation serves exclusively the examination or treatment of the individual person [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(24) Medizinphysik-Experte:** A person with a Master's degree in medical physics or a person trained in medical physics equivalent to a university degree, who possesses the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(25) Nachsorgemaßnahmen:** Monitoring, maintenance, and restoration of the effectiveness of remediation measures or other measures to prevent or reduce exposure in existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(26) Notfall:** An event that can result in significant adverse effects on humans, the environment, or property due to ionizing radiation. No emergency exists if it can be foreseen that an event that occurred within the scope of a planned activity can be managed by the measures regulated for planned exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    *   **Überregionaler Notfall:** An emergency in the federal territory whose adverse effects are not expected to be limited to the state in which it occurs, or an emergency outside the federal territory that is expected to have adverse effects within the scope of this law that are not merely local.
    *   **Regionaler Notfall:** An emergency in the federal territory whose adverse effects are expected to be essentially limited to the state in which it occurs.
    *   **Lokaler Notfall:** An emergency that is expected to have essentially only local adverse effects within the scope of this law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(27) Organ-Äquivalentdosis:** The result of multiplying the energy deposited by ionizing radiation in an organ or tissue by the mass of the organ or tissue, by a weighting factor stipulated by legal ordinance according to § 175 Absatz 2 Nummer 1 to account for the effect for the type or energy of radiation compared to photon and electron radiation. If multiple types or energies of radiation are present, the contributions are added [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(28) Radon:** The radionuclide Rn-222 and its decay products [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(29) Referenzwert:** A fixed value in existing exposure situations or emergency exposure situations that serves as a standard for checking the adequacy of measures. A reference value is not a limit value [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(30) Röntgeneinrichtung:** A device or apparatus in which X-rays can be generated by accelerated electrons with a threshold energy of at least 5 kiloelectronvolts, with the acceleration of the electrons limited to an energy of 1 megaelectronvolt, and which is operated for the purpose of generating X-rays. An X-ray device also includes application devices, auxiliary devices, required software, and devices for medical diagnosis [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(31) Röntgenstrahler:** A component of an X-ray device, consisting of an X-ray tube and a tube protective housing, and which also includes high voltage generation in the case of a power supply unit [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(32) Rückstände:** Materials that accumulate in the industrial and mining processes mentioned in Annex 1 and that meet the specified requirements [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(33) Sanierungsmaßnahmen:** Measures that 1. serve to eliminate or reduce contamination, or 2. permanently prevent or reduce the spread of radionuclides or the ionizing radiation emanating from them [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(34) Offene radioaktive Stoffe:** All radioactive substances with the exception of contained radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(35) Umschlossene radioaktive Stoffe:** Radioactive substances that are constantly enclosed by an all-sided, dense, inert casing or are constantly embedded in solid inert substances, such that the escape of radioactive substances is safely prevented under normal operational stress; the dimension of the contained radioactive substance must be at least 0.2 centimeters. Contained radioactive substances are not radioactive substances used due to their radioactivity and whose casing can be opened without destroying it [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(36) Hochradioaktive Strahlenquellen:** Contained radioactive substances whose activity corresponds to or exceeds the values stipulated in a legal ordinance according to § 24 Satz 1 Nummer 11. High radioactive sources are not fuel elements and solidified high radioactive fission product solutions from the processing of nuclear fuel, nor constantly dense and solid transport or storage containers with radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(37) Störstrahler:** A device or apparatus in which X-rays can be generated by accelerated electrons with a threshold energy of at least 5 kiloelectronvolts, and where the acceleration of the electrons is limited to an energy of 1 megaelectronvolt, without the device or apparatus being operated for the purpose of generating X-rays. An electron microscope is also considered a spurious radiation source, where the generated X-rays are evaluated by detectors [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(38) Teleradiologie:** Examination of a person with X-rays under the responsibility of a doctor who possesses the required expertise in radiation protection and who is not located at the site of the technical execution (teleradiologist) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(39) Umgang:** 1. The extraction, generation, storage, processing, handling, other use, and disposal of a) artificially generated radioactive substances and b) naturally occurring radioactive substances due to their radioactivity, for use as nuclear fuel or for the generation of nuclear fuel; 2. The operation of irradiation devices; and 3. The searching, extraction, and processing of radioactive mineral deposits in the sense of the Mining Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **(40) Zusatz radioaktiver Stoffe:** Purposeful addition of radionuclides to substances to generate special properties, if 1. the addition of artificially generated radionuclides leads to their specific activity in the product exceeding 500 microbecquerels per gram, or 2. the addition of naturally occurring radionuclides leads to their specific activity in the product exceeding one-fifth of the free limits stipulated in a legal ordinance according to § 24 Satz 1 Nummer 10. It is irrelevant whether the addition is based on radioactivity or on other properties [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].

## Regulatory Principles and Procedures

**General Principles:**
*   **§ 6 Rechtfertigung von Tätigkeitsarten:** New activities that may involve exposures to humans and the environment must be justified by balancing their economic, social, or other benefit against the potential health impairment they may cause. The professional exposure, the exposure of the population, and medical exposure must be considered when justifying. The justification of existing activities can be reviewed as soon as substantial new findings about the benefit or effects of the activity or substantial new information about other procedures and techniques become available [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **§ 8 Vermeidung unnötiger Exposition und Dosisreduzierung:** Any person who plans, carries out, or has carry out an activity is obliged to avoid any unnecessary exposure or contamination of humans and the environment. Furthermore, they are obliged to keep any exposure or contamination of humans and the environment as low as possible, even below the limit values. This must be done by considering all circumstances of the individual case: 1. for activities according to § 4 Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 1 bis 7 and 9, the state of science and technology must be observed; 2. for activities according to § 4 Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 8, 10, and 11, the state of the art must be observed [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **§ 9 Dosisbegrenzung:** Any person who plans, carries out, or has carry out an activity is obliged to ensure that the dose limits stipulated in this law and the legal ordinances issued on the basis of this law are not exceeded [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].

**Authorization and Reporting:**
*   **§ 10 Genehmigungsbedürftige Errichtung von Anlagen:** A permit is required for the construction of facilities for generating ionizing radiation, including: 1. Accelerator or plasma facilities capable of generating more than $10^{12}$ neutrons per second; 2. Electron accelerators with an electron end energy of more than 10 Megaelectronvolts, if the average beam power can exceed 1 kilowatt; 3. Electron accelerators with an electron end energy of more than 150 Megaelectronvolts; 4. Ion accelerators with an ion end energy of more than 10 Megaelectronvolts per nucleon, if the average beam power can exceed 50 watts; or 5. Ion accelerators with an ion end energy of more than 150 Megaelectronvolts per nucleon [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **§ 12 Genehmigungsbedürftige Tätigkeiten:** A permit is required for: 1. Operating a facility for generating ionizing radiation (with exceptions); 2. Using ionizing radiation from an irradiation device that is part of a facility approved according to § 7 Absatz 1 Satz 1 des Atomgesetzes, in connection with application to humans or application in veterinary medicine; 3. Handling other radioactive substances (with exceptions); 4. Operating an X-ray device (with exceptions); or 5. Operating a spurious radiation source (with exceptions) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
*   **§ 14 Besondere Voraussetzungen bei Tätigkeiten im Zusammenhang mit der Anwendung am Menschen:** A permit for an activity under § 12 Absatz 1 Nummer 1, 2, 3, or 4 in connection with the application of ionizing radiation or radioactive substances to humans is only granted if, in addition to the respective requirements of § 13, the applicant or the appointed radiation protection officer is approved as a doctor or dentist, or is temporarily permitted to exercise the medical or dental profession. Furthermore, the involvement of a Medical Physics Expert is required depending on the type of application (e.g., individual radiation plan vs. standardized treatment) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
