# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 2/13)

This section of the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) details specific definitions, the legal requirements for planned exposure situations, and the detailed permitting and notification procedures for facilities and activities involving ionizing radiation and radioactive substances.

## Definitions (Pages 14-20)

The text provides several key definitions:
* **Einsatzkraft (Personnel performing tasks in emergencies):** A person who performs a specified task during an emergency or other dangerous situation and who may be exposed to radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Einzelperson der Bevölkerung (Individual person of the population):** A person who is not exposed to professional or medical exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Freigrenzen (Release limits):** Values of activity and specific activity of radioactive substances set in a legal ordinance pursuant to § 24 Satz 1 Nummer 10, serving as a benchmark for monitoring requirements for activities related to these substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Kontamination (Contamination):** Pollution with substances containing one or more radionuclides [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Materialien (Materials):** Substances containing naturally occurring radionuclides or contaminated with such substances. Exclusions apply to substances containing both natural and artificial radionuclides that are the subject of activities under § 4 Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 1 bis 9 und 11, or those originating from emergencies, or those contaminated in the environment due to nuclear weapon tests [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Notfall (Emergency):** An event that can result in significant adverse effects on humans, the environment, or property due to ionizing radiation. No emergency exists if an event expected from a planned activity can be managed by measures regulated for planned exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    * **Types of Emergencies:**
        * *Überregionaler Notfall:* An emergency in the federal territory whose adverse effects are expected not to be limited to the state where it occurs, or an emergency outside the federal territory expected to have adverse effects within the scope of this law that are not merely local.
        * *Regionaler Notfall:* An emergency in the federal territory whose adverse effects are expected to be essentially limited to the state where it occurs.
        * *Lokaler Notfall:* An emergency expected to have essentially only local adverse effects within the scope of this law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Umschlossene radioaktive Stoffe (Encapsulated radioactive substances):** Radioactive substances that are constantly enclosed by an all-sided, dense, inactive casing, or are constantly embedded in solid inactive substances such that the escape of radioactive substances is safely prevented under normal operational stress; the dimension of the encapsulated radioactive substance must be at least 0.2 centimeters. Exclusions apply to substances used for their radioactivity and whose casing can be opened without destroying it [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].

## Planned Exposure Situations (Pages 16-17)

### Principles of Radiation Protection (Strahlenschutzgrundsätze)
* **Justification (§ 6):** New activities that may involve exposure to humans and the environment must be justified by weighing their economic, societal, or other benefit against the potential health impairment. Professional, population, and medical exposures must be considered during justification [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Review of Justification (§ 6):** The justification of existing activities can be reviewed when significant new findings about the benefit or effects of the activity, or significant new information about other procedures and techniques, become available [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Avoidance and Dose Reduction (§ 8):** Any unnecessary exposure or contamination of humans and the environment must be avoided. Furthermore, any exposure or contamination must be kept as low as possible, even below limit values, considering all circumstances of the individual case [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Dose Limitation (§ 9):** Any activity must ensure that the dose limits set in this law and the resulting legal ordinances are not exceeded [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].

## Ionizing Radiation and Facilities (Pages 17-23)

### Facility Establishment and Operation
* **Permit Required Facilities (§ 10):** A permit is required for establishing facilities for generating ionizing radiation, including:
    1. Accelerator or plasma facilities capable of generating more than $10^{12}$ neutrons per second [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    2. Electron accelerators with an electron end energy of more than 10 Megaelectronvolts, if the average beam power can exceed 1 kilowatt [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    3. Electron accelerators with an electron end energy of more than 150 Megaelectronvolts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    4. Ion accelerators with an ion end energy of more than 10 Megaelectronvolts per nucleon, if the average beam power can exceed 50 watts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    5. Ion accelerators with an ion end energy of more than 150 Megaelectronvolts per nucleon [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **Permit Requirements (§ 11):** A permit can only be issued if:
    1. There are no facts suggesting doubts about the reliability of the applicant, their legal representative, or authorized persons [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    2. It is guaranteed that a radiation protection officer (*Strahlenschutzbeauftragter*) with the required expertise is appointed and can establish or allow the establishment of the facility [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    3. It is guaranteed that the exposure of persons due to the operation does not exceed the limits permitted for individuals in generally accessible areas outside the operational site [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    4. Environmental protection and measures against disturbances are maintainable [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    5. The required protection against disturbances or third-party influences is guaranteed [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    6. The activity is not unjustified, or there are no significant doubts about its justification based on a report published under § 7 Absatz 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].

### Operational Activities
* **Permit Required Activities (§ 12):** A permit is required for:
    1. Operating a facility for generating ionizing radiation (with exceptions) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    2. Using ionizing radiation from a beam device that is part of a facility approved under the Atomic Energy Act for the splitting of nuclear fuel, in connection with human or animal application [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    3. Handling other radioactive substances (with exceptions) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    4. Operating an X-ray facility (with exceptions) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    5. Operating a spurious radiation source (*Störstrahler*) (with exceptions) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].

### Specific Applications
* **Medical Applications (§ 14):** Permits for activities involving the application of ionizing radiation or radioactive substances to humans require, in addition to § 13 requirements:
    1. The applicant or the appointed radiation protection officer must be approved as a doctor or dentist, or temporarily allowed to practice [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
    2. Guarantee of medical physics expertise involvement (e.g., for individualized dose plans or standardized treatments) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
* **X-ray Facilities (§ 19):** Operating an X-ray facility requires notification or a permit depending on the type of operation (e.g., technical radiography, treatment, teleradiology) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 2/13].md].
