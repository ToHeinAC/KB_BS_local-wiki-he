Tätigkeiten (Activities) are defined under the Strahlenschutzgesetz (StrlSchG) and represent a comprehensive list of actions that involve radioactive substances or the generation of ionizing radiation, thereby necessitating regulatory oversight [summary-strlschg-01-md].

## Scope of Regulated Activities
The law enumerates numerous activities that fall under its scope, including:
*   **Handling:** The acquisition, generation, storage, processing, other use, and disposal of both artificially generated and naturally occurring radioactive substances, especially when used as nuclear fuel or for generating nuclear fuel [summary-strlschg-01-md].
*   **Facility Operation:** Establishing, operating, or decommissioning facilities for ensuring and final disposal of radioactive waste [summary-strlschg-01-md].
*   **Radiation Generation:** Establishing and operating facilities for generating ionizing radiation, as well as operating and testing X-ray or scatter radiation equipment [summary-strlschg-01-md].
*   **Contamination/Exposure Increase:** Actions that can increase exposure or contamination from naturally occurring radioactivity, provided these actions do not already fall under other listed activities [summary-strlschg-01-md].

## Principles Governing Activities
The execution of any activity is subject to strict regulatory principles:

*   **Justification:** Any new activity type must be justified by balancing its economic, societal, or other benefits against the potential health impairment it may cause. This assessment must consider professional, population, and medical exposures [summary-strlschg-02-md].
*   **Optimization:** Operators are obligated to avoid unnecessary exposure or contamination of humans and the environment. Furthermore, exposure or contamination must be kept as low as possible, even below regulatory limits, by considering the state of science and technology [summary-strlschg-02-md].
*   **Dose Limitation:** It is mandatory to ensure that established dose limits, set by this law and subsequent regulations, are not exceeded [summary-strlschg-02-md].

## Types of Activities
The concept distinguishes between:
*   **Activity (Tätigkeit):** The specific actions listed in the law (e.g., handling, operating facilities) [summary-strlschg-01-md].
*   **Activity Type (Tätigkeitsart):** This refers to the totality of activities that are substantially similar in terms of their assessment under the principle of justification [summary-strlschg-01-md].

## Exclusions
The law explicitly excludes certain activities, such as agricultural, forestry, and civil engineering processing of the Earth's surface, unless these actions are specifically aimed at removing contamination [summary-strlschg-01-md].
