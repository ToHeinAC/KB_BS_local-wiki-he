The Radiation Protection Law (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG) is the comprehensive legal framework governing the protection of humans and the environment from the harmful effects of ionizing radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md]. The *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) serves as a detailed ordinance implementing many technical and procedural aspects of this law [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].

## Scope and Purpose
The law establishes detailed regulations for activities involving radioactive materials and ionizing radiation, covering areas such as:
*   Permitting and authorization for facilities and processes [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   Managing radioactive residues and waste [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   Establishing dose limits and monitoring requirements for exposed persons and the general population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   Defining procedures for emergency preparedness and response in the event of radiological incidents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Key Regulatory Areas
The law mandates strict controls across multiple domains, including:
*   **Medical Applications:** Regulations govern the use of radioactive materials or ionizing radiation on humans for medical research, requiring specific permits and notifications [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Industrial Use:** It regulates the type approval and operation of facilities generating ionizing radiation, such as X-ray sources and scatter sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Transport:** Specific rules apply to the transport of radioactive materials, requiring proof of specialized knowledge for continued authorization [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Detailed Technical Criteria (Implemented by StrlSchV)
The *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) specifies detailed technical criteria for assessing risk and determining significance:
*   **Significance Criteria (Planned Exposure):** Criteria for determining the significance of an event in a planned exposure situation include exceeding dose limits for professionals or the general population, or exceeding permissible derivation limits for radioactive substances in air or water [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
*   **Risk Assessment:** Risk assessment for activities must systematically consider criteria such as the expected exposure level, the activity level of handling radioactive substances, and the risk of unintentional exposures [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
*   **Dose Measurement:** The *Verordnung* specifies detailed measurement quantities for external radiation, including the Depth-person dose $H_p(10)$, Eye lens-person dose $H_p(3)$, and Surface-person dose $H_p(0.07)$ for persons, and corresponding ambient dose rates for sites [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
*   **Dose Calculation:** The calculation of the effective dose ($E$) involves weighting organ equivalent doses using radiation weighting factors ($w_T$) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].

## Transitional Provisions
The law contains extensive transitional provisions, which detail how permits and authorizations issued under previous legal versions remain valid or transition to the new framework, often referencing specific cut-off dates (e.g., December 31, 2018) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
