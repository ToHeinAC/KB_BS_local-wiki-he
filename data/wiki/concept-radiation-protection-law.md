The Radiation Protection Law (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG) is the German federal law governing the protection against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation. It establishes the fundamental legal framework for radiation safety in Germany [summary-strlschg-md-teil-12-13.md].

## Core Principles
The StrlSchG mandates a comprehensive approach to radiation safety, covering the entire lifecycle of radioactive substances and activities. Key principles include:
*   **Justification of Activities:** Any activity involving ionizing radiation must be justified [concept-rechtfertigung-tätigkeitsarten.md].
*   **Dose Limitation:** Exposure must be kept as low as reasonably achievable (ALARA) and must not exceed legally defined dose limits [concept-radiation-dose-limits.md].
*   **Protection:** The law mandates protection for humans and the environment from ionizing radiation [summary-strlschg-md-teil-12-13.md].

## Scope of Control
The law regulates several key areas:
*   **Radioactive Substances:** It defines and controls radioactive substances, governing their handling and disposal [concept-radioactive-stoffe.md].
*   **Waste Management:** The management of contaminated waste is strictly regulated, involving the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) and the *Atomrechtliche Entsorgungsverordnung* (AtEV) [concept-kontaminierte-abfaelle.md].
*   **Exposure:** The law distinguishes between professional exposure [concept-professional-exposure.md] and public exposure [concept-public-exposure.md] [summary-strlschg-md-teil-12-13.md].

## Implementation Details
The StrlSchG is supplemented by the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) [summary-strlschg-md-teil-12-13.md]. Furthermore, specific technical facilities, such as radionuclide laboratories, must comply with detailed design rules outlined in DIN 25425-1 [summary-din-25425-1-2021-05-md-teil-1-2.md].
