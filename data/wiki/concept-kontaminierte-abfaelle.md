The management of waste contaminated by or potentially contaminated by ionizing radiation is strictly regulated under the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) and detailed further in the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md]. The process involves rigorous measurement, classification, and disposal protocols.

## Regulatory Framework
The management of such waste is governed by the need to prevent undue exposure to the public and professionals. Key regulatory steps include:
1.  **Classification:** Determining the contamination level based on specific radionuclide concentrations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
2.  **Monitoring:** Establishing monitoring limits for residues, primarily based on the U-238 and Th-232 decay chains [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
3.  **Clearance:** Determining if the material can be cleared for non-radioactive use based on site-specific criteria [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].

## Monitoring and Disposal Criteria (StrlSchV)
The *StrlSchV* provides detailed technical appendices for disposal:

*   **General Monitoring Limit:** For residues, the sum of the maximum specific activities of U-238 and Th-232 must satisfy $C_{U238max} + C_{Th232max} \leq C$ [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md]. The general monitoring limit $C$ is $1 \text{ Bq/g}$ for dry mass.
*   **Groundwater Deposition:** For common disposal, the effective dose rate must not exceed $1 \text{ mSv}$ per calendar year, based on specific activity calculations involving $C_M$ values dependent on the disposal area size [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].

## Site-Specific Clearance Procedures (StrlSchV)
The ordinance details procedures for clearing materials:

*   **Building/Room Clearance:** Surface contamination values must not exceed the values specified in Anlage 4 Tabelle 1 Spalte 12 if future reuse is not excluded, or Spalte 13 if the structure is to be demolished [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
*   **Soil Clearance:** For surface contamination, the averaging area can be up to $100 \text{ m}^2$, or the averaging mass up to 1 Megagramm [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].

## Exposure Assessment Principles
When assessing exposure, the principles require using realistic pathways and including all potential exposures from the planned disposal or recycling route [StrlSchV.md [Teil 12/15].md].
