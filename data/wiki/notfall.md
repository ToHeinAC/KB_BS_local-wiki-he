In the context of radiation protection, a **Notfall** (Emergency) is defined as an event that can result in significant adverse effects on humans, the environment, or property due to ionizing radiation [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].

## Types of Emergencies
The law classifies emergencies based on the expected geographical scope of the adverse effects:
1. **Overregional Emergency (Überregionaler Notfall):** An emergency whose adverse effects are expected not to be limited to the country where it occurs, or an emergency outside the country expected to have effects within the scope of the law [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
2. **Regional Emergency (Regionaler Notfall):** An emergency within the country whose adverse effects are expected to be essentially limited to the country where it occurs [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
3. **Local Emergency (Lokaler Notfall):** An emergency expected to have essentially only local adverse effects within the scope of the law [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].

## Distinction from Planned Events
The law clarifies that an event resulting from a planned activity is not considered an emergency if the measures regulated for planned exposure situations are expected to manage the event [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
