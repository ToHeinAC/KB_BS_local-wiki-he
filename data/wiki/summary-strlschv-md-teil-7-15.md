This document provides excerpts from the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV), which details specific operational and procedural requirements for the application of ionizing radiation and radioactive substances in medicine and research.

## Quality Assurance and Facilities (§ 128, § 130)
The responsible authority designates medical and dental facilities to ensure quality when applying ionizing radiation or radioactive substances to humans (§ 128). A facility can only be designated if:
1. No facts suggest concerns regarding the required independence for performing its tasks [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
2. The necessary personnel, technical, and organizational resources are available [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
3. The personnel possess the required qualifications and experience [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
4. The working methods are expected to ensure proper performance, including adherence to medical science requirements [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
5. Appropriate measures for quality assurance are available [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].

The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that the commencement and cessation of activities involving ionizing radiation or radioactive substances are immediately reported to the designated medical or dental facility [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].

Medical and dental facilities are subject to quality assurance measures (§ 130). These measures specifically check whether:
1. The application is justified and medical science requirements are observed [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
2. The equipment and procedures meet the necessary quality standards according to the state of science and technology to keep exposure as low as possible [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
3. X-ray equipment and procedures meet the necessary quality standards to keep exposure as low as possible [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
4. Diagnostic reference values are not unjustifiably exceeded [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
5. A procedure exists for systematically identifying and handling occurrences during application [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
6. Written working instructions have been created according to § 121 Abs. 1 Satz 1 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].

## Medical Physics Expert Involvement (§ 131, § 132)
The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that a Medical Physics Expert (MPE) is involved in:
*   Developing the radiation plan and conducting the treatment when radioactive substances or ionizing radiation are used based on an individual dose plan [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
*   Standardized treatments, open radioactive substance examinations, CT/3D imaging, and interventions using X-ray equipment with significant exposure [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
*   Consultation for all other applications where optimization of radiation protection or ensuring required quality is warranted [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].

The MPE is responsible for:
1. Quality assurance during planning and execution, including physical-technical quality assurance [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
2. Selecting equipment, devices, and facilities [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
3. Monitoring the exposure of persons [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
4. Monitoring compliance with diagnostic reference values [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
5. Investigating occurrences [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
6. Conducting risk assessments [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
7. Instructing and training personnel [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].

## Research Consent and Information Duties (§ 133 - § 136)
For research purposes, the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation requires consent after information and questioning (§ 133).
*   **Consent (§ 134):** The written consent must cover the application and necessary follow-up examinations. Consent can be revoked at any time [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
*   **Information (§ 135):** The leading doctor or dentist must provide written information on the nature, significance, scope, and risks of the application, and the person must be informed about the conditions and duration of the applications and the possibility of withdrawing consent [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
*   **Minors/Incapable Persons (§ 136):** Applications to persons incapable of understanding the risks, or minors, are only permitted if:
    1. The research goal cannot be achieved otherwise [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
    2. The person has a disease or suspected disease relevant to the research [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
    3. The goal is to diagnose, save life, restore health, alleviate suffering, or improve procedures related to the disease [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
    4. Legal representatives provide consent after being informed and questioned [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].

## Operational Requirements and Reporting (§ 137 - § 142)
*   **Prohibitions (§ 137):** Applications are prohibited for pregnant women or institutionalized persons. The effective dose for a healthy person must not exceed 20 mSv [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
*   **Personnel (§ 145):** Applications must be performed by approved doctors or dentists, or by personnel who possess the required qualifications or knowledge and work under constant supervision [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
*   **Reporting (§ 142):** The responsible party must submit a final report to the competent supervisory authority no later than twelve months after the end of the research project, detailing the exposure for every included person [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
*   **Retention (§ 140):** Consent forms and records must be kept for 30 years after the declaration/application [StrlSchV.md [Teil 7/15].md].
