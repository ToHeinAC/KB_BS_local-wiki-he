# DIN 25425-4 Radionuklidlaboratoratorien – Teil 4: Regeln für den Personenschutz

This document, DIN 25425-4, provides the specific rules concerning personal protection (*Regeln für den Personenschutz*) within radionuclide laboratories. It details the necessary measures to ensure that personnel working in such facilities are protected from radiation exposure during all operational phases [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].

## Scope of Personal Protection
The rules govern the protection of individuals against radiation exposure resulting from the handling of radioactive substances within the laboratory environment [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]]. This encompasses both external exposure and internal contamination risks.

## Key Protection Measures
The standard outlines several critical areas for maintaining personal safety:

### General Principles
Protection measures must be selected based on the activity level, the chemical-physical properties of the radioactive substances, and the handling method. All planned activities must be analyzed to determine exposure conditions, applying the optimization principle according to the ALARA principle (As Low As Reasonably Achievable) [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].

### External Exposure Protection (Äußere Exposition)
*   Personnel potentially exposed to external professional exposure exceeding 1 mSv/a must be protected by suitable measures, such as shielding [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
*   Shielding materials selection depends on the radiation type:
    *   **Photons:** High atomic number materials, such as lead, are effective [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
    *   **Beta rays:** Materials from elements with low atomic numbers, such as plastics or aluminum, must be used. Care must be taken regarding secondary bremsstrahlung radiation [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].

### Internal Contamination Protection (Inkorporation)
*   Suitable measures must be taken to protect against the incorporation of open radioactive substances [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
*   This involves counteracting activity spread into the room air through directed airflow and negative pressure zoning [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
*   For handling open radioactive substances, the potential incorporation risk must be determined according to RiPhyKo Part 2 [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].

### Organizational Measures (Organisatorische Schutzmaßnahmen)
*   **Behavioral Principles:** Stay time in radiation protection areas must be limited to the necessary duration, and distance from radiation sources must be kept as large as possible [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
*   **Personnel Categories:** Personnel are classified into Category A or Category B based on expected exposure. Category A personnel are subject to annual medical monitoring [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
*   **Pregnancy:** Handling open radioactive substances is prohibited for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as internal incorporation cannot be ruled out [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].

### Monitoring and Control
*   **Dosimetry:** Body doses for external exposure are generally measured using official personal dosimeters worn at a representative body surface location [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
*   **Contamination Control:** After activities, the workplace and clothing must be checked for contamination. If contamination is suspected on accessible traffic areas, an immediate contamination check must be performed [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
*   **Documentation:** The documentation requirements are governed by the StrlSchG and StrlSchV. Key records include:
    *   Personal dosimetry measurements (75 years after birth, but min. 30 years after employment end) [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
    *   Incorporated activity (75 years after birth, but min. 30 years after employment end) [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
    *   Radioactive waste/residues (30 years) [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].

### Personal Protective Equipment (PSA)
PSA includes lab coats, protective overalls, protective gloves, and lab shoes. Additional items may be required, such as:
*   Respirator mask (if contamination cannot be ruled out or during abrasive decontamination) [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].
*   Protective goggles (if eye contamination cannot be ruled out or if eye lens exposure > 15 mSv/a) [DIN 25425-4_2019-12.md [Teil 2/2]].

## Procedural Requirements for Directive Creation
The creation of a formal Radiation Protection Directive (*Strahlenschutzanweisung*) must follow detailed guidelines, including:
*   **Legal Basis:** The directive must cite the legal basis and the specific approval notice granted under the StrlSchV [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Organizational Structure:** It must define the radiation protection organization, naming the responsible person, authorized person, and officer [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Operational Rules:** The directive must detail general rules covering PPE use, prohibiting mouth pipetting, and forbidding eating, drinking, or makeup within the controlled area [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Emergency Planning:** It must include sections for emergency behavior, fire procedures, and designated reporting routes [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
*   **Waste Management:** Specific rules for waste disposal, requiring separate collection and labeling [DIN 25425-4-1_2013-06.md].
