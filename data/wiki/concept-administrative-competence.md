# Administrative Competence in Radiation Protection

The German Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) delegates specific, highly detailed competencies to various federal authorities, ensuring that different aspects of radiation safety are managed by specialized bodies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## Key Competency Areas and Responsible Authorities
The distribution of authority is detailed across multiple sections of the law:

* **General Permits & Oversight:** The **Federal Radiation Protection Agency (BfS)** holds broad authority over medical research applications, facility type approvals, and establishing registers for various exposures and sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
* **Specific Competencies:** The competencies of other federal bodies are specialized, including:
    * **BAFA:** Competence related to economic affairs and export control [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * **BASE:** Competence related to nuclear waste management [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * **PTB:** Competence related to physical-technical standards [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * **LBA:** Competence related to aviation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * **EBA:** Competence related to railway authorities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## Expert Designation and Continuity
* **Expert Designation (§ 217):** Official designations of experts (*Sachverständigen*) made before December 31, 2018, remain valid (§ 172 Absatz 1 Nummer 1, 3 oder 4) for a maximum of five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
* **Transitional Rules:** The law contains numerous transitional provisions ensuring that permits and authorizations issued under previous legal frameworks remain valid until specified dates, provided certain conditions are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
