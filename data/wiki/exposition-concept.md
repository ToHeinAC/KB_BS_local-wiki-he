In the context of the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG), **Exposition** refers to the incidence of ionizing radiation on the human body.

## Definition
Exposition is defined as the incidence of ionizing radiation on the human body, which can occur either externally (outside the body) or internally (inside the body), or it may refer to the measure of this incidence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Types of Exposure Situations
The law distinguishes between several types of exposure situations:
*   **Planned Exposure Situation:** An exposure situation that arises from activities and in which an exposure is caused or can be caused [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Emergency Exposure Situation:** An exposure situation resulting from an emergency, provided the situation does not fall under Paragraph 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
*   **Existing Exposure Situation:** An exposure situation that already exists when a decision regarding its control must be made [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Categories of Exposure
The law distinguishes three main categories of exposure:
1. Population exposure (exposure of persons who are neither professionally nor medically exposed) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
2. Occupational exposure (exposure of a person in an employment relationship performing a regulated activity, or performing such an activity themselves) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
3. Medical exposure (exposure to a patient or asymptomatic person during medical or dental examination/treatment, or for medical research purposes) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].
