# Federal Office for Nuclear Waste Management (BASE)

The Federal Office for Nuclear Waste Management (Bundesamt für die Sicherheit der nuklearen Entsorgung, BASE) is a federal authority tasked with oversight and regulation concerning nuclear waste and large radioactive sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## Core Competencies
BASE's primary responsibilities include:
* **Large Source Transport:** Permitting the transport of large sources and handling their revocation and withdrawal. A large source is defined as a radioactive substance whose activity exceeds 1,000 Terabecquerel per transport or shipment unit [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
* **Site Selection Oversight:** Performing competencies designated in § 184 regarding:
    * Above-ground exploration (§ 16 Paragraph 1 of the Site Selection Act) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Underground exploration (§ 18 Paragraph 1 of the Site Selection Act) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Construction, operation, and decommissioning of federal facilities under the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Serving as the competent supervisory authority for the Asse II shaft facility [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## Administrative Roles
BASE is also responsible for:
* Levying fees and expenditures for official acts related to its designated areas of competence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
