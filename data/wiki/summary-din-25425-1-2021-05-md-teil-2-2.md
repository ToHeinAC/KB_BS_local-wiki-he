The document DIN 25425-1:2021-05 provides detailed technical rules for the design and layout of radionuclide laboratories (*Radionuklidlaboratorien*). It covers requirements for waste management, residual material handling, special room design, and general protection measures based on room categorization.

## 10. Abwasserführung (Wastewater Drainage)
The design of wastewater collection containers for contaminated wastewater must ensure they are closed and, if not double-walled, placed in a sealed and easily decontaminable trough capable of containing at least the volume of the largest container. The entire container room may be designed as a trough. Containers must be secured against floating in the event of potential room flooding [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].

Specific requirements for the trough include:
*   A pump sump and a moisture sensor with an alarm device [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].
*   The trough floor must have a gradient towards the pump sump [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].
*   The outlet must contain safety devices that prevent uncontrolled discharge even in case of malfunction [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].

Furthermore, wastewater containers must be equipped with a level measurement and warning system, and a mixing device must be included to allow for the sampling of a representative wastewater sample at a suitable location [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md]. Ventilation for the network and containers must be connected to the exhaust system of the radionuclide laboratory or run separately over the roof. If radioactive substances can escape from the wastewater, the exhaust air from the containers must be filtered, and condensation formation in the filter must be prevented by appropriate measures, potentially requiring the installation of a heater before the filter [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].

## 11. Reststoffführung (Residual Material Management)
The technical design of residual material handling must ensure that residues generated during activities with radioactive substances are collected at the point of origin, sorted according to their physical, chemical, and biological properties, and their intended disposal route [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md]. Facilities must be provided to prepare these residues for transport and orderly disposal. This also applies to the disposal of released solid or liquid substances (release from the scope of the Radiation Protection Act) [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md]. If residues are handled such that there is a risk of incorporation or contamination, the requirements for laboratory rooms according to Sections 7 to 10 apply [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].

### Storage Rooms for Residual Materials
The location of rooms for the collection, treatment, and storage of residues must be planned to ensure:
a) The shortest possible routes between laboratory rooms and the residue room [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].
b) Good accessibility with usual means of transport, while observing necessary shielding [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].
c) Suitable loading and unloading capability for transport vehicles [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].
d) Sufficient possibilities for potentially required contamination control [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].

Required facilities in storage rooms include: handling of residues, reception of residue containers from laboratories, storage of residues with short-lived radionuclides for activity decay, assembly of shipments, cooling for putrefying and fermentable residues, a safety cabinet according to DIN EN 14470-1, sufficiently dimensioned containment troughs for residue solutions, loading aids, and residue documentation [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].

### Ventilation and Special Rooms
The residue room must generally be connected to the exhaust system of the radionuclide laboratory or ventilated separately. The number of air changes must be calculated considering the possibility of releasing radioactive substances into the room air and the occupancy time of the personnel [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].

Specialized areas include:
*   **Airlocks (Schleuse):** The entrance and exit to the connected area of rooms 1 to 8 should pass through a personnel airlock (Personenschleuse) from RK2 onwards. If no airlock is established, a changing facility must be present in the access area. The personnel airlock must contain a changing wardrobe, a washing facility (handwashing basin with hand shower, minimum interior dimensions approx. 50 cm × 38 cm), and contamination control measuring devices [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].
*   **Radioactive Material Storage:** For the storage of radioactive substances, a dedicated room may be necessary. Care must be taken for sufficient shielding, security against loss, and against access by unauthorized persons. Generally, DIN 25422 must be observed [DIN 25425-1_2021-05 .md [Teil 2/2].md].
