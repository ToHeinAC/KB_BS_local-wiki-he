Ionizing radiation refers to electromagnetic radiation or particulate radiation that possesses sufficient energy to cause ionization—the removal of electrons from atoms or molecules [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

### Context in Radiation Protection Law
In the context of the German Radiation Protection Act ($\text{StrlSchG}$), ionizing radiation is a central subject of control, necessitating strict regulatory oversight to protect human health and the environment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

### Applications and Control
The law regulates the use of ionizing radiation in several contexts:
*   **Medical Research:** The application of ionizing radiation to humans for medical research requires specific permits and rigorous review by the Federal Radiation Protection Agency ($\S 185$ (1) 1) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   **Industrial/Technical Use:** The use of ionizing radiation, such as in X-ray equipment or scatter sources, is subject to type approval and operational permits [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   **Monitoring:** The Act mandates monitoring for exposure to ionizing radiation, including cosmic radiation during spacecraft operations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

### Legal Implications
The handling and use of ionizing radiation are heavily regulated, requiring adherence to specific procedures outlined in the StrlSchG, including documentation, notification, and the establishment of safety measures to ensure exposure remains below permissible limits [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
