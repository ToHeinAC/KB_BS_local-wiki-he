# Property-Casualty Insurance

Property-Casualty (P&C) insurance refers to coverage against physical damage to property and liability arising from unforeseen events, excluding life or health risks. This category is highly sensitive to physical risk accumulation and climate change [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Scope of Coverage
P&C coverage typically addresses:
*   **Natural Catastrophes (Nat-cat):** Losses from events such as hurricanes, earthquakes, and floods [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Man-made Events:** Damage resulting from accidents, terrorism, or infrastructure failure [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Cyber Risks:** Increasingly integrated, P&C coverage now includes losses stemming from digital attacks, which are modeled as complex, correlated risks [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Market Cyclicality
The P&C market is noted for its cyclical nature. Results are heavily influenced by the frequency and severity of loss years. The period between 2022 and 2025 was characterized as a "hard market," leading to exceptional underwriting margins for reinsurers [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Reinsurance Role
P&C reinsurance is crucial for managing the massive, correlated risks associated with large geographic areas. The ability of reinsurers to pool risk efficiently across multiple lines and geographies is central to the P&C market structure [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
