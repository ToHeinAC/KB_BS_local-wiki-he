The Strahlenschutzgesetz (StrlSchG) distinguishes three primary categories of human exposure to ionizing radiation, each with specific regulatory considerations [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].

## 1. Population Exposure
This category covers the exposure of individuals who are not involved in professional or medical activities related to radiation. It represents the general public's exposure [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].

## 2. Professional Exposure
Professional exposure applies to several groups, including:
*   Individuals employed or self-employed in activities regulated by the law [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   Flight and space personnel [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   Personnel performing specific tasks under the Atomic Energy Act [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   Emergency response personnel during their deployment [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].

## 3. Medical Exposure
Medical exposure occurs when radioactive substances or ionizing radiation are applied to a person for the purpose of:
*   Medical or dental examination or treatment [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   Medical research, provided the person consents or has a legal representative who consents [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
*   Voluntary support or caregiving roles involving exposure to radiation [summary-strlschg-01-md.md].
