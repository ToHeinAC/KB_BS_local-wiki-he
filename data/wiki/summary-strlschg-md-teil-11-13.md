# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 11/13)

This document provides a detailed summary of the later sections of the German Radiation Protection Act (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), covering administrative procedures, the competencies of various federal authorities, fee structures, and transitional rules [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## Administrative Procedures and Communication

### Written and Electronic Form (§ 182)
1. **Form Requirement:** Permits and construction approvals (*Genehmigungen und Bauartzulassungen*) must be issued in writing [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
2. **Electronic Use:** If the electronic form is used for an administrative act requiring written form, it must be equipped with a permanently verifiable qualified electronic signature according to § 37 Paragraph 4 of the Administrative Procedure Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
3. **Reporting:** Notification, registration, and reporting duties can be fulfilled electronically if the recipient opens access for this purpose and determines the procedure and the necessary requirements for data transmission. Measures must ensure data protection and data security appropriate to the current state of technology, particularly guaranteeing the confidentiality and integrity of the data; encryption procedures must be used when using publicly accessible networks. For personal data, the measures must comply with Articles 24, 25, and 32 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
4. **Paper Copies:** If the application, notification, registration, report, or communication is electronic, the responsible authority must provide paper copies of the electronically transmitted documents upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

### Costs and Fees (§ 183)
Fees and expenditures (*Gebühren und Auslagen*) are levied for various official actions, including:
* Decisions concerning specific provisions of the Atomic Energy Act (Atomgesetz) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
* Other supervisory measures specified in the cost regulation for the Atomic Energy Act and the Radiation Protection Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
* Other official acts, including inspections and investigations by the Federal Radiation Protection Agency (BfS), where it is competent according to § 185 Paragraph 1 Number 1 to 8 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
* Decisions of the Federal Office for Nuclear Waste Management (BASE) regarding applications under § 27 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
* Other official acts, including inspections and investigations by the Federal Aviation Office (LBA) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

Fees are charged in cases of:
* Withdrawal or revocation of an official act by the responsible party who is at fault, provided fees have not already been charged [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
* Rejection of an application for an official act for reasons other than the authority's lack of competence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
* Withdrawal of an application or notification after the start of substantive processing, but before its completion [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
* Full or partial rejection or withdrawal of an objection against an official act or a fee decision [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## Competencies of Federal Authorities

The Act details the specific competencies of several federal agencies:

* **Federal Radiation Protection Agency (BfS) (§ 185):** The BfS is responsible for:
    * Permitting for the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for medical research, as well as revocation and withdrawal of such permits [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Reviewing notifications of the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for medical research, and the prohibition of application [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Reviewing the operation of spacecraft and prohibiting such operation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Type approval for devices containing radioactive substances and facilities for generating ionizing radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Quality assurance measures for determining the body dose of flight personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Monitoring compliance with protection requirements from cosmic radiation during spacecraft operations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Establishing and maintaining a register of Ethics Committees evaluating research projects involving radioactive substances or ionizing radiation in humans for medical research [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Establishing and maintaining a register of occupational exposures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Establishing and maintaining a register of high-radioactivity sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Reviewing the justification of activity types, including those involving consumer goods or type-approved devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * (If authorized by the Federal Government) Being the central body for data collection, processing, and evaluation of significant incidents, especially concerning the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Recognizing sites for measuring Radon-222 activity concentration and carrying out quality assurance measures for internal and Radon exposure measurements [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

* **Federal Office for Nuclear Waste Management (BASE) (§ 186):** BASE is responsible for:
    * Permitting the transport of large sources and their revocation/withdrawal. Large sources are radioactive substances whose activity exceeds 1,000 Terabecquerel per transport or shipment unit [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Performing competencies designated in § 184 regarding:
        * Above-ground exploration (§ 16 Paragraph 1 of the Site Selection Act) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
        * Underground exploration (§ 18 Paragraph 1 of the Site Selection Act) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
        * Construction, operation, and decommissioning of federal facilities under the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
        * Being the competent supervisory authority for the Asse II shaft facility [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

* **Physikalisch-Technische Bundesanstalt (PTB) (§ 187):** The PTB is responsible for:
    * Type approval of scatter sources and type approval under § 45 Paragraph 1 Number 2 to 6 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Implementing quality assurance measures for external exposure measurement sites [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Providing radioactivity standards for comparison measurements [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

* **Cross-Border Movements (§ 188):** The Federal Office for Economic Affairs and Export Control (BAFA) is responsible for issuing permits for the cross-border movement of consumer goods, as well as their revocation and withdrawal. Customs authorities assist in monitoring cross-border movements of radioactive substances, residues, or goods containing them [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

* **Aviation Authority (LBA) (§ 189):** The LBA is responsible for:
    * Reviewing the notification of aircraft operation and prohibiting operation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Recognizing calculation programs for determining the body dose of flight personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Monitoring compliance with protection requirements from cosmic radiation during aircraft operations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    * Certifying expertise in radiation protection required in connection with aircraft operations, and recognizing courses for acquiring this expertise [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

* **Railway Authority (EBA) (§ 190):** The EBA's competence for the supervision and permitting of the transport of other radioactive substances is governed by § 24 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 and 3 of the Atomic Energy Act. The competence for permitting the transport of large sources is determined by § 186 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## Penalties and Transitional Rules

### Penal Provisions (§ 194)
Violations can constitute an administrative offense (*Ordnungswidrigkeit*) if one:
1. Acts intentionally or negligently contrary to numerous specific provisions listed in the Act (e.g., failing to provide required documentation, violating dose limits, etc.) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
2. Operates an unpermitted facility or performs prohibited activities (e.g., operating an unpermitted facility, using ionizing radiation without a permit) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
3. Fails to submit a required notification, report, or measurement in the prescribed manner, or fails to provide required documentation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
4. Fails to take required measures (e.g., failing to ensure contamination removal, failing to appoint a Radiation Protection Officer) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

### Transitional Provisions (§ 196 - § 201)
The Act includes several provisions ensuring continuity for permits and operations granted before December 31, 2018, or 2020, requiring specific proof of compliance with updated standards (e.g., for medical treatments or handling radioactive materials) by specific deadlines [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
