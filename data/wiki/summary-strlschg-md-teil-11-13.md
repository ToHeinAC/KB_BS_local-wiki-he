This document provides excerpts from the German Radiation Protection Act ($\text{StrlSchG}$), detailing administrative procedures, cost collection, and jurisdictional competencies among various federal authorities.

### Administrative Procedures and Communication ($\S 182$)
The section on written form and electronic communication ($\S 182$) stipulates that permits and construction approvals under the Act or its related ordinances must be issued in writing ($\S 182$ (1)). If the electronic form is used for an administrative act requiring written form, it must be accompanied by a permanently verifiable qualified electronic signature according to $\S 37$ Paragraph 4 of the Administrative Procedure Act ($\S 182$ (2)).

Electronic submission of reporting, registration, and notification duties is permitted if the recipient opens access and determines the process and technical requirements for data transfer. Such measures must ensure data protection and data security corresponding to the current state of technology, guaranteeing confidentiality and integrity. When using publicly accessible networks, encryption procedures must be applied. For personal data, measures must comply with Articles 24, 25, and 32 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ($\S 182$ (3)). If an electronically transmitted document is unsuitable for processing by the recipient, the recipient must immediately inform the sender, specifying the technical framework conditions for reception ($\S 182$ (3)). If the application, notification, registration, reporting, or communication is electronic, the responsible authority may request paper copies of the electronically transmitted documents ($\S 182$ (4)).

### Costs and Fee Collection ($\S 183$)
Fees and expenditures (costs) are levied for various official actions, including:
1. Determinations according to $\S 177$ in conjunction with $\S 13$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 of the Atomic Energy Act ($\S 183$ (1) 1).
2. Decisions according to $\S 179$ Paragraph 1 Number 1 in conjunction with $\S 17$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 3, Paragraph 2 to 5 of the Atomic Energy Act, and for decisions according to $\S 179$ Paragraph 1 Number 2 in conjunction with $\S 19$ Paragraph 3 of the Atomic Energy Act ($\S 183$ (1) 2).
3. Other supervisory measures specified in the Cost Ordinance for the Atomic Energy Act and the Radiation Protection Act, according to $\S 179$ Paragraph 1 Number 2 in conjunction with $\S 19$ of the Atomic Energy Act ($\S 183$ (1) 3).
4. Other official actions, including inspections and investigations by the Federal Radiation Protection Agency, where it is competent according to $\S 185$ Paragraph 1 Number 1 to 8 ($\S 183$ (1) 4).
5. Decisions of the Federal Office for the Safety of Nuclear Waste regarding applications according to $\S 27$ Paragraph 1, where it is competent according to $\S 186$ Paragraph 1 ($\S 183$ (1) 5).
6. Other official actions, including inspections and investigations by the Federal Aviation Office, where it is competent according to $\S 189$ ($\S 183$ (1) 6).
7. Services provided by the Federal Physical-Technical Institute:
    *   Official actions including inspections and investigations according to $\S 187$ Paragraph 1 Number 1 or 2 ($\S 183$ (1) 7 a).
    *   Provision of radioactivity standards for comparison measurements by the Federal Radiation Protection Agency, where the radiation protection officer is to participate in emissions measurements conducted according to the ordinance $\S 81$ Sentence 2 Number 7 ($\S 183$ (1) 7 b).
8. Decisions of the Railway Federal Office regarding applications according to $\S 27$ Paragraph 1, where it is competent according to $\S 190$ Sentence 1 ($\S 183$ (1) 8).

Costs are also collected for:
*   Withdrawal or revocation of an official action under Paragraph 1 or 2, if the affected person is responsible and costs have not already been collected ($\S 183$ (3) 1).
*   Rejection of an application for an official action under Paragraph 1 or 2 for reasons other than the authority's lack of competence ($\S 183$ (3) 2).
*   Withdrawal of an application for an official action or notification under Paragraph 1 or 2 after the commencement of substantive processing, but before its completion ($\S 183$ (3) 3).
*   Complete or partial rejection or withdrawal of an objection against: a) an official action under Paragraph 1 or 2, or b) a cost decision set by Paragraph 1 or 2 in conjunction with the Cost Ordinance for the Atomic Energy Act and the Radiation Protection Act ($\S 183$ (3) 4).

The fee amount is specified relative to the respective cases ($\S 183$ (4)).

### Jurisdictional Competencies ($\S 184$ to $\S 192$)
**State Authorities ($\S 184$):** The states execute, as their own matter, specific parts of the law, including parts of Chapter 3 and Chapter 4, with exceptions ($\S 184$ (1)). Generally, administrative tasks are executed by the states on behalf of the federal government, subject to certain provisions ($\S 184$ (2)).

**Federal Radiation Protection Agency ($\S 185$):** The Federal Radiation Protection Agency is responsible for:
1. Permits for the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for medical research, including revocation and withdrawal of permits ($\S 185$ (1) 1).
2. Review of notifications regarding the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for medical research, including prohibition of application ($\S 185$ (1) 2).
3. Review of notifications regarding the operation of spacecraft and prohibition of operation ($\S 185$ (1) 3).
4. Type approval for devices containing other radioactive substances and for facilities generating ionizing radiation ($\S 185$ (1) 4).
5. Implementing quality assurance measures for determining the body dose of flight personnel ($\S 185$ (1) 5).
6. Monitoring compliance with protection requirements against cosmic radiation exposure during spacecraft operation ($\S 185$ (1) 6).
7. Establishing and maintaining a register of ethics committees evaluating research projects using radioactive substances or ionizing radiation for medical research, including registration and revocation ($\S 185$ (1) 7).
8. Establishing and maintaining a register of professional exposures ($\S 185$ (1) 8).
9. Establishing and maintaining a register of high-radioactive sources ($\S 185$ (1) 9).
10. Reviewing the justification of activities and reporting on the justification according to $\S 7$ ($\S 185$ (1) 10).
11. Reviewing the justification of activities with consumer goods or type-approved devices and issuing a statement on the justification according to $\S 38$ ($\S 185$ (1) 11).

The Federal Government may designate the Federal Radiation Protection Agency as competent for:
1. Retrospective determination of individual population exposures from activities specified in the ordinance $\S 81$ Sentence 2 Number 2 ($\S 185$ (2) 1).
2. Determining, creating, and publishing diagnostic reference values, determining medical exposure of persons, and necessary surveys based on ordinances $\S 86$ Sentence 2 Number 7 and 8 ($\S 185$ (2) 2).
3. Managing and issuing identification numbers for high-radioactive sources ($\S 185$ (2) 3).
4. Serving as the central body for establishing and operating a system for collecting, processing, and evaluating information on significant incidents, especially concerning the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans ($\S 185$ (2) 4).
5. Recognizing sites for measuring Radon-222 activity concentration ($\S 185$ (2) 5).
6. Implementing quality assurance measures for measurement sites for internal exposure and exposure through Radon ($\S 185$ (2) 6).

**Federal Office for the Safety of Nuclear Waste ($\S 186$):** This office is responsible for:
1. Permitting the transport of large sources and their revocation/withdrawal. Large sources are radioactive substances whose activity exceeds 1,000 Terabecquerel per transport or shipment unit ($\S 186$ (1)).
2. Carrying out competencies designated in $\S 184$ for: a) surface exploration ($\S 16$ Paragraph 1 of the Site Selection Act), b) underground exploration ($\S 18$ Paragraph 1 of the Site Selection Act), c) construction, operation, and decommissioning of federal facilities ($\S 9a$ Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 of the Atomic Energy Act), and for the supervisory authority of the Asse II shaft facility ($\S 186$ (2)).

**Federal Physical-Technical Institute ($\S 187$):** This institute is responsible for:
1. Type approval of scatter sources ($\S 45$ Paragraph 1 Number 1) and type approval according to $\S 45$ Paragraph 1 Number 2 to 6 ($\S 187$ (1) 1).
2. Implementing quality assurance measures for external exposure measurement sites according to the ordinance $\S 169$ Paragraph 4 ($\S 187$ (1) 2).
3. Providing radioactivity standards for comparison measurements according to the ordinance $\S 81$ Sentence 3 ($\S 187$ (1) 3).

**Federal Office for Economic Affairs and Export Control ($\S 188$):** This office is responsible for:
1. Issuing permits for the cross-border transport of consumer goods, as well as their revocation and withdrawal. This also applies where ordinances $\S 24$ Sentence 1 Number 7 and $\S 30$ stipulate the requirement for permits and approvals, and the review of notifications or registrations for cross-border transports ($\S 188$ (1)).
2. Customs authorities assist in monitoring cross-border transports of radioactive substances, consumer goods, or products containing radioactive substances, or residues. Customs authorities may: 1. Halt shipments, transport means, containers, loading materials, and packaging materials containing radioactive substances, residues, or consumer goods/products listed in Sentence 1, and their transport means for monitoring. 2. Inform the competent authorities of a suspected violation of prohibitions and restrictions under this Act or the ordinances derived from $\S 24$ Sentence 1 Number 7 and $\S 30$, based on factual grounds arising during their duties. 3. In cases of Number 2, order that shipments according to Number 1 be presented to the competent authorities at the expense and risk of the disposer ($\S 188$ (2)).

**Federal Aviation Office ($\S 189$):** This office is responsible for:
1. Reviewing notifications regarding the operation of aircraft and prohibiting operation.
2. Recognizing calculation programs for determining the body dose of flight personnel.
3. Monitoring compliance with protection requirements against cosmic radiation exposure during aircraft operation ($\S 189$ (3)).
4. Certifying expertise in radiation protection, if required in connection with aircraft operation.
5. Recognizing courses serving the acquisition of required expertise in radiation protection in connection with aircraft operation ($\S 189$ (5)).

**Railway Federal Office ($\S 190$):** The jurisdiction for the Railway Federal Office is determined by $\S 186$ Paragraph 1 for the permit for the transport of large sources.

**Ministry of Defence ($\S 191$):** For aircraft operated within the Ministry of Defence's scope, the Ministry itself or its designated departments are responsible for the tasks under $\S 189$ Number 1 and 3.

**Federal Radiation Protection Agency ($\S 192$):** The Federal Radiation Protection Agency is responsible for procuring and making protective agents available according to $\S 104$, unless another authority is designated by law ($\S 192$ (1)).

### Penal Provisions ($\S 194$ to $\S 201$)
$\S 194$ details the offenses and penalties for violations of the Act. Violations can include, but are not limited to:
*   Willfully or negligently violating specific provisions listed in the statute ($\S 194$ (1) 1).
*   Operating or transporting radioactive substances without the required permit ($\S 194$ (1) 2).
*   Failing to submit a notification, or submitting one that is not correct, not complete, or not timely ($\S 194$ (1) 3).
*   Violating an enforceable order ($\S 194$ (1) 4).
*   Failing to provide a notification, or providing one that is not correct, not complete, or not timely ($\S 194$ (1) 5).

The penalties range from a fine up to fifty thousand Euros for certain violations, and up to one hundred thousand Euros for others ($\S 194$ (2)).

### Transitional Provisions ($\S 196$ to $\S 201$)
Several sections address the continuation of permits and notifications issued before December 31, 2018, ensuring continuity for facilities and activities related to ionizing radiation, radioactive substances, and X-ray equipment ($\S 196$ to $\S 201$).
