This document provides the eleventh part of the *Gesetz zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (StrlSchG), detailing administrative procedures, competencies of federal authorities, cost regulations, and transitional provisions.

## § 182 Schriftform, elektronische Kommunikation
*   **Written Form:** Permits and construction approvals under this law or a supporting ordinance must be issued in writing [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   **Electronic Form:** If the electronic form is used for an administrative act where written form is required, it must be equipped with a permanently verifiable qualified electronic signature according to § 37 Paragraph 4 of the Administrative Procedure Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   **Reporting/Notification:** Notification, registration, and reporting duties can be fulfilled electronically if the recipient opens access for this purpose and determines the procedure and the requirements necessary for data transmission. Measures ensuring data protection and data security appropriate to the current state of technology must be taken, particularly ensuring the confidentiality and integrity of the data; encryption procedures must be used when using generally accessible networks. For personal data, the measures must comply with Articles 24, 25, and 32 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md]. If an electronically transmitted document is unsuitable for processing by the recipient, the recipient must immediately inform the sender, stating the technical framework conditions applicable for receipt [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   **Paper Copies:** If the application, notification, registration, reporting, or communication occurs electronically, paper copies of the electronically transmitted documents must be provided to the competent authority upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 183 Kosten; Verordnungsermächtigung
*   **Fees and Expenses:** Fees and expenses (costs) are levied for:
    1.  Determinations according to § 177 in conjunction with § 13 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 of the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    2.  Decisions according to § 179 Paragraph 1 Number 1 in conjunction with § 17 Paragraph 1 Sentence 3, Paragraph 2 to 5 of the Atomic Energy Act, and for decisions according to § 179 Paragraph 1 Number 2 in conjunction with § 19 Paragraph 3 of the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    3.  Other supervisory measures specified in the Cost Ordinance for the Atomic Energy Act and the Radiation Protection Act according to § 179 Paragraph 1 Number 2 in conjunction with § 19 of the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    4.  Other official acts including inspections and investigations by the Federal Office for Radiation Protection (BfS), insofar as it is competent according to § 185 Paragraph 1 Number 1 to 8 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    5.  Decisions of the Federal Office for the Safety of Nuclear Waste regarding applications according to § 27 Paragraph 1, insofar as it is competent according to § 186 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    6.  Other official acts including inspections and investigations by the Federal Aviation Office, insofar as it is competent according to § 189 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    7.  For the following services of the Federal Physical-Technical Institute (PTB):
        *   Official acts including inspections and investigations according to § 187 Paragraph 1 Number 1 or 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
        *   The provision of radioactivity standards for comparative measurements by the BfS, which the Radiation Protection Officer must participate in to ensure the quality of emission measurements to be carried out by the BfS according to the ordinance under § 81 Sentence 2 Number 7 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
        *   Decisions of the Railway Federal Office on applications according to § 27 Paragraph 1, insofar as it is competent according to § 190 Sentence 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   **Cost Collection:** Costs are incurred in cases of:
    1.  Withdrawal or revocation of an official act according to Paragraph 1 or 2, if the affected person is responsible and costs have not already been levied according to Paragraph 1 or 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    2.  Rejection of an application for an official act according to Paragraph 1 or 2 for reasons other than the incompetence of the authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    3.  Withdrawal of an application for an official act or a notification according to Paragraph 1 or 2 after the start of substantive processing, but before its completion [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    4.  Complete or partial rejection or withdrawal of an objection against:
        a) an official act according to Paragraph 1 or 2, or
        b) a cost decision set according to Paragraph 1 or 2 in conjunction with the Cost Ordinance for the Atomic Energy Act and the Radiation Protection Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 185 Zuständigkeit des Bundesamtes für Strahlenschutz; Verordnungsermächtigung
The BfS is responsible for:
1.  The permit for the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for the purpose of medical research, as well as the withdrawal and revocation of the permit [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
2.  The examination of the notification of the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for the purpose of medical research, as well as the prohibition of the application [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
3.  The examination of the notification of space vehicle operations and the prohibition of operation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
4.  The construction approval according to § 45 Paragraph 1 Number 1 for facilities containing other radioactive substances, and the construction approval according to § 45 Paragraph 1 Number 7 for facilities generating ionizing radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
5.  Carrying out quality assurance measures when determining the body dose of flight personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
6.  Monitoring compliance with requirements for protection against exposure of persons by cosmic radiation during the operation of space vehicles under this law or a resulting ordinance, including the certification of the required expertise in radiation protection and the recognition of courses for acquiring this expertise [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
7.  Establishing and maintaining a register of ethics committees that evaluate research projects on the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans for the purpose of medical research, the registration of the ethics committees, and the revocation of the registration [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
8.  Establishing and maintaining a register of professional exposures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
9.  Establishing and maintaining a register of high-activity radioactive sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
10. Examining the justification of activity types and the report on the justification according to § 7, and examining the justification of activity types with consumer goods or construction-approved facilities and providing a statement on the justification according to § 38 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
11. The Federal Government is authorized by ordinance with the consent of the Bundesrat to determine that the BfS is responsible for:
    1.  The retrospective determination of exposures of individual persons in the population by approved or notified activities specified in the ordinance under § 81 Sentence 2 Number 2, [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    2.  The determination, creation, and publication of diagnostic reference values, the determination of the medical exposure of persons, and the required surveys for each, based on an ordinance under § 86 Sentence 2 Number 7 and 8 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    3.  Managing and issuing identification numbers for high-activity radioactive sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    4.  As the central body for the establishment and operation of a system for collecting, processing, and evaluating information on significant incidents, particularly in the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans according to the ordinance under § 90 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 Number 6 to 8 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    5.  The recognition of facilities for measuring Radon-222 activity concentration and carrying out quality assurance measures for measuring sites for internal exposure and exposure by Radon [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 186 Zuständigkeit des Bundesamtes für die Sicherheit der nuklearen Entsorgung
*   The Federal Office for the Safety of Nuclear Waste (BASE) is responsible for the permit for the transport of large sources and their withdrawal and revocation. Large sources are other radioactive substances whose activity exceeds 1,000 Terabecquerel per transport or shipment unit [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   BASE also performs the competencies designated in § 184 as:
    1.  Approving and supervisory authority in the context of surface exploration according to § 16 Paragraph 1 of the Site Selection Act,
    2.  The underground exploration according to § 18 Paragraph 1 of the Site Selection Act,
    3.  The establishment, operation, and decommissioning of federal facilities according to § 9a Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 of the Atomic Energy Act, and
    4.  The supervisory authority responsible for the Asse II shaft facility [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 187 Zuständigkeit der Physikalisch-Technischen Bundesanstalt
*   The PTB is responsible for:
    1.  The construction approval of interfering radiation sources according to § 45 Paragraph 1 Number 1 and the construction approval according to § 45 Paragraph 1 Number 2 to 6 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    2.  Carrying out quality assurance measures for external exposure measuring sites according to the ordinance under § 169 Paragraph 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    3.  Providing radioactivity standards for comparative measurements according to the ordinance under § 81 Sentence 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   The federal ministry for the environment, nature conservation and nuclear safety has the legal and technical supervision over the PTB for the tasks under this law.

## § 188 Zuständigkeiten für grenzüberschreitende Verbringungen und deren Überwachung
*   The Federal Office for Economic Affairs and Export Control (BAFA) is responsible for issuing a permit for the cross-border transport of consumer goods, as well as their withdrawal and revocation. The same applies insofar as the ordinances under § 24 Sentence 1 Number 7 and § 30 require permits and approvals, and the examination of notifications or registrations for cross-border transports [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   Customs authorities assist in monitoring cross-border transports of radioactive substances, consumer goods, or products under § 39 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 1 to 10, to which radioactive substances have been added or which have been activated, as well as residues [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md]. Customs authorities can:
    1.  Stop shipments of cross-border transported goods containing radioactive substances, residues, or consumer goods or products listed in Sentence 1, as well as their means of transport, containers, loading materials, and packaging materials for monitoring [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    2.  Inform the competent authorities of a suspected violation of prohibitions and restrictions under this law or the ordinances resulting from § 24 Sentence 1 Number 7 and § 30, which arises during the performance of their tasks, based on factual grounds [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    3.  In cases of Number 2, order that shipments according to Number 1 be presented to the competent authorities at the cost and risk of the disposer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 189 Zuständigkeit des Luftfahrt-Bundesamtes

## § 190 Zuständigkeit des Eisenbahn-Bundesamtes
*   The jurisdiction of the Federal Aviation Office for the supervision and approval of the transport of other radioactive substances applies analogously to the Federal Railway Office. The jurisdiction for the permit for the transport of large sources is determined according to § 186 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 191 Geschäftsbereich des Bundesministeriums der Verteidigung
*   (1) Deviating from § 189, the Federal Ministry of Defence is responsible for the tasks under § 189 Number 1 and 3 regarding the operation of aircraft operated within the scope of the Federal Ministry of Defence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   (2) For the scope of the Federal Ministry of Defence, the competencies designated in § 184 are exercised by this ministry or the designated departments. In case of § 184 Paragraph 2, this is done in consultation with the Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 192 Zuständigkeiten von Verwaltungsbehörden des Bundes bei Aufgaben des Notfallschutzes und der Überwachung der Umweltradioaktivität
*   (1) The BfS is responsible for the procurement and provision of protective agents according to § 104, unless another competence is established by law or on the basis of a law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   (2) The Federal Government is authorized by ordinance without the consent of the Bundesrat to determine which federal authorities, federal bodies, or other bodies shall perform the tasks of the federal government mentioned in §§ 104, 105, 106 Paragraph 2 Number 5, §§ 113 to 116, 120 Paragraph 1 and 2 Sentence 2, and § 161 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 193 Informationsübermittlung
*   The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety can transmit the following information, contained in radiation protection permits issued by the authorities competent according to §§ 184 to 191, to the federal authorities responsible for foreign trade for the performance of their tasks regarding permits or the monitoring of foreign trade:
    1.  Holder of the permit,
    2.  Legal basis of the permit,
    3.  The essential content of the permit.
*   If this information is insufficient in an individual case, further information from the radiation protection permit can be transmitted.
*   Recipients may only process the transmitted information for the purpose for which it was transmitted, unless otherwise legally determined [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 193a Ausstattung der zuständigen Behörden
*   The competent authorities have the necessary financial resources and personnel staffing to perform their legal tasks [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 197 Genehmigungsbedürftige Tätigkeiten (§ 12)
*   (1) A permit for the operation of facilities generating ionizing radiation, issued before December 31, 2018, remains a permit according to § 12 Paragraph 1 Number 1 with all ancillary provisions. This applies to permits related to the application to humans for treatment with ionizing radiation, for which an individual irradiation plan is based, if it is demonstrated to the competent authority by December 31, 2020, that the requirements of § 14 Paragraph 1 Number 2 Letter a, Number 3 Letter a, and Number 4 are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   (2) A permit for the handling of other radioactive substances, issued before December 31, 2018, remains a permit according to § 12 Paragraph 1 Number 3 with all ancillary provisions. This applies to permits:
    1.  for the handling of high-activity radioactive sources only, if it is demonstrated by December 31, 2020, that the prerequisite of § 13 Paragraph 4 is met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    2.  in connection with the application to humans for treatment with radioactive substances and ionizing radiation, for which an individual irradiation plan is based, if it is demonstrated by December 31, 2020, that the requirements of § 14 Paragraph 1 Number 2 Letter a, Number 3 Letter a, and Number 4 are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
    3.  in connection with the application to humans for standardized treatment with radioactive substances and for investigation with radioactive substances, which may be associated with a significant exposure of the investigated person, if it is demonstrated by December 31, 2022, that the requirements of § 14 Paragraph 1 Number 2 Letter b, Number 3 Letter b, and Number 4 are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   The competent authority may require the owner of a permit according to Sentence 1 to provide security performance within two years after the entry into force of this law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 198 Genehmigungsbedürftiger Betrieb von Röntgeneinrichtungen und Störstrahlern (§ 12)
*   (1) A permit for the operation of X-ray facilities, with the exception of the X-ray facilities mentioned in Paragraphs 2 and 3, issued before December 31, 2018, remains a permit according to § 12 Paragraph 1 Number 4 with all ancillary provisions. For permits related to the application to humans for standardized treatment with ionizing radiation and for investigation with ionizing radiation, which may be associated with a significant exposure of the investigated person, it must be demonstrated to the competent authority by December 31, 2022, that the requirements of § 14 Paragraph 1 Number 2 Letter b, Number 3 Letter b, and Number 4 are met. For unlimited permits for tele-radiology, it must be demonstrated to the competent authority by December 31, 2022, that the requirement of § 14 Paragraph 2 Number 4 and, where relevant, the requirements mentioned in Number 2 are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   (2) A permit for the operation of X-ray facilities for tele-radiology beyond the night, weekend, and public holiday service, issued before December 31, 2018, according to § 3 Paragraph 1 of the X-ray Ordinance in the version applicable until December 31, 2018, and limited according to § 3 Paragraph 4 Sentence 4 of the X-ray Ordinance, remains valid until the expiration of the period specified in the permit with all ancillary provisions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   (3) A permit for the operation of X-ray facilities for investigating humans in the context of voluntary X-ray screening examinations, issued before December 31, 2018, according to § 3 Paragraph 1 of the X-ray Ordinance in the version applicable until December 31, 2018, and limited according to § 3 Paragraph 4a Sentence 2 of the X-ray Ordinance, remains valid until the expiration of the period specified in the permit with all ancillary provisions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   (4) A permit for the operation of interfering radiation sources issued before December 31, 2018, remains a permit according to § 12 Paragraph 1 Number 5 with all ancillary provisions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 199 Anzeigebedürftiger Betrieb von Anlagen (§ 17)
*   A notification of the operation of a facility generating ionizing radiation, made before December 31, 2018, remains a notification according to § 17 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].

## § 200 Anzeigebedürftiger Betrieb von Röntgeneinrichtungen und Störstrahlern (§ 19)
*   (1) A notification of the operation of an X-ray facility, made before December 31, 2018, remains a notification according to § 19 Paragraph 1 Number 1. For notifications related to the application to humans for investigation with X-ray radiation, which may be associated with a significant exposure of the investigated person, the respective requirements according to § 19 Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 Number 6 in conjunction with § 14 Paragraph 1 Number 2 Letter b and Number 4 must be demonstrated to the competent authority by December 31, 2022 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
*   (2) A notification of the operation of a basic, high, or full protection device or a school X-ray facility, made before December 31, 2018, remains a notification according to § 19 Paragraph 1 Number 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 11/13].md].
