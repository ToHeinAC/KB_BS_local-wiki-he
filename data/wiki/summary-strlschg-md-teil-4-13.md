This document provides excerpts from the *Gesetz zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), detailing various regulatory aspects concerning the use, addition, and handling of radioactive substances and ionizing radiation.

## Ethikkommission und Forschungsvorhaben
The Ethics Committee (Ethikkommission) is responsible for advising on research projects from the applicant or the reporting party, considering ethical and legal aspects.
*   **Procedure:** The committee must advise orally with at least five members and issue a written statement within 60 calendar days of receiving the required documents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Multi-Center Studies:** For multi-center studies, the statement from one Ethics Committee is sufficient [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Combined Review:** If a research project is reviewed by an Ethics Committee under both medicinal product law or medical device law, and under the StrlSchG, the statement must contain the evaluation according to the medicinal product law or medical device law, as well as the radiation protection law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Evaluation Criteria:** The Ethics Committee evaluates whether the research project is ethically justifiable by assessing five points:
    1.  Suitability for scientific knowledge gain according to the state of science [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    2.  Suitability for answering the scientific question, including the number of persons involved [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    3.  Whether the risk to the individual is justifiable in relation to the potential benefit for society [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    4.  Whether inclusion is justifiable if a particularly vulnerable group of people is involved [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    5.  Whether the written information provided to the person, their legal representative, or authorized representative is sufficient regarding benefits and risks, thus enabling informed consent [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Legal Remedies:** Legal remedies against statements from the Ethics Committee can only be asserted simultaneously with the legal remedies permissible against the substantive decision [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 37 Verordnungsermächtigung (Regulatory Authorization)
The Federal Government is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to determine the special requirements to be observed when applying radioactive substances or ionizing radiation for the purpose of medical research, ensuring proper execution of the research project and protection of the involved persons [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The ordinance can specifically regulate:
1.  Duty to inform and requirements for consent [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Prohibitions and restrictions on application to individual groups of people [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Medical or dental examinations of involved persons before the start of the application [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  The authority of the competent authority to order medical or dental examinations if approved or reported dose values are exceeded [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
5.  Limits and measures for compliance with limits [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
6.  Measures for restricting and monitoring the exposure of involved persons [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
7.  Recording and retention obligations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
8.  Notification and reporting obligations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The ordinance can also specify the provisions of the ordinance for which the radiation protection responsible person must ensure compliance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The fundamental right to physical integrity (Article 2 Paragraph 2 Sentence 1 of the Basic Law) is restricted according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 Number 3 and 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 38 Rechtfertigung von Tätigkeitsarten (Justification of Activities)
The Federal Office for Radiation Protection (Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz) must assess the justification of the activity type within twelve months of receiving an application forwarded by the competent authority according to § 41 Paragraph 5, § 43 Paragraph 2, or § 46 Paragraph 3, and publish a statement containing a determination of the justification of the activity type [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md]. This statement must anonymize business and trade secrets and personal data [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The Federal Government is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to regulate:
1.  Documents the applicant must submit to the Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Guidelines for the examination procedure according to Paragraph 1, including the participation of authorities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Evaluation criteria that the Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz must particularly consider in the procedure according to Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  The transmission of information by the competent authorities to the Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz regarding permits for consumer goods according to § 40 or § 42, and type approvals according to § 45 Paragraph 1 Numbers 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7, and how the Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz publishes a list of these permits or type approvals for which activity types have already been granted [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
5.  How the Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz publishes the statement on the justification of the activity type [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
6.  How the Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz forwards the statement to the competent authorities of other member states of the European Atomic Community and third countries [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 39 Unzulässiger Zusatz radioaktiver Stoffe und unzulässige Aktivierung (Prohibited Additions)
The addition of radioactive substances is prohibited in the manufacture of the following products [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md]:
1.  Toys according to § 2 Paragraph 6 Number 5 of the Food and Feed Code [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Jewelry [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Food, including drinking water and food additives, according to the Food and Feed Code [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  Feed according to the Food and Feed Code [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
5.  Products according to § 2 Number 1 of the Tobacco Products Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
6.  Tattooing agents, including comparable substances and mixtures intended to be introduced into or under the human skin and remain there, even temporarily [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
7.  Cosmetic products [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
8.  Gas-glühstrümpfe (gas-glow stockings), unless intended for illuminating public streets [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
9.  Lightning protection systems [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
10. Food staples according to § 2 Paragraph 6 Number 1 of the Food and Feed Code [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The cross-border transfer of products according to § 42 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, to which radioactive substances have been added, and the placing on the market of such products are also prohibited. Sentences 1 and 2 do not apply to the addition of radionuclides for which no free limits are set [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 and 2 apply analogously to the activation of such products if this leads to a specific activity in the product of more than 500 microbecquerel per gram, or if the free limits for the specific activity set in a regulation according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10 are exceeded in jewelry [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
Otherwise, the legal provisions for the products listed in Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Numbers 1 to 10 remain unaffected [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 40 Genehmigungsbedürftiger Zusatz radioaktiver Stoffe und genehmigungsbedürftige Aktivierung (Permit-Required Additions)
A permit is required when radioactive substances are added during the manufacture of:
*   Consumer goods, medicinal products (except radioactive medicinal products), pesticides, or fertilizers, if these goods are to be acquired or handed over within the scope of this law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   The permit requirement applies analogously to the activation of the mentioned products. § 39 remains unaffected [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The permit does not replace a permit according to § 12 Paragraph 1 Number 1 or 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
A permit according to Paragraph 1 is not required for the addition of:
1.  Noble gases obtained from the air, if the isotope ratio in the addition corresponds to that in the air [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Radionuclides for which no free limits are set according to the regulation according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The application for a permit must include the necessary documents for examination, especially those listed in Annex 2 Part B, and for the manufacture of consumer goods, those listed in Annex 2 Part F [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 41 Voraussetzungen für die Erteilung der Genehmigung (Permit Requirements)
The competent authority must grant the permit according to § 40 for the manufacture of consumer goods if:
1.  The activity of the added radioactive substances is as low as possible according to the state of the art [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  It is proven that:
    a) the free limits of the activity set in a regulation according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10 are not exceeded in the consumer good, OR
    b) for individual persons, an effective dose in the range of 10 microsievert in a calendar year can occur [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  A recall concept is outlined, stating that the consumer good can be returned free of charge to the applicant or a designated body after use, if:
    a) the specific activity of the added artificial radioactive substances in the consumer good exceeds the free limits of the specific activity set in a regulation according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10, OR
    b) the specific activity of the added natural radioactive substances in the consumer good exceeds 0.5 Becquerel per gram [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  The material containing the radioactive substances is safely covered or the radioactive substance is firmly embedded in the consumer good, and the ambient dose rate at a distance of 0.1 meters from the touchable surface does not exceed 1 microsievert per hour under normal use conditions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
5.  It is guaranteed that the consumer good is accompanied by information that:
    a) explains the radioactive addition,
    b) describes the intended use, and
    c) points out the recall obligation according to § 44 and the body obligated to recall, if the specific activity of the added artificial radioactive substances in the consumer good exceeds the free limits of the specific activity set in a regulation according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10, or the specific activity of the added natural radioactive substances in the consumer good exceeds 0.5 Becquerel per gram [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
6.  The addition concerns other radioactive substances according to § 3 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
7.  The conditions for a permit for handling according to § 13 Paragraph 1 to 3 are met when adding [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
8.  The use of the consumer good is not an unjustified activity type according to a regulation according to § 6 Paragraph 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
9.  The Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz has not determined in a statement according to § 38 Paragraph 1 that the intended use or storage of the consumer good constitutes an unjustified activity type [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
For consumer goods predominantly used in professional, non-domestic areas, the competent authority may allow deviations from Paragraph 1 Number 2 Letter a and Number 4, provided that the ten-fold the free limit set in a regulation according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10 is not exceeded in a single consumer good [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
For medicinal products, pesticides, or fertilizers, the competent authority must grant the permit if:
1.  The addition concerns other radioactive substances according to § 3 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  It is proven that the free limits of the activity or specific activity set in a regulation according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10 are not exceeded in the medicinal product, pesticide, or fertilizer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  The conditions of § 13 Paragraph 1 to 3 for a handling permit are met when adding [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
Paragraphs 1 to 3 apply analogously to the activation of the products mentioned in these paragraphs [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The competent authority forwards the permit application to the Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz if the intended use or storage of the consumer good, for which the addition of radioactive substances or its activation was applied for, constitutes a new activity type. The procedure according to § 38 must be applied; the competent authority suspends the permit procedure until its completion [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 42 Genehmigungsbedürftige grenzüberschreitende Verbringung von Konsumgütern (Cross-Border Transfer)
A permit is required for the cross-border transfer of consumer goods that have been added with radioactive substances or that have been activated:
1.  Into the scope of this law, OR
2.  From the scope of this law into a state that is not a member state of the European Union [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
Exemptions apply for:
1.  Transfer of goods in the course of travel, which are neither intended for trade nor commercial use [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Customs-supervised transit [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Consumer goods whose manufacture is permitted according to § 40 and for which it has been proven according to § 41 Paragraph 1 Number 2 Letter b that an effective dose of only 10 microsievert in the calendar year can occur for individual persons [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  Products into which consumer goods are built, if the manufacture of the consumer goods according to § 40 or their transfer according to Paragraph 1 is permitted [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
5.  Consumer goods added with:
    a) noble gases obtained from the air, if the isotope ratio in the addition corresponds to that in the air, OR
    b) radionuclides for which no free limits are set according to the regulation according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 10 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The application for a permit must include the necessary documents for examination, and for transfer into the scope of this law, especially the documents listed in Annex 2 Part F [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 43 Voraussetzungen für die Erteilung der grenzüberschreitenden Verbringung (Cross-Border Transfer Requirements)
The competent authority must grant the permit according to § 42 if the requirements for the permit for the cross-border transfer of radioactive substances are met according to the regulation according to § 30. If the transfer is into the scope of this law, the requirements of § 41 Paragraph 1 Numbers 1 to 6, 8, and 9 must additionally be met. § 41 Paragraph 2 and § 44 Sentence 1 apply analogously; the remover replaces the manufacturer in the sense of § 44 Sentence 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
If the intended use or storage of the consumer good, to which radioactive substances have been added or which has been activated, and for which the cross-border transfer permit has been applied, constitutes a new activity type, the competent authority forwards an application for the transfer into the scope of this law to the Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz. The procedure according to § 38 must be applied; the competent authority suspends the permit procedure until its completion [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 44 Rückführung von Konsumgütern (Recall of Consumer Goods)
The manufacturer of a consumer good requiring a permit according to § 40 and having to draw up a recall concept according to § 41 Paragraph 1 Number 3 must ensure that the consumer good is recalled free of charge.
The end user must return the consumer good immediately after the end of use to the body specified in the information according to § 41 Paragraph 1 Number 5 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 45 Bauartzugelassene Vorrichtungen (Type-Approved Devices)
The type of the following devices can be approved upon application from the manufacturer or remover of the device (type-approved devices) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md]:
1.  The type of a device containing other radioactive substances according to § 3 Paragraph 1, or a scatterer, if radiation protection and safety of the device allow for use free of permit and notification according to the regulation according to § 49 Number 1 and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  The type of an X-ray source, if the radiation protection technical properties allow for the permit-free operation of an X-ray device with this X-ray source according to the regulation according to § 49 Number 1 and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  The type of an X-ray device as a basic protection device, if the high level of protection of the type, including possible openings in the protective housing for the introduction and removal of items, allows for the permit-free operation of the X-ray device according to the regulation according to § 49 Number 1 and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  The type of an X-ray device as a high protection device, if the high level of protection of the type allows for the permit-free operation of the X-ray device according to the regulation according to § 49 Number 1 and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
5.  The type of an X-ray device as a full protection device, if the particularly high level of protection of the type allows for the permit-free operation of the X-ray device without supervision by a person possessing the required expertise in radiation protection according to the regulation according to § 49 Number 1 and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
6.  The type of an X-ray device as a school X-ray device, if the radiation protection technical function of the type allows for the operation of the X-ray device in connection with teaching in schools according to the regulation according to § 49 Number 1 and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
7.  The type of a facility for generating ionizing radiation as a full protection facility, if the particularly high level of protection of the type allows for the permit-free operation of the facility without supervision by a person possessing the required expertise in radiation protection according to the regulation according to § 49 Number 1 and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
Paragraph 1 is not applicable to medical devices or accessories according to Regulation (EU) 2017/745. Paragraph 1 Number 1 is not applicable to devices containing high radioactive sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 46 Verfahren der Bauartzulassung (Type Approval Procedure)
1.  The application for type approval must include the necessary documents for examination, especially those listed in Annex 2 Part G [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  The applicant must provide the necessary type samples to the competent authority upon request. If the type of a device containing radioactive substances, the competent authority must involve the Federal Institute for Materials Research and Testing before its decision regarding the tightness, material selection, and construction of the devices or apparatus, as well as quality assurance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  If the intended use or intended operation of the devices, facilities, X-ray devices, or scatterers for which type approval is applied constitutes a new activity type, the competent authority forwards the application according to § 45 Paragraph 1 Numbers 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7 to the Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz. The procedure according to § 38 must be applied; the competent authority suspends the type approval procedure until its completion [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  The competent authority may only grant the type approval if:
    1.  The device meets the requirements set out in the regulation according to § 49 Number 1 and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    2.  No facts exist that raise concerns regarding:
        a) the reliability of the manufacturer or remover, or the person responsible for managing the manufacture, OR
        b) the technical experience required for the manufacture by the person responsible for managing the manufacture [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    3.  Overriding public interests do not stand in the way of the type approval [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    4.  The use or operation of the type-approved device does not constitute an unjustified activity type according to the regulation according to § 6 Paragraph 3, AND
    5.  The Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz has not determined in a statement according to § 38 Paragraph 1 that the intended use or operation of the type-approved device, the facility for generating ionizing radiation, the X-ray device, or the scatterer constitutes an unjustified activity type [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
5.  The type approval is limited to a maximum of ten years. It can be extended by a maximum of ten years upon application [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
6.  The competent authority shall decide on the application for approval within twelve months of receipt of the complete application documents. If the applicant provides the necessary type samples to the competent authority upon request, the competent authority shall decide on the application within twelve months of receipt of the complete application documents and the necessary type samples [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 47 Zulassungsschein (Certificate of Approval)
When the type of a device is approved according to § 45, the competent authority issues a Certificate of Approval. The certificate contains the following details:
1.  The essential features of the type-approved device for radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  The approved use of the type-approved device [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  The designation of the equipment serving radiation protection of the type-approved device [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  Substantive limitations, requirements, and time limits of the type approval [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
5.  The type mark and the details with which the type-approved device must be provided [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
6.  A reference to the obligations of the owner of the type-approved device according to the regulation according to § 49 Number 5 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
7.  For a device containing radioactive substances, requirements for the return of the device to the owner of the type approval or for the disposal of the device according to the regulation according to § 49 Number 4 and 5 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 48 Verwendung oder Betrieb bauartzugelassener Vorrichtungen (Use or Operation)
A type-approved device may:
1.  Be used without permit and notification for a type according to § 45 Paragraph 1 Number 1 according to the requirements set out in the regulation according to § 24 Sentence 1 Number 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Be operated for a type according to § 45 Paragraph 1 Numbers 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6 according to the requirements for the notification-required operation of X-ray devices according to § 19 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Be operated for a type according to § 45 Paragraph 1 Number 7 according to the requirements for the notification-required operation of facilities for generating ionizing radiation according to § 17 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
If the type-approved device was placed on the market before the expiration date of the type approval, it may also be used or operated after the expiration of this date. Sentence 2 does not apply if the competent authority has announced that the device may no longer be operated because sufficient protection against radiation damage is not guaranteed [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 49 Verordnungsermächtigung (Regulatory Authorization)
The Federal Government is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to:
1.  Determine the technical requirements for the type approval of devices that allow for use or operation free of permit and notification for the type-approved device [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Determine under which conditions the competent authority can allow deviations from the technical requirements according to Number 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Determine how and under what conditions:
    a) information about a type approval must be made public, and
    b) the determination that a type-approved device may no longer be operated must be made public [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  Determine the obligations of the owner of a type approval, including the obligation to recall the type-approved device containing radioactive substances after the end of use [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
5.  Determine the obligations of the owner of a type-approved device, including the obligation to return the type-approved device to the owner or dispose of it after the end of use [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 50 Anzeigebedürftiger Betrieb von Luftfahrzeugen (Air Travel)
A person intending to operate an aircraft registered in the German Airworthiness Register must notify the competent authority four weeks before the intended operation if the effective dose received by the flight crew during the flight, including the time spent on positioning, can exceed 1 millisievert in a calendar year due to cosmic radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md]. This applies analogously to the operation of aircraft registered in another country if the operator is of German nationality or a legal entity or partnership with its seat in the scope of this law, and the flight crew is employed under German labor law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The notification must include:
1.  Proof that the necessary number of radiation protection officers are appointed and granted the powers required for their tasks [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Proof that every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise in radiation protection, or, if a radiation protection officer is not necessary, the person obligated to notify, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal entities or other associations of persons, the authorized representative according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation possesses the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Proof that the other persons working during the activity possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding possible radiation hazards and the applicable protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  Designation of a recognized calculation program or proof that suitable measuring devices are used, which meet the requirements of the regulation issued pursuant to § 76 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 Number 11 for determining the body dose [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
In case of a significant change in the notified operation, Paragraphs 1 and 3 apply analogously [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The notification obligation also applies to aircraft operated within the operational area of the Federal Ministry of Defence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 51 Prüfung des angezeigten Betriebs von Luftfahrzeugen (Air Travel Review)
The competent authority reviews the documents within four weeks of receiving the notification. If the authority informs the notifier in writing before the deadline that all proofs according to § 50 Paragraph 3 have been provided, the notifier may commence the activity upon receipt of the notification [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The competent authority may prohibit the notified operation if:
1.  A required requirement is not or no longer met; this only applies after the deadline in Paragraph 1 if remedy is not provided in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Facts exist that raise concerns regarding the reliability of the person obligated to notify, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal entities or other associations of persons, the authorized representative according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation, or the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  There is a significant or repeated violation of the provisions of this law or the regulations issued on the basis of this law, or against the based orders and directives of the supervisory authorities, and remedy is not provided in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 52 Anzeigebedürftiger Betrieb von Raumfahrzeugen (Space Travel)
A person with its seat in the scope of this law intending to operate spacecraft and employing space-traveling personnel employed under German labor law must notify the competent authority two months before the intended commencement of the activity if the effective dose received by the space-traveling personnel by cosmic radiation during the operation of the spacecraft can exceed 1 millisievert in a calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The notification must include:
1.  Proof that the necessary number of radiation protection officers are appointed and granted the powers required for their tasks [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Proof that every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise in radiation protection, or, if no radiation protection officer is necessary, the person obligated to notify, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal entities or other associations of persons, the authorized representative according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation possesses the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Proof that the other persons working during the activity possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding possible radiation hazards and the applicable protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  Plausible depiction of the intended procedure for determining the body dose according to the requirements of the regulation issued pursuant to § 76 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 Number 11 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
If it is expected that the exposure of the space-traveling personnel exceeds the dose limit according to § 78 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, a separate notification of the increased exposure is required no later than two months before the deployment of the space-traveling personnel. In this case, the limits according to §§ 77 and 78 for professional exposure of space-traveling personnel by cosmic radiation do not apply [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The separate notification must include:
1.  Depiction that the increased exposure is justified [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Proof that the increased exposure has been discussed with the space-traveling personnel to be employed and the authorized physician [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Proof that the space-traveling personnel to be employed have been instructed on the expected doses, the risks associated with the increased exposure, and the precautionary measures to be taken [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  Consent of the space-traveling personnel to the increased exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 53 Prüfung des angezeigten Betriebs von Raumfahrzeugen (Space Travel Review)
The competent authority reviews the documents within two months of receiving the notification. If the authority informs the notifier before the deadline that all proofs according to § 52 Paragraph 2 or 4 have been provided, the notifier may commence the activity upon receipt of the notification [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
In the case of a notification according to § 52 Paragraph 1, the competent authority may prohibit the deployment of personnel if:
1.  A requirement according to § 52 Paragraph 2 is not or no longer met; this only applies after the deadline in Paragraph 1 if remedy is not provided in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Facts exist that raise concerns regarding the reliability of the person obligated to notify, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal entities or other associations of persons, the authorized representative according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation, or the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  There is a significant or repeated violation of the provisions of this law or the regulations issued on the basis of this law, or against the based orders and directives of the supervisory authorities, and remedy is not provided in a reasonable time [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
In the case of a separate notification according to § 52 Paragraph 3 Sentence 1, the competent authority may additionally prohibit the deployment of space-traveling personnel if a requirement according to § 52 Paragraph 4 is not met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 54 Beendigung der angezeigten Tätigkeit (Termination of Activity)
Whoever terminates or changes an activity notified according to § 50 Paragraph 1 or § 52 Paragraph 1, such that the effective dose received by the flight or space-traveling personnel by cosmic radiation no longer exceeds 1 millisievert in a calendar year, must notify the competent authority immediately [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 55 Abschätzung der Exposition (Exposure Estimation)
1.  Whoever carries out or causes to be carried out an activity in their workplace that can be assigned to one of the fields of activity listed in Annex 3, must perform an estimation of the body dose at the workplace before the start of the activity. The estimation must be repeated immediately if the workplace is changed such that a higher exposure can occur [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  If there are indications that exposures occur during an activity according to § 4 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 10, which cannot be assigned to one of the fields of activity listed in Annex 3, that correspond to those in Annex 3, the competent authority may order that an estimation according to Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 is carried out immediately. If the workplace is changed such that a higher exposure can occur, the competent authority may order that the estimation is repeated immediately [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  The person obligated to estimate must record the results of the estimation immediately. They must keep the records until the end of the activity or until a new estimation is available and present them to the competent authority upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## § 56 Anzeige (Notification)
1.  If the estimation shows that the body dose can exceed one of the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, the person obligated to estimate must notify the competent authority in writing. The notification based on an estimation according to § 55 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 must be made at least four weeks before the intended commencement of the activity; after the expiration of this period, the notifier may commence the activity, unless the competent authority has suspended or prohibited the procedure according to § 57 Paragraph 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md]. The notification based on an estimation according to § 55 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 or § 55 Paragraph 2 must be made immediately after the estimation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
The notification must include:
1.  An expert report from a state-appointed expert according to § 172, which describes:
    a) the notified activity and the intended radiation protection measures,
    b) the possible body dose of the professionally exposed persons, and
    c) proof that the equipment and measures required to maintain the protection regulations are available and implemented during the activity [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
2.  Proof that the necessary number of radiation protection officers are appointed and granted the powers required for their tasks [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
3.  Proof that every radiation protection officer possesses the required expertise in radiation protection, or, if a radiation protection officer is not necessary, the notifier, their legal representative, or, in the case of legal entities or other associations of persons, the authorized representative according to law, articles of association, or articles of incorporation possesses the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
4.  Proof that the other persons working during the activity possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding possible radiation hazards and the applicable protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
If the notification is based on an estimation according to § 55 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 or § 55 Paragraph 2, the competent authority may determine a period for the later submission of all or individual documents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
