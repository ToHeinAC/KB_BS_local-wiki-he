# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 4/13)

This section of the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) details various regulatory aspects concerning research, consumer goods, facility approvals, and occupational exposure monitoring.

## Ethics Committee Function (§ 37)
The task of the Ethics Committee is to advise orally on the research project from ethical and legal perspectives, upon request of the applicant or the reporting party, with a minimum of five members. A written statement must be issued within 60 calendar days of receipt of the required documents. For multi-center studies, the statement from one Ethics Committee is sufficient. If the research project is reviewed by an Ethics Committee under both pharmaceutical law or medical device law and this Act, the statement must contain both the pharmaceutical or medical device law assessment and the radiation protection law assessment.

The Ethics Committee reviews and evaluates whether the research project is ethically justifiable. It issues a statement on whether:
1. The research project is suitable for achieving scientific knowledge according to the state of science,
2. The research project, including the number of persons included, is suitable for answering the scientific question,
3. The risk for the individual in relation to the potential benefit for society is justifiable,
4. The inclusion is justifiable if a particularly vulnerable group of persons is included in the research project, and
5. The written information provided to the person included in the research project, their legal representative, or the authorized representative sufficiently informs about the benefits and risks, thereby enabling informed consent.

## Consumer Goods and Additives (§ 38 - § 49)
### Unlawful Additives and Activation (§ 39)
The addition of radioactive substances is unlawful in the manufacture of the following products:
1. Toys according to § 2 Absatz 6 Nummer 5 des Lebensmittel- und Futtermittelgesetzbuchs,
2. Jewelry,
3. Food, including drinking water and food additives, according to the Food and Feed Code,
4. Feed according to the Food and Feed Code,
5. Products according to § 2 Nummer 1 des Tabakerzeugnisgesetzes,
6. Tatuaging agents, including comparable substances and mixtures intended to influence the appearance in or under the human skin and to remain there, even temporarily,
7. Cosmetic products,
8. Gas-glühstrümpfe, unless intended for illuminating public streets,
9. Lightning protection systems, and
10. Food staples according to § 2 Absatz 6 Nummer 1 des Lebensmittel- und Futtermittelgesetzbuchs.

The cross-border removal of products according to § 42 Absatz 1 Satz 1, to which radioactive substances have been added, as well as the placing on the market of such products, are also unlawful. Sections 1 and 2 do not apply to the addition of radionuclides for which no release limits are set.

### Authorized Additives and Activation (§ 40)
A permit is required when radioactive substances are added during the manufacture of consumer goods, medicinal products (with the exception of radioactive medicinal products according to § 4 Absatz 8 des Arzneimittelgesetzes), pesticides, or substances according to § 2 Satz 1 Nummer 1 bis 8 des Düngegesetzes, which are to be acquired or passed on within the scope of this Act. This applies analogously to the activation of the mentioned products. § 39 remains unaffected.

### Conditions for Permit Issuance (§ 41)
The competent authority must issue the permit for the manufacture of consumer goods if:
1. The activity of the added radioactive substances is as low as possible according to the state of the art,
2. It is proven that:
    a) in the consumer good the release limits of the activity set in a regulation according to § 24 Satz 1 Nummer 10 are not exceeded, or
    b) for individual persons in the population, an effective dose in the range of 10 microsieverts in a calendar year can occur,
3. A withdrawal concept is laid out, ensuring that the consumer good can be returned free of charge to the applicant or a designated body after use, if:
    a) the specific activity of the added artificial radioactive substances in the consumer good exceeds the release limits of the specific activity set in a regulation according to § 24 Satz 1 Nummer 10, or
    b) the specific activity of the added natural radioactive substances in the consumer good exceeds 0.5 Becquerel per gram,
4. The material containing the radioactive substances is protected against contact or the radioactive substance is firmly embedded in the consumer good, and the ambient dose rate at a distance of 0.1 meters from the touchable surface of the consumer good does not exceed 1 microsievert per hour under normal use conditions,
5. It is guaranteed that the consumer good is accompanied by information that:
    a) explains the radioactive addition,
    b) describes the intended use, and
    c) points out the obligation for return according to § 44 and the body obligated to take it back, if the specific activity of the added artificial radioactive substances in the consumer good exceeds the release limits of the specific activity set in a regulation according to § 24 Satz 1 Nummer 10 or the specific activity of the added natural radioactive substances in the consumer good exceeds 0.5 Becquerel per gram,
6. The addition concerns other radioactive substances according to § 3 Absatz 1,
7. The conditions for a permit for handling according to § 13 Absatz 1 bis 3 are met when adding,
8. the use of the consumer good is not an unjustified activity type according to a regulation according to § 6 Absatz 3, and
9. the Federal Office for Radiation Protection has not determined in a statement according to § 38 Absatz 1 that the intended use or storage of the consumer good constitutes an unjustified activity type.

## Cross-Border Movement (§ 42 - § 43)
### Permit-Required Cross-Border Movement (§ 42)
A permit is required for the cross-border removal of consumer goods to which radioactive substances have been added or which have been activated, either into the scope of this Act or from the scope of this Act into a state that is not a member state of the European Union.

### Conditions for Cross-Border Movement (§ 43)
The competent authority must issue the permit if the conditions for the permit of cross-border removal of radioactive substances according to the regulation according to § 30 are met. If the removal is into the scope of this Act, the conditions of § 41 Absatz 1 Nummer 1 bis 6, 8, and 9 must also be met. § 41 Absatz 2 and § 44 Satz 1 apply accordingly; the remover replaces the manufacturer according to § 44 Satz 1.

## Withdrawal of Consumer Goods (§ 44)
The manufacturer of a consumer good requiring a permit according to § 40 and having to draw up a withdrawal concept according to § 41 Absatz 1 Nummer 3 must ensure that the consumer good is taken back free of charge. The end user must return the consumer good immediately after the end of use to the body specified in the information according to § 41 Absatz 1 Nummer 5.

## Facility Type Approval (§ 45 - § 49)
### Approved Facilities (§ 45)
The type of the following facilities can be approved upon application from the manufacturer or remover of the facility (type-approved facilities):
1. the type of a facility containing other radioactive substances according to § 3 Absatz 1, or a scatterer, if radiation protection and safety of the facility allow for use or operation free from permit and notification according to the regulation according to § 49 Nummer 1 and 2,
2. the type of an X-ray generator, if the radiation protection technical properties allow for the permit-free operation of an X-ray facility with this X-ray generator according to the regulation according to § 49 Nummer 1 and 2,
3. the type of an X-ray facility as a basic protection device, if the high protection level of the type, including possible openings in the protective housing for the introduction and removal of objects, allows for the permit-free operation of the X-ray facility according to the regulation according to § 49 Nummer 1 and 2,
4. the type of an X-ray facility as a high protection device, if the high protection level of the type allows for the permit-free operation of the X-ray facility according to the regulation according to § 49 Nummer 1 and 2,
5. the type of an X-ray facility as a full protection device, if the particularly high protection level of the type allows for the permit-free operation of the X-ray facility without supervision by a person possessing the required expertise in radiation protection according to the regulation according to § 49 Nummer 1 and 2,
6. the type of an X-ray facility as a school X-ray facility, if the radiation protection technical function of the type allows for the operation of the X-ray facility in connection with teaching in schools according to the regulation according to § 49 Nummer 1 and 2,
7. the type of a facility for generating ionizing radiation as a full protection facility, if the particularly high protection level of the type allows for the permit-free operation of the facility without supervision by a person possessing the required expertise in radiation protection according to the regulation according to § 49 Nummer 1 and 2.

### Procedure for Type Approval (§ 46)
The applicant must attach the documents required for the examination, especially those listed in Annex 2 Part G. The competent authority must involve the Federal Institute for Materials Research and Testing before its decision on the type of a facility containing radioactive substances regarding the tightness, the choice of material, and the construction of the devices or facilities, as well as quality assurance.

## Air and Space Travel (§ 50 - § 54)
### Reporting for Air Vehicles (§ 50)
A person intending to operate an aircraft registered in the German Air Vehicle Register must notify the competent authority four weeks before the intended operation if the effective dose received by the flight crew during the flight, including the time spent on positioning, can exceed 1 millisievert in a calendar year due to cosmic radiation.

### Reporting for Space Vehicles (§ 52)
A person with its seat in the scope of this Act intending to operate space vehicles and employing space-faring personnel who are in an employment relationship under German law must notify the competent authority two months before the intended operation if the effective dose received by the space-faring personnel due to cosmic radiation during the operation of the space vehicle can exceed 1 millisievert in a calendar year.

## Natural Radioactivity Exposure Assessment (§ 55 - § 56)
### Assessment of Exposure (§ 55)
A person carrying out or having carried out an activity in their workplace that can be assigned to one of the fields of activity listed in Annex 3 must perform a workplace-specific assessment of the body dose before the start of the activity. The assessment must be repeated immediately if the workplace is changed such that a higher exposure can occur.

### Reporting (§ 56)
If the assessment shows that the body dose can exceed one of the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, the person obligated to assess must notify the competent authority in writing. The notification based on an assessment according to § 55 Absatz 1 Satz 1 must occur at least four weeks before the intended start of the activity; after the expiration of this period, the notifier may commence the activity unless the competent authority has suspended or prohibited the procedure according to § 57 Absatz 2.
