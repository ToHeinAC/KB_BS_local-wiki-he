This document details sections concerning specific operational procedures for high-risk environments, including air and space travel, and establishes detailed requirements for assessing and notifying planned exposures.

## Air Travel Operations (§ 50, § 51)
The law mandates notification for operating aircraft where the effective dose received by the flight crew due to cosmic radiation can exceed 1 millisievert (mSv) in a calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Notification Requirement:** Notification must occur four weeks before the intended operation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md]. This applies to aircraft registered in Germany, or to aircraft registered elsewhere if the operator is German or based in the jurisdiction, and the crew is employed under German labor law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Changes:** The notification requirement applies if the operation is significantly changed, or if the initial notification was not subject to the requirement [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Inspection (§ 51):** The competent authority inspects the submitted documents within four weeks of receipt. The operation may commence upon written notification that all required proofs have been provided [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md]. The operation may be prohibited if required proofs are missing or if there is a failure to remedy deficiencies in a timely manner [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Required Documentation:** Proof must be provided for:
    1.  The necessary number of radiation protection officers (Strahlenschutzbeauftragten) and their authorized powers [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    2.  The required expertise of each radiation protection officer, or the required expertise of the applicant/representative if no officer is necessary [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    3.  Proof that other persons involved possess the necessary knowledge and skills regarding potential radiation hazards and protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
    4.  Identification of an approved calculation program or suitable measuring devices for body dose determination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## Space Travel Operations (§ 52, § 53)
For operating spacecraft from within the jurisdiction, notification is required two months in advance if the effective dose received by the space crew can exceed 1 millisievert (mSv) in a calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Increased Exposure:** If the expected exposure exceeds the dose limit according to § 78 Absatz 1 Satz 1, a separate notification of increased exposure is required at least two months prior to the crew deployment. In this case, the limits set in §§ 77 and 78 for professional exposure by cosmic radiation do not apply [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Inspection (§ 53):** The competent authority inspects the documents within two months. The operation may commence upon written notification that all required proofs have been provided [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## Exposure Assessment and Notification (§ 55, § 56)
The law details procedures for estimating exposure from naturally occurring radioactivity in workplaces (§ 55).
*   **Estimation (§ 55):** Any activity performed in a workplace that can be classified under the fields listed in Anlage 3 must undergo an on-site estimation of the body dose before the activity begins. This estimation must be repeated if the workplace changes such that a higher exposure can occur [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
*   **Notification (§ 56):** If the estimation shows that the body dose can exceed the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, the activity must be reported in writing to the competent authority. This notification must occur at least four weeks before the intended activity [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].

## Termination of Activity (§ 54)
Any activity previously notified under § 50 or § 52 must be immediately reported to the competent authority if the effective dose received by the crew can no longer exceed 1 millisievert in a calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 4/13].md].
