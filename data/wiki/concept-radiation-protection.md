# Radiation Protection

Radiation Protection refers to the set of legal, technical, and organizational measures designed to prevent adverse health effects resulting from exposure to ionizing radiation, whether from medical, industrial, or environmental sources.

## Core Principles
The principles governing radiation protection, as outlined in the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG), mandate that:
1.  **Justification:** Any application of ionizing radiation or radioactive materials must provide a sufficient benefit, weighing the potential diagnostic or therapeutic benefit against the potential damage caused by the exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
2.  **Optimization (ALARA):** Exposure must be kept as low as reasonably achievable, taking into account the medical necessity and the best available scientific knowledge [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
3.  **Dose Limitation:** Exposure must not exceed legally defined dose limits for both professionals and the general public [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Scope of Protection
The scope of radiation protection covers several domains:
*   **Areas:** Establishing, demarcating, securing, and marking radiation protection areas [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Personnel:** Mandatory instruction for individuals before entering protected areas or handling radioactive materials [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Monitoring:** Requirements for technical and organizational monitoring, including the mandatory wearing of dosimeters for individuals present in protected areas [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Regulatory Oversight
The law establishes roles for various authorities, including the competent authority (zuständige Behörde) and authorized physicians (ermächtigte Ärzte), who are responsible for overseeing compliance, managing health records, and approving necessary deviations from established dose limits [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
