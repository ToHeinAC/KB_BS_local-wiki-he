# Sanierungsplan

A remediation plan (*Sanierungsplan*) is a mandatory document required for managing radioactive contaminated sites (*radioaktive Altlasten*) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Purpose and Requirement
The competent authority can compel a responsible party to submit a remediation plan when a coordinated approach is necessary due to the variety of required measures, or when the risks to individuals or the public are significant due to the nature or extent of the contamination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Required Contents
The remediation plan must specifically include:
1.  A presentation of the results of the conducted investigations regarding the type and extent of the radioactive contaminated site, along with a summary of the exposure assessment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
2.  Information about the previous and future use of the grounds to be remediated [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
3.  A presentation of the planned remediation measures, other measures to prevent or reduce exposure, and follow-up measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Authority Oversight
The competent authority can create or supplement the remediation plan itself, or have it created by an expert, if the responsible party fails to do so or if the situation is too complex [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md]. The authority can declare the plan, even with amendments or side provisions, as binding [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
