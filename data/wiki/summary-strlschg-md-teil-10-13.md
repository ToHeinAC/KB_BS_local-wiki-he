# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 10/13)

This section of the Radiation Protection Act details provisions concerning existing exposure situations, environmental monitoring, professional dose tracking, and general oversight procedures.

## § 156 Maßnahmen (Measures)
The competent authority can determine the type, scope, duration, and goal of remediation and other measures to prevent or reduce exposure based on the assessment of existing exposure situations.
* **Principles for Measures:** When determining measures, the following principles must be observed:
    1. Any unnecessary exposure or contamination of humans and the environment must be avoided [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    2. The reference values set in § 155 should be kept as low as possible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    3. Any exposure or contamination of humans and the environment must be kept as low as possible, even below the reference values [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
* The competent authority coordinates the measures specified in Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 157 Kosten; Ausgleichsanspruch (Costs; Compensation Claim)
The costs of measures ordered under § 154 Paragraph 3 and § 156 Paragraph 3 are borne by the obligated parties carrying out the measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 158 Information (Information)
The competent authority must:
1. Regularly inform the exposed and potentially exposed population about possible risks from the existing exposure situation and the available measures to reduce their exposure, and
2. Publish recommendations for individual behavior or measures at the local level and update these if necessary [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 159 Anmeldung; Anwendung der Bestimmungen zu geplanten Expositionssituationen; Verordnungsermächtigung (Registration; Application of Provisions for Planned Exposure Situations; Authorization)
The provisions of this section apply if:
1. The existing exposure situation is significant from a radiation protection perspective, especially if the reference value according to § 155 can be exceeded, or, if no reference value is set, if an effective dose of 1 millisievert in a calendar year can be exceeded, AND
2. One of the responsible parties for the exposure situation is simultaneously the cause of the existing exposure situation.
* **Obligations of the Responsible Party:** The responsible party must immediately register the existing exposure situation with the competent authority, attaching documents proving compliance with the duties outlined in Paragraph 3, Number 1 and 2, and the ordinance according to Paragraph 5 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
* The responsible party must ensure that:
    1. Any exposure or contamination of humans and the environment is kept as low as possible, considering all circumstances of the individual case [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    2. Dose limits are not exceeded for workers carrying out measures according to § 156 Paragraph 1, and body doses are determined according to § 166 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    3. The requirements of the ordinance issued according to Paragraph 5 are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 160 Verhältnis zu den Kapiteln 1 bis 4 (Relation to Chapters 1 to 4)
These provisions do not apply to existing exposure situations following an emergency, Radon in living areas and at the workplace, contaminated areas, or radioactivity in building products [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## Teil 5: Expositionssituationsübergreifende Vorschriften (Cross-Exposure Situation Provisions)

### Kapitel 1: Überwachung der Umweltradioaktivität (Monitoring of Environmental Radioactivity)
* **Federal Tasks (§ 161):** The Federal government is responsible for:
    1. Large-scale determination of radioactivity in: air, precipitation, federal waterways and the North and Baltic Seas outside federal waterways and in marine organisms, radioactivity on the ground surface, and gamma site dose rate [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    2. Developing and setting sampling, analysis, measurement, and calculation procedures for determining environmental radioactivity, as well as conducting comparison measurements and analyses [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    3. Compiling, documenting, and processing data on environmental radioactivity provided by the Federal government, the states, and bodies outside the scope of this law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    4. Creating dispersion forecasts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    5. Developing and operating decision support systems [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    6. Assessing data on environmental radioactivity determined by the Federal government or the states on its behalf, and providing data and documents for the states and informing the states about the results of the data assessment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
* **State Tasks (§ 162):** The states determine radioactivity, particularly in:
    1. Food, feed, and consumer goods, if these serve as indicators for environmental radioactivity [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    2. Medicines and their starting materials [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    3. Drinking water, groundwater, and surface waters outside federal waterways [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    4. Sewage, sludge, and waste [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    5. Soil and plants [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
* **Integrated Measurement and Information System (§ 163):** The Federal Office for Radiation Protection operates an integrated measurement and information system for monitoring environmental radioactivity, where data determined according to § 161 Paragraph 1 and § 162 Paragraph 1 are compiled [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

### Kapitel 2: Weitere Vorschriften (Further Provisions)

* **§ 166 Festlegungen zur Ermittlung der beruflichen Exposition (Determination of Professional Exposure):**
    1. Body doses of a person from professional exposure must be added if they are determined in more than one of the following areas:
        1. In professional activities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        2. In connection with Radon at the workplace [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        3. During remediation and other measures to prevent and reduce exposure at radioactive contaminated sites, as well as other activities related to radioactive contaminated sites [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        4. At registered existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    * The sum is decisive for proving that the applicable limit values have not been exceeded [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    2. Exposures outside the spatial scope of this law corresponding to Paragraph 1 must be taken into account when determining professional exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

* **§ 167 Aufzeichnungs-, Aufbewahrungs- und behördliche Mitteilungspflichten für die ermittelte Körperdosis bei beruflicher Exposition (Recording, Retention, and Official Notification Duties for Determined Body Dose in Professional Exposure):**
    1. The Radiation Protection Officer, the obligated party according to § 131 Paragraph 1 or § 145 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, and the responsible party according to § 115 Paragraph 2 or § 153 Paragraph 1 must immediately record for persons subject to professional exposure and for whom a measurement, determination, or estimation of the body dose was made:
        1. The results of these measurements, determinations, or estimations, and data serving as basis for this measurement, determination, or estimation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        2. Personal data (surname, birth name, first names, date and place of birth, gender, nationality) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        3. The personal identification number according to § 170 Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        4. For radiation pass holders, the continuous number of the radiation pass [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        5. Employment characteristics and exposure conditions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    2. The obligated parties must retain the records until the supervised person reaches the age of 75 or would have reached it, but at least 30 years after the end of the respective employment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    3. The obligated parties must present the records upon request of the competent authority or deposit them at a place designated by the authority. The reporting of determination results to a new employer upon changing employment status must occur if continued professional exposure is practiced [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    4. The obligated parties are obliged to immediately report to the competent authority:
        1. Exceedances of the body dose limit values [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        2. The body doses for particularly permitted exposures according to the ordinance of § 79 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 Number 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

* **§ 168 Übermittlung der Ergebnisse der Ermittlung der Körperdosis (Transmission of Determined Body Dose Results):**
    1. The Radiation Protection Officer, the obligated party according to § 131 Paragraph 1 or § 145 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, and the responsible party according to § 115 Paragraph 2 or § 153 Paragraph 1 must provide the data to the measurement facility (§ 169 Paragraph 1) for determining professional exposure. The competent authority must be provided with the data according to Sentence 1 and the determined body dose upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    2. If the parties mentioned in Paragraph 1 do not use a measurement facility according to § 169 Paragraph 1, they must provide the data according to § 170 Paragraph 2, including the determined body dose, to the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

* **§ 169 Bestimmung von Messstellen; Verordnungsermächtigung (Designation of Measurement Facilities; Authorization):**
    1. The competent authority designates measurement facilities for determining professional exposure for:
        1. External exposure during activities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        2. Internal exposure during activities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        3. Emergency personnel during an emergency exposure situation or another dangerous situation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        4. In connection with Radon at the workplace [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        5. In connection with measures at radioactive contaminated sites and other activities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        6. At registered existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    2. A measurement facility can only be designated if:
        1. It has sufficient personnel to perform its tasks, and this personnel, especially the head of the facility and other leading experts, possesses the required qualification, suitability, and experience [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        2. It has the required procedures for determining exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        3. It has the required spatial and technical equipment, especially the necessary measuring devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        4. It operates an appropriate quality management system [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        5. No facts exist suggesting doubts about the reliability of the head of the facility or other leading experts, and the facility has the required independence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    3. The facility must record the results of the determination of professional exposure and communicate them in writing to the respective person who commissioned the measurement (§ 168 Paragraph 1). The facility must retain the records for five years after the determination. It must immediately communicate these results, including the data according to § 168 Paragraph 1, to the competent authority responsible for the person according to Sentence 1, upon request or if it deems it necessary based on its findings [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

* **§ 170 Strahlenschutzregister; Verordnungsermächtigung (Radiation Protection Register; Authorization):**
    1. Data on professional exposures obtained under this law or an ordinance based on this law are recorded in a register maintained by the Federal Office for Radiation Protection for the purposes of monitoring dose limits, observing radiation protection principles, checking for a claim against a statutory accident insurance provider, and scientific research in the field of radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    2. The following data are entered into the Radiation Protection Register:
        1. The personal identification number according to Paragraph 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        2. The respective personal data [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        3. Employment characteristics and exposure conditions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        4. The operational number of the employing facility [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        5. Name and address of the Radiation Protection Officer, the obligated party according to § 131 Paragraph 1 and § 145 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, and the responsible party according to § 115 Paragraph 2 and § 153 Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        6. Information about a radiation pass registered according to an ordinance based on this law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        7. Information about the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
        8. The body dose determined according to this law or an ordinance based on this law resulting from professional exposure, the exposure conditions, and the findings of the competent authority regarding this body dose and the exposure conditions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
    3. The Federal Office for Radiation Protection assigns a personal identification number for each person for whom entries are made. This number is derived from the insurance number according to § 147 of the Sixth Book of the Social Code, which is assigned to the respective person, using non-reversible encryption. The insurance number must be deleted after derivation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
