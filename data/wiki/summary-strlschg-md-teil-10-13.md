This document provides the tenth part of the *Gesetz zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), detailing specific operational requirements, monitoring, record-keeping, and supervisory procedures.

## § 166 Festlegungen zur Ermittlung der beruflichen Exposition
The body doses from professional exposure must be added if they are to be determined in several of the following areas under this law or a related ordinance:
1. Activities as a professionally exposed person.
2. In connection with Radon at the workplace.
3. During remediation and other measures to prevent and reduce exposure from radioactive contamination sites and other activities related to radioactive contamination sites.
4. In case of reportable existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
The sum is decisive for proving that the applicable limit values have not been exceeded. Furthermore, exposures outside the spatial scope of this law corresponding to Paragraph 1 must be considered when determining professional exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 167 Aufzeichnungs-, Aufbewahrungs- und behördliche Mitteilungspflichten für die ermittelte Körperdosis bei beruflicher Exposition
The radiation protection responsible party, the obligated party under $\S 131$ Paragraph 1 or $\S 145$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, and the responsible party under $\S 115$ Paragraph 2 or $\S 153$ Paragraph 1 must immediately record for persons subject to professional exposure and for whom a measurement, determination, or estimation of the body dose was carried out:
1. The results of these measurements, determinations, or estimations, as well as data used for them.
2. Personal data: surname, birth name, first names, date and place of birth, gender, nationality.
3. The personal identification number according to $\S 170$ Paragraph 3 Sentence 1.
4. For radiation pass holders: the continuous number of the radiation pass.
5. Employment characteristics and exposure conditions.
Records must be kept until the supervised person reaches the age of 75 or would have reached it, but at least 30 years after the end of the respective employment. The obligated parties must submit the records upon request of the competent authority or deposit them at a designated location. The obligated parties must also communicate the determination results to the new employer upon change of employment relationship if professional exposure continues. They must hand over records no longer needed to the state authority responsible according to state law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 168 Übermittlung der Ergebnisse der Ermittlung der Körperdosis
The radiation protection responsible party, the obligated party under $\S 131$ Paragraph 1 or $\S 145$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, and the responsible party under $\S 115$ Paragraph 2 or $\S 153$ Paragraph 1 must provide the data to the measurement facility for determining professional exposure (if used) or provide the data, including the determined body dose, to the competent authority if no measurement facility is used [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 169 Bestimmung von Messstellen; Verordnungsermächtigung
The competent authority designates measurement facilities for determining professional exposure for:
1. External exposure during activities.
2. Internal exposure during activities.
3. Emergency personnel during an emergency exposure situation or other danger situation.
Additionally, facilities can be designated for:
* Radon at the workplace.
* In connection with measures at radioactive contamination sites.
* In case of other existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
A measurement facility must possess sufficient personnel, the required procedures, the necessary spatial and technical equipment, operate an appropriate quality management system, and maintain required independence. The facility must record the results and communicate them in writing to the person who commissioned the measurement. The facility must retain the records for five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 170 Strahlenschutzregister; Verordnungsermächtigung
Data on professional exposures collected under this law are recorded in a Radiation Protection Register (Strahlenschutzregister) for monitoring dose limits, observing radiation protection principles, and scientific research. The following data are entered:
1. The personal identification number according to Paragraph 3.
2. The respective personal data.
3. Employment characteristics and exposure conditions.
4. The operational number of the employing facility.
5. Name and address of the radiation protection responsible party, the obligated party under $\S 131$ Paragraph 1 and $\S 145$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, and the responsible party under $\S 115$ Paragraph 2 and $\S 153$ Paragraph 1.
6. Data on a radiation pass registered under a related ordinance.
7. Data on the competent authority.
8. The body dose determined due to professional exposure, the exposure conditions, and the findings of the competent authority regarding this body dose and the exposure conditions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
The Federal Office for Radiation Protection (Bundesamt für Strahlenschutz) assigns a personal identification number for unique assignment. This number is derived from the insurance number (Versicherungsnummer) using non-reversible encryption. Data are submitted by the measurement facilities, the competent authorities, or the responsible parties [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 171 Verordnungsermächtigung für Vorgaben in Bezug auf einen Strahlenpass
The Federal Government is authorized to establish regulations regarding a radiation pass, which can specify when a pass is required for monitoring dose limits, what data must be entered, the form of the pass, and who can make entries [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 172 Bestimmung von Sachverständigen; Verordnungsermächtigung
The competent authority designates experts for various activities, including:
1. Inspection of X-ray facilities, including the issuance of certificates, and inspection of X-ray facilities or scatter radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
2. Inspection of workplaces with exposure from naturally occurring radioactivity.
3. Inspection of facilities for generating ionizing radiation, of irradiation devices, and of devices for gamma radiography.
4. Leakage testing of enclosed radioactive substances and construction-approved devices containing radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
Designated experts must be independent of those involved in the manufacturing, distribution, or maintenance of the facilities. They must possess the required expertise in radiation protection [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 173 Verordnungsermächtigungen für Mitteilungspflichten bei Fund und Erlangung
The Federal Government is authorized to regulate the mandatory reporting to competent authorities regarding:
1. The finding, loss, and recovery of substances, if there is concern that their activity or specific activity exceeds the values set by ordinance.
2. The presence of water in a water supply or sewage system containing radionuclides whose activity concentration exceeds the set values or limits.
3. The suspicion or knowledge that an ownerless radiation source has been melted down or used in any other metallurgical way [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 174 Verordnungsermächtigung für behördliche Befugnisse bei kontaminiertem Metall
The Federal Government is authorized to regulate that contaminated metal may only be used, placed on the market, or disposed of according to the provisions of the competent authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 175 Dosis- und Messgrößen; Verordnungsermächtigung
(1) For determining the organ equivalent dose, both external and internal exposure must be considered, unless otherwise specified. For internal exposure, exposure occurring outside the reference period due to radionuclides ingested during the reference period must also be considered. This applies analogously to the effective dose [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
(2) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety is authorized to regulate:
1. Detailed requirements for determining the organ equivalent dose, including weighting factors for different types of radiation and radiation energies, and details of averaging over tissue or organ.
2. Detailed requirements for determining the effective dose, including the tissues or organs to be considered and the weighting factors, and setting out the determination of the effective dose for the unborn child.
3. How and for what period the dose from ingested radionuclides must be considered for internal exposure.
4. Which measuring quantities must be used for determining external exposure and how this determination must be carried out.
5. The data to be used as a basis for determining the body dose from field radiation or activity units.
6. Which units must be used for radiation protection quantities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 176 Haftung für durch ionisierende Strahlung verursachte Schäden
Liability for damages caused by ionizing radiation within the scope of this law and related ordinances is governed by §§ 25 to 40 of the Atomic Energy Act (Atomgesetz) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 177 Vorsorge für die Erfüllung gesetzlicher Schadensersatzverpflichtungen
Provision for the fulfillment of statutory compensation obligations within the scope of this law is governed by §§ 13 to 15 of the Atomic Energy Act and the Atomic Law Compensation Provision Ordinance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 180 Aufsichtsprogramm; Verordnungsermächtigung
(1) In the context of radiation protection supervision for planned exposure situations, the competent authority shall establish a supervision program that accounts for the possible extent and type of risks associated with the activities. The Federal Government is authorized to regulate the requirements for the design of the supervision program [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
(2) The competent authority records the results of every on-site inspection and transmits them to the radiation protection responsible party. In cases covered by Part 2 Chapter 2 Section 8 Subsection 2, the results must be transmitted to the obligated party. If the results concern an external employee, the radiation protection responsible party or the obligated party must also communicate these results to the employer of the external employee, except for trade and business secrets [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].

## § 181 Umweltverträglichkeitsprüfung
If an Environmental Impact Assessment (UVP) is required for projects needing a permit under the Environmental Impact Assessment Act, the UVP is an inseparable part of the procedures for granting the permit [StrlSchG.md [Teil 10/13].md].
