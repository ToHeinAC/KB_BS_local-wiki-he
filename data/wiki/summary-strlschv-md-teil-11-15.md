This document provides excerpts from the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV), which is the implementing ordinance detailing specific technical regulations for the protection against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation. The content primarily consists of extensive tables detailing various radioactive isotopes, their physical properties, and decay characteristics.

## Isotope Data and Decay Parameters
The source material presents comprehensive data across numerous isotopes, including:
*   **Half-lives:** Values are provided in various units (days 'd', hours 'h', years 'a', seconds 's', minutes 'm'). Examples include ${}^{135}\text{Cs}$ with a half-life of $2.3 \times 10^6$ years [StrlSchV.md [Teil 11/15].md], and ${}^{192}\text{Mo}$ with a half-life of $11.8$ days [StrlSchV.md [Teil 11/15].md].
*   **Activity/Concentration:** Several columns detail measured values, often using scientific notation (e.g., $1 \text{ E}+9$, $1 \text{ E}-1$).
*   **Specific Isotopes:** Data is provided for isotopes such as ${}^{90}\text{Sr}$ (Strontium-90), ${}^{137}\text{Cs}$ (Cesium-137), ${}^{60}\text{Co}$ (Cobalt-60), and various isotopes of Iodine ($\text{I}$) and Cesium ($\text{Cs}$) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 11/15].md].

## Structure and Scope
The tables are highly structured, listing isotopes alphabetically and providing multiple quantitative measurements across various parameters. The data appears to be used for technical assessments required under the broader radiation protection framework [StrlSchV.md [Teil 11/15].md].
