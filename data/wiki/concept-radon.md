# Radon

Radon refers to the radioactive gas Radon-222, which poses a long-term radiation risk, particularly in indoor e[nvironments] [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Elevated Exposure Risks
The law specifies certain work areas where elevated exposure due to Radon is possible, including:
*   Workplaces in underground mines, shafts, and caves, including visitor mines [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Workplaces in radon spas and radon adits [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Workplaces in facilities for water extraction, treatment, and distribution [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

## Associated Mineral Sources
Radiologically relevant mineral primary raw materials that can contribute to Radon risk include:
*   Acidic magmatic rocks as well as resulting metamorphic and sedimentary rocks [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Sedimentary rock with high organic content such as oil, copper, and alunite shale [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Travertine [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
