# Wertausgleich

Value equalization (*Wertausgleich*) is a financial mechanism related to remediation efforts at radioactive contaminated sites [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Principle
If public funds are used for measures fulfilling the duties under § 139 or § 143, and the property value is increased by these measures, but the owner has not or not fully covered the costs, the owner must make a value equalization payment to the public cost carrier, amounting to the increase in value caused by the measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Calculation and Timing
* **Calculation:** The increase in property value is determined by the difference between the value the property would have if the measures had not been carried out (initial value) and the market value after the investigation and remediation measures are completed (final value) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
* **Due Date:** The equalization amount becomes due when the remediation measures are completed and the amount has been set by the competent authority. The obligation expires if the amount is not set by the end of the fourth year after the completion of the measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Exemptions
The obligation for value equalization does not arise if a release from responsibility or cost bearing for radioactive contaminated sites exists under the Federal Environmental Law of June 29, 1990 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
