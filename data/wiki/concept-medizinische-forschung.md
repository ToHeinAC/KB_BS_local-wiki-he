# Medical Research (Radiation Protection)

This concept outlines the stringent regulatory framework governing the use of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation on humans for medical research, as detailed in the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]. The requirements differentiate between applications needing a full permit and those requiring only notification.

## Authorization Requirements (Permit-Required)
A full permit is required if the application is not covered by the notification rules (§ 32) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]. Key criteria for obtaining this permit include:
* **Medical Justification:** The radiation-related risks must be medically justified, considering the expected significance of the results for developing medical examination methods or treatment procedures, potentially factoring in the medical benefit for the person [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Necessity:** The substances or application types must correspond to the research purpose and cannot be replaced by other methods resulting in no or lower exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Minimization:** The exposure and activity must be demonstrably irreducible without jeopardizing the research purpose [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Personnel:** The number of participants must be limited to the necessary measure for the research goal [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Oversight:** A favorable statement from an Ethics Committee (§ 36) must be provided [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Expertise:** Applications must be conducted by a physician with required expertise in radiation protection and at least two years of experience in applying radioactive substances or ionizing radiation on humans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Security:** Necessary security for statutory compensation obligations must be established [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Prerequisites:** A permit under § 12 Absatz 1 Nummer 1 bis 4 for application on humans, or the operation of an X-ray device notified under § 19 Absatz 1 for application on humans, must be available [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

## Notification Requirements (Notification-Required)
Notification is required if the research project involves testing safety or efficacy for treating adult, sick people, provided that the application itself is not the subject of the research project [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md]. The notification must confirm:
* The application aligns with recognized standard examination procedures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* The purpose justifies the type and frequency of application [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* Only adult persons suffering from a tested disease are included [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* Prerequisites regarding permits (§ 12) or X-ray device operation (§ 19) are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

## Procedural Aspects
* **Review Timeline:** The authority must confirm completeness within 14 calendar days. The substantive review must be completed within 28 calendar days of confirmation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Prohibition:** The authority can prohibit the application if required criteria are not met, or if deficiencies persist after an appropriate period [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].
* **Security:** Proof of necessary security must be provided, requiring insurance coverage of at least 500,000 Euro per person for death or permanent disability [StrlSchG.md [Teil 3/13].md].

## Transitional Provisions (StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13])
The Act contains specific provisions ensuring the continuity of applications and authorizations for medical research:
* **Permit Continuation (§ 205):** Applications for radioactive substances or ionizing radiation on humans for medical research that were permitted under previous versions of the Radiation Protection Ordinance or X-ray Ordinance remain valid as permits (§ 31) or notifications (§ 32) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
* **Procedure Continuation:** Applications initiated before December 31, 2018, must follow the procedures of the previous law until the specified deadlines [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
