# Cloud Applications

Cloud Applications refer to the delivery of enterprise software solutions via subscription models over the internet, representing a significant shift from traditional on-premise software licensing.

## Market Significance

The adoption of cloud applications is a central theme in modern enterprise IT spending, driven by the need for scalability, flexibility, and rapid feature updates [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].

*   **Growth Driver**: For SAP SE, cloud revenue is the largest and fastest-growing revenue stream. In 2025, Cloud Revenue reached €21.0B, exhibiting a 23% growth rate (or 26% at constant currency) [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].
*   **Cloud ERP Suite**: The Cloud ERP Suite specifically demonstrated robust growth, increasing by 28% (or 32% at constant currency) [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].
*   **Backlog**: The total cloud backlog for SAP is substantial, amounting to €77B, reflecting strong future demand [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].

## Technological Integration

The modern cloud application suite is increasingly defined by its integration with advanced technologies, most notably Artificial Intelligence (AI) [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].

*   **AI Integration**: AI Copilots, such as Joule, are being embedded directly into cloud functions across Finance, Supply Chain, and HR, making AI a core component of the application experience [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].
*   **Data Foundation**: The ability of the platform to unify data sources, such as through the SAP Business Data Cloud, is critical for enabling these advanced AI use cases [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].

## Competitive Context

The cloud applications market is highly competitive, featuring major players such as Microsoft (Azure + Dynamics 365) and Oracle (Fusion Cloud ERP) [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].

*   **SAP's Position**: SAP is noted for its rapid growth in this sector, growing significantly faster than competitors like Salesforce and Workday [SAP SE Investment Research Overview.md].
