# Notfall-Expositionssituationen

An emergency exposure situation (*Notfall-Expositionssituation*) refers to a radiation risk scenario arising from an unforeseen event [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Contamination Management
When contamination occurs in an emergency situation, the management follows specific legal provisions:
*   **Applicable Law:** The provisions for contaminated sites apply, utilizing reference values set out in § 93 Abs. 1 or § 93 Abs. 2 or 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
*   The law provides detailed provisions for the special emergency plan, covering the competencies of the Federation and the States, and the coordination of response measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

## Public Information and Response
In the event of an emergency, the affected population receives information and recommendations, which may include:
*   Information about the emergency, its origin, spread, and expected development [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   Behavior recommendations such as restrictions on consuming possibly contaminated foodstuffs and water, simple hygiene and decontamination rules, recommendations for staying indoors, and precautions for evacuation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
*   In the pre-warning phase, recommendations may include switching on relevant communication channels and preparatory advice for facilities with public tasks [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].

## Planning Scope
Emergency planning requires the coordination of information exchange, assistance, and coordination at the federal level, between the Federation and the States, and with international organizations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 13/13].md].
