The *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) is the German regulation titled *Verordnung zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung*. It serves as the implementing legislation for the protection against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, updating previous directives to align with the EU Directive 2013/59/Euratom [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].

## Legal Status and Scope
*   **Title:** Verordnung zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung (Strahlenschutzverordnung - StrlSchV) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
*   **Issuance Date:** 29.11.2018 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
*   **Legal Basis:** The StrlSchV implements the EU Directive 2013/59/Euratom and supersedes several older directives, including 89/618/Euratom, 90/641/Euratom, 96/29/Euratom, 97/43/Euratom, and 2003/122/Euratom [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
*   **Entry into Force:** The regulation entered into force on December 31, 2018 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].

## Key Definitions (Teil 1)
The regulation establishes precise definitions for technical and legal concepts:
*   **Ableitung (Emission):** Defined as the release of liquid, particulate, or gaseous radioactive substances via designated routes [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
*   **Äquivalentdosis (Equivalent Dose):** Calculated as the product of the energy dose in ICRU soft tissue and the quality factor Q of the ICRU (according to Anlage 18 Teil D). If multiple radiation types/energies are present, the total equivalent dose is the sum of individual contributions [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
*   **Dosisrichtwert (Dose Limit):** Defined as an effective dose or organ-equivalent dose that serves as the upper value for considering exposure during the planning and optimization of protective measures for persons in planned exposure situations [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
*   **Vorkommnis (Incident):** An event in a planned exposure situation that has led to, could lead to, or might lead to an unintended exposure. No incident exists if the event is irrelevant for radiation protection [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
*   **Störfall (Accident):** A sequence of events where the operation of the nuclear facility, the facility generating ionizing radiation, or the activity cannot be continued for safety reasons, and for which the facility must be shut down or for which precautionary measures must be taken [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].

## Regulatory Procedures
The StrlSchV details procedures for various exposure scenarios:

### Planned Exposure Situations (Teil 2)
*   **Justification of Activities (§ 2, § 3, § 4):** Activities must be justified. The procedure for checking justification based on § 7 or § 38 of the Radiation Protection Act requires submitting specific documentation (Anlage 2) and involves assessment by the Federal Office for Radiation Protection (BfS) regarding the suitability and fitness of the item, facility, or process [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
*   **Exemptions (§ 5 - § 11):** The regulation outlines specific cases where a permit or notification is not required, such as for certain handling of radioactive substances (§ 5) or the operation of certain facilities (§ 7, § 8, § 9) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].

### Emergency Exposure Situations (Teil 3)
*   The regulation mandates specific provisions for dosimetry and medical monitoring of emergency response personnel (*Einsatzkräfte*) during accidents or incidents (§ 150, § 151) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].

### Existing Exposure Situations (Teil 4)
*   **Radioactive Contaminated Sites (Altlasten):** Procedures are detailed for determining exposure, setting test values, and establishing principles for remediation plans (*Sanierungspläne*) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
*   **Waste Management:** Covers the handling of lost, found, or acquired contaminated metal (§ 167-170) and general principles for waste management [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].

### Cross-Cutting Provisions (Teil 5)
*   **Registers and Passes:** The law mandates the maintenance of the Radiation Protection Register (*Strahlenschutzregister*) (§ 173) and the use of the Radiation Pass (*Strahlenpass*) (§ 174) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 1/15].md].
