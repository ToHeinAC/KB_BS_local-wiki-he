Emergency planning, within the context of radiation protection, refers to the structured preparation and protocols established by federal and state authorities to manage the consequences of radiological accidents or incidents. This planning is codified within the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) and involves multiple levels of governmental coordination.

## Planning Frameworks
The law mandates several layers of planning documentation:
*   **General Federal Emergency Plans (Anlage 5):** These plans outline the general legal bases, tasks, and responsibilities of federal and state authorities involved in emergency response. They include:
    *   Information exchange and coordination procedures across federal, state, EU, and international levels [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   Reference scenarios and dose values for assessing exposure to the population and emergency personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   Optimized protection strategies, including criteria for triggering alarms and implementing protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Special and General Federal Emergency Plans (Anlage 4 & Anlage 6):** These documents compile specific recommendations and guidelines from expert commissions (such as the Radiation Protection Commission) regarding accident protection in the vicinity of nuclear facilities and general radiological incident management [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Key Planning Components
Emergency planning requires the establishment of specific technical and procedural guidelines:
*   **Dose Assessment:** Protocols for estimating radiation doses and comparing these estimates against established reference values [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Information Management:** Procedures for the radiological situation picture, including the exchange of information with the federal radiological situation center [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Public Guidance:** Guidelines for informing the public and providing behavioral recommendations during a radiological emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
