The document provides transitional provisions and detailed appendices from the German Radiation Protection Act (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), effective as of June 27, 2017. The sections primarily address the continuation of permits, authorizations, and procedures that were established under previous versions of the law, particularly those valid before December 31, 2018, or earlier dates.

## Transitional Provisions for Authorizations and Permits

The text details how various activities and authorizations granted prior to specific cut-off dates remain valid or transition to the new legal framework:

*   **Inspection/Testing:** An inspection, testing, maintenance, and repair of X-ray facilities or scatter sources conducted before December 31, 2018, is considered an inspection according to $\S 22$ Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Employment in Third-Party Facilities ($\S 202$):** A permit for employment in third-party facilities issued before December 31, 2018, remains valid as a permit according to $\S 25$ Paragraph 1 with all ancillary provisions until the date specified in the permit, and at the latest until December 31, 2023 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Notification of Employment ($\S 203$):** A notification of tasks related to operating a third-party X-ray facility or scatter source, made before December 31, 2018, remains valid as a notification according to $\S 26$ Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Transport of Radioactive Materials ($\S 204$):**
    *   A permit for transport issued before December 31, 2018, remains valid as a permit according to $\S 27$ Paragraph 1 with all ancillary provisions, provided that the specialized knowledge required by $\S 29$ Paragraph 1 Number 2 is proven to the competent authority by December 31, 2021 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   If a permit under $\S 4$ Paragraph 1 of the Atomic Energy Act, issued before December 31, 2018, extended to a required transport of radioactive materials, this extension remains valid as an extension to a required transport according to $\S 27$ Paragraph 1, provided the specialized knowledge required by $\S 29$ Paragraph 1 Number 2 is proven by December 31, 2021 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Medical Research ($\S 205$):**
    *   An application for the use of radioactive materials or ionizing radiation on humans for medical research, authorized under the versions of the Radiation Protection Ordinance or X-ray Ordinance valid until December 31, 2018, remains valid as a permit according to $\S 31$ [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   A similar application, authorized under the versions valid until December 31, 2018, remains valid as a notification according to $\S 32$ [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   Application procedures for medical research begun before December 31, 2018, are concluded according to the provisions valid before that date.
    *   Registrations of ethics committees under the versions valid until December 31, 2018, remain valid as registrations according to $\S 36$ Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Addition of Radioactive Materials and Activation ($\S 206$):**
    *   A permit for the addition of radioactive materials and activation issued before December 31, 2018, remains valid as a permit according to $\S 40$ Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   If a return concept ($\S 41$ Paragraph 1 Number 3) is required for a permit issued from December 31, 2018, that was not previously required, Paragraph 1 only applies if a return concept was created for consumer goods manufactured between December 31, 2019, and that date [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   The use, storage, and disposal of consumer goods that were exempt from permits before August 1, 2001, or under $\S 117$ Paragraph 6 Sentence 1 of the Radiation Protection Ordinance valid until December 31, 2018, still require no permit [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Cross-Border Transport ($\S 207$):** A permit for the cross-border transport of consumer goods issued before December 31, 2018, remains valid as a permit according to $\S 42$ with all ancillary provisions [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Type Approval ($\S 208$):**
    *   Type approvals for devices, facilities, and X-ray sources that were valid on December 31, 2018, remain valid until the expiration date stated in the approval certificate and can be extended as an approval according to $\S 45$ Paragraph 1 Number 7 upon application [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   Devices whose type approval expired before December 31, 2018, but were continued to operate according to the versions of the Radiation Protection Ordinance or X-ray Ordinance valid until December 31, 2018, may continue to operate accordingly [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   For devices containing radioactive materials with a type approval issued before August 1, 2001, the regulations of $\S 4$ Paragraph 1, 2 Sentence 2 and 5 in conjunction with Annex II Number 2 or 3 and Annex III Part B Number 4, $\S 29$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1, of the Radiation Protection Ordinance of June 30, 1989, remain applicable; after the expiration of this type approval, the regulation of $\S 23$ Paragraph 2 Sentence 3 of the Radiation Protection Ordinance of June 30, 1989, remains applicable [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   Devices whose type approval expired before August 1, 2001, and were continued to operate according to the versions valid until December 31, 2018, may continue to operate without a permit [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   Type approvals for facilities generating ionizing radiation issued before June 5, 2021, remain valid as type approvals according to $\S 45$ Paragraph 1 Number 7 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Notification and Reporting Requirements

*   **Air Vehicles ($\S 209$):** Activities defined in $\S 4$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 11, undertaken before December 31, 2018, and requiring a notification according to $\S 50$, may continue if the notification was made by December 31, 2020 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Activities ($\S 210$):**
    *   A notification of an activity defined in $\S 4$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 10, carried out before December 31, 2018, remains valid as a notification according to $\S 56$ Paragraph 1, provided that the documents required by $\S 56$ Paragraph 2 Sentence 1 were submitted to the competent authority by December 31, 2020 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   If an activity defined in $\S 4$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 Number 10 was undertaken before December 31, 2018, without a notification being required, an estimation according to $\S 55$ Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 must be conducted by December 31, 2020 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Radiation Protection Officers ($\S 211$):** An appointment of a radiation protection officer made before December 31, 2018, remains valid as an appointment according to $\S 70$ Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Dose Limits ($\S 212$):** The limit value according to $\S 78$ Paragraph 2 Number 1 must be observed starting January 1, 2019, and $\S 80$ must be applied for determining population exposure starting January 1, 2019 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Early Detection ($\S 213$):** An authorization for voluntary X-ray series examinations for determining transmissible diseases in regions or for population groups with above-average incidence of disease, under the versions valid until December 31, 2018, remains valid as an authorization according to $\S 84$ Paragraph 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Workplaces in Indoor Areas ($\S 214$):**
    *   A notification of work performed before December 31, 2018, that was assignable to a work area listed in Annex XI Part A of the Radiation Protection Ordinance valid until December 31, 2018, remains valid as a notification according to $\S 129$ Paragraph 1, with the condition that measures to reduce Radon-222 exposure, if required by $\S 128$ Paragraph 1, must be taken by December 31, 2020 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   A measurement of Radon-222 activity concentration conducted before December 31, 2018, as part of an estimation according to $\S 95$ Paragraph 1 in conjunction with Annex XI Part A of the Radiation Protection Ordinance valid until December 31, 2018, fulfills the measurement obligation according to $\S 127$ Paragraph 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Radioactive Contamination ($\S 215$):** Permits issued before December 31, 2018, for remediation, protection, or aftercare measures on sites mentioned in Article 3 of the Unification Treaty of September 6, 1990, or for decommissioning and remediation of uranium ore mining facilities, remain valid, provided they were issued after the entry into force of the Unification Treaty or before it but still valid [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Appendices Summary

*   **Anlage 1 (Residues):** Defines residues as materials such as sludges and deposits from the extraction, processing, and refining of crude oil and natural gas, or deep geothermal energy; gravel, sands, resins, and granular activated carbon from groundwater treatment; unprocessed phosphate gypsum, sludges from its processing, and dusts and slags from the processing of raw phosphate (phosphorite) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md]. It also includes materials derived from the ores listed in Number 4, and dusts and sludges from flue gas cleaning during primary smelting in the iron and non-ferrous metallurgy [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 2 (Application Documents):** Details the required documentation for applications for permits under $\S 12$ Paragraph 1 Number 1 and 2, $\S 12$ Paragraph 1 Number 3 and $\S 40$, $\S 12$ Paragraph 1 Number 4, $\S 12$ Paragraph 1 Number 5, $\S 25$, and $\S 40$ Paragraph 1 and $\S 42$ Paragraph 1. These include safety reports, supplementary plans, evidence of radiation protection officer availability, and proof of liability insurance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 3 (Activity Fields):** Lists specific activity fields, including:
    1. Grinding of toroidized welding electrodes and AC welding with toroidized electrodes [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    2. Handling and storage of toroidized gas glow stockings [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    3. Handling and storage of thorium-containing optical components [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    4. Use of thorium or uranium in natural isotope composition for chemical-analytical or chemical-preparative purposes [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    5. Handling of products from toroidized alloys [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    6. Extraction, use, and processing of pyrochlore ores [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    7. Use and processing of slag from the smelting of copper ore [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    8. Processing of niobium and tantalum ores [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    9. Handling of sludges and deposits from the extraction, processing, and refining of crude oil and natural gas, or deep geothermal energy [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    10. Processing of zircon-containing substances for the manufacture of fire-resistant materials [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    11. Maintenance of clinker kilns in cement production and boilers in coal power plants [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    12. Storage of monitored residues and removal of contamination from sites according to $\S 64$ [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 4 (Emergency Plans):** Lists documents concerning preliminary federal emergency plans, including recommendations from the Radiation Protection Commission regarding accident protection in the vicinity of nuclear facilities and guidelines for public information during nuclear emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 5 (Federal Emergency Plans):** Outlines the essential elements of the general federal emergency plan, covering legal bases, tasks, and responsibilities of federal and state authorities, reference scenarios, dose values, and optimized protection strategies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 6 (Special Emergency Plans):** Outlines the essential elements of the special federal emergency plans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
