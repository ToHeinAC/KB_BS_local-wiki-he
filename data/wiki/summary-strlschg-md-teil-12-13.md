This document summarizes the transitional provisions of the Radiation Protection Act (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), detailing how permits, registrations, and procedures valid before specific cut-off dates (e.g., 31.12.2018) continue under the new legal framework.

## Transitional Provisions for Permits and Registrations

The source outlines several sections detailing the continuation of previous authorizations:

*   **§ 202 Genehmigungsbedürftige Beschäftigung in fremden Anlagen oder Einrichtungen (§ 25):** A permit issued before December 31, 2018, remains valid as a permit according to § 25 Absatz 1, including all ancillary provisions, until the date specified in the permit, and no later than December 31, 2023 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **§ 203 Anzeigebedürftige Beschäftigung im Zusammenhang mit dem Betrieb fremder Röntgeneinrichtungen und Störstrahler (§ 26):** An announcement of activities related to operating a foreign X-ray facility or scatterer made before December 31, 2018, remains valid as an announcement according to § 26 Absatz 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **§ 204 Genehmigungsbedürftige Beförderung radioaktiver Stoffe (§ 27):** Permits for the transport of radioactive substances issued before December 31, 2018, remain valid as permits according to § 27 Absatz 1 with all ancillary provisions, provided that the expertise required by § 29 Absatz 1 Nummer 2 is proven to the competent authority by December 31, 2021 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **§ 205 Medizinische Forschung (§§ 31, 32):**
    *   Applications for the use of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation on humans for medical research, permitted under the versions of the Radiation Protection Ordinance or X-ray Ordinance valid until December 31, 2018, remain valid as permits according to § 31 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   Applications permitted under the versions valid until December 31, 2018, remain valid as announcements according to § 32 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   Registration of ethics committees under the versions valid until December 31, 2018, remain valid as registrations according to § 36 Absatz 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **§ 206 Genehmigungsbedürftiger Zusatz radioaktiver Stoffe und genehmigungsbedürftige Aktivierung (§ 40):** A permit for the addition of radioactive substances and activation issued before December 31, 2018, remains valid as a permit according to § 40 Absatz 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **§ 207 Genehmigungsbedürftige grenzüberschreitende Verbringung von Konsumgütern (§ 42):** A permit for the cross-border transport of consumer goods issued before December 31, 2018, remains valid as a permit according to § 42 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **§ 208 Bauartzulassung (§ 45):**
    *   Type approvals for devices, facilities, and X-ray facilities that were valid on December 31, 2018, remain valid until the expiry date stated in the approval certificate and can be extended as an approval according to § 45 Absatz 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
    *   Devices whose type approval expired before December 31, 2018, but were continued to operate according to the versions valid until December 31, 2018, may continue to operate accordingly under § 48 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **§ 210 Anzeigebedürftige Tätigkeiten (§ 56):** An announcement of an activity performed before December 31, 2018, remains valid as an announcement according to § 56 Absatz 1, provided that the documents required by § 56 Absatz 2 Satz 1 were submitted by December 31, 2020 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Annexes Summary

*   **Anlage 1 (zu § 5 Absatz 32):** Defines residues ("Rückstände") as materials such as sludges and deposits from oil/gas extraction and deep geothermal energy; kyanite, sand, and activated charcoal from groundwater treatment; unprocessed phosphate gypsum, sludges, dusts, and slags from raw phosphate processing; and various geological materials (Bauxite, Columbite, etc.) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md]. It specifies that materials are *not* residues if their specific activity for U-238 and Th-232 decay chains is below 0.2 Bq/g, or if they are introduced as raw materials in technological processes [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 2 (zu § 16, § 25 Absatz 2, § 40 Absatz 4, § 46 Absatz 1):** Lists required documents for various applications, including a Safety Report (Sicherheitsbericht) detailing the facility, associated risks, and necessary equipment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 3 (zu § 55 Absatz 1):** Lists specific activity fields ("Tätigkeitsfelder") such as grinding torqued welding electrodes, handling thorium-containing optical components, and processing pyrochlore ores [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 4 (zu § 97 Absatz 5):** Lists documents concerning preliminary federal emergency plans, including recommendations from the Radiation Protection Commission (e.g., guidelines for public information, criteria for alarming disaster protection authorities) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 5 (zu § 98):** Details essential elements of the general federal emergency plan, covering legal bases, responsibilities, reference values for population exposure, and optimized protection strategies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Anlage 6 (zu § 99):** Details essential elements of special federal emergency plans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
