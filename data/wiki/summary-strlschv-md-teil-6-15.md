This document provides excerpts from the *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV), which details specific technical and organizational requirements for radiation protection, particularly concerning accident planning, medical applications, and equipment quality assurance.

## Emergency Planning and Accident Dose Limits
The source outlines specific body dose limits to be considered when planning for accidents involving the release of radioactive substances into the environment [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md]:
1. An effective dose of 50 millisieverts (mSv).
2. An organ-equivalent dose of the thyroid of 150 millisievert (mSv).
3. An organ-equivalent dose of the skin, hands, forearms, feet, and ankles of each 500 millisievert (mSv).
4. An organ-equivalent dose of the eye lens, gonads, uterus, and bone marrow (red) of each 50 millisievert (mSv).
5. An organ-equivalent dose of the bone surface of 300 millisievert (mSv).
6. An organ-equivalent dose of the large intestine, lung, stomach, bladder, breast, liver, esophagus, other organs or tissues according to Annex 18 Part C Number 2 Footnote 1, where not mentioned under Number 4, of each 150 millisievert (mSv).

The *Strahlenschutzverantwortliche* must ensure that planning for technical or structural protective measures against accidents at nuclear power plants, up to decommissioning, is carried out [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md]. Furthermore, planning for other facilities must consider the potential extent of damage to limit exposure in case of accidents [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].

## Incident and Accident Response
*   **Preventive Measures (§ 105):** The *Strahlenschutzverantwortliche* must ensure that systematic measures are taken when applying radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans to: 1. prevent an incident, 2. detect an incident, and 3. keep the adverse effects as low as possible in case of an incident [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].
*   **Emergency Measures (§ 106):** The *Strahlenschutzverantwortliche* must ensure that necessary information and consultation are provided to the authorities responsible for disaster protection and public safety for their planning to defend against dangers from ionizing radiation and to limit or eliminate adverse effects of an incident or accident [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].
*   **Reporting (§ 108):** The *Strahlenschutzverantwortliche* must ensure that the occurrence of an accident, an incident, or any other significant incident is immediately reported to the competent authority [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].

## Medical Applications and Patient Protection
*   **Justification (§ 119):** The physician or dentist establishing the justifying indication must check, in addition to compliance with § 83 Abs. 3 StrlSchG, whether the planned application is a recognized procedure in medical science or a clinical trial that requires special justification by the physician or dentist [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].
*   **Protection of Special Groups (§ 120):** The applying physician or dentist must inquire about pregnancy in fertile individuals. If pregnancy is present or cannot be excluded, the urgency of the application must be assessed. For open radioactive substances, the rules for breastfeeding apply [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].
*   **Equipment Requirements (§ 114):** A X-ray facility used on humans must have a function that displays the parameters for determining the exposure received by the examined or treated person, or, if not possible by state-of-the-art technology, a way to determine the received exposure otherwise [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].

## Quality Assurance and Record Keeping
*   **Quality Assurance (§ 115):** Before commissioning, the *Strahlenschutzverantwortliche* must ensure that the required quality for the application is achieved through an acceptance test by the manufacturer or supplier [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].
*   **Constancy Check (§ 116):** The *Strahlenschutzverantwortliche* must ensure that facilities are regularly checked after commissioning to ensure that the required quality is still achieved (constancy check) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].
*   **Record Keeping (§ 127):** The *Strahlenschutzverantwortliche* must ensure that records, X-ray images, digital image data, and other examination data are stored so that they remain available and readable throughout the retention period defined by § 85 Abs. 2 StrlSchG [StrlSchV.md [Teil 6/15].md].
