# Cyber Risk

Cyber Risk refers to the potential for financial loss, operational disruption, or data breach resulting from malicious digital activity or system failure. It has emerged as one of the most consequential and rapidly growing areas of insurance coverage [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Growth and Market Size
The global cyber insurance market was estimated at approximately USD 15 billion in 2025. Munich Re projects this market to expand to USD 28 billion by 2030, representing a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 15% [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Key Challenges and Trends
1.  **Inadequate Defenses:** A significant finding from the 2026 Cyber Survey is that 90% of corporate leaders globally do not feel adequately protected against cyberattacks [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
2.  **Automation of Fraud:** Nearly half of all reported cyber fraud cases are now AI-assisted or fully automated, which materially increases the complexity of accumulation risk modeling for reinsurers [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
3.  **Modeling Complexity:** The CFO has flagged that cyber accumulation risk modeling remains one of the most complex underwriting challenges for the industry [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Reinsurance Implications
The rapid growth and increasing complexity of cyber risk mean that deep-dive data analytics capability is a critical competitive differentiator for reinsurers [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
