# Radiation Protection Officer (Strahlenschutzverantwortlicher)

The Radiation Protection Officer (*Strahlenschutzverantwortlicher*) is a legally defined role responsible for overseeing radiation protection measures during activities involving ionizing radiation or radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Core Duties and Obligations
The officer is responsible for ensuring that:
1. **Protective Measures:** Suitable protective measures are implemented for the protection of humans and the environment against harmful effects of ionizing radiation, considering the state of science and technology. This includes providing suitable rooms, equipment, and devices, regulating operational processes, and ensuring adequate personnel [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
2. **Dose Minimization:**
    * All unnecessary exposure or contamination of humans and the environment must be avoided (per § 8 Abs. 1) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].
    * Any exposure or contamination must be kept as low as possible, even below limit values, considering all individual circumstances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 5/13].md].

## Role in Contaminated Sites
In the context of radioactive contaminated sites, the officer is responsible for ensuring that remediation measures are carried out, including monitoring the population exposure during remediation works [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
