The principle of "Minimization of Exposure" is a mandatory obligation under the German Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) [StrlSchG_02.md]. This principle dictates that radiation protection measures must be implemented to reduce potential harm to the lowest practicable level.

## Core Obligations
Any entity planning, carrying out, or allowing an activity must adhere to two primary minimization goals:
1.  **Avoidance:** The primary goal is to prevent any unnecessary exposure or contamination of humans and the environment [StrlSchG_02.md].
2.  **Optimization:** If exposure or contamination cannot be entirely avoided, the activity must be managed to keep these levels as low as possible, even if they fall below established regulatory limits [StrlSchG_02.md].

## Technical Requirements
Achieving minimization requires considering the current state of scientific knowledge and technology. The specific technical standard to be observed depends on the nature of the activity being performed [StrlSchG_02.md].
