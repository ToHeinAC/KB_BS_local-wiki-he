# Reinsurance

Reinsurance is the practice where an insurance company (the ceding company) transfers a portion of its risk to another insurer (the reinsurer) in exchange for a premium. This mechanism allows primary insurers to manage their exposure to catastrophic, large-scale, or highly volatile risks [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Function and Importance
The primary function of reinsurance is risk mitigation and capital management. By distributing risk across multiple reinsurers globally, the potential financial impact of a single catastrophic event (such as a major natural disaster or cyber attack) can be contained, thereby protecting the solvency and stability of the ceding company [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Market Dynamics
The reinsurance market is highly cyclical and can be influenced by:
*   **Hard Markets:** Periods of high loss frequency and severity, leading to increased pricing and exceptional results for reinsurers [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Soft Markets:** Periods of lower loss frequency, which can lead to softening pricing and volume contraction, as demonstrated by renewals in January and April 2026 [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Key Segments
Reinsurance services are specialized across various risk types:
*   **Property-Casualty (P&C) Reinsurance:** Covers risks related to physical damage from natural catastrophes and man-made events [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Life and Health (L&H) Reinsurance:** Manages mortality and morbidity risks associated with human life and health [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Global Specialty Insurance (GSI):** Handles niche, complex risks such as cyber, marine, and aviation liabilities [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Market Players
The reinsurance market remains oligopolistic in peak-capacity and specialty segments, though it is increasingly competitive in standard P&C property lines due to capital from alternative risk transfer markets (ILS) [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2].md].
