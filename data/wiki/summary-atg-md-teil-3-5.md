This document provides excerpts from the *Gesetz über die friedliche Verwendung der Kernenergie und den Schutz gegen ihre Gefahren* (Atomgesetz - AtG), detailing various regulatory aspects concerning nuclear energy use, site management, and state supervision.

## Property Rights and Expropriation
The text addresses compensation and expropriation procedures, stating that for expropriation and compensation, the provisions of $\text{§§ 93 bis 103 und 106 bis 122 des Baugesetzbuches}$ apply [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md]. Specifically, for the expropriation of mining rights and rights under $\text{§ 9e Absatz 1 Satz 1 Nummer 3}$, $\text{§ 116 des Baugesetzbuches}$ applies, with the caveat that the exercise of these rights can be temporarily withdrawn and, if necessary for the purposes mentioned in $\text{§ 9d Absatz 1 und 2}$, temporarily transferred to the applicant [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].

## Site Preparation and Change Restrictions
The law details procedures for site preparation and the establishment of change restrictions:

*   **§ 9f Vorarbeiten an Grundstücken:** Owners and other rights holders must tolerate the entry and passage over properties for the preparation of planning approvals ($\text{Planfeststellung}$) according to $\text{§ 9b}$ and for above-ground site investigations for radioactive waste disposal facilities. Measurements, soil, and groundwater investigations are permitted, provided the intent is given to the owner/rights holder in advance [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md]. After completion, the previous state of the properties must be restored. If immediate financial disadvantages arise from the measures, appropriate compensation in money must be paid [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **§ 9g Veränderungssperre (Change Restriction):** Planning areas can be established by legal ordinance for a maximum of ten years to secure planning areas for projects under $\text{§ 9b}$ or site investigations for radioactive waste disposal facilities, preventing significant value-enhancing or project-hindering changes on the surface or subsurface [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md]. This restriction can be extended twice, each time for up to ten years, provided the conditions persist.
    *   Changes are prohibited until the planned utilization. Maintenance work and continuation of previously lawful use are exempt [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    *   If the restriction lasts longer than five years, the owner/rights holder can claim appropriate compensation for the resulting financial disadvantages from the project developer [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].

## Licensing and Authorization ($\text{§ 11}$ and $\text{§ 12}$)
The law grants authority for regulations concerning licensing and protective measures:

*   **§ 11 Ermächtigungsvorschriften:** Regulations can stipulate that:
    1.  The searching for, handling of, transport of, and export/import of radioactive substances require a permit or notification, specifying conditions, procedures, and reporting duties [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    2.  Safety-critical plant components can only be installed if a legitimate interest exists for pre-fabrication and if a verification process proves compliance with $\text{§ 7 Abs. 2 Nr. 3}$ [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    3.  Radioactive substances may be prohibited from use or trade if necessary to protect life and health from radioactive dangers or to enforce international obligations [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    4.  Import, export, and transit of radioactive substances require a permit or consent for EU law implementation [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **§ 12 Ermächtigungsvorschriften (Protective Measures):** Regulations can specify:
    1.  Preventive and monitoring measures for individuals and the general public when handling/transporting radioactive substances, or when operating/owning facilities under $\text{§ 7}$ [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    2.  Prevention measures to ensure that specific radiation doses and concentrations of radioactive substances in air and water are not exceeded [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    3.  How records must be kept regarding the generation, acquisition, possession, disposal, and measurement of ionizing radiation doses [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    4.  Obligations of the facility owner to report deviations from the permit application or permit [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    5.  Reporting of safety-critical deviations, especially accidents, to the supervisory authority [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    6.  How radioactive waste must be delivered to state collection points and federal facilities, and under what conditions alternative interim storage is permissible [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
    7.  Requirements for the safe recovery and disposal of radioactive residues and dismantled plant parts [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].

## Reliability Check ($\text{§ 12b}$)
To protect against unauthorized removal or release of radioactive substances, supervisory authorities conduct a reliability check on:
1.  Applicants/Permit holders and responsible persons concerning facilities/activities under $\text{§§ 4, 6, 7, 9, 9a Abs. 3}$ or facilities for generating ionizing radiation under $\text{§ 5 Absatz 2}$ [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
2.  Persons working at/operating facilities under $\text{§ 7}$, federal facilities under $\text{§ 9a Absatz 3}$, or facilities for generating ionizing radiation under $\text{§ 5 Absatz 2}$ [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
3.  Persons handling or transporting radioactive substances, and experts ($\text{Sachverständige}$) [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].

The check requires prior written consent. Authorities can request information from police, constitutional protection agencies, and customs crime offices [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].

## Compensation and Liability ($\text{§ 13, § 14, § 15}$)
*   **§ 13 Vorsorge für die Erfüllung gesetzlicher Schadensersatzverpflichtungen:** The administrative authority must determine the provision for statutory compensation obligations ($\text{Deckungsvorsorge}$) in the permit procedure, which must be reviewed every two years or upon significant change [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **§ 14 Haftpflichtversicherung:** If the provision is covered by liability insurance, the provisions of the Insurance Contract Act apply, with a two-month period for the claim deadline [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
*   **§ 15 Rangfolge der Befriedigung:** If a nuclear event occurs, the provision for compensation must not impair the coverage of other affected parties. Claims under $\text{§ 28 Absatz 3}$ must not impair the coverage of other affected parties [AtG.md [Teil 3/5].md].
