# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 7/13)

This section of the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) details extensive provisions concerning emergency planning, public protection, waste management, and regulatory oversight following potential radiation incidents.

## § 89 Verordnungsermächtigungen zu der Sicherheit von Strahlungsquellen
The Federal Government is authorized to issue ordinances regarding:
1. **Bookkeeping:** Keeping records on the generation, acquisition, possession, location, release, and other retention of radioactive substances, and records of dose and dose rate measurements, including how reports must be submitted and documents stored [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. **Security and Storage:** Requirements for the securing and storage of radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3. **Equipment Maintenance:** Requirements for the maintenance and checking of equipment, devices, and other apparatus, and who must perform this maintenance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4. **Leak Testing:** Requirements for leak testing of encapsulated radioactive substances and who must perform this testing [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5. **Measurement Devices:** Which radiation measuring devices must be used and what requirements apply to them [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6. **Labeling:** Which areas, rooms, devices, apparatus, containers, enclosures, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, and which type-approved devices must be labeled, how and under what conditions the labeling must occur, and in which cases labels must be removed [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7. **Release:** Requirements for the release of radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
8. **Retrieval:** Requirements for the retrieval of high-radioactive sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
9. **Irradiation Rooms:** Cases where X-ray rooms or irradiation rooms must be used in activities with radiation sources, and what requirements apply to these rooms [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
10. **Personnel:** Which persons are allowed to apply radiation or technically perform the application of radiation in activities with radiation sources, how and when persons must be supervised during such activities, and what documents must be available [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
11. **Precautions:** What further precautions and monitoring measures must be taken for the control of radioactive substances for the protection of individuals and the general public, and what such measures must be [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
12. **Documentation:** What further obligations regarding documentation, storage, notification, submission, and retention exist in connection with the duties listed in numbers 1 to 11 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 90 Verordnungsermächtigung für Pflichten, Aufgaben und Befugnisse bei Vorkommnissen
The Federal Government is authorized to establish, by ordinance, duties of the Radiation Protection Officer and official tasks and powers concerning incidents in planned exposure situations. This can specify:
1. Measures the Radiation Protection Officer must initiate to keep exposures during such an incident as low as possible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. Measures the Radiation Protection Officer must take to prevent such incidents in the future [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3. How the Radiation Protection Officer must record and investigate an incident, and for what period they must retain related records [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4. How the Radiation Protection Officer must report an incident to the supervisory authority, and must report information and findings on the causes and effects of the incident, as well as measures for remediation or limitation of the effects, and measures to prevent incidents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5. How the supervisory authority records, checks, and evaluates reports according to number 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6. How the Federal Office for Radiation Protection maintains a central body for recording, processing, and evaluating information and findings on incidents related to the use of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation on humans, and the supervisory authority transmits this information and findings [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7. Under what conditions and how the supervisory authority and the central body publish information and findings on incidents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

**Data Retention:** The Radiation Protection Officer must record the name, first name, date and place of birth, gender, and address, as well as data on the exposure and the health consequences of a person exposed to an incident. If the Radiation Protection Officer must report the incident to the competent authority and protective measures are necessary, the data must be transmitted immediately. The data must be secured against unauthorized access by technical and organizational measures. In other cases than in sentence 2, the data must be submitted to the competent authority upon request. The data must be retained for 30 years and deleted immediately after this period expires [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 91 Verordnungsermächtigung für Informationspflichten des Herstellers oder Lieferanten von Geräten
The Federal Government is authorized to determine that the manufacturer or supplier of facilities for generating ionizing radiation, X-ray equipment, scatter radiation, irradiation devices, and other equipment used in connection with activities must provide the Radiation Protection Officer with information about these devices. This can specify:
1. When the manufacturer or supplier must provide information for which of the mentioned devices to the Radiation Protection Officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. What data and documents must be provided [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3. For what purposes the documents must be suitable and what requirements they must meet [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4. That the information must also be available to the person intending to become a Radiation Protection Officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 92 Notfallschutzgrundsätze
(1) The provisions of these paragraphs (Emergency Protection Principles) must be considered when assessing dangers that can arise during emergencies from ionizing radiation in the following cases:
1. When issuing, reviewing, and amending emergency plans and ordinances under this chapter and § 117 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. During the emergency response by the competent authorities and the authorities and organizations participating in the emergency response based on this law, the ordinances mentioned in number 1, and federal and state legal provisions for defending against dangers to human health, the environment, or public safety, insofar as they are applicable to radiological dangers, and directly applicable EU/Euratom law that does not make conclusive provisions for member states regarding radiological dangers [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The reference values established in this law and the ordinances issued based on this law for the protection of the population and the emergency services during emergencies should be kept as low as possible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(3) During emergencies, the exposure of the population and emergency services, as well as the contamination of the environment, must be kept as low as possible through appropriate measures, taking into account the state of science and all circumstances of the respective emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 93 Referenzwerte für den Schutz der Bevölkerung
(1) For the protection of the population, a reference value of **100 millisieverts (mSv)** applies to the effective dose that affected persons would receive from the emergency within one year through all exposure pathways, if the planned protective measures were implemented [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized to establish supplementary appropriate reference values for organ equivalent doses by ordinance, particularly to facilitate cooperation with other EU/Euratom member states or third countries [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(3) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized to establish a lower reference value for the effective dose, related to one year or a single exposure, by ordinance for an already occurred emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 94 Dosiswerte und Kontaminationswerte für den Schutz der Bevölkerung
(1) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety shall establish dose values by ordinance, which serve as radiological criteria for the appropriateness of the following protective measures:
1. Recommendation to stay in buildings [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. Distribution or recommendation to take iodine tablets [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3. Evacuation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
These values relate to the dose that affected persons would receive in a specific period after the onset of the emergency without protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized to establish limit values for emergency contamination or dose rates by ordinance for possible emergencies, for an already occurred emergency, and for an exposure situation existing after an emergency, for:
1. Individuals of the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. Drinking water [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3. Food, feed, necessities, cosmetic products, and products according to § 2 No. 1 of the Tobacco Products Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4. Medicines and their starting materials, as well as medical devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5. Other products, objects, and substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6. Vehicles, goods, or luggage [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7. Contaminated areas, especially contaminated land and water, whose exceeding suggests a danger for individuals of the population due to ionizing radiation. These limits serve the implementation of optimized protection strategies according to § 98 (3) No. 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 95 Bewirtschaftung von Abfällen, die infolge eines Notfalls kontaminiert sein können
(1) The Federal Government shall establish contamination values by ordinance for possible emergencies, for an already occurred emergency, and for an exposure situation existing after an emergency, for waste and other objects or substances that are contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency. If these contamination values are not exceeded, it is assumed that the required protection of man and environment against the harmful effect of ionizing radiation is ensured when managing this waste, as well as establishing, operating, or using the facilities listed below, according to the Circular Economy Act and other federal laws applicable to waste and the ordinances based on these laws, without additional special protective measures:
1. Facilities where this waste is disposed of [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. Wastewater facilities that receive wastewater contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3. Facilities where this waste or other objects or substances are stored, used, or treated, or can be stored, used, or treated, especially as fuel, raw material, material, precursor product, lubricating, dissolving, or other auxiliary agent [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 95a Auskunftsverlangen, Betretensrechte, Mitwirkungs- und Duldungspflichten
(1) Information on waste and other objects or substances contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency, on the establishment, operation, and use of the facilities mentioned in § 95 (1) sentence 2, on land where such waste, other objects or substances, or such facilities may be located, and on other objects or substances subject to supervision according to § 178 sentence 2 must be provided to the employees and agents of the competent authority upon request by:
1. Generators and owners of waste or other objects or substances contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. Those obligated to dispose of waste contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3. Owners and operators, and former operators of:
    a) companies that dispose of or have disposed of such waste [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
    b) the facilities mentioned in § 95 (1) sentence 2, even if these facilities are decommissioned [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4. Owners and owners of land where the facilities mentioned in § 95 (1) sentence 2 are operated or were operated, as well as collectors, transporters, dealers, and brokers of waste contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 96 Eilverordnungen
(1) In case of urgency after an emergency, the Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety can issue ordinances (Emergency Ordinances) without the consent of the Bundesrat and without the agreement of the involved federal ministries, concerning:
1. Regulations according to §§ 93, 94, and 95 (1) and 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. Regulations according to § 95 (2) and 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 97 Gemeinsame Vorschriften für die Notfallpläne
(1) The Federation and the States shall develop emergency plans according to §§ 98, 99, 100, and 101. These plans must present planned appropriate reactions to possible emergencies based on specific reference scenarios. These emergency reactions include:
1. Protective measures, which include:
    a) Measures to prevent or reduce exposure and contamination of man or environment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
    b) Measures for medical treatment or prophylaxis after exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. Other measures to be taken by the involved authorities and organizations to prevent or keep the adverse effects of the emergency on human health or the environment as low as possible, especially measures for checking, preparation, execution, monitoring, amendment, or revocation of protective measures, as well as cooperation and coordination in emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The emergency plans shall enable the involved authorities and organizations to make coordinated decisions immediately and carry out appropriate measures in time during an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 98 Allgemeiner Notfallplan des Bundes
(1) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety assesses possible exposure situations. The Federal Government issues a general emergency plan of the Federation based on its proposal. This plan is adopted as a general administrative regulation with the consent of the Bundesrat [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The general emergency plan of the Federation must include:
1. Reference scenarios that serve as the basis for planning emergency reactions for the Federation and the States, and
2. The following general plans for possible emergencies within or outside the scope of this law:
a) the plans of the Federation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
b) the plans of the European Union and the European Atomic Community, their member states, and third states, as well as the plans of international organizations and the plans within the framework of international treaties [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 99 Besondere Notfallpläne des Bundes
(1) The Federal Government supplements and specifies the general emergency plan of the Federation with special emergency plans of the Federation upon proposal from the responsible federal ministries. These special emergency plans are adopted as general administrative regulations with the consent of the Bundesrat [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The special emergency plans of the Federation must present plans, in particular, for the following application areas:
1. Disaster protection, general danger control, and assistance, as well as medical treatment and prophylaxis after exposure of the population and emergency services [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. Drinking water extraction and supply [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3. Production of plant and animal products, food, feed, necessities, cosmetic products, and products according to § 2 No. 1 of the Tobacco Products Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4. Medicines and their starting materials, as well as medical devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5. Other products, objects, and substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6. Transport of goods [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7. Transboundary transport of persons, vehicles, goods, and luggage [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
8. Contaminated areas, especially contaminated land and water [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
9. Disposal of waste and disposal of wastewater, as well as the establishment, operation, and use of the facilities mentioned in § 95 (1) sentence 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 100 Allgemeine und besondere Notfallpläne der Länder
The States shall develop general and special emergency plans. These state emergency plans supplement and specify the general emergency plan of the Federation and the special emergency plans of the Federation, insofar as the States are responsible for the planning or execution of protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 101 Externe Notfallpläne für ortsfeste Anlagen oder Einrichtungen mit besonderem Gefahrenpotential
(1) The authorities responsible for disaster protection or public safety shall develop special emergency plans (external emergency plans) for the surroundings of nuclear facilities, facilities according to § 9a (3) sentence 1 second part of the Atomic Energy Act, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, or facilities according to § 5 (1) 12 of this Act, insofar as emergencies in the facility or establishment could lead to severe health impairments for a non-negligible number of persons in the surroundings of the facility or establishment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The external emergency plans supplement and specify the planning contained in the general and special emergency plans of the Federation and the States. They must take into account local conditions as well as the procedures and precautions of the Radiation Protection Officer for internal emergency protection of the facility [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 102 Notfallübungen
(1) The authorities and organizations involved in the emergency response according to the emergency plans of the Federation and the States, as well as those responsible for the training and further education of emergency personnel according to § 115 (1), must regularly conduct emergency drills [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The emergency drills must be appropriately differentiated according to the type of drill, scope, emergency scenarios, and participants. In particular, the following must be tested and practiced:
1. The organizational precautions for the emergency response [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. The exchange of information and cooperation of the authorities, organizations, and Radiation Protection Officers involved in the emergency response regarding:
    a) situation assessment and situation evaluation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
    b) the coordination of decisions of the competent authorities [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
    c) the execution of appropriate protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 103 Überprüfung und Änderung der Notfallpläne
(1) The emergency plans of the Federation and the States must be regularly reviewed and, if necessary, amended, taking into account the experiences from emergency drills, findings from emergencies domestically or abroad, and changes in the state of science and law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The information supplementing the emergency plans, such as contact details of the competent authorities and participating organizations or directories of applicable legal provisions, must be updated and regularly reviewed upon changes. The dates for the reviews must be specified in the emergency plans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 104 Beschaffung von Schutzwirkstoffen
(1) The competent authority according to § 192 (1) must procure protective agents to the extent required for supplying the population in the federal territory in case of possible emergencies. It makes these protective agents available to the States for stockpiling, distribution, and dispensing to the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) Protective agents are medicines:
1. suitable for preventing the uptake of radioactive iodine into the human thyroid gland, or
2. suitable for preventing the uptake of radionuclides into the human body or for removing radionuclides from the human body [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 105 Information der Bevölkerung über die Schutzmaßnahmen und Empfehlungen für das Verhalten bei möglichen Notfällen
(1) The competent federal bodies shall publish the emergency plans of the Federation according to § 10 of the Environmental Information Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(2) The competent federal bodies shall:
1. Inform the population in an appropriate manner according to § 10 of the Environmental Information Act about:
    a) the basic concepts of radioactivity and the effects of radioactivity on man and the environment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
    b) the emergencies considered in the emergency plans and their consequences for the population and the environment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
    c) planned measures for warning and protecting the population in case of possible emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2. Give the population recommendations for behavior in case of possible emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(3) The States inform the population about the matters listed in paragraph 2 number 1 according to state law and give recommendations for behavior in case of possible emergencies that supplement and specify the recommendations of paragraph 2 number 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
(4) The information and behavioral recommendations must be updated regularly and upon substantial changes and published without being requested. They must be constantly accessible to the public [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 106 Radiologisches Lagezentrum des Bundes
(1) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety establishes a federal radiological situation center [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
