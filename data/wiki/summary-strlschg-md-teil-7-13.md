This document provides excerpts from the German Radiation Protection Act (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), specifically detailing sections related to the management of radiation sources, incident reporting, emergency planning, and public safety measures. The text covers provisions from § 89 through § 106.

## § 89 Verordnungsermächtigungen zu der Sicherheit von Strahlungsquellen
The Federal Government is authorized to establish regulations by ordinance, with the consent of the Federal Council, concerning the protection of people against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation and the control and securing of radioactive materials. This ordinance can specify requirements regarding:
1.  The maintenance of a book detailing the generation, acquisition, possession, location, disposal, and other whereabouts of radioactive materials, as well as measurements of dose and dose rates, including how reports must be submitted and documents kept [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Requirements for the securing and storage of radioactive materials [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  Requirements for the maintenance and verification of equipment, devices, and other apparatus, and who must perform this maintenance and verification [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  Requirements for the leak testing of enclosed radioactive materials and who must perform the leak testing [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5.  Which radiation measuring devices must be used and what requirements apply to them [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6.  Which areas, rooms, devices, apparatus, containers, enclosures, facilities for the generation of ionizing radiation, and which type-approved devices must be marked, how and under what conditions the marking must occur, and in which cases markings must be removed [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7.  Requirements for the disposal of radioactive materials [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
8.  Requirements for the return of high-radioactive sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
9.  In which cases X-ray rooms or irradiation rooms must be used for activities involving radiation sources, and what requirements apply to these rooms [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
10. Which persons are permitted to apply radiation or technically perform the application during activities involving radiation sources, how and when persons must be instructed during such activities, and what documents must be available when performing these activities, including keeping records of the instructions and presenting them to the authority upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
11. What further precautionary and monitoring measures must be taken for the control of radioactive materials for the protection of individuals and the general public, and what such measures must be [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
12. What further obligations regarding records, retention, notification, submission, and deposit exist in connection with the duties according to numbers 1 to 11 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The ordinance can also specify provisions of the ordinance for whose compliance the radiation protection officer must ensure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 90 Verordnungsermächtigung für Pflichten, Aufgaben und Befugnisse bei Vorkommnissen; Aufzeichnungs-, Übermittlungs- und Aufbewahrungspflichten
The Federal Government is authorized to establish regulations by ordinance, with the consent of the Federal Council, concerning the duties of the radiation protection officer, as well as the tasks and powers of the authorities, in the context of incidents in planned exposure situations. The ordinance can specifically stipulate:
1.  What measures the radiation protection officer must initiate to keep exposures in such an incident as low as possible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  What measures the radiation protection officer must take to prevent such incidents in the future [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  How and in what manner the radiation protection officer must record and investigate an incident, and for what period they must retain related records [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  How the radiation protection officer must report an incident to the supervisory authority, and must report information and findings on the causes and effects of the incident, as well as measures for remediation or limitation of the effects, and measures for preventing incidents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5.  How the supervisory authority records, checks, and evaluates reports according to number 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6.  How the Federal Office for Radiation Protection maintains a central body for the recording, processing, and evaluation of information and findings about incidents in connection with the application of radioactive materials or ionizing radiation to humans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7.  How the supervisory authority and the central body transmit information and findings about an incident in connection with the application of radioactive materials or ionizing radiation to humans, and their related evaluation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
8.  Under what conditions and in what manner the supervisory authority and the central body publish information and findings about incidents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The radiation protection officer must ensure that for an incident subject to the ordinance according to paragraph 1, the name, first names, date and place of birth, gender, and address, as well as data on the exposure of the person exposed by the incident and the health consequences of the exposure are recorded immediately. If the radiation protection officer must report the incident according to the ordinance according to paragraph 1 and measures for the protection of the exposed person are necessary, they must transmit the data immediately to the responsible authority. The data must be secured against unauthorized access by technical and organizational measures. In other cases than in sentence 2, the data must be transmitted to the responsible authority upon request. The data must be retained for 30 years and must be deleted immediately after the expiration of this period [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 91 Verordnungsermächtigung für Informationspflichten des Herstellers oder Lieferanten von Geräten
The Federal Government is authorized to establish regulations by ordinance, with the consent of the Federal Council, stipulating that the manufacturer or supplier of facilities for the generation of ionizing radiation, X-ray equipment, scatter radiation, irradiation devices, and other equipment, devices, and apparatus used in connection with activities must provide the radiation protection officer with information about these devices. The ordinance can specifically stipulate:
1.  When the manufacturer or supplier must provide information to the radiation protection officer for which of the mentioned devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  What details and documents must be provided [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  For what purposes the documents must be suitable and what requirements they must meet [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  That the information must also be available to the person intending to become the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 92 Notfallschutzgrundsätze
The provisions of the following paragraphs (Emergency Protection Principles) must be considered as requirements when assessing dangers that may arise during emergencies due to ionizing radiation, in the following cases:
1.  When enacting, reviewing, and amending emergency plans and ordinances under this chapter and under § 117 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  During the emergency response by the responsible authorities and the authorities and organizations participating in the emergency response based on this law, the ordinances mentioned in number 1, and federal and state legal provisions for the defense against dangers for human health, the environment, or public safety, insofar as they are applicable to radiological dangers, and directly applicable acts of the European Union and the European Atomic Community, insofar as they do not make conclusive provisions for the Member States regarding radiological dangers [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The reference values set in this law and in the ordinances issued on the basis of this law for the protection of the population and the emergency services during emergencies should be kept as low as possible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The exposure of the population and the emergency services as well as the contamination of the environment during emergencies must be kept as low as possible by appropriate measures, taking into account the state of science and all circumstances of the respective emergency, even below the reference values [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 93 Referenzwerte für den Schutz der Bevölkerung; Verordnungsermächtigungen
For the protection of the population, a reference value of 100 millisieverts applies in the planning of protective measures and in the decisions regarding their implementation in an emergency. This value relates to the effective dose that affected persons would receive within one year through all exposure pathways if the planned protective measures were implemented. The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized to stipulate procedures and assumptions for assessing the extent to which this reference value is undercut, maintained, or exceeded by ordinance, with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized to stipulate supplementary appropriate reference values for organ equivalent doses by ordinance, with the consent of the Federal Council, for possible emergencies or for an already occurred emergency. This applies particularly to facilitate cooperation with other Member States of the European Union and the European Atomic Community or third countries in the protection of the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized to stipulate a lower reference value for the effective dose, relative to one year or a single exposure, by ordinance, with the consent of the Federal Council, for an already occurred emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 94 Dosiswerte und Kontaminationswerte für den Schutz der Bevölkerung; Verordnungsermächtigungen
The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety shall stipulate, by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council, dose values that serve as a radiological criterion for the appropriateness of the following protective measures:
1.  Recommendation to stay in buildings [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Distribution of iodine tablets or recommendation for taking iodine tablets [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  Evacuation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
These values refer to the dose that affected persons would receive in a specific period after the emergency without protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized, by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council, for possible emergencies, for an already occurred emergency, and for an exposure situation remaining after an emergency, to stipulate limit values for emergency-related contaminations or dose rates:
1.  For individual persons of the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  For drinking water [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  For food, animal feed, necessities, cosmetic products, and products within the meaning of § 2 Number 1 of the Tobacco Products Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  For medicinal products and their starting materials, as well as for medical devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5.  For other products, objects, and substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6.  For vehicles, goods, or luggage [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7.  For contaminated areas, especially for contaminated properties and waters, assuming that exceeding these values indicates a danger for individual persons of the population due to ionizing radiation. These limit values serve the implementation of optimized protection strategies according to § 98 Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 Number 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized, by ordinance without the consent of the Federal Council, to repeal, declare temporarily inapplicable, or adapt the wording of the ordinances according to paragraph 2, insofar as they have become indefinitely or temporarily inapplicable by legal acts of the European Atomic Community or the European Union issued in immediately applicable legal acts [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

In the ordinances according to paragraphs 1 and 2, procedures and assumptions for measuring, calculating, or estimating dose values, contamination values, or dose rate values may also be stipulated, or prerequisites may be stipulated under which these values apply [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

Ordinances according to paragraph 2 are concluded in agreement with the Federal Ministry of Health, the Federal Ministry of Food and Agriculture, the Federal Ministry of Economics and Energy, the Federal Ministry of Labor and Social Affairs, the Federal Ministry of Transport and Digital Infrastructure, the Federal Ministry of the Interior, for Building and Home, and the Federal Ministry of Finance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 95 Bewirtschaftung von Abfällen, die infolge eines Notfalls kontaminiert sein können, Errichtung und Betrieb von Anlagen; Verordnungsermächtigungen
The Federal Government stipulates, by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council, contamination values for waste and other objects or substances that are contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency, for possible emergencies, for an already occurred emergency, and for an exposure situation remaining after an emergency. If these contamination values are undercut, it is assumed that the required protection of man and environment against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation is ensured when managing this waste, as well as the construction and operation or use of the following facilities, according to the Circular Economy Act and other federal laws applicable to waste and to the laws based on these laws and the ordinances based on these laws, without additional special protective measures:
1.  Facilities where this waste is disposed of [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Wastewater facilities that receive wastewater contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  Facilities where this waste or these other objects or substances are stored, used, or treated, or can be stored, used, or treated, especially as fuel, raw material, material, precursor product, lubricating, dissolving, or other auxiliary agent [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

To ensure the protection of man and environment against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, the Federal Government regulates by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council for the prevention, recovery, disposal, or other management of waste that is contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency, the additional requirements and exceptions to the following legal provisions for the construction and operation of the facilities mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, or allows the issuance of exceptions to these legal provisions by the responsible authorities:
1.  The Circular Economy Act and other federal laws applicable to waste and the ordinances based on these laws [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Federal laws governing the construction and operation of the facilities mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, and the ordinances based on these laws [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

Exceptions may only be regulated, permitted, or granted insofar as dangers for human health are not expected thereby and European Union or European Atomic Community acts do not contradict this. In such exceptions, significant disadvantages for the general public or the neighborhood must be avoided or reduced, insofar as this is possible and appropriate considering the radiological situation and the other significant circumstances of the respective emergency. For the exceptions and the supplementary regulations, requirements for prevention against harmful environmental impacts and other dangers, as well as against significant disadvantages and significant nuisances, must be considered, particularly by taking measures corresponding to the state of the art [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The regulations according to Paragraph 2 relate in particular to:
1.  The hierarchy of measures for waste prevention and waste management [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Requirements for the harmlessness of recovery [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  The order and execution of waste disposal [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  Requirements for the construction and operation of landfills and their approval, including the approval procedure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5.  Requirements for the monitoring of waste management [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6.  Requirements for collectors, transporters, dealers, and brokers of waste, and their respective approval, including the approval procedure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7.  Requirements for the construction, nature, operation, and essential change of the facilities mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, the approval of these facilities including the approval procedure, as well as the condition of the facility and the facility property after cessation of operation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
8.  Requirements for the use of the wastewater facilities mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 Number 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
9.  Requirements for the use of waters, especially for the introduction and discharge of substances into a body of water; the requirements can also be stipulated for the place of the discharge of wastewater or before its mixing [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
10. Requirements for fulfilling the wastewater disposal obligation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
11. Requirements for monitoring water properties [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
12. Measurement methods and procedures, particularly in the context of wastewater disposal and monitoring of water properties [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
13. Duties of the operators of the facilities mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
14. The prerequisites under which the responsible authorities can grant exceptions by ordinance according to Paragraph 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
15. The requirements to be fulfilled for the duties arising from Paragraph 2 Sentence 2 and 3 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The states stipulate which legal persons are obligated as public disposal carriers in the sense of § 17 of the Circular Economy Act to dispose of such waste from private households and from other sources that cannot be treated, stored, or disposed of in the facilities intended for the disposal of other waste, due to their emergency-related contamination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

For ordinances according to Paragraphs 1 to 3, § 94 Paragraph 3 and 4 apply accordingly [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 95a Auskunftsverlangen, Betretensrechte, Mitwirkungs- und Duldungspflichten
Information regarding waste and other objects or substances contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency, regarding the construction, operation, and use of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, regarding properties where such waste, other objects or substances, or such facilities may be located, and regarding other objects or substances subject to supervision according to § 178 Sentence 2, must be provided by the employees and agents of the responsible authority for supervision upon request to:
1.  Generators and possessors of waste or other objects or substances contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Those obligated to dispose of waste contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  Owners and operators, and former operators of:
    a) companies that dispose of or have disposed of such waste [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
    b) the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, even if these facilities are decommissioned [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  Owners and possessors of properties where the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 are operated or have been operated, as well as
5.  Collectors, transporters, dealers, and brokers of waste contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The persons obligated to provide information according to Paragraph 1 must allow the employees and agents of the responsible authority for supervision to enter the properties, as well as the business and operational premises, for the purpose of checking compliance with their obligations under § 95, the ordinances under § 95, or the emergency ordinances under § 96, during usual business hours, to inspect documents, and to conduct technical investigations and checks, including the determination of emissions and immissions. The persons obligated to provide information according to Paragraph 1 are further obliged to allow entry to the business and operational properties and premises outside of usual business hours, and entry into residential premises, if this is necessary to prevent urgent dangers for public safety or order. The fundamental right to the inviolability of the home (Article 13 Paragraph 1 of the Basic Law) is restricted in this respect [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

Operators of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 must make these facilities accessible to the employee or agent of the responsible authority, and must provide the necessary working personnel, tools, aids, especially fuels and drives, and documents, and allow the state authority to inspect the condition and operation of the facility at their own expense upon order of the responsible authority [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The official powers according to Paragraphs 1 to 3 also extend to checking whether certain substances or objects:
1.  are not or no longer to be considered waste, or
2.  are to be considered waste, other objects, or substances for which the contamination value stipulated in an ordinance according to § 95 Paragraph 1 is undercut [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

For the persons obligated to provide information according to this provision, § 55 of the Code of Criminal Procedure applies accordingly [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The knowledge and documents obtained according to Paragraphs 1 to 3 are not subject to §§ 93, 97, 105 Paragraph 1, § 111 Paragraph 5 in connection with § 105 Paragraph 1, and § 116 Paragraph 1 of the Fiscal Code. This does not apply if the tax authorities require the knowledge for the execution of a tax offense procedure and an associated tax assessment procedure, for which there is a mandatory public interest in prosecution, or if it concerns intentionally false statements by the person obligated to provide information or the persons acting for them [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 96 Eilverordnungen
In case of urgency following an emergency, the Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety can issue regulations according to §§ 93, 94, and 95 Paragraph 1, and the Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety or the ministry responsible for waste management regulations outside the scope of the Circular Economy Act or for regulations on the construction and operation of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, can issue regulations according to § 95 Paragraph 2 and 3 by ordinance without the consent of the Federal Council and without the agreement of the participating federal ministries (Emergency Ordinances), insofar as no corresponding regulations exist or the existing regulations are inadequate [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

Emergency ordinances expire at the latest six months after their entry into force. Their validity period can only be extended by an ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council and in agreement with the participating federal ministries. Emergency ordinances that change existing regulations must be repealed immediately if requested by the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 97 Gemeinsame Vorschriften für die Notfallpläne
Federal states shall draw up emergency plans according to §§ 98, 99, 100, and 101. These emergency plans must present the planned appropriate reactions to possible emergencies based on specific reference scenarios. The emergency reactions to be presented include:
1.  Protective measures, which include:
    a) Measures to prevent or reduce exposure and contamination of man or environment, and
    b) Measures for medical treatment or prevention after an exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Other measures that are to be taken by the participating authorities and other organizations in an emergency to prevent or keep the adverse effects of the emergency for human health or the environment as low as possible, particularly measures for the examination, preparation, execution, monitoring, amendment, or repeal of protective measures, as well as cooperation and coordination in emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The emergency plans shall enable the authorities and organizations participating in the emergency response to make coordinated decisions immediately and to carry out the appropriate measures in time during an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The authorities responsible for drawing up the emergency plans:
1.  must coordinate their emergency plans to the extent necessary for the preparation of a coordinated emergency response, and
2.  must endeavor, within their areas of responsibility, to coordinate their emergency plans with other Member States of the European Union and the European Atomic Community, as well as with third countries according to the principles of reciprocity and equivalence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

A select circle of representatives from science, the affected economy, environmental associations, municipalities and municipal associations, organizations participating in emergency prevention and response, other interested parties, and the highest state authorities responsible for the respective area shall be invited to the drafts of the federal emergency plans, the ordinances according to §§ 93 to 95 and 117 Paragraph 1, and the drafts of essential changes to these emergency plans and ordinances. Sentence 1 does not apply to the enactment of emergency ordinances according to §§ 93 to 95 and 117 Paragraph 2, or to the enactment, changes, and additions of ordinances and emergency plans for an emergency according to §§ 94 and 111. A select circle of interested parties shall be invited to the drafts of the general and special emergency plans of the states and essential changes to these emergency plans. The states can restrict the invitation to relevant state- or area-specific concretizations or additions to the optimized protection strategies and measures provided for in the federal emergency plans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 98 Allgemeiner Notfallplan des Bundes
The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety assesses possible emergency exposure situations. The Federal Government issues a general federal emergency plan based on its proposal. The general federal emergency plan is adopted as a general administrative regulation with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The general federal emergency plan must include:
1.  Reference scenarios that serve as the basis for the planning of emergency responses for the Federation and the states, and
2.  The following general plans for possible emergencies within or outside the scope of this law:
    a) the plans of the Federation,
    b) the plans of the European Union and the European Atomic Community, their Member States, and third countries, and
    c) the plans of international organizations and the plans within the framework of international treaties [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The general federal emergency plan particularly includes:
1.  Strategies optimally adapted to the respective reference scenario for the protection of the population and the emergency services, which also consider particularly vulnerable persons (optimized protection strategies), and
2.  The further elements listed in Annex 5 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The general federal emergency plan may also contain information on the emergency plans of the states, municipalities, and municipal associations, as well as of other organizations participating in emergency prevention and response, or summarize these emergency plans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 99 Besondere Notfallpläne des Bundes
On proposal of the federal ministries responsible for the respective subject areas, the Federal Government supplements and concretizes the general federal emergency plan with special federal emergency plans. The special federal emergency plans are adopted as general administrative regulations with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The special federal emergency plans must present the plans particularly for the following areas of application:
1.  For disaster protection, general danger control and assistance, as well as for medical treatment and prevention after an exposure of the population and the emergency services [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  For drinking water extraction and supply [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  For the production of plant and animal products, for food, animal feed, necessities, cosmetic products, and products within the meaning of § 2 Number 1 of the Tobacco Products Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  For medicinal products and their starting materials, as well as for medical devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5.  For other products, objects, and substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6.  For the transport of goods [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7.  For the cross-border transport of persons, vehicles, goods, and luggage [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
8.  For contaminated areas, especially for contaminated properties and waters [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
9.  For the disposal of waste and the disposal of wastewater, as well as for the construction and operation of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The special federal emergency plans particularly include the elements listed in Annex 6. § 98 Paragraph 3 Sentence 2 applies accordingly [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 100 Allgemeine und besondere Notfallpläne der Länder
The states shall draw up general and special emergency plans. These state emergency plans supplement and concretize the general federal emergency plan and the special federal emergency plans of the Federation, insofar as the states are responsible for the planning or execution of protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 101 Externe Notfallpläne für ortsfeste Anlagen oder Einrichtungen mit besonderem Gefahrenpotential
The authorities responsible for disaster protection or public safety shall draw up special protection plans (external emergency plans) according to their state law for the environment of nuclear facilities, facilities according to § 9a Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 second sentence of the Atomic Energy Act, facilities for the generation of ionizing radiation, or facilities according to § 5 Paragraph 12 of this Act, insofar as emergencies in the facility or facility can lead to severe health impairments for a non-negligible number of persons in the vicinity of the facility or facility [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The external emergency plans supplement and concretize the plans contained in the general and special emergency plans of the Federation and the states. They take into account the local conditions as well as the procedures and precautions of the radiation protection officer for the internal emergency protection of the facility [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 102 Notfallübungen
The authorities and organizations participating in the emergency response according to the federal and state emergency plans, as well as those responsible for the training and advanced training of emergency personnel according to § 115 Paragraph 1, shall regularly conduct emergency drills.

The emergency drills must be appropriately differentiated according to the type of the drill, scope, emergency scenarios, and participants. In particular, the organizational precautions for the emergency response and the exchange of information and cooperation of the participating authorities, organizations, and radiation protection officers during:
a) situation assessment and situation evaluation,
b) the coordination of the decisions of the responsible authorities, and
c) the execution of appropriate protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 103 Überprüfung und Änderung der Notfallpläne
The federal and state emergency plans must be regularly reviewed and, if necessary, amended, taking into account the experiences from emergency drills, findings from emergencies domestically or abroad, and changes in the state of science and the legal situation.

The information supplementing the emergency plans, such as the contact details of the responsible authorities and participating organizations or the directories of applicable legal provisions, must be updated and regularly reviewed upon changes. The cut-off dates for the reviews must be stipulated in the emergency plans [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 104 Beschaffung von Schutzwirkstoffen
The authority responsible according to § 192 Paragraph 1 shall procure protective agents to the extent required for the supply of the population in the federal territory in the event of possible emergencies. It shall make these protective agents available to the states for stockpiling, distribution, and disposal to the population.

Protective agents are medicinal products:
1.  suitable for preventing the uptake of radioactive iodine into the human thyroid gland, or
2.  suitable for preventing the uptake of radionuclides into the human body or for removing radionuclides from the human body [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 105 Information der Bevölkerung über die Schutzmaßnahmen und Empfehlungen für das Verhalten bei möglichen Notfällen
The responsible bodies of the Federation publish the federal emergency plans according to § 10 of the Environmental Information Act.

The responsible bodies of the Federation:
1.  inform the population in an appropriate manner according to § 10 of the Environmental Information Act about:
    a) the basic concepts of radioactivity and the effects of radioactivity on man and the environment, and
    b) the emergencies considered in the emergency plans and their consequences for the population and the environment, and
2.  provide the population with recommendations for behavior in possible emergencies.

The states inform the population about the matters mentioned in Paragraph 2 Number 1 according to state law and provide the population with recommendations for behavior in possible emergencies that supplement and concretize the recommendations of Paragraph 2 Number 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The information and behavior recommendations must be updated regularly and upon substantial changes and published without request. They must be constantly accessible to the public [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 106 Radiologisches Lagezentrum des Bundes
The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety shall establish a federal radiological situation center [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
