This summary details the seventh part of the *Gesetz zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (StrlSchG) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]. This section primarily addresses emergency preparedness, emergency response, and the management of contaminated materials and information during radiological incidents.

## § 89 Verordnungsermächtigungen zu der Sicherheit von Strahlungsquellen
The Federal Government is authorized to issue ordinances concerning the protection of people from the harmful effects of ionizing radiation and the control and securing of radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]. These ordinances can specify:
1.  How and where records must be kept regarding the generation, acquisition, possession, location, release, and other whereabouts of radioactive substances, and regarding measurements of dose and dose rates [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  The requirements for the securing and storage of radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  The requirements for the maintenance and testing of equipment, devices, and other facilities, and who must perform this maintenance and testing [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  The requirements for leak testing of enclosed radioactive substances and who must perform the leak testing [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5.  Which radiation measuring devices must be used and what requirements apply to them [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6.  Which areas, rooms, devices, facilities, containers, enclosures, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, and which type-approved facilities must be marked, how and under what conditions the marking must occur, and in which cases markings must be removed [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7.  The requirements for the release of radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
8.  The requirements for the return of high-radioactive sources [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
9.  In which cases X-ray rooms or irradiation rooms must be used for activities involving radiation sources, and what requirements apply to such rooms [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
10. Which persons are permitted to apply radiation or technically perform the application of radiation during activities involving radiation sources, how and when persons must be instructed during such activities, and what documents must be available when performing these activities. Records must be kept and presented to the authority upon request [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
11. What further precautions and monitoring measures must be taken to protect individuals and the general public, and what such measures must be [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
12. What further record-keeping, storage, notification, submission, and presentation obligations exist in connection with the duties according to numbers 1 to 11 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The ordinance can also specify the provisions of the ordinance for which the radiation protection officer must ensure compliance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 90 Verordnungsermächtigung für Pflichten, Aufgaben und Befugnisse bei Vorkommnissen
The Federal Government is authorized to issue ordinances concerning the duties of the radiation protection officer, as well as the official tasks and powers in the context of planned exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]. Specifically, the ordinance can stipulate:
1.  What measures the radiation protection officer must initiate to keep exposures during such an incident as low as possible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  What measures the radiation protection officer must take to prevent such incidents in the future [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  How the radiation protection officer must record and investigate an incident, and for what period they must retain these records [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  How the radiation protection officer must report an incident to the supervisory authority, and must report information and findings about the causes and effects of the incident, as well as measures for remediation or limitation of the effects, and measures for preventing incidents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5.  How the supervisory authority records, checks, and evaluates reports according to number 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6.  How the Federal Office for Radiation Protection maintains a central body for the collection, processing, and evaluation of information and findings about incidents in connection with the application of radioactive substances or ionizing radiation to humans, and how the supervisory authority and the central body transmit information and findings about incidents and their evaluation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7.  Under what conditions and in what manner the supervisory authority and the central body publish information and findings about incidents [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

**Data Collection for Incidents:**
For an incident falling under the ordinance of paragraph 1, the radiation protection officer must immediately record the name, first name, date and place of birth, gender, and address, as well as data on the exposure and the health consequences of the exposed person. If the radiation protection officer must report the incident to the competent authority and measures are required to protect the exposed person, the data must be transmitted immediately to the competent authority. The data must be secured against unauthorized access by technical and organizational measures. In other cases than in sentence 2, the data must be transmitted to the competent authority upon request. The data must be retained for 30 years and must be deleted immediately after the expiration of this period [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 91 Verordnungsermächtigung für Informationspflichten des Herstellers oder Lieferanten von Geräten
The Federal Government is authorized to issue ordinances stipulating that the manufacturer or supplier of facilities for generating ionizing radiation, X-ray facilities, scatter radiation, irradiation devices, and other equipment, devices, and facilities used in connection with activities, must provide the radiation protection officer with information about these devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]. This can specify:
1.  When the manufacturer or supplier must provide information to the radiation protection officer for which of the mentioned devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  What details and documents must be provided [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  For what purposes the documents must be suitable and what requirements they must meet [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  That the information must also be available to the person intending to become the radiation protection officer [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 92 Notfallschutzgrundsätze
The provisions of the following paragraphs (Emergency Protection Principles) must be considered when assessing dangers that may arise during emergencies due to ionizing radiation in the following cases [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]:
1.  When issuing, reviewing, and amending emergency plans and ordinances under this chapter and § 117 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  During the emergency response by the competent authorities and the authorities and organizations assisting in the emergency response based on this law, the ordinances mentioned in number 1, and federal and state legal provisions for the defense against dangers applicable to human health, the environment, or public safety, insofar as they are also applicable to radiological dangers, and directly applicable EU and Euratom acts that do not make conclusive provisions for member states regarding radiological dangers [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The reference values set in this law and in the ordinances issued based on this law for the protection of the population and the emergency services during emergencies should be kept as low as possible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

During emergencies, the exposure of the population and the emergency services, as well as the contamination of the environment, must be kept as low as possible, considering the state of science and all circumstances of the respective emergency, through appropriate measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 93 Referenzwerte für den Schutz der Bevölkerung
For the protection of the population, a reference value of 100 millisieverts for the effective dose applies when planning protection measures and making decisions about their implementation in an emergency. This is the dose that affected persons would receive within one year from the emergency through all exposure pathways if the planned protection measures were implemented [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety is authorized to specify procedures and assumptions for estimating the extent to which this reference value is kept below, maintained, or exceeded by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety is authorized to specify supplementary appropriate reference values for organ equivalent doses for possible emergencies or for an already occurred emergency by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council. This applies particularly to facilitate cooperation with other member states of the European Union and the European Atomic Community or third countries in the protection of the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety is authorized to specify a lower reference value for the effective dose, relative to one year or a single exposure, for an already occurred emergency by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 94 Dosiswerte und Kontaminationswerte für den Schutz der Bevölkerung
The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety shall specify dose values by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council, which serve as a radiological criterion for the appropriateness of the following protective measures:
1.  Call to stay in buildings [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Distribution of iodine tablets or call for the ingestion of iodine tablets [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  Evacuation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
These values relate to the dose that affected persons would receive in a specific period after the emergency without protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety is authorized to specify limit values for emergency contamination or dose rates by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council for possible emergencies, for an already occurred emergency, and for an exposure situation existing after an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]. These limits apply to:
1.  Individual persons of the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Drinking water [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  Food, feed, consumer goods, cosmetic products, and products within the meaning of § 2 Number 1 of the Tobacco Products Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  Medicines and their starting materials, as well as medical devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5.  Other products, objects, and substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6.  Vehicles, goods, or luggage [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7.  Contaminated areas, especially contaminated properties and waters, where exceeding these limits is assumed to pose a danger to individual persons of the population due to ionizing radiation. These limits serve the implementation of optimized protection strategies according to § 98 Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 Number 1 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 95 Bewirtschaftung von Abfällen, die infolge eines Notfalls kontaminiert sein können
The Federal Government shall specify contamination values for waste and other objects or substances that are contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency by ordinance with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]. If these contamination values are not exceeded, it is assumed that the necessary protection of man and environment against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation is ensured when managing this waste, as well as when constructing, operating, or using the facilities listed below, according to the Circular Economy Act and other federal laws applicable to waste and the ordinances based on these laws and ordinances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]:
1.  Facilities where this waste is disposed of [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Wastewater facilities that receive wastewater contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  Facilities where this waste or other objects or substances are stored, used, or treated, or can be stored, used, or treated, especially as fuel, raw material, material, precursor product, lubricating, dissolving, or other auxiliary agent [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 95a Auskunftsverlangen, Betretensrechte, Mitwirkungs- und Duldungspflichten
The authorities have the right to request information concerning waste and other objects or substances contaminated or potentially contaminated by an emergency, concerning the construction, operation, and use of the facilities listed in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, concerning properties where such waste, other objects or substances, or such facilities may be located, and concerning other objects or substances subject to supervision according to § 178 Sentence 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The persons obligated to provide information according to Paragraph 1 have the right to the authorities to inspect the properties and business/operating premises, and to conduct technical investigations and tests, including the determination of emissions and immissions, during usual business hours [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]. They are further obliged to allow entry to the business and operating properties and premises outside of usual business hours, and entry into residential premises, if necessary to prevent immediate dangers to public safety or order. The fundamental right to the inviolability of the home (Article 13 Paragraph 1 of the Basic Law) is restricted in this respect [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 96 Eilverordnungen
In case of urgency after an emergency, the Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety can issue ordinances (emergency ordinances) without the consent of the Federal Council and without the agreement of the involved federal ministries, concerning regulations according to §§ 93, 94, and 95 Paragraph 1, and the Federal Ministry responsible for waste management outside the scope of the Circular Economy Act or for regulations on the construction and operation of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 97 Gemeinsame Vorschriften für die Notfallpläne
Federal states shall develop emergency plans according to §§ 98, 99, 100, and 101. These plans must present the planned appropriate reactions to possible emergencies based on specific reference scenarios. The emergency reactions must include:
1.  Protective measures, which include:
    a) Measures to avoid or reduce exposure and contamination of man or environment, and
    b) Measures for medical treatment or prophylaxis after exposure,
2.  Other measures to be taken by the involved authorities and organizations to prevent or keep the adverse effects of the emergency on human health or the environment as low as possible, especially measures for checking, preparation, execution, monitoring, amendment, or repeal of protective measures, as well as cooperation and coordination in emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 98 Allgemeiner Notfallplan des Bundes
The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety assesses possible emergency exposure situations. The Federal Government issues a general federal emergency plan based on its proposal. This plan is adopted as a general administrative regulation with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The general federal emergency plan must include:
1.  Reference scenarios that serve as the basis for the planning of emergency responses for the Federation and the states, and
2.  The following general plans for possible emergencies within or outside the scope of this law:
    a) The plans of the Federation,
    b) The plans of the European Union and the European Atomic Community, their member states, and third states, and
    c) The plans of international organizations and the plans within the framework of international treaties [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 99 Besondere Notfallpläne des Bundes
The Federal Government supplements and specifies the general federal emergency plan with special federal emergency plans on the proposal of the federal ministries responsible for the respective subject areas. These plans are adopted as general administrative regulations with the consent of the Federal Council [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The special federal emergency plans must include plans, in particular, for the following application areas:
1.  Disaster protection, general danger control, and assistance, as well as medical treatment and prophylaxis after exposure of the population and the emergency services [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
2.  Drinking water extraction and supply [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
3.  The production of plant and animal products, food, feed, consumer goods, cosmetic products, and products within the meaning of § 2 Number 1 of the Tobacco Products Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
4.  Medicines and their starting materials, as well as medical devices [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
5.  Other products, objects, and substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
6.  The transport of goods [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
7.  Cross-border traffic of persons, vehicles, goods, and luggage [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
8.  Contaminated areas, especially contaminated properties and waters [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
9.  The disposal of waste and the removal of wastewater, as well as the construction and operation of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 100 Allgemeine und besondere Notfallpläne der Länder
The states shall develop general and special emergency plans. These state emergency plans supplement and specify the general federal emergency plan and the special federal emergency plans, insofar as the states are responsible for the planning or execution of protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 101 Externe Notfallpläne für ortsfeste Anlagen oder Einrichtungen mit besonderem Gefahrenpotential
The authorities responsible for disaster protection or public safety shall develop special protection plans (external emergency plans) for the surroundings of nuclear facilities, facilities according to § 9a Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 second part of the Atomic Energy Act, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, or facilities according to § 5 Paragraph 12 of this law, insofar as emergencies in the facility or establishment could lead to severe health impairments for a non-negligible number of people in the surroundings of the facility or establishment [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

These external emergency plans supplement and specify the planning contained in the general and special plans of the Federation and the states. They take into account local conditions as well as the procedures and precautions of the radiation protection officer for the internal emergency protection of the facility [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 102 Notfallübungen
The authorities and organizations involved in the emergency response according to the federal and state emergency plans, as well as the responsible parties for the training and advanced training of emergency personnel according to § 115 Paragraph 1, shall conduct regular emergency drills [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The emergency drills must be appropriately differentiated according to the type of the drill, scope, emergency scenarios, and participants. Specifically, the following must be tested and practiced:
1.  The organizational precautions for the emergency response, and
2.  The exchange of information and cooperation of the authorities, organizations, and radiation protection officers involved in the emergency response regarding:
    a) Situation assessment and evaluation,
    b) Coordination of the decisions of the competent authorities, and
    c) The execution of appropriate protective measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 103 Überprüfung und Änderung der Notfallpläne
The federal and state emergency plans must be regularly reviewed and, if necessary, amended, taking into account the experiences from emergency drills, findings from emergencies domestically or abroad, and changes in the state of science and law [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 104 Beschaffung von Schutzwirkstoffen
The authority responsible according to § 192 Paragraph 1 shall procure protective agents to the extent required for the supply of the population in the federal territory in the event of possible emergencies. It shall make these protective agents available to the states for stockpiling, distribution, and release to the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

Protective agents are medicines:
1.  suitable for preventing the uptake of radioactive iodine into the human thyroid, or
2.  suitable for preventing the uptake of radionuclides into the human body or for removing radionuclides from the human body [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 105 Information der Bevölkerung über die Schutzmaßnahmen und Empfehlungen für das Verhalten bei möglichen Notfällen
The competent federal bodies shall publish the federal emergency plans according to § 10 of the Environmental Information Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The competent federal bodies shall:
1.  Inform the population in an appropriate manner according to § 10 of the Environmental Information Act about:
    a) The basic concepts of radioactivity and the effects of radioactivity on man and the environment, and
    b) The emergencies considered in the emergency plans and their consequences for the population and the environment, and
    c) Planned measures for warning and protection of the population in the event of possible emergencies, and
2.  Provide the population with recommendations for behavior in the event of possible emergencies [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

The states shall inform the population about the matters mentioned in Paragraph 2 Number 1 according to state law and provide recommendations for behavior in the event of possible emergencies that supplement and specify the recommendations of Paragraph 2 Number 2 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].

## § 106 Radiologisches Lagezentrum des Bundes
The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety shall establish a federal radiological situation center [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md].
