The *Atomgesetz* (AtG), or Law for the Peaceful Use of Nuclear Energy and Protection Against Its Dangers, is a comprehensive German federal law governing the nuclear energy sector. This summary details specific regulatory aspects concerning licensing, decommissioning, waste management, and financial provisions as outlined in the source text [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Licensing and Authorization Requirements (§ 7)

The source details stringent requirements for the issuance of operating permits for nuclear facilities:

*   **General Permit Conditions:** A permit may only be issued if:
    1.  There are no facts suggesting concerns regarding the reliability of the applicant or the responsible persons for the facility's construction, operation, and supervision, and these persons possess the required expertise [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
    2.  It is guaranteed that other persons working at the facility possess the necessary knowledge regarding safe operation, potential dangers, and applicable protective measures [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
    3.  The necessary precautions against damage from the construction and operation of the facility, required by the state of science and technology, have been taken [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
    4.  The required precautions for fulfilling statutory compensation obligations have been taken [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
    5.  The required protection against disruptions or other third-party influences is guaranteed [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
    6.  Overriding public interests, particularly concerning environmental impacts, do not contradict the choice of the site [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Decommissioning and Dismantling:** The decommissioning, safe enclosure, or dismantling of a facility requires a permit, similar to the initial operation permit [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Stakeholder Involvement:** The permit procedure must involve all federal, state, municipal, and other territorial authorities whose jurisdiction is affected [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Waste Management and Disposal (§ 9a)

The management of radioactive residues and waste generated from nuclear facilities is highly regulated:

*   **Waste Handling:** Anyone handling radioactive substances outside licensed facilities, or operating facilities generating ionizing radiation, must ensure that resulting radioactive residues and dismantled components are safely recycled or disposed of as radioactive waste (direct final disposal) according to the specified purposes [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Disposal Proof:** Operators must provide proof of adequate precautions for radioactive residues and waste, including those from the processing of irradiated nuclear fuel, ensuring they are disposed of as radioactive waste [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Intermediate Storage:** Operators must ensure that safe storage for irradiated nuclear fuel and waste requiring retrieval from processing facilities is guaranteed in interim storage facilities until delivery to a final disposal facility [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **National Infrastructure:** The states are responsible for establishing state collection points for radioactive waste generated in their territory, while the federal government must establish facilities for the securing and final disposal of radioactive waste [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Compensation and Financial Provisions (§ 7e, § 7g)

The Act addresses financial equalization and compensation related to changes in nuclear energy policy:

*   **Compensation Claims:** Specific compensation claims are detailed for investments rendered worthless due to legislative changes, including claims for electricity quantities from specific power plants (e.g., RWE Nuclear GmbH claim of 860,398 million Euro for 25,900,000 GWh from Mülheim-Kärlich) [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Public Law Contracts:** Federal ministries are authorized to conclude public law contracts with energy companies to regulate rights and obligations concerning electricity transfers, compensation payments, and adjustments to compensation regulations [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Procedural Details

*   **Preliminary Decisions (Vorbescheid):** A preliminary decision can be issued regarding specific questions necessary for obtaining a facility permit, such as the choice of site [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Third-Party Objections:** If a facility permit or preliminary decision has become final, third parties can no longer raise objections based on facts that could have been raised during the initial review process [AtG.md [Teil 2/5].md].
