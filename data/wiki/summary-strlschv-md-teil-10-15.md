This document provides excerpts from the *Verordnung zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (Strahlenschutzverordnung - StrlSchV), which is the detailed ordinance implementing the German Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG). The content heavily focuses on transitional provisions, technical requirements, and specific exemption/limitation values effective around the end of 2018 and the start of 2021.

## Transitional Provisions for Permits and Authorizations
The ordinance details how previous authorizations continue to apply under the new legal framework:
*   **Building Permit Approvals (§ 185):** Building permit approved devices containing radioactive substances must be checked for integrity and tightness every ten years after the expiration of the original permit, unless shorter periods were specified. If the permit expired more than ten years before December 31, 2018, the check must occur by December 31, 2021.
*   **Residues (§ 186):** A release granted under the previous version of the StrlSchV (valid until December 31, 2018) remains valid under § 29 if the competent authority grants its consent by June 30, 2019.
*   **Release (§ 187):** Releases granted under the previous StrlSchV (valid until December 31, 2018) remain valid under the new sections, with specific rules applying to the values used (e.g., requiring adherence to values from Anlage 4 Tabelle 1 Spalte 3 starting January 1, 2021).

## Professional Qualifications and Organization
The document outlines requirements for personnel and facilities:
*   **Operational Organization (§ 188):** For facilities operating before December 31, 2018, a contract must be concluded for the operational organization of radiation protection by December 31, 2019. Furthermore, radiation protection instructions must be created or updated by January 1, 2020.
*   **Required Expertise (§ 189):** The ordinance confirms that expertise acquired before specific dates (e.g., August 1, 2001, or July 1, 2002) remains valid. For instance, qualifications established before December 31, 2018, are recognized as continuing until December 31, 2026, for certain courses.

## Dose and Emission Limits (Anlage 4)
Appendix 4 details various exemption and limit values:
*   **Surface Contamination:** For surface contamination measurements (§ 57 and § 58), the sum of the ratio of existing activity ($A_{s,i}$) to the respective surface contamination values ($O_i$) must not exceed 1, provided the proportion of unconsidered nuclides does not exceed 10 percent [StrlSchV.md [Teil 10/15].md].
*   **General Limits:** The table provides specific values for various radionuclides (e.g., H-3: 1 E+9 Bq/g; C-14: 1 E+7 Bq/g; Ar-37: 1 E+8 Bq/g).

## Exemptions and Non-Justified Activities (Anlage 1 & 3)
*   **Non-Justified Activities (Anlage 1):** Lists activities that are not justified, including the use of radioactive sources for leak detection (unless collected) and the use of X-rays for checking clothing/shoes [StrlSchV.md [Teil 10/15].md].
*   **Exempt Activities (Anlage 3):** Details activities that are exempt from permitting or notification, such as handling substances where the specific activity does not exceed 500 Mikrobecquerel per gram (Part A) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 10/15].md].
