The *Atomgesetz* (AtG), or Law for the Peaceful Use of Nuclear Energy and Protection Against its Dangers, is a foundational German federal law governing the nuclear sector. This summary details various sections of the law concerning administrative oversight, facility operation, liability, and financial provisions [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Administrative Oversight and Authority
The law grants significant powers to the supervisory authority (*Aufsichtsbehörde*). This authority can order the elimination of any state that contradicts the law or its implementing regulations, or that poses dangers to life, health, or property due to the effect of ionizing radiation. Specifically, the authority can mandate:
1.  What protective measures must be implemented [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
2.  That radioactive substances are stored or kept at a location determined by the authority [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
3.  That the handling of radioactive substances, or the establishment and operation of facilities mentioned in § 7, is temporarily or permanently ceased, if a required permit is not issued or is legally revoked [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].

The law also details the responsibilities of specific federal bodies:
*   **§ 23d (Authority for Nuclear Waste Disposal):** This body is responsible for the planning and permitting (§ 9b) and revocation thereof, supervision of federal facilities (§ 9a Abs. 3 Satz 1) and the Asse II shaft facility (§ 19 Absatz 5), issuing mining law permits, and managing the state storage of nuclear fuel [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **§ 24 (State Authority Jurisdiction):** The states execute the remaining administrative tasks on behalf of the federal government. The Federal Railway Authority (*Eisenbahn-Bundesamt*) oversees the transport of radioactive substances by rail and cable car, unless jurisdiction is assigned under § 23d [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Facility Operation and Safety Assessment
The law mandates continuous safety improvements for nuclear facilities:
*   **§ 19a (Review, Assessment, and Continuous Improvement of Nuclear Technical Facilities):** Operators of facilities for the commercial generation of electricity from nuclear fuel must conduct a safety review and assessment, and continuously improve the nuclear safety of the facility. These results must be submitted to the supervisory authority by a specified date, and subsequently every ten years [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   For other nuclear technical facilities, a review and assessment of nuclear safety must be conducted every ten years, and the results submitted to the supervisory authority [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   The assessments must include checking measures to prevent accidents and mitigate accident consequences, including the physical barriers and administrative precautions that must fail before life, health, and property are damaged by ionizing radiation [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Liability Provisions
The AtG establishes comprehensive liability rules for nuclear damage:
*   **§ 25 (Liability for Nuclear Facilities):** If a nuclear damage results from an event originating from a nuclear facility, the owner is liable according to this law, supplementing the Paris Convention and the Joint Protocol [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **§ 26 (Liability in Other Cases):** If damage is caused by the effect of a nuclear fission process, radioactive substances, or ionizing radiation from a facility, the owner of the affected substance or facility is liable to compensate for the resulting damage, unless the damage could not have been avoided even with the utmost care by the owner and the persons acting on their behalf, and the damage is not based on a defect in the protective facilities or their operation [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **§ 31 (Liability Limits):** The liability of the owner of a nuclear facility under the Paris Convention is unlimited in sum. If the damage occurs in another state, the liability is limited to the amount that the other state provides for the replacement of nuclear damage in relation to the Federal Republic of Germany [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Financial and Administrative Costs
The law regulates the financial aspects of nuclear activities:
*   **§ 21 (Costs):** Fees and expenditures are charged for decisions regarding applications under various sections of the law, including those related to the state storage of nuclear fuel [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **§ 21a (Costs for Facilities):** Costs are charged for the use of facilities under § 9a Abs. 3 [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **§ 21b (Contributions):** Contributions are levied to cover the necessary costs for planning, acquiring land and rights, research, exploration, maintenance, and construction of federal facilities for the orderly disposal of radioactive waste [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Public Information and Review
The law mandates transparency:
*   **§ 24a (Public Information):** Competent authorities must inform the public about the intended operation of nuclear facilities and about reportable incidents and accidents [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **§ 24b (Self-assessment and International Review):** The responsible federal ministry must conduct a self-assessment of the law, enforcement, and organizational framework for nuclear safety and the safe disposal of spent fuel and radioactive waste. This process must be repeated at least every ten years, and the results are reported to EU member states and the European Commission [AtG.md [Teil 4/5].md].
