# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 8/13)

This section of the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) details provisions concerning the transition from acute emergency situations to ongoing, existing exposure situations, and provides comprehensive regulations regarding the protection against Radon exposure.

## Transition to Existing Exposure Situations (§ 118, § 119)

When a regional or transregional emergency stabilizes, the focus shifts to managing the resulting *existing* exposure situation.

* **Assessment (§ 118):** The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety (BMU) assesses whether the effective dose in the affected population is expected to exceed 1 millisievert (mSv) in the following year. If so, comprehensive reviews are required to determine if protective, remediation, or other measures are necessary to ensure that the effective dose can be reduced as soon as possible below the 20 millisievert limit, and to establish a reference value for the effective dose received from all exposure pathways [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].
* **Management (§ 119):** In an existing situation, the provisions of §§ 92 and 106 to 111 apply. The reference values are those set out in § 118 (Abs. 4 or 6), and the plans are those set out in § 118 (Abs. 2, 3, and 5) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].

## Public Information and Recommendations (§ 120)

* **Information Flow:** The BMU informs the affected population about an overregional or regional existing exposure situation. State authorities supplement and concretize the federal information and behavioral recommendations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].
* **Local Situations:** For local existing exposure situations, state authorities inform the population about the reference values (§ 118 Abs. 6) and the protective, remediation, and other measures taken or planned, providing behavioral recommendations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].

## Protection Against Radon (Sections 121–129)

The Act establishes specific rules for managing Radon-222-Aktivitätskonzentration in buildings and workplaces.

### General Provisions
* **Area Designation (§ 121):** The competent authority must designate areas where the average Radon-222-Aktivitätskonzentration in the air is expected to exceed the reference values (§ 124 or § 126) in a significant number of buildings with occupied rooms or workplaces. This designation must be reviewed at least every ten years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].
* **Action Plan (§ 122):** The BMU creates a Radon action plan, developed with the participation of the states, outlining measures to manage long-term risks from Radon ingress from soil, building products, or water [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].
* **Building Measures (§ 123):** Those constructing a building with occupied rooms or workplaces must take suitable measures to prevent or significantly impede Radon ingress from the building ground. This is considered fulfilled if standard moisture protection measures are maintained, and additionally, the measures specified in the relevant ordinance are followed in designated areas [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].

### Radon in Occupied Rooms (§ 124, § 125)
* **Reference Value (§ 124):** The reference value for the average Radon-222-Aktivitätskonzentration in the air in occupied rooms is set at 300 Becquerel per cubic meter [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].
* **Public Information (§ 125):** The BMU and state authorities must inform the population about Radon exposure risks in occupied rooms, the importance of Radon measurements, and available technical means for reducing concentrations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].

### Radon at Workplaces (§ 126, § 127, § 128)
* **Reference Value (§ 126):** The reference value for the average Radon-222-Aktivitätskonzentration in the air at workplaces is 300 Becquerel per cubic meter [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].
* **Measurement (§ 127):** The person responsible for a workplace must commission measurements if the workplace is located in the ground or basement floor of a building in a designated area, or if the type of workplace belongs to a specified field [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md]. Measurements must be recorded, and records must be kept until the end of employment or until new measurements are available [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].
* **Reduction (§ 128):** If the Radon-222-Aktivitätskonzentration exceeds the reference value at a workplace, the responsible person must immediately implement measures to reduce it. The success of these measures must be verified by a measurement within 30 months of the exceedance being known [StrlSchG.md [Teil 8/13].md].
