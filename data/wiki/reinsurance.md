# Reinsurance

Reinsurance is the practice where an insurance company (the ceding company) transfers a portion of its risk to another insurance company (the reinsurer) in exchange for a premium. This mechanism is fundamental to the global insurance and financial stability, allowing insurers to underwrite risks that would otherwise exceed their capital capacity.

## Function and Purpose

The primary function of reinsurance is risk mitigation and capital management. By transferring risk, primary insurers can:
1.  **Increase Capacity:** Underwrite large, complex, or high-severity risks (e.g., major natural catastrophes or systemic cyber events) that would otherwise exceed their single-entity risk appetite.
2.  **Stabilize Profitability:** Smooth out volatile underwriting results by distributing extreme losses across multiple capital bases [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]].
3.  **Optimize Capital Use:** Allow insurers to maintain a strong Solvency II ratio by backing up large exposures with reinsurance treaties [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]].

## Market Structure and Players

The reinsurance market is highly specialized and tends to be oligopolistic in peak-capacity and specialty segments [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]]. Major global players include Munich Re, Swiss Re, and Hannover Re [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]].

### Types of Reinsurance
Reinsurance covers various lines of risk, including:
*   **Property & Casualty (P&C) Reinsurance:** Covers risks related to physical damage from perils like natural catastrophes (nat-cat) [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]].
*   **Life & Health (L&H) Reinsurance:** Manages mortality and morbidity risk across large populations [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]].
*   **Global Specialty Insurance (GSI):** Covers niche, high-severity risks such as marine, aviation, and cyber liabilities [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]].

## Market Dynamics and Trends

### Pricing Cycles
The market is sensitive to pricing cycles. Periods of high loss frequency and severity (hard markets) lead to higher reinsurance pricing and improved underwriting margins for reinsurers [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]]. Conversely, soft markets can lead to pricing pressure, as seen during the January and April 2026 renewals where prices declined 2.5% to 3.1% [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]].

### Emerging Trends
1.  **Cyber Risk:** Cyber reinsurance is a rapidly growing segment, with the global market projected to expand from USD 15 billion in 2025 to USD 28 billion by 2030 [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]].
2.  **Alternative Capital:** Alternative risk transfer markets, such as catastrophe bonds (cat bonds) and Insurance-Linked Securities (ILS), compete for nat-cat capacity, though traditional reinsurance remains dominant for complex, long-tail risks [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 1/2]].
