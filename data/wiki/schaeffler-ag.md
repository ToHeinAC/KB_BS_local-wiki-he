# Schaeffler AG

Schaeffler AG is a German multinational corporation specializing in motion technology and industrial components. The company has undergone a significant strategic transformation, evolving from a traditional bearing specialist into a major player in the electrification sector.

## Corporate Overview
*   **Headquarters**: Herzogenaurach, Germany [^4].
*   **Global Presence**: The company operates in **55 countries** across **250+ locations**[^5].
*   **Workforce**: It employs approximately **110,000 people** globally[^5].
*   **Market Cap**: As of the overview date, the market capitalization was approximately **€9.5 billion**[^2].
*   **Ticker/ISIN**: The stock trades under ticker **SHA0 (Xetra)** with ISIN **DE000SHA0100**[^1][^2].

## Business Structure and Strategy
The core business model is structured around four primary divisions, following the merger with Vitesco Technologies (completed October 2024): [^9][^6]
1.  **E-Mobility**: This division focuses on electric drives, controls, mechatronics, and e-axles, representing over 40% of the automotive revenue.
2.  **Powertrain & Chassis**: Covers traditional engine/transmission systems and chassis components for ICE, hybrid, and EV vehicles.
3.  **Vehicle Lifetime Solutions**: Focuses on the aftermarket, including spare parts and repair solutions.
4.  **Bearings & Industrial**: Manages industrial bearings, linear guides, and components for wind, rail, and robotics applications.

The merger with Vitesco Technologies is central to the company's current strategy, transforming it into an "electrification powerhouse" and providing strong mechanical heritage in EV bearings and e-drives[^7][^8]. The company anticipates realizing annual synergies of **€600M by 2029**[^6][^7][^8].

## Market Position and Moat
Schaeffler AG maintains a position targeting premium engineered segments, rather than leading global bearing volume, which is dominated by SKF[^13].

*   **Intangible Assets**: The company benefits from strong brands (FAG, INA, LuK) and an IP portfolio spanning over 75 years[^11][^10].
*   **Competitive Edge**: High switching costs exist for Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) due to the integration of complex systems[^12].
*   **Threats**: Key threats include price pressure from Chinese EV suppliers (such as BYD, C&U, Wanxiang) in the EV component space[^13][^14], and the necessity to increase R&D spending to match competitors like Bosch and ZF[^13].

## Investment Summary
The investment thesis is based on the successful execution of the Vitesco merger and the diversification into high-growth areas like e-mobility and industrial automation. While the E-Mobility segment is projected to be loss-making (-14% to -17% margin) in 2025[^17], the improving EBIT margin and attractive dividend yield (~4.8% in 2026) provide a moderate case for investment, particularly over a 5-year horizon, provided the debt load can be managed and synergies realized.
