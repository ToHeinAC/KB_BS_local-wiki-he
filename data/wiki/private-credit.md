# Private Credit

Private credit refers to debt financing provided directly to private companies or assets, bypassing traditional public capital markets such as stock exchanges or corporate bond markets [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 2/2].md].

## Investment Scope
For large financial institutions like reinsurers, private credit represents a significant asset class. Munich Re, for example, has reported investments in private credit amounting to as much as €2.5 billion ($2.9 billion) [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 2/2].md].

## Characteristics
Unlike publicly traded bonds, private credit investments are often customized, providing direct exposure to specific underlying assets or industries. This structure allows investors to potentially achieve different risk/return profiles compared to liquid public market instruments [Munich Re (MUV2) — Full Deep-Dive Investment Report.md [Teil 2/2].md].
