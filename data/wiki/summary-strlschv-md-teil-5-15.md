The *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) is a detailed administrative regulation concerning the protection against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, supplementing the main *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG). This source summary details various mandatory procedures for radiation protection, including record keeping, equipment maintenance, and environmental monitoring.

## Documentation and Record Keeping (§ 86, § 33 Abs. 1)
The Radiation Protection Officer (*Strahlenschutzverantwortliche*) must ensure detailed record keeping regarding substances for which conformity with a release certificate has been established. This includes:
*   Bookkeeping of the substances, specifying:
    *   The determinations made according to Annexes 4 and 8, particularly the specific activity, radionuclides, dilution mass, and dilution area.
    *   The mass of the substances.
    *   The method of free measurement and the time of determination.
*   Annual reporting to the competent authority regarding:
    *   The mass of the substances.
    *   The respective type of release according to § 35, § 36, or § 37 (1).
    *   The actual remaining amount in the case of a specific release for disposal and a specific release of metal scrap for recycling.

Furthermore, records concerning substances must be retained for 30 years from the time of determination (§ 33 Abs. 1 Nr. 1) and must be deposited with the competent authority or handed over to a designated body if the activity ceases before the retention period expires [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].

## Storage and Security (§ 87)
The *Strahlenschutzverantwortliche* must ensure that:
1.  Radioactive substances whose activity exceeds the release limit of Annex 4 Table 1 Column 2, and whose specific activity exceeds the release limit of Annex 4 Table 1 Column 3, are secured against loss, misuse, and access by unauthorized persons [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].
2.  Radioactive substances whose activity exceeds the release limit of Annex 4 Table 1 Column 2, and whose specific activity exceeds this limit by a hundred percent, are additionally stored in protected rooms or containment vessels until they are processed or used [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].
3.  Nuclear fuels are stored such that no critical state can arise during storage [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].

## Maintenance and Inspection (§ 88, § 97)
The StrlSchV mandates regular technical inspections for radiation-generating equipment:
*   **General Equipment:** Facilities for generating ionizing radiation, irradiation devices, and devices for gamma radiography must be maintained at least annually and inspected between maintenance periods by an expert designated according to § 172 (1) No. 3 of the Radiation Protection Act for technical safety, safety, and radiation protection [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].
*   **X-ray Equipment:** X-ray facilities must be inspected at least every five years by an expert designated according to § 172 (1) No. 1 of the Radiation Protection Act, specifically for technical safety, safety, and radiation protection [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].
*   **General Requirement:** The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that the operating manual is kept ready for:
    *   Facilities for generating ionizing radiation.
    *   X-ray facilities.
    *   Irradiators and devices containing contained radioactive substances [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].

## Environmental Monitoring and Dose Limits (§ 99, § 102, § 103)
The regulation sets specific limits for derived emissions into the environment:
*   **Derived Dose Limit:** For planning, construction, operation, decommissioning, and safe containment of nuclear technical facilities, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, and related installations, the limit for the effective dose from derived radioactive substances via air or water for individuals in the population is **0.3 millisieverts (mSv)** in a calendar year [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].
*   **Alternative Monitoring:** If the competent authority deems it unnecessary to set activity amounts and concentrations, the evidence of compliance with the 0.3 mSv limit can be assumed if the concentrations according to Annex 11 Part D for derived radioactive substances via air or water do not exceed the limits in the annual average [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].
*   **Monitoring:** The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that emissions are monitored and reported to the competent authority annually, specifying the type and activity of the emissions [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].

## Measurement Devices (§ 90)
The *Strahlenschutzverantwortliche* must ensure that:
*   Appropriate radiation measuring devices are used for measuring personal dose, site dose, site dose rate, surface contamination, and the activity of air and water [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].
*   For continuous measuring devices, failure must be indicated by a clearly perceptible signal, unless two or more independent measuring devices serve the same measurement purpose [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].
*   Records of function testing and maintenance must be kept for ten years from the date of the test or maintenance [StrlSchV.md [Teil 5/15].md].
