# Radioaktive Stoffe

Radioactive substances are materials containing radionuclides whose activity or specific activity is regulated under the Act. The definition and handling of these substances are governed by various parts of the Radiation Protection Act (StrlSchG) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## Residues (Rückstände)
Residues (*Rückstände*) are defined materials, such as sludge/deposits from oil/gas extraction, gravel/sands/resins from groundwater treatment, and dust/slag from phosphate processing [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

**Exclusion Criteria:** Materials are *not* considered residues if their specific activity for U-238 and Th-232 decay chains is below 0.2 Bq/g, and they are not used as construction products or introduced as raw materials in technological processes [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Contamination and Disposal
The law governs sites contaminated by radioactive substances, defining responsibilities and remediation plans for such contamination [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

*   **Residues:** When residues are removed from contaminated grounds, the provisions of the Act apply, unless the residues are used in the remediation of other radioactive contaminated sites [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## Added Substances and Transfer
*   **Added Substances (§ 206):** Permits are required for the addition of radioactive substances to consumer goods, requiring details on the physical/chemical nature, activity, and specific activity of the added radionuclides [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].
*   **Transboundary Transfer (§ 207):** Permits are required for the cross-border transfer of consumer goods containing radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md [Teil 12/13].md].

## Annexes Details
