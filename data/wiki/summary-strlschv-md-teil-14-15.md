The *Strahlenschutzverordnung* (StrlSchV) is the Ordinance concerning the protection against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, effective as of November 29, 2018 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md]. This document details numerous technical appendices and tables, including criteria for determining the significance of incidents, dose measurement methodologies, and activity concentrations of radionuclides.

## Key Appendices and Criteria

### Anlage 14 (zu § 108) – Criteria for Significance in Medical/Non-Medical Exposure
This appendix sets criteria for determining the significance of an event during medical exposure or non-medical application [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].

**I. Investigations without Interventions (Excluding conventional radiography and digital dental/cranial tomography):**
*   **Group Level:** An exceedance of the average over the last 20 consecutive investigations of the same type at the same device by more than 100 percent of the respective diagnostic reference value, provided the diagnostic reference value of a single investigation was exceeded by 200 percent.
*   **Individual Level:**
    *   Exceedance of the volume-based CT dose index in the brain of 120 Milligray, or for other CT applications of 80 Milligray, or the total dose-area product of a plain X-ray of 20,000 Centigray-cm² [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md]. For digital volume tomography, the first exceeded value (CT or plain X-ray) applies.
    *   Exceedance of the intended effective dose by more than 20 Millisieverts or an organ dose by more than 100 Millisieverts from radioactive substances during a single investigation.
    *   Other criteria include repeat applications (due to misidentification, setting error, or device defect) or misidentification of persons/body parts, if the resulting additional total exposure meets the criteria above [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
    *   Occurrence of a deterministic effect not expected for the specified investigation.

**II. Interventions:**
*   **Group Level:** Exceedance of the average over the last 20 consecutive interventions of the same type by more than 100 percent of the respective diagnostic reference value, provided the diagnostic reference value of a single investigation was exceeded by 200 percent.
*   **Individual Level (For investigation purposes):**
    *   Exceedance of the total dose-area product of 20,000 Centigray-cm² [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
    *   Other criteria include repeat applications or misidentification of persons/body parts, if the resulting additional total exposure meets the criteria above [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
*   **Individual Level (For treatment purposes):**
    *   Exceedance of the total dose-area product of 50,000 Centigray-cm² if a deterministic skin damage of the second or higher grade occurs acutely or within 21 days after the interventional investigation.
    *   Other criteria include misidentification of persons/body parts or occurrence of an unexpected deterministic effect [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].

**III. Treatments with Ionizing Radiation/Enclosed Radioactive Substances:**
*   Deviation of the total dose in the target volume or at the reference point by more than 10 percent from the dose specified in the irradiation plan, provided the deviation is at least 4 Gray.
*   Unplanned exceedance of the dose limitation specified in the working instructions for risk organs, if the exceedance is more than 10 percent.
*   Deviation of the mean total dose by more than 10 percent from the specified mean dose in the target volume or for risk organs.
*   Deviation from the total treatment time specified in the irradiation plan by more than one week, unless caused by the treated person.
*   Misidentification of persons or irradiation plans.
*   Occurrence of a deterministic effect not expected for the specified treatment.

**IV. Treatments with Open Radioactive Substances:**
*   Deviation of the administered total activity by more than 10 percent from the specified activity.
*   Occurrence of a deterministic effect not expected for the specified treatment.
*   Misidentification of persons or body parts, or misidentification of the radioactive substance.
*   Occurrence of a paravasate after injection of the radioactive substance, if more than 15 percent of the intended activity was misapplied.
*   Contamination by a radioactive substance, if an unintentional exposure of the treated person occurred and the resulting effective dose exceeded 20 Millisieverts or the organ equivalent dose exceeded 100 Millisieverts.

**V. Care and Accompanying Persons:**
*   Any unintentional exceedance of the effective dose of 1 Millisievert for a care and accompanying person.

**VI. Medical Research:**
*   For applications approved under § 31 StrlSchG: Any significant occurrence according to the criteria in Sections I to V.
*   For applications shown under § 32 StrlSchG: Any significant occurrence according to the criteria in Sections I, II, and V.
*   For investigations for medical research: Any exceedance of the dose limits according to § 137 Paragraph 2 or 3 StrlSchG [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].

**VII. Near Misses:**
*   Any discovered event of near-miss exposure outside of quality assurance measures for which one of the criteria of Sections I to VI would apply if the exposure had actually occurred.

### Anlage 15 (zu § 108) – Criteria for Significance in Planned Exposure Situations
This appendix defines criteria for planned exposure situations [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md]:
1. Exposure of a professionally exposed person who exceeds a body dose limit—effective dose or organ equivalent dose—according to § 78 StrlSchG, unless the exposure is a particularly permitted exposure according to § 74 StrlSchG.
2. Exposure of an individual in the population who exceeds a limit according to § 80 StrlSchG.
3. Exceedance of the permissible derivation of radioactive substances with air or water.
4. Release of radioactive substances:
    a) within an area marked as a control area, unless it is marked as a exclusion area, if the site dose rate exceeds 3 Millisieverts per hour for more than 24 hours.
    b) within a monitoring area, requiring the establishment of a new control area, or
    c) into the environment with activities above the release limits according to Anlage 4 Table 1 Column 2.
5. Contamination:
    a) Contamination within a control area, in an area that cannot be contaminated by design, exceeding one thousand times the values of Anlage 4 Table 1 Column 5, and whose total activity in Becquerel is more than one hundred times the values of Anlage 4 Table 1 Column 2.
    b) Contamination within a monitoring area, in an area that cannot be contaminated by design, exceeding one hundred times the values of Anlage 4 Table 1 Column 5 and whose total activity in Becquerel is more than ten times the values of Anlage 4 Table 1 Column 2, or
    c) Contamination not covered by letters a or b, exceeding ten times the values of Anlage 4 Table 1 Column 5 and whose total activity in Becquerel exceeds the values of Anlage 4 Table 1 Column 2.
6. Extraordinary event sequence or operating condition of significant safety engineering importance during the operation of an X-ray facility, a licensed scatter source, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, or during the handling or transport of radioactive substances.

### Anlage 16 (zu § 149) – Risk Assessment Criteria
This appendix outlines criteria for determining the type and extent of risk associated with an activity [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md]:
Regular on-site inspections by a radiation protection officer must be based on a systematic assessment of the risks associated with the activity, particularly using the following criteria:
1. Expected exposure during normal use of ionizing radiation or radioactive substances to humans.
2. Expected exposure during normal use of ionizing radiation or radioactive substances in applications without targeted exposure to persons.
3. Activity level of the licensed handling of enclosed and open radioactive substances.
4. Risk of incorporations when handling open radioactive substances.
5. Risk of unintentional exposures.
6. Available protective facilities to prevent unintentional exposures in X-ray facilities, scatter sources, facilities for generating ionizing radiation, and enclosed radioactive substances, as well as the scope of required radiation protection measures for the safe execution of activities.
7. Other risk-relevant conditions for activities according to § 4 Paragraph 1 StrlSchG in planned exposure situations.

### Anlage 17 (zu § 159) – Activity Index
This appendix defines the Activity Index ($I$) for building materials, considering the building material density ($\rho$) in $\text{kg/m}^3$ and the building material thickness ($d$) in meters, using the specific activities of Radium-226 ($^{226}\text{Ra}$), Thorium-232 (or its decay product Radium-228) ($^{232}\text{Th}$), and Potassium-40 ($^{40}\text{K}$) in the building material in $\text{Bq/kg}$ [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
*   If the surface density $\rho \cdot d$ exceeds 500 $\text{kg/m}^2$, the value 500 $\text{kg/m}^2$ is used instead.
*   The reference value of 1 millisievert per year is considered maintained if the Activity Index $I$ does not exceed 1.
*   For thin-layer materials (thickness up to 0.03 meters) used only in combination with a supporting or supporting surface (wall, ceiling, floor)—e.g., tiles—a contribution of 0.48 is to be added to the index for general consideration of the underlying surface.
*   If the building material thickness in the structure is unknown, $d = 0.2$ meters is to be set.

### Anlage 18 (zu den §§ 171, 197) – Dose and Measurement Units
This appendix details measurement quantities for external radiation (Teil A) and body dose calculation (Teil B) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].

**Part A: Measurement Quantities for External Radiation:**
1. **For Person Dose:** Depth-person dose $H_p(10)$, Eye lens-person dose $H_p(3)$, and Surface-person dose $H_p(0.07)$ [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
2. **For Site Dose:** Ambient equivalent dose $H^*(10)$, directional equivalent dose in 3 mm depth $H'(3,\Omega)$, and directional equivalent dose in 0.07 mm depth $H'(0,07,\Omega)$ [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].

**Part B: Calculation of Body Dose:**
1. **Organ Equivalent Dose $H_T$:** $H_{T,R} = w_R D_{T,R}$, where $D_{T,R}$ is the mean energy dose generated by radiation $R$ in the tissue or organ $T$, and $w_R$ is the radiation weighting factor from Part C Number 1 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
2. **Effective Dose $E$:** The effective dose is the weighted mean of organ equivalent doses, considering the radiation sensitivity of various organs or tissues via weighting factors $w_T$ from Part C Number 2 [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].

## Radionuclide Activity Concentration Tables
The source contains extensive tables detailing the activity concentrations ($\text{Ci}$) of various radionuclides in air ($\text{Bq/m}^3$) and water ($\text{Bq/m}^3$) across multiple pages (Pages 161 through 172). These tables list specific isotopes (e.g., $\text{Sm-153}$, $\text{Dy-155}$, $\text{Pb-214}$, $\text{U-238}$, $\text{Pu-239}$, $\text{Th-232}$, etc.) and their measured concentrations under different binding states (A = adsorbed, E = elemental, O = organic) [StrlSchV.md [Teil 14/15].md].
