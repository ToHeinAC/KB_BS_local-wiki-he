# Radioactive Waste Management

Radioactive Waste Management refers to the systematic process of handling, treating, storing, and disposing of materials contaminated by ionizing radiation. This field is governed by overarching principles detailed in frameworks like the Joint Convention Deutschland [jc_8_bericht_deutschland_atomenergie_bf.md [Teil 3/25].md].

## Core Principles
Effective waste management requires:
*   **Classification:** Accurate classification of waste streams is necessary to determine the appropriate handling and disposal pathway [jc_8_bericht_deutschland_atomenergie_bf.md [Teil 3/25].md].
*   **Treatment:** Waste must undergo necessary treatment processes to stabilize the materials before they can be safely disposed of [jc_8_bericht_deutschland_atomenergie_bf.md [Teil 3/25].md].
*   **Disposal:** The final disposal must occur in secure, deep geological repositories, meeting international safety standards [jc_8_bericht_deutschland_atomenergie_bf.md [Teil 3/25].md].

## Scope
The management scope covers all radioactive waste, ensuring that regulatory compliance is maintained throughout the entire waste lifecycle [jc_8_bericht_deutschland_atomenergie_bf.md [Teil 3/25].md].
