The *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) is the German law concerning the protection against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation. It is defined as the "Gesetz zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG) vom 27.06.2017" [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md].

## General Provisions (Teil 1)

The law regulates the protection of humans and, regarding the long-term protection of human health, the environment from the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, specifically in three contexts:
1. Planned exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 1 (1)].
2. Emergency exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 1 (1)].
3. Existing exposure situations [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 1 (1)].

The law does not regulate exposure from cosmic radiation for individuals or workers, nor does it regulate above-ground exposure from naturally occurring radionuclides in undisturbed Earth's crust, or exposure from radionuclides naturally present in the human body and cosmic radiation near ground level [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 1 (2)].

### Key Definitions

*   **Exposure (Exposition):** The incidence of ionizing radiation on the human body, either external (outside the body) or internal (inside the body), or the extent of this incidence [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (1)].
*   **Planned Exposure Situation (Geplante Expositionssituation):** An exposure situation that arises from activities and in which an exposure is caused or can be caused [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (2)].
*   **Emergency Exposure Situation (Notfallexpositionssituation):** An exposure situation that arises from an emergency, provided the situation does not fall under paragraph 4 [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (3)].
*   **Existing Exposure Situation (Bestehende Expositionssituation):** An exposure situation that already exists when a decision regarding its control must be made [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (4)].
*   **Exposition Categories:** The law distinguishes three categories: population exposure, professional exposure, and medical exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (5)].
    *   **Professional Exposure:** Includes exposure of a person in an employment relationship or performing the activity, flight/space personnel, or persons fulfilling tasks under the Atomic Energy Act [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (7)].
    *   **Medical Exposure:** Includes application to a patient or asymptomatic person for medical/dental examination or treatment, or application for medical research purposes [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 2 (8)].
*   **Radioactive Substances (Radioaktive Stoffe):** These are all substances containing one or more radionuclides whose activity or specific activity cannot be disregarded according to the regulations of this law or a related ordinance [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 3 (1)].
*   **Activities (Tätigkeiten):** Defined broadly, including handling, acquisition, storage, establishment, operation, and decommissioning of facilities, as well as the addition of radioactive substances to consumer goods or the activation of products [StrlSchG.md [Teil 1/13].md, § 4 (1)].

## Structure Overview

The Act is divided into several parts addressing different scenarios:

*   **Part 2: Radiation Protection in Planned Exposure Situations:** Covers principles like justification, dose limitation, and specific regulations for facilities (e.g., § 10, § 12, § 19) and activities (e.g., medical research § 31).
*   **Part 3: Radiation Protection in Emergency Exposure Situations:** Addresses emergency management principles, dose/contamination values, and planning (e.g., § 92, § 105).
*   **Part 4: Radiation Protection in Existing Exposure Situations:** Covers post-emergency situations, protection against Radon (§ 124), and contaminated areas (§ 136).
*   **Part 5: Cross-Cutting Regulations for Exposure Situations:** Deals with environmental radioactivity monitoring (§ 161) and general regulations (§ 166).
*   **Part 6: Radiation Protection Law Supervision, Administrative Procedures:** Covers supervision and administrative oversight (§ 178).

## Key Procedural Requirements

The law establishes detailed requirements for:
*   **Operational Organization of Radiation Protection (BOStrSch):** Defining the roles of the Radiation Protection Officer and the Radiation Protection Responsible Person (§ 69, § 70).
*   **Dose Limits:** Setting limits for professional lifetime doses (§ 77) and population exposure (§ 80).
*   **Permitting and Notification:** Establishing requirements for facilities and activities that require permits or notifications (e.g., § 10, § 17).
