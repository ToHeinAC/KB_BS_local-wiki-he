State supervision (*staatliche Aufsicht*) is the mandatory regulatory oversight mechanism governing all activities involving radioactive substances and facilities under the *Atomgesetz* (AtG) [AtG.md].

## Scope of Supervision
The supervisory authorities are responsible for ensuring compliance with:
*   The provisions of the AtG and its implementing regulations and orders [AtG.md].
*   The requirements stipulated in the permit or general authorization [AtG.md].
*   The adherence to any subsequent supplementary conditions or requirements [AtG.md].

## Supervisory Powers
Supervisory authorities are empowered to:
*   **Access and Inspection:** They can enter and conduct all necessary inspections at locations where radioactive substances or facilities are located, or where such radiation is presumed to act. This right supersedes the constitutional right to inviolability of the dwelling when necessary for their duties [AtG.md].
*   **Information Gathering:** They can demand necessary information from responsible or employed persons at the site [AtG.md].
*   **Personnel Checks:** They conduct mandatory reliability checks on all responsible persons, experts, and personnel involved in the handling or transport of radioactive materials [AtG.md].

## Reporting and Information Flow
The system mandates the reporting of deviations:
*   Facility owners must report any deviations from the permit application or permit to the supervisory authority [AtG.md].
*   The supervisory authority must report safety-critical deviations, such as accidents, to the competent authorities [AtG.md].
