# Sub-ground Areas

Sub-ground areas, including basements (Keller) and semi-underground rooms, are primary concern zones in radon protection due to their direct contact with the soil and the potential for radon gas accumulation from the surrounding earth [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Radon Risk Profile
The risk in these areas stems from the accumulation of radon gas emanating from the soil (Bodenluft). The concentration can be significantly higher than in above-ground spaces [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Mitigation Strategies for Sub-ground Areas
Mitigation requires a combination of sealing and active ventilation:

### 1. Sealing and Containment
*   **Secondary Sealing:** Establishing a secondary sealing perimeter is crucial to separate the occupied space from the sub-ground area [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Under-pressure Management:** The design should aim to manage the pressure differential, often by maintaining a slight positive pressure in the living areas relative to the sub-ground areas [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].

### 2. Ventilation Techniques
*   **Source Removal:** The most effective methods involve removing radon gas at the source:
    *   **Drainage Pipes:** Collecting soil air via a pipe system and venting it outside [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
    *   **Central Collection Shafts:** Utilizing a shaft to draw air from below the floor structure [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
    *   **External Radon Wells:** Utilizing wells outside the building to create a large under-pressure zone in the surrounding soil [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Dilution:** If source removal is not feasible, increasing the air exchange rate (Luftwechsel) in the sub-ground area can dilute the concentration, though this must be balanced against cooling risks [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Operational Considerations
*   **Cooling Risk:** Increased ventilation in basements, especially in winter, must be managed to prevent excessive cooling or frost damage [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Monitoring:** Continuous monitoring and regular inspection of the ventilation system are necessary to ensure sustained protection.
