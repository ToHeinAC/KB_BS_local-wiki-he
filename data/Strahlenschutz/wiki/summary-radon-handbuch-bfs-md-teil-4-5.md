# Radon-Handbuch_BfS (Teil 4/5)

This document provides comprehensive technical guidelines for the prevention and remediation of radon gas in buildings, covering both new constructions (Neubau) and existing buildings (Bestand). It details various sealing, ventilation, and engineering measures, emphasizing the synergy between different protective strategies.

## General Principles and Synergies
The effectiveness of radon protection relies on a systematic approach, noting that successful mitigation often requires combining multiple measures [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].

*   **Synergies:** The document highlights that measures for moisture protection (Feuchte- und Grundwasserschutz) align completely with radon protection, as both require the sealing of leaks. Similarly, soundproofing elements (Schallschutz) can contribute to radon tightness.
*   **Conflict Potential:** A potential conflict arises with thermal protection (Wärmeschutz): If the building envelope is insulated and air exchange is reduced, or if new heating systems are installed without sealing potential radon pathways from the ground, the radon concentration inside the house could be higher than previously measured [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Planning Approach:** Radon prevention should be integrated into the planning phase, distinguishing between "Standard-Gebäudeschutz" and "besondere Radonprävention" based on the assessed radon load of the site [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].

## Radon Mitigation Measures (Radonsanierung)

The guidelines differentiate between simple and comprehensive remediation strategies:

### 1. Sealing Measures (Abdichtung)
Sealing is crucial for both primary and secondary containment perimeters:
*   **Primary Sealing Perimeter:** Involves sealing penetrations, joints, and cracks in structural elements against earth [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Secondary Sealing Perimeter:** Involves sealing walls, ceilings, and doors between living and basement areas [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Best Practice:** The document stresses the importance of airtightness and suggests that the planning should aim for minimal penetrations of the building envelope through utility lines or shafts [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].

### 2. Ventilation and Ventilation Systems (Lüftung)
Ventilation is a key active measure, requiring careful design to avoid negative side effects:
*   **Pressure Management:** The goal is often to create a slight positive pressure (Überdruck) in the living areas relative to the sub-ground areas, or to manage negative pressure (Unterdruck) in the sub-ground areas [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **System Types:**
    *   **Mechanical Ventilation with Heat Recovery (Wärmerückgewinnung):** This is recommended as it allows for high air exchange rates without excessive energy loss, even in winter [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
    *   **Under-pressure Creation:** Can be achieved via dedicated ventilation systems or by creating a vacuum in the sub-ground area [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Drainage/Ventilation of Sub-ground Areas:** Methods include:
    *   **Drainage Pipes (Drainagerohre):** Collecting radon-laden soil air and venting it outside. Effectiveness depends on achieving a full-area under-pressure [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
    *   **Central Collection Shafts (Sammelschacht):** Collecting soil air below the floor structure, effective if a continuous void exists or if the subsoil has high permeability (e.g., gravel) [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
    *   **External Radon Wells (Radonbrunnen):** Establishing wells outside the building can lead to cost-effective remediation, as they can create a large under-pressure area in the surrounding soil [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Technical Requirements for Fans (Ventilatoren):** Fans must have high lifespan (e.g., minimum 80,000 hours), adjustable speed, and a visible operating indicator. Efficiency is critical, as operational costs can exceed €500 over 10 years [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].

### 3. Specific Building Scenarios
*   **Basement/Sub-ground Ventilation:** If radon enters primarily from the sub-ground, increased air exchange in the basement (e.g., cross-ventilation) can dilute the concentration. However, this must be balanced against the risk of severe cooling in winter [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
*   **Modernization:** When renovating, it is recommended to proceed stepwise, distinguishing between "simple" and "comprehensive" remediation, as comprehensive measures are significantly more complex than those for new builds [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 4/5].md].
