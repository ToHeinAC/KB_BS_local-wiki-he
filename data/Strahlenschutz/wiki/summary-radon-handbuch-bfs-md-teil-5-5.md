The *Radon-Handbuch_BfS* provides comprehensive guidance on the risks associated with Radon and its decay products in indoor environments, detailing measurement techniques, risk assessment, and remediation strategies [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md]. The document emphasizes that the danger from natural radioactivity cannot be perceived by the senses and can only be proven through measurements [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## General Principles and Risk Communication
The handbook stresses the need for public education, advising citizens to be informed by factual information based on science and technology, rather than succumbing to fear regarding potential "radiation exposure" [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md]. The goal is to show actionable protective measures for inhabitants, building owners, and professionals [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Remediation Potential of Remediation Measures
The potential for reducing Radon concentrations varies significantly depending on the implemented measure [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

| Remediation Method | Achievable Reductions of Radon Concentration | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Simple sealing measures | 0 to 25 % | Applicable in areas up to 1,000 Bq/m³ |
| Negative pressure maintenance in basements, basement ventilation using small fan | 50 to 80 % | |
| Elaborate insulation layers in or on floors without involving walls | 30 to 80 % | |
| Elaborate insulation layers in or on floors involving walls | 50 to 90 % | Walls sealed continuously with foil |
| Negative pressure maintenance within floor constructions involving walls | 10 to 90 % | |
| *Result strongly depends on the type of subsequent walls* | | |
| Negative pressure maintenance under the building (drainage system point suction) | 10 to 95 % | Depending on soil permeability and location of drainage pipes |
| Elaborate insulation in newly inserted floors, negative pressure maintenance under the building | up to 99 % | With a continuous sealing layer through the entire building, except for unavoidable weak points |

## Inspection and Evidence Preservation Checklist (Checkliste Beweissicherung/Hausbegehung)
The BfS recommends analyzing buildings with Radon concentrations exceeding 100 Bq/m³ in living areas to determine if a successful remediation is possible [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md]. The inspection must focus on identifying potential entry and dispersion paths of radon gas [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

**Key Inspection Areas:**

*   **Boundaries (Abgrenzungen):** Determining which structures and rooms border the ground. All known areas must be mapped.
*   **Basements (Keller):** Moisture and odor suggest insufficient air exchange and/or damp spots. Radon concentration measurement in affected rooms is necessary [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Living Areas (Aufenthaltsräume):** Odor and moisture are indicators. In ground-floor rooms directly above ground, the same considerations as basements apply.
*   **Floor in the lowest story (Boden im untersten Geschoss):** Assessing the construction material (natural soil, floorboards, slabs, stones, continuous concrete slab). Checking for cracks or holes. The state of the joint structure must be examined, as flat, linear, or point weak spots are potential leak zones [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Basement walls against the ground (Kellerwände gegen das Erdreich):** Assessing materials (e.g., hollow walls, rubble stone, reinforced concrete). Checking for cracks, holes, and the condition of joints to the floor and ceiling.
*   **Basement walls against living areas (Kellerwände gegen Aufenthaltsräume):** Assessing materials and joint conditions. The upper closure of intermediate walls is often leaky. Multi-shelled walls can create ideal dispersion paths [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Basement windows, ventilation openings (Kellerfenster, Lüftungsöffnungen):** Documenting material and condition.
*   **Pipes/Lines from/to outside (Leitungen von/nach außen):** Checking if utility lines (electrical mains, TV cables, gas mains, water/sewage pipes, oil lines, ventilation pipes, heat pump ground source lines, etc.) are sealed against radon and the masonry/floor.

## Technical Appendices (ANHANG)
The appendix provides detailed inspection points for various building systems, including:

*   **Pipes and lines inside the building:** Electrical lines, sewage pipes, hot/cold water pipes, phone cables, gas pipes, TV cables, chimneys, and heating pipes [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Heating:** Checking combustion air supply, chimney flaps, and the closure status of the chimney explosion flap.
*   **Crawlspaces (Kriechkeller):** Assessing structural and building condition and ventilation status.
*   **Basement Ceiling/Floor:** Assessing construction, visible cracks, and joint conditions.
*   **Basement Doors:** Special attention must be paid to the sealing capability of the doors and their thresholds [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Overall Pressure Situation:** Analyzing the pressure situation in the building, which varies by day and year, and the ventilation setup [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
