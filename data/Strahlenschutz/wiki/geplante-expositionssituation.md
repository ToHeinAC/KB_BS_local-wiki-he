## Key facts
- Geplante Expositionssituation
- Regulatory Focus

A planned exposure situation is defined as an exposure situation that arises from activities and in which an exposure is caused or can be caused [StrlSchG.md, § 2 (2)].

### Regulatory Focus
This category of exposure is central to the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) as it governs activities that are anticipated or scheduled, rather than those resulting from an unforeseen event.

Key aspects regulated under planned exposure situations include:
*   **Activities:** The law details specific activities that fall under this scope, such as the establishment, operation, and decommissioning of facilities generating ionizing radiation [StrlSchG.md, § 4 (1)].
*   **Principles:** The principles of radiation protection mandate the justification of activities, the avoidance of unnecessary exposure, and dose limitation [StrlSchG.md, § 7, § 8, § 9].
*   **Permitting:** Many activities require specific permits or notifications to ensure compliance with safety standards [StrlSchG.md, § 10, § 12].
