Ionizing radiation refers to electromagnetic radiation or particulate radiation that possesses sufficient energy to cause ionization—the removal of electrons from atoms or molecules [StrlSchG.md].

Ionizing radiation refers to radiation that has enough energy to remove electrons from atoms or molecules, thereby ionizing them [StrlSchG.md, § 2 (1)].
### Context in Radiation Protection Law
In the context of the German Radiation Protection Act ($\text{StrlSchG}$), ionizing radiation is a central subject of control, necessitating strict regulatory oversight to protect human health and the environment [StrlSchG.md].

### Applications and Control
The law regulates the use of ionizing radiation in several contexts:
*   **Medical Research:** The application of ionizing radiation to humans for medical research requires specific permits and rigorous review by the Federal Radiation Protection Agency ($\S 185$ (1) 1) [StrlSchG.md].
*   **Industrial/Technical Use:** The use of ionizing radiation, such as in X-ray equipment or scatter sources, is subject to type approval and operational permits [StrlSchG.md].
*   **Monitoring:** The Act mandates monitoring for exposure to ionizing radiation, including cosmic radiation during spacecraft operations [StrlSchG.md].

### Legal Implications
The handling and use of ionizing radiation are heavily regulated, requiring adherence to specific procedures outlined in the StrlSchG, including documentation, notification, and the establishment of safety measures to ensure exposure remains below permissible limits [StrlSchG.md].
### Types and Effects
*   **Exposure:** The law defines "Exposure" as the incidence of ionizing radiation on the human body, which can occur either externally (outside the body) or internally (inside the body), or it can refer to the measure of this incidence [StrlSchG.md, § 2 (1)].
*   **Sources:** Sources of ionizing radiation include radioactive substances [StrlSchG.md, § 2 (1)].
*   **Protection:** The *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) establishes comprehensive rules for protecting humans and the environment from the harmful effects of ionizing radiation [StrlSchG.md, § 1].
### Legal Context
The management of ionizing radiation is governed by strict legal procedures, including:
*   **Planned Activities:** Requiring assessment of planned exposure situations [StrlSchG.md, § 2 (2)].
*   **Emergency Response:** Establishing protocols for emergency exposure situations [StrlSchG.md, § 2 (3)].
*   **Dose Limitation:** Setting specific dose limits for professional and population exposure [StrlSchG.md, § 77, § 80].
