Radon ($\text{Rn}-222$) is a naturally occurring, colorless, odorless, and tasteless radioactive gas that emanates from the decay of radium-226 found in soil and certain building materials [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md]. Because it is invisible and undetectable by human senses, its presence and associated health risks require specialized measurement techniques [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Sources and Pathways
The primary source of indoor radon is the decay of radium in the ground, leading to gas ingress into buildings [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md]. Radon can enter buildings through various pathways, including:
*   **Soil Gas Ingress:** The most common source, entering through cracks, gaps, and unsealed utility lines connecting the building to the ground [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Building Components:** Radon can diffuse through porous materials like concrete, stone, and unsealed joints in the foundation and walls [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Utility Lines:** Pipes and conduits passing from outside to inside the building must be checked to ensure they are sealed against radon gas ingress [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Health Risks and Assessment
Radon gas is a known cause of lung cancer, and its concentration in indoor air is a critical public health concern [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Measurement:** The presence of radon must be confirmed by measurements, typically using passive sampling methods [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Risk Assessment:** Buildings with radon concentrations exceeding 100 Bq/m³ in living areas are recommended for analysis to determine the feasibility of remediation [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Mitigation and Remediation
Mitigation strategies aim to reduce the concentration of radon gas by interrupting its pathways into the building envelope [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md]. The effectiveness of remediation is highly dependent on the specific measure implemented:
*   **Simple Sealing:** Can achieve reductions ranging from 0 to 25 % [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Negative Pressure in Basements:** Using small fans for basement ventilation can achieve reductions between 50 % and 80 % [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Advanced Measures:** The most effective measures involve elaborate insulation layers combined with negative pressure maintenance under the building, which can achieve reductions up to 99 % [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Building Physics Considerations
Effective radon protection requires treating the building as a sealed geometric body. The integrity of the building envelope is paramount. This involves:
*   **Air Tightness:** The fresh air supply must come through designated openings or ventilation systems, and the exhaust air must be clearly defined [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Structural Integrity:** All structural elements, including basement walls, floors, and utility penetrations, must be assessed for potential leakages [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
