# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG

The *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) is the German law concerning the protection against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation. The law details comprehensive requirements for radiation safety, covering everything from establishing protective areas to setting dose limits for the general population and professionals [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## General Requirements for Radiation Protection
The law specifies detailed requirements for radiation protection areas, including:
*   Establishing and defining the characteristics of such areas [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   Conditions under which entry into these areas is permitted [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   Mandatory instruction for individuals before entering protective areas, operating equipment, or handling radioactive materials [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   Requirements for personal protective equipment (PPE) and technical/organizational monitoring procedures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Dose Limits and Exposure Control
The StrlSchG establishes strict limits for radiation exposure:

### Professional Exposure Limits
*   **Annual Effective Dose Limit:** The general limit for professionally exposed persons is 20 millisieverts (mSv) per calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Special Cases:** The competent authority may permit an effective dose of 50 mSv in an individual year, provided that 100 mSv is not exceeded over five consecutive years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Minors:** For professionally exposed persons under 18 years old, the effective dose limit is 1 mSv per calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Organ-Specific Limits:** Limits are set for the lens of the eye (20 mSv/year), local skin dose (500 mSv/year), and extremities (hands, forearms, feet, ankles, each 500 mSv/year) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

### Public Exposure Limits
*   **Annual Effective Dose Limit:** For individuals in the general population, the limit for the sum of effective doses is 1 mSv per calendar year from activities like licensed or reported operations, state storage of nuclear fuel, or dealing with radioactive mineral deposits [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Organ-Specific Limits:** The sum of organ-equivalent doses is limited to 15 mSv/year for the eye lens and 50 mSv/year for the local skin dose [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Health and Documentation
The law mandates rigorous documentation and medical oversight:
*   **Health Records:** The health record must contain details on working conditions, results of medical monitoring, and received body dose data [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Retention Period:** The health record must be kept until the person reaches the age of 75, or at least 30 years after ceasing professional activity, and must be destroyed no later than 100 years after birth [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Reporting:** The Radiation Protection Officer must ensure that records of body dose are immediately recorded for individuals subject to physical radiation protection control or who were in protective areas [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].

## Emergency and Environmental Protection
*   **Accidents:** The law outlines the duties of the Radiation Protection Officer regarding preparation for accidents and emergencies, including informing the public and taking immediate measures to avert danger [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Environmental Monitoring:** Provisions exist for determining expected exposure of the general population from planned activities, including setting dose limits for derived emissions in air and water [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
*   **Source Register:** A register is maintained for high-radioactive sources, recording details such as the license holder, identification number, and location of handling or storage [StrlSchG.md [Teil 6/13].md].
