The *Atomgesetz* (AtG), or Law on the Peaceful Use of Nuclear Energy and Protection Against its Dangers, is the foundational German federal law governing the entire lifecycle of nuclear energy use, from research and development to waste management and emergency response [AtG.md].

## Scope and Purpose
The AtG establishes the legal framework for:
*   **Regulation of Nuclear Activities:** It governs the peaceful use of nuclear energy, including the establishment, operation, and decommissioning of nuclear facilities [AtG.md].
*   **Protection:** It mandates comprehensive protection measures for humans and the environment against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation [AtG.md].
*   **Authorization:** It establishes the requirement for permits and authorizations for activities involving radioactive substances or ionizing radiation [AtG.md].

## Key Regulatory Procedures
The law details specific procedures for managing risks and resources:

### Site Management and Waste
The AtG governs the management of contaminated sites and waste:
*   **Site Preparation:** It outlines procedures for site preparation ($\text{§ 9f}$) and site investigations ($\text{§ 9g}$).
*   **Change Restriction:** It allows for the establishment of a change restriction ($\text{Veränderungssperre}$) to prevent changes that could compromise the safety or feasibility of radioactive waste disposal facilities [AtG.md].
*   **Waste Disposal:** It mandates the delivery of radioactive waste to state collection points and federal facilities, detailing the required procedures for interim storage and disposal [AtG.md].

### Operational Control and Safety
The law imposes strict controls on operations:
*   **Supervision:** All handling, transport, and operation of radioactive substances and facilities are subject to state supervision ($\text{§ 19}$) [AtG.md].
*   **Licensing:** It requires permits for activities, and the law provides mechanisms for revoking or modifying these permits if safety standards are breached or if the provision for compensation is insufficient [AtG.md].
*   **Reliability:** A mandatory reliability check ($\text{§ 12b}$) is conducted on all responsible persons and experts to prevent unauthorized removal or release of radioactive materials [AtG.md].

### Financial Security
The AtG establishes mechanisms to ensure financial accountability for potential damages:
*   **Compensation Provision:** The administrative authority must determine a provision for statutory compensation obligations ($\text{Deckungsvorsorge}$) during the permitting process, which must be reviewed periodically [AtG.md]. This specific requirement is further detailed by the *Atomrechtliche Deckungsvorsorge-Verordnung - AtDeckV* [AtDeckV.md].
*   **Liability:** The law addresses the hierarchy of compensation claims, ensuring that the coverage provided does not impair the claims of other affected parties [AtG.md].

## Relationship to Other Laws
The AtG works in conjunction with the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG), which provides the overarching technical and procedural rules for radiation protection [concept-radiation-protection-law.md].
