The *Atomrechtliche Deckungsvorsorge-Verordnung* (AtDeckV) is a regulation concerning the financial security provision for activities and facilities where atomic law liability under international treaties or the *Atomgesetz* (AtG) may apply [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md]. The regulation was issued with an effective date of 25.01.1977 [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## General Provisions (Erster Abschnitt)

### Types of Coverage Provision (§ 1)
The coverage provision (*Deckungsvorsorge*) for facilities and activities where atomic law liability is considered can be provided by:
1. A liability insurance policy (*Haftpflichtversicherung*) or
2. Another form of financial security (*sonstige finanzielle Sicherheit*) [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

The administrative authority may permit the combination of multiple security measures of the same or different types, provided that the effectiveness and clarity of the coverage provision are not impaired [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

### Liability Insurance (§ 2)
A liability insurance policy can only provide coverage if it is taken out with an insurance company authorized to operate the liability insurance within Germany (*im Inland*) [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md]. For cross-border transport according to § 4a of the AtG, it can also be taken out with an insurance company of a third state that is authorized to operate liability insurance in its seat country, provided that a company authorized under Sentence 1 or an association of such companies assumes the duties of a liability insurer [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

If the Federal Government and the States are obligated to indemnify the Obligated Party for damages or ensure the satisfaction of resulting claims, the insurance contract must contain the obligation for the insurer to notify the administrative authority immediately of any change in the contract, any damage event, any claim for damages, and any payment fulfilling damage obligations, as soon as such circumstances become known [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

### Other Financial Security (§ 3)
Other financial security can only provide coverage if it is guaranteed that, while its invocation is expected, it is available within the framework set by the administrative authority for the determination of the coverage provision and can be immediately drawn upon to fulfill statutory compensation obligations mentioned in § 13 of the AtG [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

For a third party whose main residence or business headquarters is abroad, other financial security can only be accepted if the third party either possesses sufficient assets within Germany for the duration of their obligation to cover their obligation, or if it is ensured that a decision of a German court regarding the obligation can be enforced in the state where the third party has sufficient assets, based on an international agreement [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

For another state, other financial security can only be accepted if that state submits to the jurisdiction of the Federal Republic of Germany or if it is otherwise guaranteed that it will fulfill its obligation [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

### Scope of Coverage Provision (§ 4)
The coverage provision must extend to the statutory compensation obligations under § 13 paragraph 5 of the AtG in the version of July 15, 1985 (BGBl. I S. 1565), as last amended by Article 1 of the Act of August 10, 2021 (BGBl. I S. 3530), in the currently applicable version, for damages arising from a nuclear event to the owner of the nuclear facility [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

For activities or facilities where liability under § 26 of the AtG may be considered, the coverage provision must extend to the statutory compensation obligations under § 13 paragraph 5 of the AtG, arising in connection with the permitted activity or facility, due to effects designated in § 26 paragraphs 1 sentence 1 and 2 of the AtG:
1. For the Obligated Party,
2. For the persons ordered by the Obligated Party to perform work,
3. In the case of transport, also for persons involved in the transport alongside the Obligated Party, or who have or are authorized to perform material, service, or work services for the transport, or who were ordered to perform such services or were involved in the transport [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

The coverage provision must include damage events that occur or affect abroad, for which the Obligated Party is liable under international treaties or liability provisions applicable abroad, in the sense of § 13 paragraph 5 of the AtG [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

The coverage provision may not be designated or used for obligations other than those mentioned in paragraphs 1 to 3, up to the set amount [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

The administrative authority may allow exceptions to paragraphs 1 to 3 if:
1. They are justified with regard to the type of coverage provision and
2. They do not unduly impair the interests of the entire group of affected parties, and in cases where an exemption from compensation obligations under § 34 of the AtG is considered, also the interests of the exempted parties [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

The coverage provision required from the owner of a nuclear facility does not have to extend to compensation obligations arising from the handling or transport of radioactive substances outside the nuclear facility for them [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

Paragraphs 1 and 3 to 6 apply to the owner of a reactor ship [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

### Notification and Display of Coverage Provision (§ 5)
1. The coverage provision must be proven to the administrative authority in a suitable form [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
2. The administrative authority must inform the insurer or the third party who assumed other financial security about the granting, withdrawal, and revocation of a permit [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
3. Any person who wishes to claim damages for which the coverage provision is relevant can request the administrative authority to disclose the name and address of the insurer or the third party who committed to providing other financial security [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
4. The responsible authority for receiving a notice of the non-existence or termination of the insurance contract or the contract for other financial security (§ 14 of the AtG in conjunction with § 117 paragraph 2 of the Insurance Contract Act) is the permitting authority or, if a permit is not required, the otherwise responsible administrative authority [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

### Impositions (§ 6)
When setting the coverage provision, the Obligated Party must be ordered to:
1. Make changes to the coverage provision only with prior approval of the administrative authority,
2. Immediately notify the administrative authority of any change to the coverage provision that occurs without their fault, and, insofar as compensation obligations are relevant, any damage event, any claim for damages, and any payment fulfilling compensation obligations, as soon as such circumstances become known,
3. Prove to the administrative authority, upon request, that the coverage provision is available in the set amount and scope, and that the prerequisites under which the coverage provision could have been provided in another way than by a liability insurance policy still exist, and
4. Replenish the coverage provision, insofar as it is not available in full for every damage event, if a reduction of more than 1 percent has occurred or is expected due to one or more damage events [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Coverage Sums (§ 7)
1. When setting the amount of the coverage provision (Coverage Sum), a standard coverage sum (*Regeldeckungssumme*) must be assumed, unless the coverage sum is directly determined in this section [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

The coverage sum amounts to:
1. In the case of § 8a paragraph 1, no less than 80 million Euros and at most 125 million Euros,
2. In the case of § 8b, at most 125 million Euros,
3. In the case of § 9 paragraph 1, no less than 70 million Euros and at most 2.5 billion Euros,
4. In the cases of § 11 paragraph 1 and § 12 paragraph 1, no less than 70 million Euros and at most 500 million Euros, and
5. In the case of § 11 paragraph 2, no less than 70 million Euros and at most 700 million Euros [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Handling and Transport (§ 8)
1. Unless otherwise provided in this section, the standard coverage sum is determined:
    1. When handling nuclear fuels according to Annex 1,
    2. When handling other radioactive substances according to Annex 2 [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

The standard coverage sum when handling high-radioactive sources in the sense of § 4 paragraph 36 of the Radiation Protection Act is determined independently of the type of radioactive substance according to Annex 2 [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

2. If the handling of other radioactive substances is aimed at their application in medicine or if they enter the air, water, soil, or vegetation without further spread being prevented, the standard coverage sum is double the values specified in Annex 2 [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

3. When handling radioactive waste in a national collection point or another facility approved for the disposal of radioactive waste, the standard coverage sum is 7 million Euros, unless the national collection point or the other facility is a nuclear facility in the sense of § 2 paragraph 4 of the AtG. If other radioactive waste is handled in another facility approved for the disposal of radioactive waste according to Sentence 1, and this waste originates from a facility in the sense of § 7 of the AtG, the standard coverage sum is 70 million Euros [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

4. For the transport of radioactive substances, paragraph 1 applies accordingly; for the transport of other radioactive substances, the values of Annex 2 Column 2 are to be applied [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

5. The coverage sum for transport shall not exceed the amount of 35 million Euros. An excess is only permissible if the amount according to Sentence 1 is not appropriate under the circumstances of the individual case; in this case, the administrative authority may increase the coverage sum up to a maximum limit of double the sum according to Sentence 1. § 16 paragraph 2 applies accordingly [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Transport of Nuclear Materials (§ 8a)
1. For the transport of nuclear materials, which are not exempt from the application of the Paris Convention, the standard coverage sum is 80 million Euros and increases:
    1. according to Annex 3, if nuclear fuels in the sense of § 2 paragraph 1 sentence 2 of the AtG are transported with an approved mass of more than 250 kilograms,
    2. according to Annex 4, if the approved total activity exceeds $10^{12}$ times the release limits according to Annex 4 Table 1 Column 2 of the Radiation Protection Ordinance of November 29, 2018 (BGBl. I S. 2034, 2036), as last amended by Article 83 of the Act of August 10, 2021 (BGBl. I S. 3436), in the currently applicable version.

The increase amounts according to Annex 3 and Annex 4 must be determined separately and then added to the amount of 80 million Euros to a standard coverage sum of at most 125 million Euros [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

2. Nuclear materials sent from an owner of a nuclear facility to a recipient for use are exempt from the application of the Paris Convention for the period during which they are outside a nuclear facility, if:
    1. The shipment meets the provisions of Annex 5 and
    2. The nuclear materials are transported in compliance with the legal regulations applicable to the respective mode of transport concerning the transport of dangerous goods, or, if such regulations are missing, precautions required by the state of science and technology against damage from the transport of the nuclear materials are taken in other ways [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

For the application of Annex 5, the definitions of the legal regulations applicable to the respective mode of transport concerning the transport of dangerous goods are to be used [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

3. For the transport of nuclear materials exempt from the application of the Paris Convention pursuant to paragraph 2, § 8 paragraph 4 and 5 apply [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Damage Coverage (§ 8b)
For the determination of the coverage provision for damages according to § 26 paragraph 1a of the AtG, § 8a paragraph 1 applies accordingly [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Reactors (§ 9)
1. The standard coverage sum amounts to 70 million Euros for reactors with a maximum power of up to 25 Megawatts and 2.5 million Euros for every further Megawatt up to a maximum of 2.5 billion Euros. The standard coverage sum amounts to 70 million Euros increased by the amount determined according to Annex 3 for the approved mass of nuclear fuels, if this calculation yields a higher value than the calculation of the standard coverage sum according to Sentence 1. Maximum power is the thermal continuous power with which the reactor may be operated according to the permit [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

2. The standard coverage sum determined according to paragraph 1 includes the coverage provision for facilities for the storage of nuclear fuels or radioactive products and waste, which are intended for self-use or originate from the reactor and are temporarily stored until further use or disposal, if the facilities are considered a nuclear facility in the sense of § 2 paragraph 4 sentence 3 of the AtG [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

The coverage provision determined according to paragraph 1 also includes the coverage provision for:
1. Storage according to § 6 paragraph 3 of the AtG or
2. Corresponding storage on the premises of a facility for the splitting of nuclear fuels for research purposes, if the facilities are considered a nuclear facility in the sense of § 2 paragraph 4 sentence 3 of the AtG [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Ship Reactors (§ 10)
The standard coverage sum for reactors used to power ships (ship reactors) amounts to 500,000 Euros per Megawatt of maximum power, but at most 200 million Euros. § 9 paragraph 1 Sentence 2 and 3 apply accordingly [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Other Nuclear Facilities (§ 11)
1. For nuclear facilities that are not reactors, the standard coverage sum is 70 million Euros and increases:
    1. according to Annex 3, if the facility may handle nuclear fuels in the sense of § 2 paragraph 1 sentence 2 of the AtG with an approved mass of more than 250 kilograms,
    2. according to Annex 4, if the approved total activity of the facility exceeds $10^{12}$ times the release limits according to Annex 4 Table 1 Column 2 of the Radiation Protection Ordinance [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

The increase amounts according to Annex 3 and Annex 4 must be determined separately and then added to the amount of 70 million Euros to a standard coverage sum of at most 500 million Euros [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

2. If the owner of a nuclear facility may store irradiated nuclear fuels from facilities for the splitting of nuclear fuels for commercial electricity generation, paragraph 1 applies, with the proviso that the standard coverage sum of the nuclear facility shall not exceed 700 million Euros [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

3. For facilities for the generation, processing, or treatment of nuclear fuels, whose standard coverage sum is determined according to paragraph 1, § 9 paragraph 2 applies accordingly [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Decommissioning of Nuclear Facilities (§ 12)
1. If a nuclear facility is decommissioned or taken out of operation in any other way, and only activated and contaminated facility parts and radioactive substances for testing purposes remain on site, the standard coverage sum amounts to 70 million Euros increased by the amount determined according to Annex 4 according to the activity remaining in the facility [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

2. If the determination of the activity is not possible or only possible with disproportionate effort due to the special nature of the individual case, the administrative authority may reduce the coverage sum up to 5 percent of the coverage sum last set before the decommissioning or other removal, provided that the reduced coverage sum is not less than 70 million Euros [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Nuclear Facilities in Advanced Decommissioning (§ 12a)
1. The competent authority accepts the decommissioning of a nuclear facility, upon the owner's application, in which only activated and contaminated facility parts and radioactive substances for testing purposes remain, from the application of the Paris Convention, if:
    1. The facility complies with the activity-related provisions according to Annex 6 and
    2. The associated exposure of a representative person of the population in an event leading to an unintentional exposure does not exceed an effective dose of 1 millisievert in a calendar year without further protective measures [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

2. For nuclear facilities exempt from the application of the Paris Convention pursuant to paragraph 1, the standard coverage sum is determined according to the activity remaining in the facility according to Annex 2 Column 3. If the determination of the activity is not possible or only possible with disproportionate effort due to the special nature of the individual case, the administrative authority may reduce the coverage sum up to 3.5 million Euros [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Facilities for Generating Ionizing Radiation (§ 13)
1. The standard coverage sum for facilities for generating ionizing radiation, the construction and operation of which require a permit, is 25 million Euros. Deviating from Sentence 1, the standard coverage sum for particle accelerators operated for the generation of radioisotopes for use in positron emission tomography or single-photon emission tomography in connection with medical practice is 1.5 million Euros [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

2. If only the operation of the facility requires a permit, the standard coverage sum is:
    1. 5 million Euros, if the facility is used in medical practice,
    2. 1.5 million Euros, if more than $10^8$ neutrons are generated per second or the end energy of the accelerated electrons is more than 10 MeV or the end energy of the accelerated ions is more than 1 MeV per nucleon,
    3. 500,000 Euros in all other cases [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Application of Radioactive Substances to Humans in Medical Research (§ 15)
When radioactive substances or ionizing radiation are applied to humans in medical research, the coverage provision must be in an appropriate ratio to the risks associated with the application and must be set based on the risk assessment such that at least 500,000 Euros are available for the case of death or permanent disability of every person to whom the radioactive substances or ionizing radiation are applied [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Determining the Coverage Sum in Individual Cases (§ 16)
1. If the standard coverage sum according to the circumstances of the individual case is not appropriate, the administrative authority may increase the coverage sum within the maximum limit of § 13 paragraph 3 Sentence 2 of the AtG and while observing the maximum and minimum limits according to § 7 paragraph 2, or reduce it up to one-third of the standard coverage sum [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

2. When determining the appropriate coverage sum according to the circumstances of the individual case, particular consideration must be given to:
    1. Whether and to what extent it is possible or can be excluded that other persons other than the Obligated Party and their employees suffer damage to life, health, body, and property,
    2. What degree of safety is achieved by protective measures and protective facilities,
    3. Whether and to what extent, considering meteorological and hydrological conditions, it is possible or can be excluded that the radioactive substances are dispersed, especially as gases, aerosols, or liquids,
    4. What duration of danger must be assumed, especially with regard to the half-life of the radioactive substances,
    5. Whether and to what extent, considering the type, mass, or nature of the radioactive substances, damages due to nuclear events resulting from nuclear fission processes can be excluded even under unfavorable circumstances,
    6. Whether and to what extent, in the case of transport, considering the mode of transport, the transport route, the packaging, and the nature of the radioactive substances, particularly high or low dangers exist [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Coverage Sum for Multiple Handling (§ 18)
1. If the Obligated Party handles multiple substances or multiple portions of a substance due to one or more permits, a separate coverage sum must be set for each activity requiring coverage provision [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

However, a total coverage sum must be set if there is such a close spatial and temporal connection during multiple handling outside a nuclear facility that the multiple substances or portions must be considered as similarly dangerous as a single substance, whose activity or mass corresponds to the total activity or total mass of the substances or portions [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

2. When setting the total coverage sum, the total activity, expressed in multiples of the activity release limits, must be assumed for enclosed and open other radioactive substances [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

3. If substances belonging to both groups mentioned in Sentence 1 are handled, the coverage sums determined separately for each group are to be added together; however, the total must not exceed the coverage sum that would result if all substances were open other radioactive substances [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

4. For transport, paragraphs 1 to 3 apply accordingly [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Rounding of the Coverage Sum (§ 19)
1. The coverage sum must be set at full 50,000 Euros.

2. If an intermediate amount is derived from the provisions on the coverage sum, and it is below 25,000 Euros, it must be rounded down; otherwise, it must be rounded up [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## New Determination of Coverage Provision (§ 20)
If the coverage provision for an activity permitted before the entry into force of this regulation no longer meets the requirements of this regulation, the coverage provision must be newly set at the next determination according to § 13 paragraph 1 Sentence 2 of the AtG, whereby the new determination must take place for nuclear facilities and for the transport of nuclear materials no later than six months after the entry into force of this regulation, and in other cases within one year after the entry into force of this regulation [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

### Annex 5 (for § 8a paragraph 2)
Provisions for the exclusion of small quantities of nuclear materials outside a nuclear facility from the application of the Paris Convention
(Source: BGBl. I 2022, 129 - 137)

**Part A: General**
For shipments containing radionuclides, subject to the supplementary application of Part B for shipments of fissile materials, the following applies:

1. If a shipment contains a radionuclide, the total activity per mode of transport must not exceed one hundred times the A2 value of the radionuclide. For the radionuclide, the respective A2 value according to Table 1 is to be used, if the type and activity of the radionuclide are known. Otherwise, the respective A2 value according to Table 2 is to be used for the radionuclide present.

2. If a shipment contains several radionuclides, the sum of the ratios of the activity B(i) and one hundred times the respective A2(i) values of the individual radionuclides (sum formula) must not exceed the value of 1 per mode of transport:
$$
\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} B(i)}{100 \cdot \sum_{i=1}^{n} A2(i)} \le 1
$$
where B(i) is the activity of radionuclide i and A2(i) is the A2 value of radionuclide i. For the calculation, the respective A2 values according to Table 1 are to be used for the radionuclides, if the type and activity of the radionuclides are known. Otherwise, the respective A2 values according to Table 2 are to be used for the radionuclides present [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

**Part B: Shipments of Fissile Materials**
A shipment containing fissile materials must meet the requirements of Part A and the fissile materials must be exempt from classification "SPALTBAR" according to the legal regulations applicable to the respective mode of transport concerning the transport of dangerous goods [AtDeckV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
