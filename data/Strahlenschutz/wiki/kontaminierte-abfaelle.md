The management of waste contaminated by or potentially contaminated by ionizing radiation is strictly regulated under the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG).

## Regulatory Framework
The law establishes specific rules for:
*   **Contamination Values (§ 95):** The Federal Government must stipulate contamination values for waste and objects contaminated by an emergency. If these values are undercut, it is assumed that protection for man and environment is ensured during management.
*   **Scope of Management (§ 95):** Regulations cover the disposal, recovery, and other management of such waste, including requirements for facilities, wastewater facilities, and storage/treatment sites.
*   **Exceptions:** Exceptions to existing waste management laws can be granted, but only if no danger to human health is expected and if the measures taken avoid significant disadvantages for the public.

## Rights and Obligations
*   **Information Access (§ 95a):** The law grants the competent authority's employees and agents the right to request information, inspect properties, and conduct technical investigations from generators, owners, operators, and waste handlers. This right extends to properties and premises, even outside usual business hours, if necessary to prevent urgent danger.
*   **Data Security:** Information obtained during these inspections is subject to specific rules regarding its use and retention, exempting it from certain fiscal laws.
