Sanierung Maßnahmen (Remediation Measures) for radon gas focus on interrupting the pathways of gas ingress and reducing the concentration within the structure [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md]. The effectiveness of these measures is highly dependent on the specific implementation and the integrity of the building envelope [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Remediation Techniques and Effectiveness
The achievable reduction potential varies significantly based on the method employed [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md]:

*   **Simple Sealing:** Can achieve reductions ranging from 0 to 25 %, suitable for areas up to 1,000 Bq/m³ [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Negative Pressure in Basements:** Using small fans for basement ventilation can achieve reductions between 50 % and 80 % [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Insulation Layers:**
    *   In or on floors without involving walls: 30 % to 80 % reduction [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
    *   In or on floors involving walls: 50 % to 90 % reduction, provided walls are continuously sealed with foil [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Underground Drainage System:** Negative pressure maintenance under the building via drainage point suction can achieve 10 % to 95 % reduction, depending on soil permeability [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
*   **Comprehensive Measures:** The highest reduction potential (up to 99 %) is achieved through elaborate insulation in newly inserted floors combined with negative pressure maintenance under the building, provided there is a continuous sealing layer throughout the entire structure [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].

## Inspection Protocol for Remediation Planning
Before implementing measures, a detailed inspection must be conducted to identify all potential radon entry and dispersion paths. This includes:
1.  **Structural Assessment:** Checking all boundaries, basement walls, and floors for cracks and weak spots.
2.  **Utility Assessment:** Ensuring all pipes and lines are sealed against radon gas.
3.  **Pressure Analysis:** Analyzing the overall pressure situation within the building, as this dictates the necessary ventilation strategy [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 5/5].md].
