# Gesetz über die friedliche Verwendung der Kernenergie und den Schutz gegen ihre Gefahren (Atomgesetz)

This summary details excerpts from the *Gesetz über die friedliche Verwendung der Kernenergie und den Schutz gegen ihre Gefahren* (Atomgesetz - AtG), originally issued on December 23, 1959 [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md]. The law's purpose is multifaceted, covering the orderly phasing out of nuclear energy use, protecting public health, and ensuring international compliance [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].

## Purpose of the Law (§ 1)
The primary objectives of the AtG include:
1.  Ensuring the orderly phasing out of the use of nuclear energy for commercial electricity generation until the point of cessation [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
2.  Protecting life, health, and property from the dangers of nuclear energy and the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, and compensating for damages caused by these sources [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
3.  Preventing the endangerment of the internal or external security of the Federal Republic of Germany through the application or release of nuclear energy or ionizing radiation [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
4.  Guaranteeing the fulfillment of international obligations of the Federal Republic of Germany in the field of nuclear energy and radiation protection [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].

## Definitions (§ 2)
The AtG establishes key definitions:

*   **Radioactive Substances:** These are defined as all substances containing one or more radionuclides, whose activity or specific activity cannot be disregarded in connection with nuclear energy or radiation protection according to the relevant laws [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
*   **Nuclear Fuels:** These are specific fissile materials, including:
    1.  Plutonium 239 and Plutonium 241 [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
    2.  Uran enriched with isotopes 235 or 233 [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
    3.  Any substance containing one or more of the materials listed in points 1 and 2, and which can sustain a self-supporting chain reaction in a suitable facility [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
*   **Exclusion of Activity:** The AtG specifies conditions under which the activity or specific activity of a substance can be disregarded:
    *   If the substance falls below established exemption limits, or if it is a substance generated during a licensed activity that falls below established exemption values, and has been released [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
    *   If it is a naturally occurring substance that is not used as nuclear fuel or for generating nuclear fuel, and is not subject to monitoring under the law [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].

## Environmental and Disposal Requirements
The AtG mandates a structured approach to waste disposal, particularly concerning the national strategy:
*   **Environmental Impact Assessment (UVP):** If an obligation for an Environmental Impact Assessment exists under the Environmental Impact Assessment Act, the UVP is an inseparable part of the procedures for granting permits or development plans under this law [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
*   **National Disposal Program:** The Federal Government must establish a National Disposal Program detailing the national strategy for the responsible and safe disposal of spent fuel elements and radioactive waste. This program must include:
    *   Overall goals for the national strategy [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
    *   A national inventory of all spent fuel elements and radioactive waste, including estimates of future quantities, classified by type and properties [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
    *   Concepts or plans for disposal from the point of generation to final disposal [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
    *   A cost estimate and the underlying assumptions [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
*   **Disposal Principles:** Disposal must adhere to principles such as limiting waste generation through suitable design and operation procedures, considering the interdependencies of disposal steps, and ensuring safety, including passive safety aspects [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].

## Licensing and Transport
The AtG regulates the movement and storage of nuclear materials:

*   **Import/Export:** Importing or exporting nuclear fuels requires a permit, ensuring that the materials are used in compliance with the law and international obligations [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
*   **Transport:** Transporting nuclear fuels outside a closed site requires a permit. This permit requires proof of reliability for the applicant, the carrier, and the personnel involved, and ensuring that the transport is conducted according to regulations for dangerous goods [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
*   **Storage:** Keeping nuclear fuels outside state storage requires a permit, which must confirm the necessity for such storage and ensure safety measures against damage and third-party interference [AtG.md [Teil 1/5].md].
