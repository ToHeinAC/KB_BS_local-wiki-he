# Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG (Teil 9/13)

This summary details sections of the German Radiation Protection Act (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG), covering mandatory reporting, exposure assessment, occupational protection, and procedures for radioactive contaminated sites.

## § 128 Mandatory Reporting (Arbeitsplatz)
The responsible party (§ 128 Abs. 1) must immediately report the workplace to the competent authority if a measurement (§ 128 Abs. 2 Satz 1) does not show a reduction below the reference value (§ 126). The submission must include:
1. Information on the type of workplace and the number of affected employees.
2. The results of measurements according to § 127 Abs. 1.
3. Information on measures taken to reduce the Radon-222 activity concentration, as well as the results of measurements according to § 128 Abs. 2.
4. Further planned measures to reduce exposure.

If the responsible party fails to take measures based on § 128 Abs. 4, they must immediately report the workplace to the competent authority upon becoming aware of the special reasons. The submission requires the documents from Paragraph 1 Sentence 2; deviating from Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, the reason why no measures were taken must be justified. If the presented reasons do not justify waiving measures, the competent authority may order measures to reduce the Radon-222 activity concentration in the air at that workplace [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

A third party (§ 129 Abs. 3) operating or having operate activities in foreign premises must immediately report this activity if it is carried out at multiple workplaces requiring reporting under Paragraph 1 Sentence 1. The responsible parties for the workplaces must provide the necessary information to the third party [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## § 130 Assessment of Exposure
The obligated party (§ 130 Abs. 1) must conduct an assessment of the Radon-222 exposure, potential alpha energy exposure, or body dose due to Radon exposure related to the workplace within six months of the reporting. If the reporting is done by a third party (§ 129 Abs. 3 Satz 1), the assessment must cover the entire activity. The assessment must be repeated immediately if the workplace changes in a way that could lead to higher exposure. The results must be recorded and submitted immediately to the competent authority. The results of the assessment must be kept for five years [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

If the assessment shows that the effective dose cannot exceed 6 millisieverts in the calendar year, the obligated party must regularly check the Radon exposure. They must keep the exposure as low as possible through suitable radiation protection measures based on general occupational safety regulations and considering all individual circumstances. The competent authority may require the submission of corresponding proof [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

If the assessment shows that the effective dose can exceed 6 millisieverts in the calendar year, the requirements of occupational radiation protection according to § 131 and the regulation according to § 132 Satz 2 Nummer 6 must be met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## § 131 Occupational Radiation Protection
If the result of the assessment according to § 130 Absatz 3 requires compliance with occupational radiation protection requirements, the obligated party must take suitable measures, considering all individual circumstances, to keep the exposure to Radon as low as possible [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

Specific requirements include:
1. Determining the Radon-222 exposure, potential alpha energy exposure, or body dose of employees at workplaces requiring reporting in a suitable manner through measurement.
2. Ensuring that dose limits are not exceeded and body doses are determined according to § 166. The regulations and limits of §§ 77 and 78 Absatz 1 and 3 Satz 1 and 3 apply accordingly.
3. Ensuring that the requirements of occupational radiation protection according to the regulation enacted pursuant to § 132 Satz 2 Nummer 6 are met [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## § 131a Change or Termination of the Reported Workplace
The responsible party who reported a workplace under § 129 must immediately notify the competent authority of the following changes:
1. The termination of the workplace.
2. Changes that demonstrably result in the Radon-222 activity concentration in the air at the reported workplace no longer exceeding the reference value according to § 126; proof must be provided by measurement according to § 127 Absatz 1.
3. Changes that demonstrably result in an assessment of the exposure related to the reported workplace according to § 130 Absatz 1, showing that the effective dose no longer can exceed 6 millisieverts in the calendar year [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## § 133 Reference Value
The reference value for the effective dose from external exposure of individuals in residential rooms due to gamma radiation from building products is 1 millisievert in the calendar year, in addition to the effective dose from external exposure outdoors [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## § 136 Radioactive Contaminated Sites
Radioactive contaminated sites are properties, parts of properties, buildings, or bodies of water contaminated by completed human activities, if the contamination causes or can cause an exposure exceeding the reference value of 1 millisievert in the calendar year for individuals in the population [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

**Responsibility for Radioactive Contaminated Sites (§ 137):**
Responsible parties include:
1. Whoever caused the contamination.
2. A person who follows in total legal succession to the person listed in Number 1.
3. The owner of the radioactive contaminated site.
4. Whoever exercises actual control over the radioactive contaminated site.
5. Whoever relinquishes ownership of the radioactive contaminated site.

Responsibility also extends to those who are legally liable for a legal entity, and to the former owner if they knew or should have known about the contamination and the ownership was transferred after December 31, 2018 (unless the transfer relied on trust that was justifiable considering the individual circumstances) [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## § 138 Suspicion of Radioactive Contaminated Sites
If a person listed in § 137 has grounds for suspecting the existence of a radioactive contaminated site, they must notify the competent authority immediately. If the competent authority has grounds for suspicion, it shall take appropriate measures to determine the facts. If there is sufficient suspicion, the competent authority can require the persons listed in § 137 to carry out the necessary investigations, particularly regarding the type, level, and extent of the contamination and the exposure [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## § 139 Authority Powers for Measures
If a radioactive contaminated site exists, the competent authority can require the responsible party to:
1. Conduct investigations into the type and extent of the radioactive contaminated site, as well as the exposure and possible remediation and other measures to prevent or reduce exposure.
2. Report the results of these investigations to the competent authority.
3. Ensure remediation measures, other measures to prevent or reduce exposure, or follow-up measures so that the reference value according to § 136 Absatz 1 is undercut.
4. Monitor the exposure of the population resulting from the remediation work.
5. Carry out further measures even after implementing measures according to Number 3, if necessary to secure the goal of remediation or other measures to prevent or reduce exposure.
6. Monitor the emissions and immissions containing radionuclides emanating from the radioactive contaminated site, including direct radiation [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## § 143 Remediation Planning
In cases of radioactive contaminated sites where a coordinated approach is necessary due to the variety of required measures, or where risks to the individual or the public arise due to the type or extent of the contamination, the competent authority can require the responsible party to submit a remediation plan. This plan must include:
1. A presentation of the results of the investigations, regarding the type and extent of the radioactive contaminated site, and a summary of the exposure assessment.
2. Information on the previous and future use of the contaminated properties.
3. A presentation of the planned remediation measures, other measures to prevent or reduce exposure, and follow-up measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].

## § 145 Protection of Workers
For remediation and other measures to prevent and reduce exposure at radioactive contaminated sites, the person carrying out the measures professionally, or having them carried out by employees under their supervision, must conduct an assessment of the body dose of the employees before the start of the measures. The assessment must be repeated immediately if the work situation changes in a way that could lead to higher exposure. The results must be recorded, kept for five years, and submitted to the competent authority upon request.

If the assessment shows that the body dose could exceed one of the values for classification as a professionally exposed person, the obligated party must report the execution of the measures to the competent authority before their commencement. The submission must include:
1. Information on the measures to be carried out.
2. The assessment of the body dose.
3. The number of affected employees.
4. Information on the precautions and measures planned for the reduction of occupational exposure during the execution of the measures [StrlSchG.md [Teil 9/13].md].
