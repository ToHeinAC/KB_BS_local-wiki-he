# Atomrechtliche Entsorgungsverordnung - AtEV

The *Atomrechtliche Entsorgungsverordnung* (AtEV) is a regulation concerning the requirements and procedures for the disposal of radioactive waste. It serves to implement the Council Directive 2013/59/Euratom, establishing fundamental safety standards for protection against the hazards of exposure to ionizing radiation [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Scope and Applicability
The AtEV outlines obligations for entities conducting activities governed by the Atomic Energy Act (*Atomgesetz*) or the Radiation Protection Act (*Strahlenschutzgesetz* - StrlSchG) [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

### Waste Generation and Storage (§ 1)
Any entity planning or conducting activities under specific sections of the Atomic Energy Act or the StrlSchG is obligated to:
1.  Estimate the expected annual generation of radioactive waste for the entire duration of the activity, and communicate this, along with the planned storage location, to the competent authority [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
2.  After commencing the activity, prove the storage of radioactive waste by estimating the expected annual generation for the current calendar year and subsequent years, and providing details on the storage [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*Note: These requirements do not apply to irradiated fuel elements and radioactive waste from reprocessing covered by Annex Part D.*

### Waste Recording and Documentation (§ 2)
Entities performing activities under the relevant laws must:
1.  Record radioactive waste according to Annex Parts A and B, and irradiated fuel elements/reprocessed waste according to Part D [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
2.  Maintain these records in electronic accounting systems that can be provided to the competent authority upon request [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Waste Handling and Disposal Procedures
The AtEV details mandatory procedures for waste management:

### Treatment and Packaging (§ 3)
The competent authority or a designated body can order the type of treatment and packaging for radioactive waste before delivery, requiring proof of compliance [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Packaging:** Containers must bear a marking according to Annex Part B [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Treatment:** Procedures must be used for treating and packaging radioactive waste to create disposal-capable waste packages, following approval from the designated third party [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

### Disposal Obligations (§ 5)
Radioactive waste must be delivered to a federal facility for securing and disposal if it originates from:
1.  State custody of nuclear fuel according to § 5 Abs. 3 and 4 of the Atomic Energy Act [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
2.  Storage according to § 6 of the Atomic Energy Act [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
3.  Facilities requiring approval under § 7 of the Atomic Energy Act [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
4.  Activities under § 9 of the Atomic Energy Act [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
5.  Activities falling under § 2 Abs. 3 of the Atomic Energy Act, if not subject to the approval requirement for handling nuclear fuel under § 9 [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

### Transport and Transfer (§ 4)
*   **Handover:** The waste giver must obtain a written declaration of acceptance readiness from the recipient and provide the recorded data from § 2 Abs. 1 in electronic form [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Transport:** If waste is transported, notification must be given to the competent authority at least five working days before transport begins, using a transport notification according to Annex Part C [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].

## Annex Details
The regulation includes detailed annexes for technical compliance:
*   **Annex Part A:** Categorization of radioactive waste with negligible heat generation (e.g., RA Rohabfall, VA Vorbehandelter Abfall, P2 Produktkontrollierte Abfallprodukte) [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Annex Part B:** Structure for electronic accounting systems, including unique identifiers (e.g., `_E1 KWG2 20163 R4 0000015`) [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
*   **Annex Part C:** Required data for the transport notification.
*   **Annex Part D:** Data required for irradiated fuel elements and radioactive waste from reprocessing in ingots (Kokillen) [AtEV.md [Teil 1/2].md].
