The *Atomrechtliche Entsorgungsverordnung* (AtEV) is the German federal regulation governing the requirements and procedures for the disposal of radioactive waste. It implements Council Directive 2013/59/Euratom and applies to entities operating under the *Atomgesetz* (AtG) or the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) [AtEV.md].

## Scope and Applicability
The AtEV obliges any entity planning or conducting activities under the relevant sections of the AtG or StrlSchG to:
- Estimate expected annual generation of radioactive waste for the entire duration of the activity and report planned storage locations to the competent authority [AtEV.md].
- After commencing the activity, prove the storage of radioactive waste annually including projections for current and subsequent years [AtEV.md].

These requirements do not apply to irradiated fuel elements and radioactive waste from reprocessing covered by Annex Part D.

## Waste Recording and Documentation (§ 2)
Entities must record radioactive waste according to Annex Parts A and B, and irradiated fuel elements/reprocessed waste according to Part D. Records must be maintained in electronic accounting systems accessible to the competent authority on request [AtEV.md].

## Waste Handling and Disposal

### Treatment and Packaging (§ 3)
The competent authority or a designated body may order specific treatment and packaging for radioactive waste before delivery. Containers must bear markings per Annex Part B. Approved procedures must be used for treating and packaging waste to produce disposal-capable waste packages [AtEV.md].

### Transport and Transfer (§ 4)
The waste originator must obtain a written declaration of acceptance readiness from the recipient and provide the electronic records from § 2 Abs. 1. Transport must be notified to the competent authority at least five working days in advance using the transport notification form in Annex Part C [AtEV.md].

### Disposal Obligations (§ 5)
Radioactive waste must be delivered to a federal facility for securing and disposal if it originates from state custody of nuclear fuel, storage under § 6 AtG, facilities requiring approval under § 7 AtG, or activities under § 9 AtG [AtEV.md].

## Technical Annexes

### Annex Part A — Waste Categorization
Categorizes radioactive waste with negligible heat generation: RA (Rohabfall), VA (Vorbehandelter Abfall), P2 (Produktkontrollierte Abfallprodukte) [AtEV.md].

### Annex Part B — Electronic Accounting
Defines the structure of electronic accounting systems including unique container identifiers (e.g. `_E1 KWG2 20163 R4 0000015`) and waste characterization parameters [AtEV.md].

### Annex Part C — Transport Notification
Specifies required data fields for the transport notification form [AtEV.md].

### Annex Part D — Irradiated Fuel and Reprocessing Waste
Defines data requirements for irradiated fuel elements and vitrified reprocessing waste (Kokillen), including:

**Spent Fuel Elements:** burnup, discharge date, number of reactor cycles, burnup increment per cycle, mean fuel element power, peaking factor, oxide layer thickness, and isotopic composition of U and Pu at discharge [AtEV.md].

**Pellet Fuel Elements (Kugelbrennelemente):** number of fuel element canisters per container, canister identification, empty mass, position in container, number of spheres per canister, material, total mass, average and maximum burnup, burnup distribution, sphere type counts (HEU/LEU/Moderator/Absorber), and maximum fissile inventory per sphere [AtEV.md].

**Vitrified Radioactive Waste:** fixing agent quality, glass matrix composition, mixing ratio (waste:frit:additives), waste oxide content, glass frit chemical composition, homogeneity of activity distribution, volume and mass of the Kokille, and glass product state (density, transformation temperature) [AtEV.md].

**Compacted Radioactive Waste:** ingot position in container, ingot material, pressing pressure, pressling dimensions (weight, diameter, height), whether casings and structural parts were compacted together with technology waste, cartridge material, empty cartridge weight, and number of presslings [AtEV.md].
