The Radiation Protection Register (Strahlenschutzregister) is a central repository established under the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) for collecting and managing data related to professional radiation exposure [StrlSchG.md].

## Purpose and Scope
The register serves multiple purposes:
1.  Monitoring dose limits and observing radiation protection principles [StrlSchG.md].
2.  Checking claims against statutory accident insurance [StrlSchG.md].
3.  Scientific research in the field of radiation protection [StrlSchG.md].

## Data Recorded
The following data are entered into the register:
*   The personal identification number according to § 170 (3) [StrlSchG.md].
*   The respective personal data [StrlSchG.md].
*   Employment characteristics and exposure conditions [StrlSchG.md].
*   The operating number of the employing operation [StrlSchG.md].
*   Name and address of the radiation protection responsible person, the obligated party, and the responsible party [StrlSchG.md].
*   Data on a radiation pass registered according to a law supporting this law [StrlSchG.md].
*   Data on the competent authority [StrlSchG.md].
*   The body dose determined due to professional exposure, the exposure conditions, and the findings of the competent authority regarding this body dose and the exposure conditions [StrlSchG.md].

## Management and Access
*   **Identification:** The Federal Radiation Protection Agency assigns a personal identification number, derived from the insurance number, to ensure unique assignment [StrlSchG.md].
*   **Submission:** Data are submitted by measurement stations, competent authorities, or the responsible parties [StrlSchG.md].
*   **Access:** Information can be provided to competent authorities, measurement stations, or responsible parties/employers upon request, subject to data protection laws [StrlSchG.md].
*   **Research:** Transmission of personal data for research purposes requires consent from the affected persons, unless public interest significantly outweighs the privacy interest [StrlSchG.md].
