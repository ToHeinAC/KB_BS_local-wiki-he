This document summarizes excerpts from the *Gesetz zum Schutz vor der schädlichen Wirkung ionisierender Strahlung* (Strahlenschutzgesetz - StrlSchG) from Teil 7/13, detailing various regulatory powers, reporting obligations, and emergency planning procedures related to ionizing radiation.

### § 89 Verordnungsermächtigungen zu der Sicherheit von Strahlungsquellen
The Federal Government is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to determine measures for the protection of people against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation and for the control and securing of radioactive materials. These measures include provisions regarding:
1.  Maintaining a register on the generation, acquisition, possession, location, disposal, and other whereabouts of radioactive materials, and regarding measurements of dose and dose rates, including the manner of reporting and document retention.
2.  Requirements for the securing and storage of radioactive materials.
3.  Requirements for the maintenance and inspection of equipment, devices, and other apparatus, and who must perform this maintenance and inspection.
4.  Requirements for leak testing of enclosed radioactive materials and who must perform the leak testing.
5.  Which radiation measuring devices must be used and what requirements apply to them.
6.  Which areas, rooms, devices, apparatus, containers, enclosures, facilities for the generation of ionizing radiation, and which type-approved devices must be marked, how and under what conditions the marking must occur, and in which cases markings must be removed.
7.  Requirements for the disposal of radioactive materials.
8.  Requirements for the return of high-radioactive sources.
9.  In which cases X-ray rooms or irradiation rooms must be used for activities involving radiation sources, and what requirements apply to these rooms.
10. Which persons are permitted to apply radiation or technically perform the application during activities involving radiation sources, how and when persons must be instructed during such activities, and what documents must be available when performing these activities, including the requirement to keep records of the instructions and present them to the authority upon request.
11. Further preventive and monitoring measures for the control of radioactive materials for the protection of individuals and the general public, and what such measures must be.
12. Further obligations regarding recording, retention, notification, submission, and deposit in connection with the obligations under numbers 1 to 11.
The ordinance can also stipulate provisions of the ordinance for whose compliance the radiation protection officer must ensure. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 90 Verordnungsermächtigung für Pflichten, Aufgaben und Befugnisse bei Vorkommnissen; Aufzeichnungs-, Übermittlungs- und Aufbewahrungspflichten
The Federal Government is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to stipulate the duties of the radiation protection officer, as well as the tasks and powers of the authorities, concerning incidents in planned exposure situations. Specifically, the ordinance may stipulate:
1.  What measures the radiation protection officer must initiate to keep exposures in such an incident as low as possible.
2.  What measures the radiation protection officer must take to prevent such incidents in the future.
3.  How the radiation protection officer must record and investigate an incident, and for what period they must retain related records.
4.  How the radiation protection officer must report an incident to the supervisory authority, and must report information and findings on the causes and effects of the incident, as well as measures for remediation or limitation of the effects, and measures for preventing incidents.
5.  How the supervisory authority records, checks, and evaluates reports according to number 4.
6.  How the Federal Office for Radiation Protection maintains a central body for the recording, processing, and evaluation of information and findings on incidents related to the application of radioactive materials or ionizing radiation.
7.  How the supervisory authority and the central body transmit information and findings on an incident related to the application of radioactive materials or ionizing radiation to humans, and their respective evaluations.
8.  Under what conditions and in what manner the supervisory authority and the central body publish information and findings on incidents.
(2) The radiation protection officer must ensure that in the event of an incident subject to Paragraph 1 of the ordinance, the name, first names, date and place of birth, gender, and address, as well as data on the exposure of the exposed person due to the incident and the health consequences of the exposure are immediately recorded. If the radiation protection officer must report the incident according to the ordinance, and measures are required to protect the exposed person, they must immediately transmit the data to the competent authority. The data must be secured against unauthorized access by technical and organizational measures. In other cases than in sentence 2, the data must be transmitted to the competent authority upon request. The data must be retained for 30 years and must be deleted immediately after the expiration of this period. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 91 Verordnungsermächtigung für Informationspflichten des Herstellers oder Lieferanten von Geräten
The Federal Government is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to stipulate that the manufacturer or supplier of facilities for the generation of ionizing radiation, X-ray equipment, scatter radiation, irradiation devices, and other equipment, devices, and apparatus used in connection with activities, must provide the radiation protection officer with information about these devices. Specifically, the ordinance may stipulate:
1.  When the manufacturer or supplier must provide information to the radiation protection officer for which of the mentioned devices.
2.  What data and documents must be provided.
3.  For what purposes the documents must be suitable and what requirements they must meet.
4.  That the information must also be available to the person intending to become the radiation protection officer. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 92 Notfallschutzgrundsätze
(1) The provisions of the following paragraphs (Emergency Protection Principles) must be considered as requirements when assessing dangers that may arise in emergencies due to ionizing radiation in the following cases:
1. When enacting, reviewing, and amending emergency plans and ordinances under this chapter and under § 117.
2. During the emergency response by the competent authorities and the authorities and organizations assisting in the emergency response based on this law, the ordinances mentioned in number 1, and federal and state legal provisions for the defense against dangers for human health, the environment, or public safety, insofar as they are applicable to radiological dangers, and immediately applicable acts of the European Union and the European Atomic Community, insofar as they do not make conclusive provisions for member states regarding radiological dangers.
(2) The reference values set out in this law and in the ordinances issued on the basis of this law for the protection of the population and the emergency services during emergencies should be kept as low as possible.
(3) The exposure of the population and the emergency services as well as the contamination of the environment during emergencies must be kept as low as possible through appropriate measures, taking into account the state of science and all circumstances of the respective emergency, even below the reference values. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 93 Referenzwerte für den Schutz der Bevölkerung; Verordnungsermächtigungen
(1) For the protection of the population, a reference value of 100 millisieverts applies when planning protection measures and making decisions on their implementation in an emergency, representing the effective dose that affected persons would receive within one year through all exposure pathways if the planned protection measures were implemented. The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to stipulate procedures and assumptions for estimating the extent to which this reference value is undercut, maintained, or exceeded.
(2) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to stipulate supplementary appropriate reference values for organ equivalent doses for possible emergencies or for an already occurred emergency. This applies particularly to facilitate cooperation with other member states of the European Union and the European Atomic Community or third countries in the protection of the population.
(3) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to stipulate a lower reference value for the effective dose, relative to a year or a single exposure, for an already occurred emergency. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 94 Dosiswerte und Kontaminationswerte für den Schutz der Bevölkerung; Verordnungsermächtigungen
(1) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety shall stipulate, by ordinance with the consent of the Bundesrat, dose values that serve as a radiological criterion for the appropriateness of the following protection measures:
1. Recommendation to stay in buildings.
2. Distribution of iodine tablets or recommendation for the ingestion of iodine tablets.
3. Evacuation.
These values relate to the dose that affected persons would receive in a specific period after the onset of the emergency without protective measures.
(2) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized by ordinance, with the consent of the Bundesrat, to stipulate limit values for emergency-related contaminations or dose rates for possible emergencies, for an already occurred emergency, and for an exposure situation remaining after an emergency:
1. For individual persons of the population.
2. For drinking water.
3. For food, feed, necessities, cosmetic products, and products within the meaning of § 2 Number 1 of the Tobacco Products Act.
4. For medicinal products and their starting materials, as well as for medical devices.
5. For other products, objects, and materials.
6. For vehicles, goods, or baggage.
7. For contaminated areas, especially for contaminated properties and waters, whose exceeding is to be assumed to constitute a danger for individual persons of the population due to ionizing radiation. These limit values serve the implementation of optimized protection strategies according to § 98 Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 Number 1.
(3) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety is authorized by ordinance without the consent of the Bundesrat to repeal, declare temporarily inapplicable, or adapt the wording of the ordinances according to Paragraph 2, insofar as they have become indefinitely or temporarily inapplicable by corresponding legal acts of the European Atomic Community or the European Union.
(4) In the ordinances according to Paragraphs 1 and 2, procedures and assumptions for the measurement, calculation, or estimation of dose values, contamination values, or dose rate values may also be stipulated, or prerequisites may be stipulated under which these values apply.
(5) The ordinances according to Paragraph 2 are concluded in agreement with the Federal Ministry of Health, the Federal Ministry of Food and Agriculture, the Federal Ministry of Economics and Energy, the Federal Ministry of Labor and Social Affairs, the Federal Ministry of Transport and Digital Infrastructure, the Federal Ministry of the Interior, for Building and Home, and the Federal Ministry of Finance. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 95 Bewirtschaftung von Abfällen, die infolge eines Notfalls kontaminiert sein können, Errichtung und Betrieb von Anlagen; Verordnungsermächtigungen
(1) The Federal Government shall stipulate, by ordinance with the consent of the Bundesrat, contamination values for waste and other objects or materials that are contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency, for possible emergencies, for an already occurred emergency, and for an exposure situation remaining after an emergency. If these contamination values are undercut, it is assumed that the necessary protection of man and environment against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation is ensured when managing this waste, as well as when establishing, operating, or using the facilities listed below, in accordance with the Circular Economy Act and other federal laws applicable to waste and to the facilities based on these laws and the ordinances based on these laws without additional special protective measures:
1. Facilities where this waste is disposed of.
2. Wastewater facilities that receive wastewater that is contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency.
3. Facilities where this waste or these other objects or materials are stored, used, or treated, or may be stored, used, or treated, especially as fuel, raw material, material, precursor product, lubricating, dissolving, or other auxiliary agent.
(2) To ensure the protection of man and environment against the harmful effects of ionizing radiation, the Federal Government shall regulate by ordinance with the consent of the Bundesrat for the prevention, recovery, disposal, or other management of waste that is contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency, the additional requirements and exceptions to the following legal provisions or allow the issuance of exceptions to these legal provisions by the competent authorities:
1. to the Circular Economy Act and other federal laws applicable to waste and to the ordinances based on these laws, and
2. to federal laws governing the establishment and operation of the facilities mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, and to the ordinances based on these laws.
Exceptions may only be regulated, permitted, or granted insofar as dangers to human health are not expected thereby and European Union or European Atomic Community acts do not contradict this. In such exceptions, significant disadvantages for the general public or the neighborhood must be avoided or reduced, insofar as this is possible and appropriate considering the radiological situation and the other considerable circumstances of the respective emergency. For the exceptions and the supplementary regulations, requirements for prevention against harmful environmental impacts and other dangers, as well as against significant disadvantages and significant nuisances, must be considered, particularly by taking measures corresponding to the state of the art.
(3) The regulations according to Paragraph 2 relate in particular to:
1. The priority of measures for waste prevention and waste management.
2. Requirements for the harmlessness of recovery.
3. The order and execution of waste disposal.
4. Requirements for the establishment and operation of landfills and their approval, including the approval procedure.
5. Requirements for the monitoring of waste management.
6. Requirements for collectors, transporters, dealers, and brokers of waste, and their respective approval including the approval procedure.
7. Requirements for the establishment, nature, operation, and essential change of the facilities mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, the approval of these facilities including the approval procedure, as well as the condition of the facility and the facility site after cessation of operation.
8. Requirements for the use of the wastewater facilities mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 Number 2.
9. Requirements for the use of waters, especially for the introduction and discharge of substances into a body of water; the requirements can also be stipulated for the location of the wastewater incident or before its mixing.
10. Requirements for fulfilling the wastewater disposal obligation.
11. Requirements for monitoring water properties.
12. Measurement methods and procedures, particularly in the context of wastewater disposal and monitoring of water properties.
13. Duties of the operators of the facilities mentioned in Paragraph 1 Sentence 2.
14. The prerequisites under which the competent authorities can grant exceptions by an ordinance according to Paragraph 2.
15. The requirements to be fulfilled for the obligations arising from Paragraph 2 Sentence 2 and 3.
(4) The states shall stipulate which legal persons are obliged as public law waste disposal operators in the sense of § 17 of the Circular Economy Act to dispose of such waste from private households and from other sources that cannot be treated, stored, or disposed of in the facilities intended for the disposal of other waste, due to their emergency-related contamination.
(5) For ordinances according to Paragraphs 1 to 3, § 94 Paragraph 3 and 4 apply accordingly. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 95a Auskunftsverlangen, Betretensrechte, Mitwirkungs- und Duldungspflichten
(1) Information regarding waste and other objects or materials that are contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency, regarding the establishment, operation, and use of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, regarding properties where such waste, other objects or materials, or such facilities may be located, as well as other objects or materials subject to supervision according to § 178 Sentence 2, must be provided to the employees and agents of the competent authority responsible for supervision according to § 178 Sentence 2 upon request:
1. Generators and owners of waste or other objects or materials that are contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency.
2. Those obliged to dispose of waste that is contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency.
3. Owners and operators, and former operators of:
    a) companies that dispose of such waste.
    b) the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, even if these facilities are decommissioned.
4. Owners and owners of properties where the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 are operated or were operated, as well as
5. Collectors, transporters, dealers, and brokers of waste that is contaminated or may be contaminated by an emergency.
(2) The persons obliged to provide information according to Paragraph 1 must allow the employees and agents of the competent authority responsible for supervision according to § 178 Sentence 2 to inspect the properties and business and operational premises during usual business hours, and to conduct technical investigations and inspections, including the determination of emissions and immissions, to check compliance with their obligations under § 95, the ordinances under § 95, or the interim ordinances under § 96. The persons obliged to provide information according to Paragraph 1 are furthermore obliged to allow access to the business and operational properties and premises outside of usual business hours, and access to residential premises, if this is necessary to prevent urgent dangers to public safety or order. The constitutional right to the inviolability of the home (Article 13 Paragraph 1 of the Basic Law) is restricted in this respect.
(3) Operators of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2 must make these facilities accessible to the employees or agents of the competent authority, and must provide the necessary workforce, tools, aids, especially fuels and propulsion units, and documents for the supervision, and allow the state authority to inspect the condition and operation of the facility at its own expense upon order of the competent authority.
(4) The official powers according to Paragraphs 1 to 3 also extend to checking whether certain substances or objects:
1. are not or no longer to be regarded as waste, or
2. are to be regarded as waste, other objects or materials for which the contamination value stipulated in an ordinance according to § 95 Paragraph 1 is undercut.
(5) For the persons obliged to provide information according to this provision, § 55 of the Code of Criminal Procedure applies accordingly.
(6) The knowledge and documents obtained according to Paragraphs 1 to 3 are not subject to §§ 93, 97, 105 Paragraph 1, § 111 Paragraph 5 in conjunction with § 105 Paragraph 1, or § 116 Paragraph 1 of the Fiscal Code. This does not apply if the tax authorities require the knowledge for the execution of a tax criminal procedure and an associated tax assessment procedure, for which there is an imperative public interest in prosecution, or if it concerns intentionally false statements by the person obliged to provide information or persons acting for them. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 96 Eilverordnungen
(1) In case of urgency after an emergency, the Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety may issue regulations according to §§ 93, 94, and 95 Paragraph 1, and the Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety or the ministry responsible for waste management regulations outside the scope of the Circular Economy Act or for regulations on the establishment and operation of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2, may issue regulations according to § 95 Paragraph 2 and 3 by ordinance without the consent of the Bundesrat and without the agreement of the participating federal ministries (Interim Ordinances), insofar as no corresponding regulations exist or the existing regulations are not adequate.
(2) Interim ordinances expire at the latest six months after their entry into force. Their duration can only be extended by an ordinance with the consent of the Bundesrat and in agreement with the participating federal ministries. Interim ordinances that change existing regulations must be repealed immediately if demanded by the Bundesrat. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 97 Gemeinsame Vorschriften für die Notfallpläne
(1) The Federation and the states shall draw up emergency plans according to §§ 98, 99, 100, and 101. These emergency plans must present the planned appropriate reactions to possible emergencies based on specific reference scenarios. The emergency reactions to be presented include:
1. Protection measures comprising:
    a) Measures to prevent or reduce exposure and contamination of man or environment, and
    b) Measures for medical treatment or prevention after an exposure.
2. Other measures to be taken by the participating authorities and other organizations in an emergency to prevent or keep the adverse effects of the emergency on human health or the environment as low as possible, particularly measures for the examination, preparation, execution, monitoring, amendment, or repeal of protective measures, as well as cooperation and coordination in emergencies.
(2) The emergency plans shall enable the participating authorities and organizations in the emergency to make coordinated decisions immediately and to carry out the appropriate measures in a timely manner.
(3) The authorities responsible for drafting the emergency plans:
1. shall coordinate their emergency plans insofar as this is necessary for the preparation of a coordinated emergency response, and
2. shall endeavor, within their respective responsibilities, to coordinate their emergency plans with other member states of the European Union and the European Atomic Community, as well as with third states according to the principles of reciprocity and equivalence.
(4) A select circle of representatives from science, the affected economy, environmental associations, municipalities and municipal associations, organizations involved in emergency preparedness and response, as well as other interested parties and the highest state authorities responsible for the respective area, shall be invited to the drafts of the emergency plans of the Federation, the ordinances according to §§ 93 to 95 and 117 Paragraph 1, and the drafts of essential amendments to these emergency plans and ordinances. Sentence 1 does not apply to the enactment of interim ordinances according to §§ 93 to 95 and 117 Paragraph 2, nor to the enactment, amendments, and supplements of ordinances and emergency plans for an emergency according to §§ 94 and 111. For the drafts of the general and special emergency plans of the states, a select circle of interested parties shall be invited by the state. The states may limit the invitation to relevant state or area-specific concretizations or supplements of the optimized protection strategies and measures provided for in the federal emergency plans. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 98 Allgemeiner Notfallplan des Bundes
(1) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety shall evaluate possible emergency exposure situations. The Federal Government shall issue a general emergency plan of the Federation based on its proposal. The general emergency plan of the Federation shall be adopted as a general administrative regulation with the consent of the Bundesrat.
(2) The general emergency plan of the Federation must include:
1. Reference scenarios that serve as the basis for the planning of emergency responses for the Federation and the states, and
2. The following general plans for possible emergencies within or outside the scope of this law:
    a) the plans of the Federation,
    b) the plans of the European Union and the European Atomic Community, their member states, and third states, and
    c) the plans of international organizations and the plans within the framework of international treaties.
(3) The general emergency plan of the Federation includes in particular:
1. Strategies optimally adapted to the respective reference scenario for the protection of the population and the emergency services, which also consider particularly vulnerable persons (optimized protection strategies), and
2. The further elements listed in Appendix 5.
The general emergency plan of the Federation may also contain indications of the emergency plans of the states, municipalities, and municipal associations, as well as of other organizations involved in emergency preparedness and response, or summarize these emergency plans. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 99 Besondere Notfallpläne des Bundes
(1) Upon proposal from the federal ministries responsible for the respective subject areas, the Federal Government supplements and concretizes the general emergency plan of the Federation with special emergency plans of the Federation. The special emergency plans of the Federation shall be adopted as general administrative regulations with the consent of the Bundesrat.
(2) The special emergency plans of the Federation must present the plans in particular for the following areas of application:
1. For disaster protection, general danger control and assistance, as well as for medical treatment and prevention after an exposure of the population and the emergency services,
2. For drinking water extraction and supply,
3. For the production of plant and animal products, for food, feed, necessities, cosmetic products, and products within the meaning of § 2 Number 1 of the Tobacco Products Act,
4. For medicinal products and their starting materials, as well as for medical devices,
5. For other products, objects, and materials,
6. For the transport of goods,
7. For the cross-border transport of persons, vehicles, goods, and baggage,
8. For contaminated areas, especially for contaminated properties and waters,
9. For the disposal of waste and the disposal of wastewater, as well as for the establishment and operation of the facilities mentioned in § 95 Paragraph 1 Sentence 2.
(3) The special emergency plans include in particular the elements listed in Appendix 6. § 98 Paragraph 3 Sentence 2 applies accordingly. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 100 Allgemeine und besondere Notfallpläne der Länder
The states shall draw up general and special emergency plans. These emergency plans of the states supplement and concretize the general emergency plan of the Federation and the special emergency plans of the Federation, insofar as the states are responsible for the planning or execution of protective measures. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 101 Externe Notfallpläne für ortsfeste Anlagen oder Einrichtungen mit besonderem Gefahrenpotential
(1) The authorities responsible for disaster protection or public safety shall draw up special protection plans (external emergency plans) according to their state law provisions for the surroundings of nuclear facilities, facilities according to § 9a Paragraph 3 Sentence 1 second sentence of the Atomic Energy Act, facilities for the generation of ionizing radiation, or facilities in the sense of § 5 Paragraph 12 of this law, insofar as emergencies in the facility or facility can lead to severe health impairments for a non-negligible number of persons in the vicinity of the facility or facility.
(2) The external emergency plans supplement and concretize the plans contained in the general and special emergency plans of the Federation and the states. They take into account the local conditions as well as the procedures and precautions of the radiation protection officer for the internal emergency protection of the facility. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 102 Notfallübungen
(1) The authorities and organizations involved in the emergency response according to the emergency plans of the Federation and the states, as well as those responsible for the training and further education of the emergency services according to § 115 Paragraph 1, shall regularly conduct emergency exercises.
(2) The emergency exercises must be appropriately differentiated according to the type of exercise, scope, emergency scenarios, and participants. In particular, the organizational precautions for the emergency response and the exchange of information and cooperation of the authorities, organizations, and radiation protection officers involved in the emergency response must be tested and practiced regarding:
a) situation assessment and situation evaluation,
b) the coordination of the decisions of the competent authorities, and
c) the execution of appropriate protective measures. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 103 Überprüfung und Änderung der Notfallpläne
(1) The emergency plans of the Federation and the states shall be regularly reviewed and, if necessary, amended, taking into account the experiences from emergency exercises, findings from emergencies domestically or abroad, and changes in the state of science and law.
(2) The information supplementing the emergency plans, such as the contact details of the competent authorities and participating organizations or the directories of applicable legal provisions, shall be updated and regularly reviewed upon changes. The deadlines for the reviews must be stipulated in the emergency plans. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 104 Beschaffung von Schutzwirkstoffen
(1) The authority responsible according to § 192 Paragraph 1 shall procure protective agents to the extent required for the supply of the population in the federal territory in the event of possible emergencies. It shall make these protective agents available to the states for stockpiling, distribution, and disposal to the population.
(2) Protective agents are medicinal products:
1. suitable for preventing the uptake of radioactive iodine into the human thyroid, or
2. suitable for preventing the uptake of radionuclides into the human body or for removing radionuclides from the human body. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 105 Information der Bevölkerung über die Schutzmaßnahmen und Empfehlungen für das Verhalten bei möglichen Notfällen
(1) The competent authorities of the Federation shall publish the emergency plans of the Federation according to § 10 of the Environmental Information Act.
(2) The competent authorities of the Federation shall:
1. Inform the population in an appropriate manner according to § 10 of the Environmental Information Act about:
    a) the basic concepts of radioactivity and the effects of radioactivity on man and the environment,
    b) the emergencies considered in the emergency plans and their consequences for the population and the environment, and
    c) planned measures for warning and protection of the population in case of possible emergencies, and
2. Give the population recommendations for behavior in case of possible emergencies.
(3) The states shall inform the population about the matters mentioned in Paragraph 2 Number 1 according to state law provisions and shall give recommendations for behavior in case of possible emergencies that supplement and concretize the recommendations according to Paragraph 2 Number 2.
(4) The information and recommendations for behavior must be updated regularly and upon essential changes and published without request in an updated version. They must be constantly accessible to the public. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]

### § 106 Radiologisches Lagezentrum des Bundes
(1) The Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, and Nuclear Safety shall establish a federal radiological situation center. [StrlSchG.md [Teil 7/13].md]
