The *Radon-Handbuch_BfS* provides comprehensive, technical guidelines for the assessment, measurement, and remediation of radon concentrations in indoor environments, detailing best practices for radiation protection beyond general regulatory requirements.

## Radon Exposure Assessment
The assessment of radon exposure is critical, as the resulting health risk is derived from long-term radon exposure [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].

### Measurement Principles
The determination of radon levels requires measurements in the most important living areas, ideally capturing annual mean values [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md]. The radon concentration in indoor rooms is dependent not only on the radon sources and diffusion paths but also significantly on weather conditions, the type of room usage, room climate control, and user behavior, leading to strong temporal fluctuations [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].

*   **Reference Values:** The BfS considers measures for reducing radon levels advisable from a value of 100 Bq/m³ in the annual mean in frequently used indoor rooms. The evaluation of measurement results checks whether the reference value of 300 Bq/m³ is exceeded [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Measurement Types:**
    *   **Long-Term Measurement:** Measurements should ideally cover a period of one year [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
    *   **Short-Term Measurement:** Short-term measurements can provide initial clues regarding sources and diffusion paths [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
    *   **Gas Tracing:** Using tracer gases and passive PFT (Polyfluorcarbon-Tracergas)-methods allows for a quantitative description of exchange processes between indoor and outdoor air, and between different rooms, providing detailed insights into diffusion paths and radon transport streams [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].

### Measurement Techniques
Several specialized techniques are recommended for accurate measurement:
*   **Radon Sniffing:** This technique involves using air-sucking probes at prominent cracks and discontinuities in materials to provide clues about whether these spots are relevant for radon diffusion [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Gamma Site Dose Rate (ODL) Measurement:** ODL measurements aim to identify materials with elevated Radium-226 concentrations. These measurements should be performed:
    *   In the immediate vicinity of the building to search for accumulations of material with elevated Radium-226 concentration, as well as for radiating passages and layers with elevated Radium concentration or other geogen-induced Radium anomalies (Note: The detection depth is only approx. 30 cm) [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
    *   Inside the building to locate construction and filling materials with elevated Radium-226 concentration [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
    *   The primary measurement point should be the center of the room at a height of 1 m above the floor. Secondary points should represent the ODL in 10 cm from the floor, ceiling, and wall surfaces [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Radon Sources and Pathways
High radon concentrations can result from multiple sources and pathways:
*   **Sources:** Primarily a geogenically elevated radon potential in the building subsoil. Other sources include surface-level mine structures that approach or run near the buildings, and building foundations/fillings using mine material [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Entry Pathways:** Key entry pathways for soil radon include:
    *   Insufficiently sealed ground-contact building areas (e.g., floors and walls of basements).
    *   Leaks in walls and floors.
    *   Material discontinuity points, such as next to utility lines.
    *   Building-connected sewage systems [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
*   **Diffusion Pathways:** Key diffusion pathways within buildings include:
    *   Free structural connections within the buildings (stair areas, doors, utility shafts, conduit runs).
    *   Non-radon-sealed floor/ceiling structures.
    *   Leaky masonry, material discontinuity, and instability points [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].

## Remediation and Mitigation Strategies
Remediation efforts must be based on a comprehensive understanding of the sources, entry paths, and diffusion paths [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].

### General Principles
1.  **Site Assessment:** The analysis of site-specific factors (permeability of the subsoil, influence of mining, groundwater level) is as important as the radon measurements themselves [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
2.  **Design Solutions (Neubau/Umbau):** Architectural solutions, such as appropriate structuring of buildings and arranging rooms with short occupancy times in basement areas, can influence the radon problem [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
3.  **Technical Measures:** Implementing radon-sealed construction structures and elements in new builds, or sealing measures in renovations (e.g., sealing utility lines from the subsoil or sealing connecting doors against radon-affected rooms), can prevent radon entry and diffusion. These measures must also be designed to avoid harmful condensation [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
4.  **Ventilation:** Creating defined pressure differences (negative or positive pressure) can reduce or eliminate radon entry. When operating under positive pressure, care must be taken to prevent building-damaging condensation, especially at low outdoor temperatures [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
5.  **Quality Control:** Success depends on continuous monitoring. Measurements must be conducted to verify the effectiveness of implemented measures, such as checking the radon-tightness of newly installed structural elements or the sealing effectiveness of injection points [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].

### Measurement Standards
For high-quality results, specific standards must be adhered to:
*   **Passive Devices:** For passive radon measuring devices, adherence to DIN 11665-4 is recommended.
*   **Electronic Devices:** For electronic radon measuring devices, DIN EN 61577-2 is recommended [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md [Teil 2/5].md].
