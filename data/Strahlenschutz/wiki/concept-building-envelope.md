The building envelope refers to the physical boundary of a structure that separates the conditioned interior environment from the external environment. In the context of radiation protection, maintaining the integrity of this envelope is crucial for preventing the ingress of contaminants, such as radon gas [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md].

## Function in Radon Protection
For radon mitigation, the building envelope must function as a highly impermeable barrier. The goal is to create a "closed geometric body" [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md].
*   **Air Tightness:** The source material specifies that fresh air supply must come through designated openings or ventilation systems, and the exhaust air must be clearly defined. An uncontrolled air exchange through undefined leaks in the building envelope is insufficient for radon protection [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md].
*   **Sealing:** All structural boundaries, including basement walls, floors, and utility penetrations, must be assessed for potential leakages. The sealing of utility lines (electrical, plumbing, gas) connecting the building to the ground is a critical component of envelope integrity [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md].

## Inspection Points
A thorough inspection of the building envelope must cover:
*   **Boundaries:** Mapping all structures and rooms that border the ground.
*   **Basement/Foundation:** Detailed inspection of basement walls against the ground, checking for cracks, holes, and the condition of joints to the floor and ceiling [Radon-Handbuch_BfS.md].
*   **Utility Penetrations:** Assessing the sealing of all pipes and lines passing through the envelope.
