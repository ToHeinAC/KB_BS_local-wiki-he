# Sanierungsplan (Remediation Plan)

A Sanierungsplan is a mandatory, comprehensive document required when dealing with radioactive contaminated sites (radioaktive Altlasten) under the Strahlenschutzgesetz (StrlSchG). It serves as the blueprint for mitigating radiation risks associated with contaminated properties.

## Purpose and Requirement
The plan is required when:
1.  The contamination is complex, necessitating a coordinated approach.
2.  The contamination poses significant risks to the individual or the general public due to its type or extent [summary-strlschg-md-teil-9-13.md].

## Required Contents
The plan must contain specific, detailed information, including:
1.  A presentation of the results from all conducted investigations, detailing the type and extent of the radioactive contaminated site, along with a summary of the exposure assessment.
2.  Information regarding the previous and future intended use of the contaminated properties.
3.  A detailed presentation of the planned remediation measures, other measures to prevent or reduce exposure, and necessary follow-up measures [summary-strlschg-md-teil-9-13.md].

## Authority Oversight
The competent authority has the power to:
*   Require the submission of the plan [summary-strlschg-md-teil-9-13.md].
*   Declare the plan, even with amendments or supplementary provisions, as legally binding (verbindlich) [summary-strlschg-md-teil-9-13.md].
*   Create the plan itself or commission its creation from an expert [summary-strlschg-md-teil-9-13.md].

## Financial and Legal Aspects
*   **Cost Bearing:** The costs of measures ordered under the plan are generally borne by the responsible parties [summary-strlschg-md-teil-9-13.md].
*   **Value Compensation:** If public funds are used for remediation, the owner may be subject to a value compensation payment (Wertausgleich) for the resulting increase in property value, unless specific exemptions apply [summary-strlschg-md-teil-9-13.md].
