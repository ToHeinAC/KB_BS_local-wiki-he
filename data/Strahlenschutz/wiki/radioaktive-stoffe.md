## Key facts
- Radioaktive Stoffe

- Legal Application

Radioactive substances are defined within the *Strahlenschutzgesetz* (StrlSchG) as all substances containing one or more radionuclides whose activity or specific activity cannot be disregarded according to the law or related ordinances [StrlSchG.md, § 3 (1)].

### Composition and Scope
*   **Radionuclides:** The definition covers substances containing radionuclides [StrlSchG.md, § 3 (1)].
*   **Core Fuels:** Specific mention is made of core fuels, including Plutonium 239 and Plutonium 241, and uranium enriched with isotopes 235 or 233 [StrlSchG.md, § 3 (1)].
*   **Exclusions:** The law specifies conditions under which the activity or specific activity of a substance can be disregarded, such as when it falls below set release limits or if it is a naturally occurring substance not used as core fuel [StrlSchG.md, § 3 (2)].

### Legal Application
The management of radioactive substances is critical across various aspects of radiation protection:
*   **Activities:** Handling, storage, and transport of these substances constitute regulated "activities" under the StrlSchG [StrlSchG.md, § 4 (1)].
*   **Waste:** The law addresses the handling of radioactive waste and residues [StrlSchG.md, § 5 (1)].
*   **Altlasten:** The concept is directly linked to the management of radioactive contaminated sites (Altlasten) [StrlSchG.md, § 136].
